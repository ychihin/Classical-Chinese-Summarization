Since the invention of writing, the ancient figures that can be understood are those mentioned in scriptures. Before Tang and Yu, emperors had posthumous titles, but there was no record of the ministers who assisted them.
However, the scholars mentioned them many times. Although the scholars did not examine Confucius's books, they still recorded these ministers in the books for the purpose of highlighting good deeds, exposing evil deeds, and persuading and warning future generations.
So I adopt this material broadly. Confucius said: If sage and benevolent, how dare I.
He also said: Whatever happens to people must be holy.
For knowledge, how can one gain benevolence.
Those who know by birth are the best; those who know by learning are the second; those who are tired and do not learn are the worst. He also said: The average person and above can speak superior words, but the superior wisdom and inferior foolishness will not change.
The scriptures explain it this way: For example, if you do good deeds to Yao, Shun, Yu, Ji, and Qi, you will have a smooth career. If you want to do evil with them, you will be killed.
Being able to do good things with them but not doing evil things with them is called superior wisdom.
Xia Jie, Shang Zhou, Guan Longfeng and Prince Bigan were killed if they wanted to do good deeds with them, while Yu Xin, Chonghou and they could have a smooth career if they did evil deeds with them.
Being able to do evil to them but not being good to them is called stupidity.
Duke Huan of Qi, if Guan Zhong assists him, he will become the overlord; if Shu Diao assists him, the country will be in chaos. If you can do good to him, you can also do evil to him, this is called a middle man.
Based on this, I arranged the nine-level order, exhausting the Sutras, successive generations, and sorting them one by one. Overall, I compiled a complete summary of ancient and modern times.
