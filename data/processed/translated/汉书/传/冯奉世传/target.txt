Feng Fengshi, courtesy name Ziming, was a native of Lu County, Shangdang County, and later moved to Duling.
His ancestor Feng Ting was the governor of Shangdang County in South Korea.
Qin attacked Shangdang and blocked the passage in the Taihang Mountains. South Korea could not defend it, so Feng Ting dedicated Shangdang City to Zhao and defended it.
The State of Zhao granted Feng Ting the title of Lord of Huayang, and together with General Zhao Kuo of the State of Zhao, he resisted the State of Qin and died in battle in Changping.
Since then, the Feng family has dispersed in various places, some staying in Lu County and some traveling to other places.
Those in Zhao became official generals, and the sons of official generals became prime ministers of the country.
After Qin destroyed the six kingdoms, Feng Ting's descendants Feng Wuze, Feng Quji, and Feng Jie all became Qin's generals.
After the rise of the Han Dynasty, Feng Tang was very famous during the reign of Emperor Wen. He was the son of the Prime Minister of Dai.
By the end of Emperor Wu's reign, Feng Fengshi was elected as a Langguan because he was a son of a good family.
During the reign of Emperor Zhao, he was appointed as the Chang Palace of Wu'an County in accordance with the size and order of his achievements.
When he was dismissed from office, he was already in his thirties. He only studied "Spring and Autumn", studied its subtle meanings, read the art of war, and knew its contents and examples. Former general Han Zeng reported to him and appointed him as the military commander.
During this period, he accompanied the army to attack the Xiongnu.
After the war, he became a Langguan again.
Prior to this, the Han Dynasty sent envoys to the Western Regions several times, but most of them failed to fulfill their mission, some were corrupt, and some were made difficult and insulted by foreign countries.
At this time, the Wusun Kingdom was very powerful and had the merit of attacking the Xiongnu. At the same time, the countries in the Western Regions had just reconciled with the Han Dynasty. The Han Dynasty was preparing to treat them well. If they wanted to take the opportunity to appease these countries, they selected people who could be sent as envoys to foreign countries.
Former general Han Zeng recommended Feng Fengshi to escort guests from Dawan and other countries back home as an envoy.
When they arrived at Yixiu City, Captain Song said that the Shache people, together with some other countries, had attacked and killed King Wannian of Shache appointed by the Han Dynasty, and also killed Xi Chongguo, the envoy of the Han Dynasty.
At this time, the Xiongnu sent troops to attack Cheshi City again, but they went back because they failed to capture it.
The Shache Kingdom sent envoys to threaten that the Northern Kingdoms had belonged to the Xiongnu. At that time, they attacked and plundered the Southern Kingdoms, and made a blood alliance with them to betray the Han Dynasty, cutting off all traffic from the Shanshan Kingdom to the west.
The Protector Zheng Ji and the Colonel Sima Yi were both trapped among the countries on the North Road.
Feng Fengshi discussed with his deputy Yan Lu and believed that if they did not attack quickly, the Shache Kingdom would become increasingly powerful, making the situation difficult to control and jeopardizing the entire Western Region.
So he notified the kings by envoys and mobilized their armies. A total of 15,000 people from the north and south attacked the Shache Kingdom and captured its city.
King Shache committed suicide and his head was sent to Chang'an.
All the countries were pacified, and Feng Fengshi's reputation shocked the Western Regions.
Feng Fengshi withdrew his troops and returned home, reporting the situation to the court.
Emperor Xuan summoned Han Zeng and said: Congratulations to the general on the competent person he recommended.
Feng Fengshi traveled west to the Kingdom of Dayuan.
The Kingdom of Dawan heard that he had killed King Shache and respected him more than other envoys.
After getting the famous horse Xianglong from Dayuan, he returned to Chang'an.
The emperor was very happy and issued an order to discuss the matter of rewarding Feng Fengshi.
The prime minister and the generals all said: The meaning of "Spring and Autumn" is that when an official is on a mission abroad, if he encounters something that is beneficial to the country, he can take matters into his own hands.
Feng Fengshi's contribution is particularly significant, and he should be awarded a title and land.
Xiao Wangzhi, the Shaofu, believed that Feng Feng was on an envoy mission and had his mission, but he pretended to be the emperor's order and went against the will and sent troops from various countries. Although he had merit, he should not be used as an example for future generations.
If Feng Fengshi is to be rewarded, it will open the door for future envoys to use Feng Fengshi as an example to mobilize armies, claim credit and reward from thousands of miles away, and cause trouble for the country among the Yi and Di tribes.
This is an unacceptable example, and Feng Fengshi should not be rewarded.
The emperor thought Xiao Wangzhi's suggestion was good, and granted Feng Fengshi the titles of Doctor Guanglu and Captain Shuiheng.
Emperor Yuan came to the throne, and Feng Fengshi was appointed as Jinwu.
More than 10,000 Hu people from the vassal state of Shangjun surrendered to the Han Dynasty and rebelled.
At the beginning, in the last years of Emperor Zhao's reign, King Yiruo, a Hu from the Xihe County vassal state, also led thousands of people to rebel. Feng Fengshi immediately led an envoy and led an army to pursue them.
Chang Hui, the right general of the Dian Kingdom, passed away, and Feng Fengshi succeeded him as the right general of the Dian Kingdom, adding the titles of various officials.
A few years later, he became Guang Luxun.
In the autumn of the second year of Yongguang's reign, a branch of the Qiang village sisters in Longxi County rebelled. The emperor summoned Prime Minister Wei Xuancheng, imperial censor Zheng Hong, Da Sima Cavalry General Wang Jie, left general Xu Jia, and right general Feng Fengshi to come to the court to discuss countermeasures.
At that time, the crops were not harvested in consecutive years, and grain was sold for more than 200 fen per stone in Kyoto, 400 fen per stone in border counties, and 500 fen per stone in Kanto.
There was famine everywhere, and the court was worried about it, but it encountered a rebellion by the Qiang people.
Wei Xuancheng and others were silent and no one spoke.
Feng Fengshi said: The Qiang bandits are rebelling within the borders of the country. If they are not killed in time, there will be no way to subdue the barbarians far away.
I am willing to lead an army against them.
The emperor asked about the number of troops needed, and replied: I heard that those who are good at using troops will not use troops twice or transport food three times. Therefore, the army should not be deployed for a long time, and the crusade in obedience to God's will should be carried out quickly.
In the past, we often failed to measure the enemy's situation, and the army suffered losses; transporting food multiple times would take a long time and consume a lot of time, and the morale of the army would be low.
There are currently about 30,000 rebel soldiers. According to the law of war, an additional 60,000 people should be mobilized.
The Qiang Rong were an army that only used bows and spears. Their weapons were not sharp. They could mobilize 40,000 men and a month would be enough to solve the problem.
The prime minister, the censor, and the two generals all believed that the people were in the harvest season and could not send more troops; 10,000 troops were stationed for defense, which was almost enough.
Feng Fengshi said: No.
The country suffered from famine, the soldiers and horses were thin and few in number, and the war equipment had been left unmaintained for a long time. All the Yi and Di tribes had the intention to despise the border officials, so the Qiang people were the first to attack.
Now ten thousand men are divided into several places to defend several places. The traitors will not be afraid when they see that our army is small in number. In battle, the army will be damaged and morale will be low, and the rules will not be able to save the people.
If this happens and the cowardice is revealed, the Qiang people will take the opportunity to attack, and all the ethnic minorities will respond together and incite each other to raise troops. I am afraid that the Han Dynasty will have to collect more than 40,000 soldiers. This cannot be solved by money.
Therefore, sending less troops will take a long time. Compared with quickly solving the problem in one battle, the benefits and risks are ten thousand times different.
Feng Fengshi persisted in fighting but could not get more troops.
Another edict was issued to increase the number of 2,000 people.
Immediately dispatched Feng Fengshi to lead 12,000 soldiers and horses to set off, in the name of leading troops to farm.
Han Chang, the captain of the Dian Kingdom and the military guard, was appointed as the deputy general and went to Longxi County to station troops in three places.
Dian Zhuguo was appointed as the right army, stationed at Baishi; the guard captain Han Chang was appointed as the front army, stationed at Lintao; Feng Fengshi was appointed as the central army, stationed at Xiji Mountain in Shouyang County.
When the former army arrived at Tongban, they first sent a school lieutenant to compete with the Qiang people in front for favorable terrain, and then sent another school lieutenant to Guangyang Valley to rescue the people.
There were so many Qiang bandits that the officers and soldiers were defeated and two captains were killed.

With the outstanding qualities of a general, leading elite troops to attack unruly bandits, there is a principle of victory in every battle.
Now he actually has the reputation of not daring to attack in the face of battle, which is too embarrassing for the Han Dynasty.
Is it because I was not familiar with military affairs before?

I'm very strange.

In the past, because the general lacked soldiers to defend, he dispatched nearby cavalry and rushed there day and night, not for offense.
Sanfu, Hedong, Hongnong Yueqi, Jishe, Youfei, Yuzhe, Yulin Orphan, Husuqi and Zhengzhong are currently being mobilized at a rapid pace.
War is a dangerous thing, and there will definitely be success or failure. I am afraid that the strategy is not agreed upon in advance, and it is not prudent to understand the enemy's situation, so I send General Fenwu.
The Art of War says that when a general goes on an expedition, he must have a supporting general to show off his power and provide advice. What doubts does the general have?
Care for the soldiers, gain military morale, and you will not regret your decision. You must be thorough in capturing the enemy. This is the responsibility of the general.
As for the transportation costs, special officials are responsible for them, so the general does not have to worry.
Wait until General Fenwu's troops arrive and attack the Qiang bandits together.
In October, the army gathered in Longxi County.
In November, attack together.
The Qiang thieves were defeated and thousands of their heads were beheaded, while the rest fled across the border.
When the outcome of the war was still undecided, the Han Dynasty recruited another 10,000 soldiers and appointed Han Anguo, the prefect of Dingxiang, as General Jianwei.
Before setting off, I heard that the Qiang rebels had been defeated. The emperor said: The Qiang rebels were defeated and punished, and fled from the border. Those soldiers who had a truce should leave more field guards to defend key points.
In February of the next year, Feng Fengshi returned to the capital and was appointed General Zuo, while Guang Luxun's position remained unchanged.
After recording his meritorious service and conferring a title, the emperor issued an edict saying: The Qiang thieves were cruel and cunning, killing officials and common people, attacking Longxi County government offices, burning post houses, and cutting off roads and bridges, which greatly violated the law of heaven.
