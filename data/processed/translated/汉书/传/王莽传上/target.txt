Wang Mang, also known as Jujun, is the nephew of Queen Xiaoyuan.
Empress Yuan's father and brothers all became marquises during the reigns of Emperor Yuan and Emperor Cheng of the Han Dynasty, held important positions, and assisted in government affairs. There were nine people in the family who were granted the title of marquis and five chief ministers. These words are recorded in the "Biography of the Empress of the Yuan Dynasty".
Only Wang Mang's father, Wang Man, died earlier and was not granted a title.
Wang Mang's uncles and brothers were all the sons of generals and marquises. They took advantage of the time when their fathers were powerful and lived a luxurious and lavish life. They showed off and compared each other with tall carriages and horses, talented singers and concubines, beautiful concubines, and novel games. Wang Mang was the only one who was lonely and poor, so he treated others with humility and lived a simple life.
He worshiped Chen Shen, a native of Pei County, as his teacher, studied "Etiquette" and "Zhou Rites", worked diligently and extensively, and dressed like an ordinary scholar.
He served his mother and widowed sister-in-law, raised his fatherless nephew, and behaved with great caution.
In addition, he made friends with outstanding talents outside and served his uncles and uncles in the family with tact, thoughtfulness and courtesy.
During the Yangshuo period, his uncle, the general Wang Feng, fell ill. Wang Mang served him and took the medicine himself. He did not comb his hair or wash his face, resulting in unkempt appearance. He went to bed without taking off his clothes for several months.
When Wang Feng was dying, she entrusted Wang Mang to the Queen Mother and Emperor Cheng. He was appointed Huang Menlang and was later promoted to Shesheng Xiaowei.
After a long time, his uncle Chengdu Marquis Wang Shang wrote to the emperor, expressing his willingness to allocate his own households to Wang Mang. Others, such as Changle Shaofu Dai Chong, Shi Zhong Jin She, Hu Qi Xiao Lieutenant Ji Hong, Shanggu Du Wei Yang Bing and Zhonglang Chen Tang, were all very famous people at the time. They all spoke for Wang Mang. From then on, the emperor considered Wang Mang to be a wise man.
In the first year of Yongshi, Emperor Cheng granted Wang Mang the title of Marquis of Xindu. The Marquis was built in the capital of Xinye County, Nanyang County, with 1,500 households.
Later he was promoted to the rank of Cavalry Captain and Guanglu Doctor, and he served as a guard in the palace. He was cautious and considerate. As his official position became higher and higher, his attitude became more and more humble.
He distributed carriages, horses, and light warm clothing, and gave alms to the guests, so that there was no extra clothing in the house.
He accepted and supported famous people and made friends with many generals, prime ministers, and officials.
Therefore, people in important positions recommended him more, and celebrities in the society advocated for him. His reputation spread throughout the government and the public, surpassing his uncles.
He dares to behave in a pretentious manner without feeling ashamed.
Wang Mang's brother Wang Yong, who had served in various Cao Cao positions, had died long ago. He had a son named Wang Guang, and Wang Mang asked him to study under a doctor.
When Wang Mang was on vacation, he drove his chariot and horses, brought sheep and wine, and went to comfort his teacher. He even gave gifts to his classmates.
It attracted many scholars to watch and the old people admired it.
