Suihong, courtesy name Meng, was from the state of Lu and Fan.
He was young, fashionable and chivalrous, and liked cockfighting and horse racing. Later, his style changed drastically and he studied "Spring and Autumn" with Ying Gong.
Because he was proficient in Confucian classics, he became a Yilang, and he reached the official position of Fuli Jieliang.
In the first month of the third year of Emperor Zhao Yuanfeng's reign, there was a roar like thousands of people coming together from the south of Laiwu Mountain in Mount Tai. When the people went to look, they saw a big stone standing up by itself. It was one foot to five feet high, as thick as forty-eight people surrounded it, and it was eight feet deep into the ground. There were three other stones as the feet of the big stone.
After the boulder stood on its own, thousands of white crows flew down and gathered around it.
At the same time, the dead trees in the Changyi Shrine and Temple came to life again, and the big willow tree that had been broken and withered and lying on the ground in Shanglin Garden actually stood up on its own and regained its vitality. There were many insects eating the leaves of the tree, and the leftover leaves were shaped like these words: Gongsun Bing has been established. Suihong then deduced the main idea of ​​"Spring and Autumn" and believed that: Stones and willows are both negative objects, symbolizing the common people at the bottom, while Mount Tai is the head of the mountains, and it is the place where emperors worshiped heaven to repay their merits after the change of dynasties.
Nowadays, big rocks stand on their own and dry willows come back to life. They are not made by human beings. Their creation shows that ordinary people will become emperors.
The resurrection of dead trees in the temple symbolizes the revival of the previously deposed Gongsun clan.
Suihong didn't know where the Gongsun family was, so he said: My ancestor Dong Zhongshu once said that even if there is a monarch who succeeds the emperor and abides by virtue, it will not prevent the saint from receiving orders from heaven.
The Han family is a descendant of Yao, and they are destined to pass on their surname to the country. Emperor Mo should tell the world about it.
He solicited talented people and abdicated the throne to him, while he abdicated and granted a land of hundreds of miles, just like the descendants of King Zhou of Yin, in order to obey the destiny.
Suihong asked his friend who was an internal official to write this book for him.
