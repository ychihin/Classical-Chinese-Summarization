Qin sent Bai Qi, Lord Wu'an, to attack and defeated the Zhao army in Changping. All 400,000 surrendered soldiers were killed.
In October of the 48th year, South Korea presented Yuan Yong.
The Qin army was divided into three armies.
Lord Wu An returns to his country.
Wang Lu led his troops to attack Wu'an and Pilao of Zhao and occupied them.
Sima Geng moved north to Taiyuan and occupied all of Han's Shangdang County.
In the first month of the year, the war ceased and Shangdang County was defended.
In October of that year, Wu Dafuling attacked Handan, the capital of Zhao.
In the first month of the forty-ninth year, conscription was expanded to reinforce the mausoleum.
Ling's performance was poor and he was dismissed from his post, and Wang Lu was appointed to lead the army in his place.
In October of that year, General Zhang Tang attacked Wei. Because Cai Wei abandoned the city and did not defend it, he was beheaded after returning.
In October of the fiftieth year, Bai Qi, Lord Wu'an, committed a crime and was demoted to the rank of scholar, and was assigned to the secret service.
Zhang Tang attacked Zheng and captured it.
In December, the army was recruited and stationed near Fencheng.
Bai Qi, Lord Wu'an, committed a crime and was executed.
Wang Luo attacked Handan but failed to capture it, so he withdrew and rushed back to Fencheng to garrison for more than two months.
Attacked the Wei army and beheaded 6,000 people, and 20,000 people from the Wei and Chu coalition forces died in the Yellow River.
Attacked Fencheng, and then followed Zhang Tang's army to capture Ningxinzhong, which was renamed Anyang.
The Yellow River Bridge was built for the first time.
In the fifty-first year, the general attacked Han, captured Yangcheng and Fushu, and beheaded 40,000 people.
Attacked Zhao State, occupied more than 20 counties, beheaded 90,000 prisoners.
The king of the Western Zhou Dynasty betrayed Qin, joined forces with the princes, and led the world's best troops out of Yique to attack Qin, preventing Qin from reaching Yangcheng.
Therefore, Qin ordered his generals to attack the Western Zhou Dynasty.
The king of Western Zhou hurriedly came to surrender, kowtowed and confessed his crime, and gave all his thirty-six cities with a population of thirty thousand.
The King of Qin accepted the offer and released him back to Zhou.
In the fifty-second year, the people of the Western Zhou Dynasty fled to the Eastern Zhou Dynasty. The nine tripods, Zhou's treasures, were moved to the Qin State, and the Zhou Dynasty was initially destroyed.
Fifty-three years later, the whole world came to submit.
Wei fell last, so Qin sent generals to attack Wei and captured Wucheng.
The king of Han came to see him; the king of Wei also entrusted the country to Qin to obey his orders.
In the fifty-fourth year, the King of Qin worshiped the Emperor of Heaven in the southern suburbs of Yongcheng.
In the autumn of the fifty-sixth year, King Zhaoxiang died, and his son King Xiaowen came to the throne.
The eighth son of the Tang Dynasty was respected as the Empress Dowager of the Tang Dynasty and was buried with King Zhaoxiang. The King of Han put on mourning clothes to express his condolences.
The princes also sent their generals to express their condolences and attend the funeral.
In the first year of King Xiaowen's reign, criminals were granted amnesty, the former king's meritorious officials were still reused, clan relatives were given preferential treatment, and the royal family's gardens were opened to the public.
King Xiaowen's period of mourning expired and he ascended the throne on Jihai day in October. He died on Xinchou day in just three days, and his son King Zhuang Xiang came to the throne.
In the first year of King Zhuang Xiang's reign, he granted a general amnesty to criminals. He still reused the heroes of the previous king, extended benevolence, treated relatives favorably, and extended grace to the people.
The king of Eastern Zhou and the princes planned to attack Qin. Qin sent Prime Minister Lu Buwei to attack him and merge all his lands into Qin's territory.
However, Qin did not interrupt his sacrificial tradition and gave the land of Yangren to Zhou Jun, allowing him to continue his sacrificial rituals.
He sent Meng Ao to attack Han, and Han sacrificed Cheng Gao and Gong.
Qin's territory expanded to Daliang, and Sanchuan County was established.
In the second year, he sent Meng Ao to attack Zhao and pacify Taiyuan.
In the third year, Meng Ao attacked Wei's Gaodu and Ji and captured them.
Meng Ao also attacked Yuci, Xincheng and Langmeng of Zhao State, capturing thirty-seven cities.
A solar eclipse occurs in April.
Wang Lu attacked Shangdang and began to establish Taiyuan County.
Wei general Wuji led the troops of the five countries to counterattack the Qin army, and the Qin army retreated to the south of the Yellow River.
Meng Ao lost the battle, got rid of the siege and evacuated.
On Bingwu in the fifth month, King Zhuang Xiang died, and his son Ying Zheng ascended the throne, becoming the first emperor of Qin.
In the twenty-sixth year after King Yingzheng of Qin came to the throne, he began to unite the world and establish thirty-six counties, known as the First Emperor.
The First Emperor died at the age of fifty-one, and his son Hu Hai ascended the throne and became the second emperor.
In the third year of the Second Emperor's reign, the princes rebelled against the Qin Dynasty one after another. Zhao Gao killed the Second Emperor and made Ziying emperor.
More than a month after Ziying took the throne, the princes killed him again, and the Qin Dynasty was destroyed.
The detailed description can be found in "The Chronicles of Qin Shihuang".
