Confucius was born in Zouyi, Changping Township, Lu State.
His ancestor was from the Song Dynasty, named Uncle Kong Fang.
Uncle Fang gave birth to Bo Xia, and Bo Xia gave birth to Shu Liang He.
Shu Lianghe and Yehe, a woman named Yan, gave birth to Confucius. They got Confucius after they went to Niqiu Mountain to pray to the gods.
Confucius was born in the 22nd year of Duke Xiang of Lu.
The top of his head was concave when he was born, so he was named Qiu. The courtesy name is Zhongni and the surname is Kong.
Shortly after Confucius was born, his uncle Liang He died and was buried in Fangshan.
Fangshan was in the east of Lu State, so Confucius could not know for sure where his father's tomb was. His mother did not tell him where his father was buried.
When Confucius was a child, he played games, often setting up various sacrificial vessels and learning the ritual movements of sacrificial offerings.
After Confucius's mother died, she temporarily placed her coffin in the Five Fathers' Road without burying her. This was out of prudent consideration.
The mother of Wan's father, who was from Zouyi, told him the burial place of Confucius's father, and then Confucius moved his mother to Fangshan to be buried with his father.
When Confucius was mourning with a filial piety belt tied around his waist, Ji Sun held a banquet to entertain celebrities, and Confucius went to attend.
Ji Sun's retainer Yang Hu stopped him and said: "Ji's family is entertaining celebrities, but you are not invited."
Confucius withdrew because of this.
When Confucius was seventeen years old, Lizi, a senior official in the state of Lu, was critically ill. Before his death, he warned his son Yizi: Confucius is a descendant of a saint, and his ancestors perished in the Song Dynasty.
His ancestor Fufu He was supposed to succeed as the king of Song Dynasty, but he gave way to his younger brother Li Gong.
When another of his ancestors, Zheng Kao's father, assisted Song Dai Gong, Song Wu Gong, and Song Xuangong in three dynasties, he was more respectful every time he was given orders. Therefore, the inscription on Zheng Kao's father's tripod says: The first appointment was received with a bow, the second appointment was received with a bow, and the third appointment was bowed and received.
When I walked, I avoided the middle of the road and walked against the wall, and no one dared to bully me. I just made batter and porridge in this cauldron to survive.
His frugality has reached this point.
I heard that although the descendants of saints may not necessarily become kings and govern, there will definitely be people with outstanding talents and virtues.
Now Confucius is young and courteous. Isn't he a man of outstanding talents and virtue?
If I die, you must take him as your teacher.
After Meng Lizi died, Meng Yizi and Nangong Jingshu, a native of Lu, went to Confucius to learn etiquette.
This year, Ji Wuzi died, and Pingzi succeeded to the throne.
Confucius' family was poor and of low status.
When he came of age, he worked as a clerk in charge of the Ji family's warehouse, and he was a cashier who could calculate money and grain fairly and accurately. He also worked as a clerk in charge of pastures, and he was able to breed livestock well.
Therefore, he was promoted to Sikong in charge of construction projects.
Not long after, he left the state of Lu, was ostracized in the state of Qi, was expelled in the states of Song and Wei, was besieged between the states of Chen and Cai, and finally returned to the state of Lu.
Confucius was nine feet six inches tall. People called him "The Long Man" because they thought he was different from ordinary people.
The State of Lu was still very kind to him, so he returned to the State of Lu.
Nangong Jingshu, a native of Lu, said to Duke Zhao of Lu: Please allow me to go on a mission to Zhou with Confucius.
Duke Zhao of Lu gave him a carriage, two horses, and a boy servant, and set off with him to the Kingdom of Zhou to learn etiquette and pay a visit to Lao Tzu.
When they were saying goodbye, Lao Tzu came out to see them off and said: I heard that wealthy people give gifts with property, and people with noble moral character give gifts with words.
I am not a wealthy person, so I can only steal the name of a noble person and send you off with words. These sentences are: A wise and thoughtful person is often threatened with death because he likes to talk about others; a knowledgeable, eloquent and insightful person is often in trouble and endangers himself, because he likes to expose the sins of others.
Children should not have selfish motives, and ministers should not think only of themselves but the monarch. After Confucius returned to the state of Lu from Zhou Dynasty, more and more disciples followed him.
At this time, Jin Pinggong was promiscuous and immoral, and the six ministers of the Han, Zhao, Wei, Zhonghang, Fan, and Zhi families controlled the state affairs and constantly sent troops to attack the feudal states in the east. King Ling of Chu had a strong army and often invaded other countries in the Central Plains; Qi was a big country and close to Lu.
The state of Lu was small and weak. If it surrendered to the state of Chu, it would anger the state of Jin. If it surrendered to the state of Jin, it would cause the state of Chu to attack. If it did not serve Qi well, Qi's army would invade the state of Lu.
Lu Zhaogong was twenty years old and Confucius was thirty years old.
Duke Jinggong of Qi and Yan Ying came to the state of Lu. Duke Jinggong asked Confucius: "In the past, Duke Qin's country was small and remote. Why was he able to dominate?"
Confucius replied: Although the Qin State is small, its ambitions are great; although its location is remote, its governance is very appropriate.
Duke Mu of Qin personally pulled Baili Xi, who had been redeemed with five black ram skins, and awarded him the title of a great official. As soon as he was released from detention, Qin Jun discussed with him for three days, and then let him take charge of the state affairs.
Using this spirit to govern the country can even rule the entire world. Being an overlord is not a big deal.
Jinggong was very happy after hearing this.
When Confucius was thirty-five years old, Ji Pingzi offended Lu Zhaogong for having a cockfight with Li Zhaobo. Zhaogong led his army to attack Pingzi. Pingzi, the Mengsun family and the Shusun family allied with him to attack Zhaogong. Zhaogong's army was defeated and fled to Qi State. Qi State placed Zhaogong in Qianhou.
Not long after that, an incident occurred in the state of Lu.
Confucius came to Qi State and became Gao Zhaozi's retainer, hoping to use Gao Zhaozi's relationship to get close to Jinggong.
Confucius was talking about music with the music officials of Qi State. When he heard Shun's "Shao" music, he started to study it. He was so intoxicated that he couldn't taste meat for three months. The people of Qi State praised him.
Qi Jinggong asked Confucius for advice on how to govern. Confucius said: The king should be like a king, the ministers should be like ministers, the father should be like father, and the son should be like son.
After hearing this, Jinggong said: "That's very good!"
If the king does not look like a king, the ministers do not look like ministers, the father does not look like a father, and the son does not look like a son, even if there is a lot of food, how can I eat it?
Another day, Jinggong asked Confucius for advice on the principles of government. Confucius said: The most important thing in managing a country is to save money.
Jinggong was very happy to hear this and planned to reward Confucius with the fields in Nixi.
Yan Ying dissuaded and said: Those Confucian scholars are eloquent, but the law cannot be used to restrain them; they are arrogant and self-righteous, and cannot be used by subordinates; they advocate funeral etiquette, exhaust their grief, and are willing to bankrupt their fortunes for a grand funeral. This practice cannot be allowed to become a trend; they lobby everywhere and beg for official salary, and they cannot be used to govern the country.
Since those sages passed away one after another, the Zhou royal family has also declined, and rituals have collapsed for some time.
Nowadays, Confucius pays attention to appearance and clothing, details the complicated etiquette of going to and from court, and deliberately pursues the deportment of hands and feet. These red tapes can be learned in several generations and cannot be understood in a lifetime.
If you want to use this set of rules to change the customs of Qi, I'm afraid this is not a good way to guide the people.
After that, although Qi Jinggong received Confucius politely, he no longer asked questions about etiquette.
One day, Jinggong comforted Confucius and said: I can't give you the same high treatment as I gave to the Ji family.
So I gave Confucius the same treatment as the Jisun family in the upper Qing Dynasty and the Mengsun family in the lower Qing Dynasty.
Some of the officials of Qi wanted to harm Confucius, and Confucius heard about it.
Jinggong said to Confucius: I am old and can no longer use you.
Confucius then left Qi and returned to Lu.
When Confucius was forty-two years old, Duke Zhao of Lu died in Qianhou, and Duke Din of Lu came to the throne.
In the summer of the fifth year after Duke Ding ascended the throne, Ji Pingzi died, and Ji Hengzi succeeded him as Shangqing.
When Ji Huanzi was digging a well, he found a pottery vessel with a large belly and a small mouth. There was something like a sheep inside. When he told Confucius, he lied that he had found a dog.
Confucius said: As far as I know, this is a sheep.
I heard that the monsters in the mountains and forests are a one-legged beast called Kui and the mountain spirit Lang Lang who can learn human voices. The monsters in the water are the dragon and the water monster called Wang Xiang. The monsters in the soil are a grave sheep of unknown male and female.
The State of Wu attacked the State of Yue and destroyed the capital of the State of Yue, Kuaiji, and obtained a large bone that could fill a cart.
The state of Wu sent an envoy to ask Confucius: What has the biggest bones?
Confucius said: Dayu summoned the gods to Kuaiji Mountain. Fangfeng arrived late, so Dayu killed him and displayed his body to the public. One section of his bones was as long as a car, which was the largest among the bones.
The envoy from Wu State asked again: "Who is the god?"
Confucius said: The gods of mountains and rivers can stir up the clouds and cause rain, which is enough to benefit the world. The gods are responsible for supervising the mountains and rivers and offering sacrifices on time. Those who guard the land and grain are princes, and they are all subordinate to the king.
Wu envoy asked again: What is the Fangfeng family responsible for offering sacrifices to?
Confucius said: The county chief of the Wanggong family, who supervises the sacrifices in Fengshan and Yushan areas, has the surname Li.
In Yu, Xia, and Shang Dynasties, he was called Wang Yi, in Zhou Dynasty, he was called Chang Zhai, and now he is called adults.
Wu Shi asked: How tall can a person be?
Confucius replied: The Ruohui family is three feet tall and is the shortest among them; the one who is only three feet tall is the tallest among them.
After hearing this, the envoy from the State of Wu said: What a great saint!
Ji Huanzi had a favorite named Zhongliang Huai, who had a grudge against Yang Hu.
Yang Hu wanted to expel Zhong Liang Huai, but Ji's retainer Gong Shan Buxu stopped him.
In the autumn of this year, Zhongliang Huai became even more arrogant, and Yang Hu captured Zhong Liang Huai.
Ji Huanzi was very angry about this, so Yang Hu imprisoned Ji Huanzi. He was not released until Ji Huan admitted defeat and made a covenant.

Therefore, Confucius no longer wanted to be an official in the state of Lu. He retired at home and concentrated on studying and sorting out the classics such as "Poems", "Books", "Rites" and "Music". More and more students, some even coming from far away, all humbly asked Confucius for advice.
In the eighth year of Lu Dinggong's reign, Gongshan Buxuan failed under Ji Huanzi, so he took advantage of Yanghu's rebellion and planned to abolish the legitimate heirs of Jisun, Mengsun, and Shusun families, and establish a bastard son who Yanghu had always loved, so he arrested Ji Huanzi.

If you feel sad, take concrete actions to express your apology.

In the summer of the thirteenth year of Duke Dinggong of Lu, Confucius said to Duke Dinggong: Weapons cannot be stored in the homes of ministers, and city walls one foot high and three hundred feet long cannot be built in the fiefdom of officials.
So he sent Zhong Yu to serve as Ji's retainer, intending to demolish the city walls of Ji Sun, Meng Sun and Shu Sun. At this time, Shusun first demolished the city wall of Liyi.
