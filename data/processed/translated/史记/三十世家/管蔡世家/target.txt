Among the ten brothers, only Wu Wangfa and Zhou Gongdan were highly virtuous and talented, and they were the right-hand men to assist King Wen. Therefore, King Wen did not establish Boyikao, but made his second son Fa the crown prince.
After King Wen died, Prince Fa came to the throne and became King Wu.

He enfeoffed the land of Lu to Shu Dan, and at the same time made Shu Dan the prime minister of the Zhou Dynasty, so he was called Zhou Gong.
Shu Zhenduo was granted the title of Cao Di, Shu Wu was granted the title of Cheng Di, and Shu Chu was granted the title of Huo Di.
Uncle Kang and Ran Jizai were young at that time, so they were not awarded the title.
After King Wu died, King Cheng succeeded to the throne at a young age, and Duke Zhou took control of the country.

Zhou Gongdan conquered the rebels according to the decree of King Cheng, executed Wu Geng, killed Guan Shu and exiled Cai Shu. During the exile, Cai Shu was only given a carriage and seventy prisoners as his entourage.
The survivors of the Southern Dynasties were also divided into two parts: part of the Song Dynasty was granted to Wei Ziqi to continue the sacrifices of the Yin Dynasty; and part of the Wei Dynasty was established to establish the Wei Kingdom and appointed Uncle Kang as the king of the Wei Kingdom, which was Uncle Kang of Wei.
He also entrusted the land of Ran to Ji Zai.

After Duke Zhou heard about it, he recommended him to be a minister of the state of Lu. The state of Lu was governed very well.
The Duke of Zhou suggested to King Cheng that Hu should be granted a title in the land of Cai, in order to perform the annual sacrificial ceremony for Uncle Cai, who was Cai Zhong. The other five uncles returned to their respective kingdoms and did not serve as officials in the Zhou court.
