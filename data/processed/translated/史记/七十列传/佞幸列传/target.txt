During the reign of Emperor Xiaowen, the favored ministers in the palace included Deng Tong as a scholar and Zhao Tong and Beigong Bozi as eunuchs.
Beigong Bozi was favored because he was a kind elder; Zhao Tong was favored because he was good at observing stars and fortune telling, and often served as Emperor Wen's companion; Deng Tong had no skills.
Deng Tong was from Nan'an, Shu County. He became a Huangtou Lang because he was good at rowing.
Emperor Xiaowen dreamed of ascending to heaven, but he couldn't go up. A yellow-headed man pushed him up to heaven from behind. He looked back and saw the man's clothes at the waist, and his belt was knotted behind his back.
After waking up from the dream, Emperor Wen went to Jiantai and secretly looked for Huang Toulang who pushed him to heaven based on what he saw in his dream. Sure enough, he saw Deng Tong, his belt tied behind his back, just as he had seen in his dream.
Emperor Wen summoned him and asked his name, and he replied that his surname was Deng Mingtong. Emperor Wen liked him and respected and favored him more and more day by day.
Deng Tong was also honest and cautious, and did not like to interact with outsiders. Although the emperor granted him a vacation, he did not want to go out.
At that time, the emperor rewarded him more than ten times, with a total of hundreds of millions of money, and his official position was promoted to senior doctor.
Emperor Wen often visited Deng Tong's house to play and play.
But Deng Tong had no other talents and could not recommend wise men. He was just cautious in doing things and trying to please the emperor.
Once, the emperor asked a person who was good at fortune telling to look at Deng Tong. After the person looked at him, he said: Deng Tong will die of poverty and hunger.
Emperor Wen said: I am the one who can make Deng Tong rich. How can I say that he will be poor?
So Emperor Wen gave Deng Tong the Tongshan in Yandao, Shu County, and allowed him to mint his own money. From then on, "Deng's money" spread throughout the country.
His wealth reached this level.
Emperor Wen once suffered from carbuncle, and Deng usually sucked the pus and blood for him.
Emperor Wen was unhappy and asked Deng Tong calmly: "Who in the world loves me the most?"
Deng Tong said: There should be no one who loves you more than the prince.
The prince came to see Emperor Wen's condition, and Emperor Wen asked him to suck the pus and blood. Although the prince sucked the pus and blood, his face showed embarrassment.
Later, when the prince heard that Deng Tong sucked pus and blood for Emperor Wen, he felt ashamed and resented Deng Tong.
When Emperor Wen died and Emperor Jing came to the throne, Deng Tong was dismissed and lived at home.
Soon after, someone reported that Deng Tong had stolen coins minted from abroad.
Emperor Jing brought the matter to the judge, and it turned out that it was true, so he closed the case and confiscated all the Deng Tong family's money, leaving hundreds of millions in debt.
The eldest princess Liu gave Deng Tong money as a prostitute, but the officials immediately confiscated it and took it back to the palace. Deng Tong was not even allowed to wear a hairpin.
So the eldest princess ordered her subordinates to lend Deng Tong only the cost of food and clothing.
After all, Deng Tong was not allowed to own a penny and lived in other people's homes until his death.

The emperor's chariot had not yet set off due to the clearing of the road, so he sent Han Yan to take the auxiliary chariot, followed by hundreds of cavalry, and galloped forward to observe the whereabouts of the wild beasts.
King Jiangdu saw it from a distance and thought it was the emperor coming, so he asked his followers to stay away and he lay down by the roadside to pay his respects.
However, Han Yan galloped past on horseback and did not see King Jiangdu.
After Han Yan passed by, King Jiangdu was furious and cried to the Queen Mother: Please allow me to return the feudal state to the court and return to the palace as a guard on duty, just like Han Yan.
The Queen Mother resented Han Yan.
Han Yan served the emperor and was not prohibited from entering or leaving the residence of the concubines in Yongxiang. Someone reported the adultery to the empress dowager.
The Empress Dowager was furious and sent an envoy to order Han Yan to commit suicide.
Emperor Wu apologized and begged the Queen Mother on his behalf, but was ultimately not accepted and Han Yan committed suicide.
