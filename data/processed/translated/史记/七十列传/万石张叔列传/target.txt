Wan Shijun's name was Fen, and his father was from the state of Zhao and his surname was Shi.
After the fall of Zhao State, he moved to Wen County.
Gaozu attacked Xiang Yu in the east and passed through Hanoi County. Shi Fen was fifteen years old at that time and was a minor official serving Gaozu.
Emperor Gaozu talked with him and liked his respectful and cautious attitude. He asked him: Who are the people in your family?
He replied: My mother is the only one in my family and unfortunately she is blind.
The family is very poor.
There is also a sister who can play the piano.
Gaozu asked again: Can you follow me?
He replied: I am willing to serve with all my strength.
Therefore, Emperor Gaozu called his sister into the palace to be a beauty, and asked Shi Fen to be the official in charge of receiving documents and interviews from the ministers. He moved his family to the middle family in Chang'an because his sister had become a beauty.
When Shi Fen served as an official until Emperor Xiaowen, he became a high-ranking official by accumulating merit.
He didn't know much about Confucianism, but he was as respectful and cautious as anyone else.
During the reign of Emperor Wen, Zhang Xiangru, Marquis of Dongyang, served as the Crown Prince and Tutor, but was later dismissed.
Emperor Wen chose someone who could be the prince's tutor. Everyone recommended Shi Fen, and Shi Fen became the prince's tutor.
When Emperor Jing came to the throne, he was appointed to the position of Jiuqing. Because he was too respectful and cautious and approached him, Emperor Jing was also afraid of him and made him the prime minister of the princes.
His eldest son Shi Jian, his second son Shi Jia, his third son Shi Yi, and his fourth son Shi Qing all had official positions of two thousand shi because of their docile temperament, respect for their elders, and caution in doing things. So Emperor Jing said: "Shi Jun and his four sons all have official positions of two thousand shi, and the honor and glory of being ministers is concentrated in their family."
He called Shi Fen the King of Wan Shi.
In the last years of Emperor Jing's reign, Wan Shijun enjoyed the salary of a doctor and returned home in old age. When the imperial court held grand ceremonies and issued orders, he attended as a minister.
When passing the palace gate, Wan Shijun must get out of the car and walk quickly to show respect. When seeing the emperor's driver, he must put his hand on the driver's chariot to express his greetings.
When his descendants and grandchildren serve as minor officials and come home to visit him, Wan Shijun must wear court clothes to receive them and not call them by their names.
When some of his descendants made mistakes, he would not scold them. Instead, he would sit on a side seat and face the table and refuse to eat.
After this, other descendants will blame the person who made the mistake one after another, and then ask for mercy from the elders of the clan. They will bare their upper body to express their apology and resolutely correct themselves, and then they will agree to their request.
When his adult children and grandchildren are around him, he must dress neatly and show a serious and tidy look, even if he is staying at home.
His servants are also very respectful and cautious.
Sometimes when the emperor would reward food and send it to his home, he would always kowtow and bow before eating it, just like in front of the emperor.
When he was attending the funeral, he was very sad and mournful.
Future generations will follow his teachings and do as he did.
Wan Shijun's family was famous in all counties and vassal states for their filial piety and prudence. Even the simple Confucian scholars in Qi and Lu thought that they were inferior to them.
In the second year of Jianyuan, Wang Zang, the doctor's order, was convicted for advocating Confucianism.
The Empress Dowager believed that most of the Confucian scholars' speeches were exaggerated and not simple enough. Now Wan Shijun's family did not talk much but practiced it. She made Wan Shijun's eldest son Shi Jian the doctor's order and his youngest son Shi Qing the internal historian.
Shi Jian's hair turned gray in his old age, but Wan Shijun's body was still healthy and disease-free.
As a doctor, Shi Jian took one day off every five days. When he went home to visit his father, he first entered the waiter's cabin, privately asked the waiter about his father, took his underwear and washed it himself in the ditch outside the door, and then handed it to the waiter. He did not dare to let his father know that he often did this.
When Shi Jian was a doctor, he had something to say to the emperor. When he could avoid others, he spoke freely and urgently;
Therefore, the emperor showed respect and courtesy to him personally.
Wan Shijun moved to Lingli.
Shi Qing, the son of the internal historian, returned drunk and did not get out of the car when he entered the inner gate.
Wan Shijun refused to eat after hearing this.
Shi Qing was frightened and bared his upper body to ask for forgiveness, but Wan Shijun still refused.
The whole clan and his brother Shi Jian also exposed their upper bodies and begged for forgiveness. Wan Shijun scolded him and said: "Nei Shi is a noble person. When he entered the inner gate, the elders in the inner city hurriedly avoided him, but Nei Shi sat in the car and remained the same, not knowing how to restrain himself. This is what he should do!"
After saying that, he ordered Shi Qing to go away.
From then on, when Shi Qing and his brothers entered the inner gate, they all got out of the car and walked home quickly.
Wan Shijun died in the fifth year of Emperor Wu Yuanshuo.
The eldest son, Lang Zhongling, Shi Jian cried so much that he could only walk with a cane. More than a year later, Shi Jian also passed away.
Wan Shijun's descendants are all filial, but Shi Jian stands out the most, surpassing Wan Shijun.
When Shi Jian was the doctor, he wrote a memorial once and approved it. When Shi Jian read it again, he said in horror: "Wrong writing!" The four points under the character "horse" and the horsetail under the curve should be five strokes, but now there are only four strokes. One stroke is missing. If the emperor finds out and blames him, he will die!
This shows that he is very cautious even when dealing with other things.
Wan Shijun's youngest son Shi Qing was a servant and was driving for the emperor. The emperor asked him how many horses he was driving. After Shi Qing counted the horses one by one with a whip, he raised his hand and said: six.
Shi Qing is the most casual among his sons, yet he is still so cautious.
Shi Qing became the Prime Minister of Qi State, and everyone in Qi State admired their family tradition, so Qi State was very stable without issuing decrees, so people built the Shixiang Temple in Shi Qing's honor.
In the first year of Emperor Wu's Yuanshou, the emperor established the prince and selected people who could be the prince's teachers from among the ministers. Shi Qing was promoted from the post of Pei Taishou to the prince's tutor, and seven years later he was promoted to the imperial censor.
In the autumn of the fifth year of Emperor Wu's Yuanding reign, Prime Minister Zhao Zhou was dismissed from office for a crime.
The emperor issued an edict to the imperial censor doctor: The late emperor respected Wan Shijun very much, and their descendants were very filial. He ordered the censor doctor Shi Qing to serve as prime minister and be granted the title of Marquis Muqiu.
At this time, the imperial court was conquering South Vietnam and East Vietnam in the south, attacking North Korea in the east, expelling the Xiongnu in the north, and conquering Dawan in the west. The country was in a troubled period.
In addition, the emperor visited various parts of the country, repaired ancient temples, went to Mount Tai to worship heaven, and to Liangfu to worship the land, and promoted rituals and music.
When the country's finances were in trouble, the emperor asked Sang Hongyang and others to seek financial gain, Wang Wenshu and others implemented strict laws, and made Erkuan and others respect Confucianism. They all reached the rank of Jiuqing, and were promoted to power alternately. Major affairs in the court did not depend on the prime minister, who was just loyal and prudent.
Shi Qing had been in power for nine years, but he had not been able to make any remarks to rectify the current situation. He once wanted to punish the emperor's close ministers Zhong Zhong and Jiuqing Xianxuan for their sins. Not only could they not plead guilty, but they were punished.
In the fourth year of the Yuanfeng reign of Emperor Wu of the Han Dynasty, two million people in Guandong were displaced, and 400,000 people were without household registration. The ministers discussed and asked the emperor to move the refugees to the frontiers as a way to punish them.
The emperor believed that the prime minister was too old and cautious to participate in such discussions, so he asked him to take leave and go home, while the officials below the imperial censor who made such requests were investigated.
The prime minister felt guilty because he was not qualified for his position, so he wrote to the emperor and said: I, Shi Qing, have been favored to be prime minister, but my ability is too poor to assist your majesty in governing the country. As a result, the warehouses in the suburbs are empty and the people are displaced. I deserve death. The emperor cannot bear to punish me according to law. I am willing to return the seals of the prime minister and the marquis, request to retire and return home, and make way for a worthy person.
The emperor said: The granaries are empty, the people are poor and displaced, but you want to ask them to be relocated. The society is already in turmoil. The social turmoil has put the country in crisis. At this time, you want to resign from your position. Who do you want to blame?
He blamed Shi Qing with an edict. Shi Qing was very ashamed, and then he went back to handling political affairs.
Shi Qing was thoughtful and cautious, but he did not have any brilliant insights or the performance of speaking for the people.
More than three years have passed since then. In the second year of Taichu, Prime Minister Shi Qing passed away and was given the posthumous title Tianhou.
Shi Qing's second son was Ming De. Shi Qing liked and valued him very much. The emperor asked Shi De to be Shi Qing's heir and inherit the title of marquis.
Later I did it too often. Because he violated the law and was sentenced to death, Namsu became an official and became a civilian after atonement.
When Shi Qing was prime minister, thirteen of his descendants were promoted from minor officials to the rank of two thousand shi.
After Shi Qing's death, he was gradually removed from office for different crimes, and the family tradition of filial piety and prudence also declined.
Wei Wan, Marquis of Jianling, was a native of Daling, Daijun.
Wei Wan became a Langguan by virtue of his driving skills and served Emperor Wen. He was promoted to Zhonglangjiang due to his continuous meritorious service. He had nothing but loyalty and prudence.
When Emperor Jing was the crown prince, he invited the emperor's closest officials to a banquet, but Wei Wan refused to go because he was ill.
When Emperor Wen was about to die, he told Emperor Jing: Wei Wan is a loyal man, you should treat him well.
When Emperor Wen died and Emperor Jing came to the throne, Emperor Jing did not reprimand Wei Wan for more than a year. Wei Wan just performed his duties more cautiously day by day.
Emperor Jing once visited Shanglinyuan and ordered Wei Wan, the lieutenant general, to ride in a carriage with him. When he came back, he asked Wei Wan: Do you know why you can ride in the same carriage with me?
Wei Wan said: From a small charioteer, I was lucky enough to be promoted to a lieutenant general due to my meritorious service. I don’t know why.
Emperor Jing asked again: When I became the crown prince, I invited you to a banquet, but you refused to come. Why?
He replied: I deserve to die, I was really sick at that time!
Emperor Jing gave him a sword.
Wei Wan said: The late Emperor once gave me a total of six swords. I dare not accept any more rewards from His Majesty.
Emperor Jing said: Swords are a favorite thing among people and are often used as gifts or in exchange for other things. How can you keep it until now? Wei Wan said: They are all still there.
The emperor sent someone to fetch the six swords. The swords were intact in their sheaths and had never been used.
When the Lang officers under the Zhong Lang general made mistakes, Wei Wan would often accept the consequences for them without arguing with others; when he had merit, he would always humbly give it to others.
The emperor believed that he was of upright character and loyal to himself, so he appointed him as the Taifu of Liu De, King of Hejian.
During the Seven Kingdoms Rebellion between Wu and Chu, the emperor appointed Wei Wan as a general. He led the army of the King of Hejian to attack the rebels of Wu and Chu, and made him a lieutenant.
Three years later, because of his military exploits, he was granted the title of Marquis of Jianling in the sixth year of Emperor Jing's reign.
The next year, Emperor Jing deposed Prince Li Liu Rong and killed the prince's uncle and others.
Emperor Jing thought Wei Wan was a loyal person and couldn't bear to let him handle this big case, so he gave him leave to go home. And let Zhi Du arrest and interrogate the Li clan members.
After handling this case, Emperor Jing appointed Liu Che, King of Jiaodong, as the crown prince, recruited Wei Wan, and appointed him as the crown prince and tutor.
After a long time, he was promoted to the imperial censor.
Five years later, he replaced Liu She, Taohou, as prime minister, and only reported matters within his duties to the court.
However, from the time he first became an official until he became prime minister, there was nothing to praise or blame.
The emperor thought that he was kind-hearted and could assist the young master. He respected and loved him very much and rewarded him with many things.
Wei Wan served as prime minister for three years, Emperor Jing died, and Emperor Wu ascended the throne.
During the Jianyuan period, when Emperor Jing was ill, many of the prisoners in various government offices were innocent people who had been wronged. As prime minister, he failed to fulfill his duties and was dismissed from his position as prime minister.
Later, Wei Wan died, and his son Wei Xin inherited the title of Marquis of Jianling.
