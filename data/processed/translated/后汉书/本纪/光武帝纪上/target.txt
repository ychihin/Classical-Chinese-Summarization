The founder of the Eastern Han Dynasty, Emperor Guangwu Liu Xiu, whose courtesy name was Uncle Wen, was born in Caiyang, Nanyang. He was the ninth-generation grandson of Liu Bang, the great ancestor of the Han Dynasty. It comes from Liu Fa, the king of Shading who was born under Emperor Jing.
Liu gave birth to Liu Mai, the Marquis of Chung Ling Festival, Liu Mai gave birth to Liu Wai, the governor of Yulin, Liu Hui, the captain of Julu, was born to Liu Wai, Liu Qin was born to Liu Qin, the Nandun order, and Liu Qin was born to Guangwu.
Guangwu lost his father at the age of nine and was raised by his uncle Liu Liang.
He is seven feet three inches tall, has thick eyebrows, a wide mouth, a protruding nose, and a full forehead.
He is industrious by nature and enjoys farming. His elder brother Bo Sheng was fond of chivalry and raising people. He often ridiculed Guang Wu for only working in the fields and compared him with Liu Bang's second brother Liu Zhong.
It was not until the reign of Wang Mang and Tianfeng that Guangwu came to Chang'an to study "Shang Shu" as a disciple and roughly understand the gist of the book.
In Wang Mang's last years, there were successive locust plagues in the country, and bandits and bandits were raging.
In the third year of Emperor Di Huang's reign, there was a serious famine in Nanyang, and most of the family's guests went out to rob and steal.
In order to avoid the government, Guangwu fled to Xinye County and transported the harvested grain to Wancheng to sell.
Li Tong and others from Wan used prophecies and talismans to tell Guangwu: The Liu family has risen again, and the Li family will be the assistant. Guangwu didn't dare to act rashly at first, but then he thought that his brother Bo Sheng would do great things by making friends with diners. Moreover, Wang Mang's regime showed signs of decline and destruction, and the world was in turmoil, so he planned with the Li family and decided to raise an army, so he began to purchase various weapons.
In October, he and Li Tong's cousin Li Yi and others raised troops in Wancheng. He was 28 years old at the time.
In November, the comet appeared in Zhangsu in the south.
Guangwu then led his retainers back to Chongling.
At this time, Bo Sheng had gathered the people to raise troops.
At first, the children of each family were very frightened. They ran and hid, saying: "Bosheng wants to kill us!"
When they saw Guangwu in crimson robes and crowns, they were all surprised and said: "Uncle Wen, who is cautious and kind, has also raised an army!"
Only then did everyone settle down. Bo Sheng then recruited troops from Xinshi and Pinglin, and together with his leaders Wang Feng and Chen Mu, went west to attack Changju.
Guangwu first rode the bull, and then he was able to ride the horse after killing Xin Yewei.
He entered Tu Tangzi Township and killed the Huyang county captain.
The wealth obtained by the army was unevenly distributed, and everyone was angry. The soldiers wanted to counterattack the Liu family.
Guangwu then collected all the property from his clan and gave it all to the soldiers. Only then did everyone feel satisfied.
He then attacked and captured Jiyang, and fought with Wang Mang's former commander Zhen Fu and Zhengliangqiu in Xiaochang'an. As a result, the Han army was defeated and had to retreat to Jiyang.
On the first day of the first lunar month of the first year of Gengshi, the Han army fought against Zhenfu and Liangqiuci in the west of Xianshui River. They defeated the enemy and killed Zhenfu and Liangqiuci.
Bo Sheng defeated Wang Mang's general Yan You and Zhizong's general Chen Mao in Huoyang, and then besieged Wancheng.
On the first day of February, Liu Xuan was proclaimed emperor. Liu Xuan appointed Bo Sheng as the Great Situ and Guang Wu as the Taichang Commander.
In March, Guangwu and other generals attacked Kunyang, Dingling and Lu, and captured them all.
A lot of cattle, horses and property were seized, as well as hundreds of thousands of grains, which were transferred to the troops under Wancheng.
When Wang Mang learned that Zhen Fu and Liang Qiuci had been killed and that the Han Emperor had been established, he was very frightened, so he sent Grand Situ Wang Xun and Grand Sikong Wang Yi to lead millions of troops, including 420,000 armored soldiers. In May, he arrived at Yingchuan and joined forces with Yan You and Chen Mao.
At first, Guangwu complained to Yan You about the rent arrears of Chonglinghou's tenants. Yan You treated Guangwu differently when he saw him.
Now, Yan You listened to the people who surrendered to him in the city and said that Guangwu did not collect property, but only trained soldiers to make plans.
Yan You smiled and said: Is he the man with the bushy eyebrows?
Why would he do this!
At the beginning, Wang Mang recruited hundreds of people from sixty-three families in the world who knew the art of war and appointed them as military officers. He selected and trained guards, recruited warriors, and carried banners and baggage for thousands of miles.
At that time, there was a giant named Big Mac, who was ten feet tall and ten inches thick at the waist. He was appointed as a lieutenant in the military base. He drove wild beasts such as tigers, leopards, rhinoceros, and elephants to boost the military's power.
The grand occasion of leaving the army was unprecedented since the Qin and Han Dynasties.
Guangwu led thousands of troops to intercept Wang Mang's army at Yangguan.
When the generals saw that Wang Xun and Wang Yi's troops were strong, they turned around and ran away, rushing back to Kunyang. They were all afraid and worried about their wives and children, so they wanted to disperse and return to their respective cities.
Guangwu suggested: Now we have few troops and food, but the foreign invaders are strong. If we work together to resist, we may be able to achieve success; if we are scattered, we will definitely not be able to survive.
Moreover, Wancheng has not been captured, and the troops there cannot rescue us. Once Kunyang is captured, all our ministries will be wiped out within one day.
Now, instead of working together to gain fame, do we want to keep our wives, children, and property? The generals said angrily: "Why did General Liu say such a thing!" Guangwu stood up with a smile.
At this time, the enemy cavalry who happened to be watching came back and said that a large number of enemies were coming to the north of the city. The army and horses were lined up for hundreds of miles, and the tail of the team could not be seen.
The generals quickly said to each other: It is better to ask General Liu for advice on how to deal with it. Guangwu also planned success or failure strategies for everyone.
The generals were worried and embarrassed, and they all said: Just do it like this!
At this time, there were only eight or nine thousand people in the city, so Guangwu asked Chengguo Duke Wang Feng and Tingwei General Wang Chang to guard the city. He, Hussar General Zong Yan, Wuwei General Li Yi and other thirteen people rode out of the south gate of the city at night to organize troops outside.
By this time, nearly 100,000 Wang Mang's troops had arrived at the city gates, and Guangwu could hardly get out of the city.
When they arrived at Lin and Dingling, they mobilized all the troops from all the barracks, but the generals were greedy for money and wanted to divide their troops and stay behind.
Guangwu said: Now if we defeat the enemy, the treasure will be ten thousand times greater, and we will achieve great success; if we are defeated by the enemy, we will not be able to save our heads, so how can we have any wealth?
Everyone followed Guangwu's advice. Yan You advised Wang Yi, saying: Kunyang City is small but strong, and Liu Xuan, who has claimed to be the emperor, is in Wancheng. If we quickly send a large army to attack, they will definitely escape. If Wancheng is defeated, Kunyang will naturally surrender. Wang Yi said: In the past, as General Huya, I besieged Zhai Yi and was blamed for not capturing him alive.
Now with an army of millions, how can we justify it if we encounter a city and cannot take it? So they surrounded Kunyang dozens of times, set up hundreds of camps, and erected a cloud chariot more than ten feet high. Looking down at the city, flags were everywhere, dust filled the sky, and the sound of golden drums could be heard for hundreds of miles.
Some troops dug tunnels, and some troops used wooden dolls and carts to attack the city.
Bows and crossbows fired wildly, arrows rained down like rain, and the people in the city had to carry door panels on their backs to draw water.
Wang Feng and others asked to surrender, but were not allowed.
Wang Xun Wang Yi thought that his success was instantaneous, and he was in a leisurely and contented mood. Meteors fell into the barracks at night, and clouds appeared during the day. The camp fell like a collapsed mountain, and it dispersed less than a foot away from the ground. All the officers and soldiers lay prone on the ground.
On the first day of June, Guangwu advanced with the army. He led more than a thousand infantry and cavalry and set up a formation four or five miles in front of Wang Mang's army. Wang Xun and Wang Yi also sent thousands of soldiers to fight.
Guangwu rushed to kill the enemy troops and killed dozens of them.
All the subordinates said happily: "General Liu has been afraid of weak enemies all his life, but today he is unstoppable when he encounters strong enemies. It is very strange, and he is at the front again."
Let us also assist the general! Guangwu rushed towards the enemy. Wang Yi and Wang Xun's troops retreated. Taking advantage of the victory, his troops attacked and killed hundreds or thousands of the enemy.
After repeated victories, the team moved forward.
At this time, Bo Sheng had captured Wancheng for three days, but Guangwu didn't know it yet, so he sent someone pretending to be an envoy from the troops under Wancheng, and brought a letter to report to the city, saying that reinforcements from Wancheng had arrived, but he pretended to accidentally lose the letter.
Wang Xun and Wang Yi's letters made me unhappy.
The Han army won several victories and became more courageous. One man was worth a hundred.
Guangwu led another 3,000 death squads and charged at the enemy's backbone from the water west of the city. Wang Xun and Wang Yi's military formations were in chaos. Guangwu used his vigor to destroy the enemy formation and killed Wang Xun.
Kunyang City also beat drums and shouted and rushed out. The internal and external attacks were combined, the sound shook the heaven and the earth. The reckless army was defeated. The fleeing soldiers trampled each other, and the casualties were scattered for more than a hundred miles.
There happened to be a strong thunder and strong wind in the sky, and the roof tiles were blown away. Heavy rain poured down and the water in the Sichuan River surged. Tigers and leopards trembled with fear. Soldiers rushed to cross the water to escape. Tens of thousands drowned and the river stopped flowing. Wang Yi, Yan You, and Chen Mao rode lightly on horseback and escaped across the river on the dead men. They were so powerful that the Mang army's food, grass, equipment, tanks, armor, and treasures were countless. They couldn't move them all for several months, and some had to be burned.
Guangwu took advantage of the situation and captured Yingyang County.
At this time, Liu Boxheng was killed by Liu Xuan, the Gengshi Emperor. Guangwu immediately rushed from his father city to Wancheng to apologize.
The officials of the Situ Mansion all came to express their condolences to Guangwu. Guangwu was not in a position to discuss anything with them in private, but he only blamed himself deeply.
He never boasted about his achievements in the Battle of Kunyang, nor did he dare to mourn for Bo Sheng. He ate, talked and laughed as naturally as usual.
Emperor Gengshi felt guilty about this, so he worshiped Guangwu as the general of Polu and named him Marquis of Wuxin.
