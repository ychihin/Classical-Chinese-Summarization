In the tenth year of Jianwu's reign, the emperor entered the pass and prepared to conquer it personally. Kou Xun followed Guangwu at that time and advised: "Chang'an is located between Luoyang and Gaoping, so it is convenient to connect. Anding and Longxi will definitely feel shaken and fearful. This is because being comfortable in one place can subdue the four directions."
Now that the soldiers and horses are tired and have just come out of danger, this is not a good strategy for His Majesty to secure the country. The historical events that happened in Yingchuan the year before last can serve as a warning. The emperor did not listen.
After marching to the county, Gao Jun still could not be captured. The emperor discussed sending an envoy to persuade Gao Jun to surrender, and said to Kou Xun: "You stopped my action before, now go for me."
If Gao Jun does not surrender immediately, I will lead Geng and other five battalions to attack. When Kou Xun came to the first city with the seal, Gao Jun sent military advisor Huangfu Wen to pay an audience, but he refused to obey any words or etiquette.
Kou Xun was angry and prepared to kill Huangfu Wen.
The generals remonstrated and said: "We have ten thousand elite soldiers with many powerful crossbows. They have blocked the Long Road in the west and have not been able to capture it for years."
I'm afraid it's not possible to ask him to surrender and kill his envoy now, right? Kou Xun refused and killed Huangfu Wen.
Let his deputy go back and tell Gao Jun: "The military advisor was rude and has been killed."
If you want to surrender, hurry up; if you don't want to surrender, just hold on. Gao Jun was frightened and immediately opened the city gate and surrendered.
All the generals celebrated and said: "Excuse me, I killed his envoy and Gao Jun surrendered the whole city. What is the reason for this?" Kou Xun said: Huangfu Wen is Gao Jun's confidant, and Gao Jun draws all his strategies from him.
Now that he is here, his words are unyielding and he will have no intention of surrender.
If you let him go, Gao Jun will succeed in his plan; if you kill him, Gao Jun will be frightened and surrender. All the generals said: We didn't expect it. So Gao Jun was arrested and returned to Luoyang.
Kou Xun was proficient in classics and cultivated virtues, and his reputation was more important than that of the court. He used all his salary and salary to give generously to his friends, old friends, and officials who followed him.
Often said: I relied on the help of scholar-bureaucrats to get where I am today. How can I enjoy wealth alone? People at that time regarded him as an elder and thought he had the talents of a prime minister.
He died in the twelfth year of Jianwu and was given the posthumous title of Weihou.
Zi Kou lost his heirship.
Kou Xun's brother from the same mother, his brother's son, and sister's son were named marquises for their military merits, a total of eight people. They were all marquises for life but were not passed down to future generations.
At first, Min Ye was planning affairs with Kou Xun. Kou Xun explained his loyalty to the emperor many times, and the emperor conferred on him the title of Marquis of Guannei and the post of governor of western Liaoning.
In the 13th year of Jianwu's reign, he again granted the title of Marquis of Jiujiao to his brother Shou, who had lost his concubine.
Later, he was granted the title of Marquis of Jieliu by Kou Shan.
After Kou Xi's death, his son Kou Li succeeded to the throne and was granted the title of Marquis of Shangxiang.
After Kou Li died, Zi Kou succeeded to the throne.
Kou Xun's granddaughter was the wife of General Deng Zhao, so the Kou family was very successful during the Yongchu period of Emperor An.
