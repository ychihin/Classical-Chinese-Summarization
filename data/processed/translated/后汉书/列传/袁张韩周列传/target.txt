◆Biography of Yuan An, Yuan An, courtesy name Shaogong, was born in Ruyang County, Runan County.
Grandfather Yuan Liang studied "Mencius' Book of Changes". During the reign of Emperor Ping of the Han Dynasty, he was recommended for his Ming Dynasty and became the crown prince. In the early years of Jianwu, he became the magistrate of Chengwu County.
Yuan An inherited the knowledge of his grandfather Yuan Liang when he was young.
He is a very dignified man, has authority, and is respected by people in the state.
He started to work as a county magistrate. Once, he went to the prefecture with a letter to do some work. He asked Yuan An to send a letter to the county magistrate. Yuan An said: "You are doing official business, and you have a post to deliver it for you. If it is a private matter, you should not ask me for merit."
He declined to accept the letter, and the person in charge was a little scared, so he stopped entrusting him.
Later, he was recommended as Xiaolian and became the magistrate of Yinping County and Rencheng County successively. Wherever he worked, officials and people respected and loved him.
In the twelfth year of Yongping, King Liu Ying of Chu conspired to rebel against the imperial court, and the case was submitted to the county for verification.
The next year, the three government offices recommended Yuan An to handle cases, believing that he could handle complex cases. The emperor appointed him as the prefect of Chu County.
At this time, thousands of people were imprisoned due to the confession of Chu King Liu Ying. Xianzong lost his temper, and the investigators were eager to finalize the case. They tortured many people to force them to confess, and many of them were sentenced to death.
After Yuan An arrived at the county, he went to the prison instead of entering the yamen, tried those who had no obvious evidence, reported them one by one, and let them be released from prison.
The officials and officials of the government all kowtowed to Yuan An and argued that anyone who agreed with Liu Ying, the king of Chu, would be guilty of the same crime as Liu Ying according to the law. Yuan An disagreed.
Yuan An said: If there is any violation of the regulations, I will bear full responsibility and will not harm you.
So they were reported one by one.
The emperor was moved to consciousness and immediately agreed, and more than 400 families were released from prison.
A year later, Yuan An was conscripted into Henan Yin.
The government's orders were very strict, but no one was ever tried for bribery.
He often said: Anyone who is an official hopes to be a prime minister at the highest level, and a prefect at the lowest level. To imprison talents in the age of sage is something I cannot bear to do.
Those who heard this were very moved and encouraged themselves to be honest and honest.
Yuan An had been in office for ten years, and the political discipline in the capital was very orderly, and his reputation was highly valued by the court.
In the eighth year of Jianchu's reign, he was promoted to imperial servant.
In the second year of Yuanhe, Meng Yun, the governor of Wuwei, wrote: The Northern Huns had already made peace, but the Southern Huns went to rob them again. The Northern Chanyu said that the Han Dynasty deceived them and wanted to invade the border.
The prisoners should be returned to comfort them.
The emperor issued an edict to summon hundreds of officials to the court for discussion. The ministers all thought that the Yi Di were cunning and greedy. After being captured, they would exaggerate and exaggerate. This should not be the case.
Yuan An said: The Northern Huns sent envoys to pay tribute and request peace, and also returned the captives to the Han Dynasty. This shows that they were afraid of the prestige of the Han Dynasty, and did not violate the treaty first.
Meng Yun, as a minister, guards the border and should not be dishonest to the Yi and Di. Letting the prisoners go back is enough to show China's lenience towards them, and it is great to bring peace to the people on the border.
Situ Huanyu changed his mind and obeyed Yuan An.
Taiwei Zheng Hong, Sikong Wulun and others all hated Yuan An.
Zheng Hong also loudly angered Huan Yu and said: Anyone who advocates the release of prisoners is disloyal to the emperor.
Huan Yu scolded him on the spot. Wu Lun and Dahonglu Wei Biao were so angry that their faces changed color. Sili Xiaowei reported the whole situation to the emperor, and Yuan An and others handed over their seals and ribbons to the emperor to plead guilty.
Suzong issued an edict saying: The long time to discuss politics shows that everyone has different opinions.
Important matters in the court should be discussed more frequently, and strategies should be decided upon by everyone. It is good to have an upright and polite attitude when speaking, but it is not a blessing for the court to dare to say anything when trouble occurs. What faults do you have that you should blame yourself for?
It’s better to put on your hat and put on your shoes!
In the end, the emperor followed Yuan An's suggestion.
The next year, Yuan An replaced Wu Lun as Sikong.
In the first year of Zhanghe, he replaced Huan Yu as Situ.
When Emperor He came to the throne, due to his young age, the Queen Mother Dou came to the court to listen to the government. The Queen Mother's brother, the cavalry general Dou Xian, went north to attack the Xiongnu. Yuan An, together with the Taiwei Song You, Sikong Renkai and Jiu Qing, went to the court to dissuade them. They believed that the Xiongnu did not invade the frontier and sent troops for no reason. It cost the country money and claimed credit from thousands of miles away. This was not a proper national policy.
Even the last few letters were withheld and not reported.
Song You was a little frightened and did not dare to submit a letter by his name, and the ministers gradually became silent.
Only Yuan An and Ren Wei still insisted on the right path and did not give in. They even took off their hats and went to the court to fight for more than ten times.
The Queen Mother still refused to listen, and everyone was worried about Yuan An, but Yuan An remained calm and composed.
Dou Xian had already sent troops to fight. His younger brothers, Weiwei Dou Du and Zhijinwu Dou Jing, each relied on their own power and openly allowed their minions to block roads and rob property in the capital.
Dou Jing also sent people to distribute messages to the border counties without authorization, mobilized cavalry commandos and capable shooters, and ordered officials from Yuyang, Yanmen, and Shanggu counties to send them to Dou Jing's residence.
The officials concerned were afraid and did not dare to say no.
Yuan An impeached Dou Jing for mobilizing border guards without authorization and disturbing the officials and the people. The governor did not wait for Fu Xin but followed Dou Jing's arrangements. He should be beheaded in public.
He also reported to Si Lixiaowei and Henan Yin to please their dependent relatives and failed to uphold justice. They requested that they be removed from their official positions and punished according to law.
These memorials were withheld and not reported.
Dou Xian and Dou Jing were even more tyrannical. They deployed their cronies in various cities and towns to collect taxes and bribes from officials and people, and other states and counties followed suit.
Yuan An and Ren Wei jointly reported the two thousand stone governors, and more than 40 people who were implicated in being demoted and dismissed from office. The Dou family held a grudge.
But Yuan An and Ren Wei have always been of noble character, and there is no way to harm them.
At that time, Dou Xian sent troops to Wuwei.
In the second year, Bei Shanyu was defeated by Geng Kui and fled to Wuhuan. There was no one in control of Beikong, and it was unknown who was in charge of the remaining Huns.
Dou Xian boasted about his achievements every day and wanted to use his favor to build a good relationship with the remaining Northern Huns, so he wrote a letter to appoint A Tong, the surrendered King of Zuoluli, as the Northern Chanyu, with General Zhonglang as his guardian, just like he had done in the past to establish the Southern Chanyu.
The emperor handed over the matter to the ministers for discussion. Ten people including Taiwei Song You, Taichang Dinghong, Guangluxun Geng Bing and others thought that this could be done.
Yuan An and Ren Wei reported that Emperor Guangwu's recruitment of the southern Xiongnu was not to allow him to settle in the interior forever, but was a temporary measure because he wanted them to resist the northern Xiongnu.

If one breaks one's promise now, other foreigners will not dare to fulfill their oaths.

The emperor issued an edict for everyone to discuss. Yuan An started a tit-for-tat debate with Dou Xian again.
Dou Xian relied on his own power insidiously, spoke proudly, exposed others' shortcomings, and even scolded Yuan An. He also brought up the example of Emperor Guangwu killing Han Xin and Dai She to intimidate Yuan An, but Yuan An never gave in.
In the end, Dou Xian still appointed the Xiongnu surrender general You Luli and King Yu Zhijian as a chanyu, but he soon rebelled, just as Yuan An expected.

From the emperor to the ministers, everyone relied on him.
In the spring of four years, Yuan An died, and the court officials were very sad.
