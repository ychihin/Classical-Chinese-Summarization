# Summary of Emperor Gaozu's Reign (Years 5-12)

In the winter of the fifth year, the King of Han pursued Xiang Yu but his allies Han Xin and Peng Yue failed to meet as scheduled. Xiang Yu defeated the Han forces, forcing the king to retreat and fortify his position. Following Zhang Liang's advice to share conquered territories, the king promised Han Xin the lands east of Chen to the Bohai Sea and Peng Yue the area north of Suiyang to Gucheng. Both then joined forces. Liu Jia invaded Chu, and Zhou Yin surrendered. In December, Han forces surrounded Xiang Yu at Gaixia. Hearing Chu songs on all sides, Xiang Yu fled with cavalry and was pursued to Dongcheng. The Chu lands were pacified, though Lu initially refused to surrender until shown Xiang Yu's head. Xiang Yu was buried with the rites of Duke of Lu.

In the sixth year, the king honored his brother as Marquis of Wu Ai, appointed Han Xin as King of Chu, and made Peng Yue King of Liang. A general amnesty was declared except for capital crimes. The princes petitioned the king to accept the title of Emperor, which he initially declined but eventually accepted on an auspicious day in the second month. Queen Lu was honored as empress, the prince as crown prince. Wu Rui was made King of Changsha, and the former King of Guangdong was restored. The capital was established at Luoyang. Soldiers were demobilized, with exemptions from taxes for those in Guanzhong and those returning home. Former slaves were freed, and military officials were granted titles and lands according to merit.

In subsequent years, the emperor dealt with various rebellions. Zang Tu of Yan rebelled and was captured. Han Xin of Chu was arrested on suspicion of rebellion and demoted to Marquis of Huaiyin. The emperor established various princes, including Liu Jia as King of Jing and Liu Fei as King of Qi. When generals grew restless over delayed rewards, Zhang Liang advised rewarding the emperor's former enemy Yong Chi first, which calmed everyone. The emperor honored his father as Supreme Emperor.

The Xiongnu besieged King Xin of Han, who surrendered. The emperor personally led campaigns against the Xiongnu and rebels, suffering defeats and victories. He established his son Ruyi as acting king of Dai. Xiao He constructed magnificent palaces, which initially angered the emperor but was justified as demonstrating imperial authority. The capital moved to Chang'an. Zhao officials plotted assassination but were exposed. The emperor dealt with Chen Xi's rebellion, personally leading forces to Handan and bribing enemy generals to surrender.

In the twelfth year, Han Xin was executed for rebellion in Chang'an. Peng Yue rebelled and was executed. Yingbu of Huainan rebelled; the emperor personally led forces against him, defeating him at Huifou. Returning through his hometown of Pei County, the emperor held a grand banquet, sang about returning home in glory, and exempted Pei from taxes. He was wounded by an arrow during the Yingbu campaign and grew ill. When Empress Lu asked about successors, he named Cao Shen, then Wang Ling with Chen Ping's assistance, and Zhou Bo as Taiwei. Emperor Gao died in Changle Palace in the fourth month. Empress Lu initially concealed his death to plot against the generals but was persuaded otherwise. He was buried at Changling and posthumously titled Emperor Gao.
