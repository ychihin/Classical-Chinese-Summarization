Han Gaozu, originally named Liu Ji, was born in Peixian County. His mother dreamed of a god during a thunderstorm, and his father saw a dragon on her, leading to his birth. He had a distinctive appearance with a high nose, raised eyebrows, a beautiful beard, and seventy-two black moles on his left thigh. He was generous and broad-minded, disliking ordinary labor, and served as a minor official, often looking down on others. He loved wine and women, frequently buying wine on credit from Wang Po and Wu Ma, who noticed strange phenomena when he was drunk and eventually forgave his debts. While performing corvée in Xianyang, he saw Qin Shihuang and declared, "A real man should be like this."

Lu Gong, a friend of the Pei County magistrate, moved to the area, and Liu Ji falsely claimed a gift of ten thousand yuan at a gathering. Lu Gong, skilled in physiognomy, recognized Liu Ji's noble appearance and married his daughter to him, despite his wife's objections. This daughter later became Empress Lu, mother of Emperor Xiaohui and Princess Lu Yuan. After resigning as pavilion chief, Liu Ji worked on a farm. An old man predicted that Empress Lu and her children would be noble, and that Liu Ji's destiny was priceless. Liu Ji later wore a distinctive bamboo hat, known as the "Liu Shi Guan."

As pavilion chief, Liu Ji was tasked with sending servants to Lishan, but many escaped. He released them all, and over ten strong men chose to follow him. While fleeing, he killed a large snake blocking the path. An old woman later claimed the snake was the son of the White Emperor, killed by the son of the Red Emperor, which Liu Ji took as a sign of his destiny. Qin Shihuang heard of an emperor's cloud in the southeast and toured to suppress it. Empress Lu could always find Liu Ji by following a cloud above his hiding place, further convincing others of his destiny.

In the first year of Qin II, Chen She rebelled, and the Pei County magistrate considered joining. Xiao He and Cao Shen advised him to recruit fugitives, including Liu Ji. When Liu Ji arrived with followers, the magistrate reneged and tried to kill Xiao He and Cao Shen, who fled to Liu Ji. Liu Ji shot a message into the city, urging the elders to kill the magistrate and join the rebellion. They did so, and Liu Ji was elected Duke of Pei after multiple refusals. He honored Huangdi and Chiyou, used red flags, and quickly expanded his army to 3,000 men. Other rebellions followed, including Xiang Liang and Xiang Yu in Wu.

Liu Ji's forces grew through various battles, including victories at Huling, Fangyu, and Xue. He faced setbacks, such as Yong Chi's betrayal to Wei, but regained Feng. He joined Xiang Liang, who made Sun Mixin King of Chu Huai. After Xiang Liang's death, Liu Ji and Xiang Yu moved King Huai to Pengcheng. King Huai appointed Liu Ji as Marquis of Wu'an and ordered that whoever first entered Guanzhong would be king. Liu Ji was chosen for his kindness, while Xiang Yu was deemed too violent.

Liu Ji advanced westward, defeating Qin forces and accepting surrenders. He entered Wuguan and reached Bashui, where Qin Prince Ying surrendered. Liu Ji spared him, entered Xianyang, and issued the "three chapters of the law," abolishing harsh Qin laws. He refused gifts from the people, winning their support. He guarded Hangu Pass against Xiang Yu, who grew angry. Xiang Yu's uncle Xiang Bo warned Zhang Liang, leading to the Hongmen Banquet, where Liu Ji narrowly escaped assassination. Xiang Yu later entered Xianyang, killed Prince Ying, and burned the palaces, disappointing the people.

Xiang Yu violated the agreement, making Liu Ji King of Han in Ba, Shu, and Hanzhong, while dividing Guanzhong among three Qin generals. Liu Ji resented this but was dissuaded from attacking. He burned the plank road to show no intention of leaving Hanzhong. Han Xin, recommended by Xiao He, was made general and advised an eastern campaign. Liu Ji defeated Zhang Han and expanded, while rebellions by Tian Rong and others weakened Xiang Yu.

Liu Ji's forces grew, and he captured Pengcheng, but Xiang Yu counterattacked, defeating him. Liu Ji regrouped in Xingyang, using strategies like Sui He persuading Yingbu to rebel. He faced a famine in Guanzhong and sent Han Xin to conquer Wei, Zhao, and Qi. After a series of battles, including the siege of Xingyang, Liu Ji escaped using a decoy. He regained Chenggao and confronted Xiang Yu at Guangwu, listing ten crimes. Xiang Yu shot him, but he feigned a minor injury.

Liu Ji recovered, made Han Xin King of Qi, and Yingbu King of Huainan. With Xiang Yu's resources dwindling, they agreed to divide the world using the chasm. However, Zhang Liang and Chen Ping advised Liu Ji to attack, leading to Xiang Yu's eventual defeat.
