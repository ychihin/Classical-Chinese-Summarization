Emperor Hui of the Han Dynasty, son of Emperor Gaozu and Empress Lu, became crown prince in the second year of Gaozu's reign. After Gaozu's death in April of the twelfth year, Hui ascended the throne on May 17, with his mother becoming empress dowager. He issued decrees rewarding subjects with titles and promoting officials based on service length: Zhonglang and Langzhong officials serving six years received third-level promotions, those serving four years received second-level promotions, and various other officials received specific rewards. Those who organized Gaozu's funeral received monetary rewards based on salary, and those who built his mausoleum received gold. Land rent was reduced to one tax per fifteen people. Certain officials and their families were exempted from corvée labor except military tax. Princes were ordered to build Gaozu's ancestral temple.

In the first year of his reign, King Liu Ruyi of Zhao died. Subjects could atone for capital crimes with money. Commoners received title promotions. Chang'an city construction began. In the second year, King Hui of Qi presented Chengyang County to increase Princess Lu Yuan's territory. Two dragons appeared in Lanling, an earthquake struck Longxi, drought occurred, and Yanghou Liu Zhong and Prime Minister Xiao He died. In the third year, 146,000 workers built Chang'an for thirty days. A Liu family princess married the Xiongnu Shanyu. The King of Minyue was titled King of East China Sea, and 20,000 princes and slaves built Chang'an. A fire broke out in royal stables, and Zhao Tuo of South Vietnam paid tribute. In the fourth year, Zhang Ao's daughter became queen. Filial and loyal sages were exempted from corvée. The emperor held a crown ceremony with amnesty, abolished restrictive Qin laws, and fires occurred in Changle and Weiyang Palaces. In the fifth year, unusual weather included thunder and fruit blossoming in winter. Another 145,000 workers built Chang'an. Severe drought struck, Prime Minister Cao Shen died, and Chang'an city was completed. In the sixth year, King Fei of Qi died. Titles could be bought, and unmarried women aged fifteen to thirty were fined. Fan Kuai died, and the west city and Aocang were built. In the seventh year, chariot officers were sent to Xingyang. Solar eclipses occurred, and Emperor Hui died in Weiyang Palace on August 12, buried in Anling on September 5. Ban Gu commented that Hui was kind and respectful but fell short of saintliness due to Empress Lu's actions.
