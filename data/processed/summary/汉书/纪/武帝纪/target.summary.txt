Emperor Xiaowu Liu Che, the second son of Emperor Jing, was born to Wang Meiren. At age four, he was made King of Jiaodong, and at seven, he became crown prince. At sixteen, in the third year of Emperor Jing's reign, his father died, and Liu Che succeeded the throne. He honored Empress Dowager Dou and his mother as empress dowagers, and granted marquis titles to his maternal uncles. In the first year of Jianyuan, he ordered officials to recommend virtuous and upright individuals, but Prime Minister Wei Wan advised against promoting those who studied Legalist or strategist philosophies, which the emperor approved. He pardoned the world, rewarded the people, exempted taxes for the elderly, and issued three-baht coins. Edicts emphasized respect for elders, exempted families of those over ninety from corvée, and improved sacrifices to rivers and seas. He pardoned first offenders in the Seven Kingdoms Rebellion, reduced palace guards, opened horse-breeding gardens to the poor, and planned to build a Mingtang. He summoned the scholar Lu Shen Gong.

In the second year, officials Zhao Wan and Wang Zang committed suicide for failing to report to the Empress Dowager, and Prime Minister Dou Ying and Taiwei Tian Chong were dismissed. A partial solar eclipse occurred, and a night was as bright as day. Maoling-eup was established. In the third year, the Yellow River flooded, causing famine and cannibalism. Residents moving to Maoling received funds and land. A comet appeared in the northwest. King Liu Ming of Jichuan was deposed for murder. Minyue besieged Dong Ou, but Han forces caused their retreat. In the fourth year, red dust blew, drought struck, and comets appeared. In the fifth year, three-baht coins were abolished, half-liang coins issued, and Doctors of the Five Classics established. The emperor's grandmother died, locusts plagued, and two kings died. In the sixth year, fires at Gaozu Temple and Gaoyuan Bedroom led the emperor to blame himself. The Empress Dowager died, and a comet appeared. Minyue attacked South Vietnam, but the Yue people killed their king and surrendered.

In the first year of Yuanguang, counties recommended one filial and incorrupt person each. Generals Li Guang and Cheng Bushi withdrew troops. The emperor pardoned the world and restored clans of the Seven Kingdoms. He issued an edict praising ancient rulers and seeking advice, leading to the rise of Dong Zhongshu and Gongsun Hong. In the second year, the emperor worshipped the Five Emperors and discussed attacking the Xiongnu. Generals stationed 300,000 troops at Mayi to lure the Xiongnu Chanyu, but he escaped. General Wang Hui died in prison. In the third year, the Yellow River changed course, and five meritorious officials were made marquises. Flooding in Puyang led to disaster relief. In the fourth year, Dou Ying was executed, Prime Minister Chong died, frost damaged crops, and an earthquake occurred. In the fifth year, King Wang De of Hejian died, roads were built, and Queen Chen was deposed. Talented individuals were recruited. In the sixth year, taxes on merchants' vehicles began. The Xiongnu invaded, and General Wei Qing captured 700 heads, while Gongsun Ao and Li Guang were defeated. The emperor pardoned soldiers for disciplinary violations.

In the first year of Shuo, the emperor issued edicts promoting virtue and talent, ordering officials to recommend filial and incorrupt individuals. Queen Wei was established. In the first year of Yuanshuo, the Xiongnu invaded, and Wei Qing captured thousands of heads. Dongyi submitted, and Canghai County was established. In the second year, the emperor granted titles to princes' sons. Wei Qing recovered Henan, establishing Shuofang and Wuyuan counties. People were moved to Shuofang and Maoling. In the third year, Canghai County was abolished, and the Xiongnu invaded Dai and Yanmen. The Empress Dowager died. In the fourth year, the emperor visited Ganquan. In the fifth year, drought struck, and Wei Qing captured over 15,000 Xiongnu heads. The emperor promoted education and set up doctoral disciples. In the sixth year, Wei Qing defeated the Xiongnu in the desert, but Zhao Xin surrendered and Su Jian fled. The emperor established martial arts reward officers.

In the first year of Yuanshou, the emperor obtained a white unicorn and wrote a song. Kings of Huainan and Hengshan rebelled and were executed. The crown prince was established. The emperor issued edicts rewarding filial piety and caring for the elderly. Generals Huo Qubing and Gongsun Ao captured thousands of Xiongnu heads. The Xiongnu king Kunxie surrendered, and Wuwei and Jiuquan counties were established. In the third year, a comet appeared, and the emperor pardoned the world. In the fourth year, Wei Qing and Huo Qubing defeated the Xiongnu in Mobei, with heavy casualties. Li Guang committed suicide. In the fifth year, half-liang coins were scrapped, and five-baht coins issued. In the sixth year, the emperor established princes and issued edicts on currency reform. He sent officials to inspect the country and relieve the needy.

In the first year of Yuanding, a tripod was found in the Fen River. In the second year, officials died, and Bailiang Terrace was built. Floods caused starvation. In the third year, the emperor moved Hangu and ordered tax evasion rewards. In the fourth year, he built Hou Tu Temple and granted titles to Zhou descendants. In the fifth year, he sacrificed to the sun and moon, found a tripod, and composed songs. In the sixth year, Nanyue rebelled, and the emperor sent generals to suppress it. The Western Qiang and Xiongnu invaded. In the first year of Yuanfeng, the emperor patrolled the border, shocking the Xiongnu. Dongyue submitted, and people were moved. He climbed Mount Song, built altars, and issued edicts on reforms.

In the first year of Taichu, the emperor revised the calendar and built a palace. In the second year, he sacrificed to Houtu. In the third year, he built fortresses. In the fourth year, Li Guangli beheaded the King of Dayuan. In the first year of Tianhan, the Xiongnu returned envoys. In the second year, Li Guangli fought the Xiongnu, but Li Ling surrendered. Witchcraft was investigated. In the third year, the emperor sacrificed at Mount Tai. In the fourth year, he sent generals against the Xiongnu. In the first year of Taishi, Gongsun Ao was executed. In the second year, the emperor obtained a white unicorn and changed gold forms. In the third year, he visited the East China Sea. In the fourth year, he sacrificed at Mount Tai. In the first year of Zhenghe, witchcraft occurred. In the second year, the crown prince fled and committed suicide. In the third year, Li Guangli surrendered to the Xiongnu. In the fourth year, the emperor plowed land and repaired Mount Tai. In the first year of Hou Yuan, he pardoned the world. In the second year, he made Prince Fuling crown prince and died in Wuzha Palace.
