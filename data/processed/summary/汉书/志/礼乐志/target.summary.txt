The principles of the "Six Classics" share a common goal, with the functions of "Li" (ritual) and "Yue" (music) being particularly urgent. For individuals, forgetting propriety leads to evil and contempt; for a state, losing it brings desolation and disorder. Ritual and music are made according to the laws of heaven and earth to communicate with gods, establish human ethics, and regulate all things. Specific rituals, such as those for marriage, drinking in the countryside, mourning, and meeting the emperor, are formulated to address human nature and prevent specific vices. Confucius stated that there is no better way to stabilize a country and govern the people than propriety, and no better way to change customs than music. When etiquette, music, government orders, and criminal laws all function smoothly, the kingly way is complete. The two are used together to create harmony and respect.

The Zhou Dynasty learned from the Xia and Yin Dynasties, creating a particularly complete etiquette system with 300 major rituals and 3,000 details. This led to widespread education, harmony, and a lack of disasters for over forty years. When the Zhou Dynasty declined, princes exceeded the law and deleted relevant documents. The Qin Dynasty then destroyed various schools, causing rituals and music to be scattered. After the Han Dynasty was established, Emperor Gaozu ordered Shusun Tong to formulate etiquette. Emperor Wen was pleased with Jia Yi's proposals to establish rituals, but ministers Zhou Bo and Guan Ying were jealous, so his suggestions were not adopted. Emperor Wu's plans were dropped due to Empress Dowager Dou's preference for Taoism. Dong Zhongshu argued that heaven advocates virtue over punishment, and that the Han Dynasty must change its education to reform the people. Emperor Xuan did not adopt Wang Ji's suggestion to select Confucian scholars and inherit old etiquette. Liu Xiang persuaded Emperor Cheng to build Piyong and arrange rituals and music, but the emperor died before the plan was completed. Wang Mang later built Piyong to confuse people and usurp the throne.

Emperor Guangwu established Mingtang and Biyong after thirty years of rule. However, moral education was not widespread because rituals and music were not in place. The rituals written by Shusun Tong were recorded with laws and collected by judges, but not passed down by Legalists. Music is loved by saints for its power to change people's minds. The former kings formulated the music of Yasong based on human emotions and the principles of music, coordinating with yin and yang to guide the five constants. Before composing music, emperors would use the music of their ancestors to teach the people. Various ancient emperors created specific pieces of music, such as Huangdi's "Xianchi" and Shun's "Shao." The "Shao" music was preserved in the state of Chen. Confucius, after hearing "Shao" in Qi, did not know the taste of meat for three months. During the Zhou Dynasty's decline, the music of Sangjian, Pushang, Zheng, and Wei appeared, causing internal disease and external political harm. After the Han Dynasty's establishment, Shusun Tong used musicians from the Qin Dynasty to produce music for the ancestral temple. Emperor Gaozu liked the music of Chu, so "Music in the Fang" has the melody of Chu music.

During Emperor Wu's reign, a Yuefu was established, and Li Yannian was appointed to compose poems and harmonize tones, resulting in nineteen chapters of songs. The text includes numerous poems from "Songs in Anshifang" and "Song of Suburban Sacrifice," which describe offerings to gods, the arrival of spirits, and the virtues of filial piety and good governance. At the time of Emperor Cheng, King Xian of Hejian presented official music for use in suburban temple dynasties, but it was not commonly used. The suburban temple poems of the Han Dynasty were criticized for lacking the deeds of ancestors and proper harmony. Emperor Ai, disliking the flourishing music of Zheng and Wei, abolished the Yuefu officials. Prime Minister Kong Guang and Grand Sikong Hewu reported on the number of musicians, recommending that 441 people be exempted as their music did not conform to the scriptures or was the music of Zheng and Wei. This memorial was approved. However, the people were long infected by these customs, and no Zhengle was produced to change them.
