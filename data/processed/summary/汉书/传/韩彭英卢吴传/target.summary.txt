Han Xin, born in Huaiyin, was poor and unable to become an official or engage in trade. He often relied on others for food. After his mother died, he buried her on a high, spacious site, hoping for future grandeur. He once ate at a pavilion chief's home, but the chief's wife resented him and prepared meals early, leaving none for him at breakfast. Han Xin understood and left. While fishing, an old woman rinsing silk fed him for dozens of days. Grateful, he promised to repay her, but she scolded him, saying she pitied him, not expecting repayment. A young man in Huaiyin insulted Han Xin, daring him to fight or crawl under his crotch. Han Xin stared, then crawled through, enduring public ridicule.

Han Xin joined Xiang Liang's army as a soldier, then served Xiang Yu as a郎中, offering advice that was ignored. He later defected to Liu Bang, King of Han, as a仓管员. Sentenced to death for a crime, he appealed to Teng Gong Xia Houying, who spared him after a conversation. Liu Bang made him a治粟都尉 but didn't value him. Han Xin fled when not promoted, but Xiao He chased him back. Xiao He convinced Liu Bang to formally appoint Han Xin as a大将, with a ceremony. Liu Bang asked Han Xin for strategies. Han Xin analyzed Xiang Yu's weaknesses—his inability to delegate, stinginess with rewards, and cruelty—contrasting Liu Bang's potential to win hearts by appointing capable men, rewarding merit, and respecting the people's wishes. He predicted easy conquest of the Three Qin states. Liu Bang followed his plan, marching east through Chencang to pacify the region.

In the second year of Han, Liu Bang conquered Wei and Henan, then attacked Pengcheng but was defeated. Han Xin later joined forces to defeat Chu troops. After Wei Bao rebelled, Han Xin, as左丞相, attacked Wei. He feigned a river crossing at Linjin while secretly crossing at Xiayang, capturing Wei Bao. Han Xin then requested 30,000 troops to conquer Yan, Zhao, and Qi. Liu Bang agreed, sending Zhang Er and others. Han Xin defeated Zhao at Jingxing, using a背水阵 (back-to-water formation) to inspire his troops to fight desperately. He captured King Zhao Xie and killed Chen Yu. Han Xin spared Li Zuoche, who advised against attacking Yan and Qi directly, suggesting instead to intimidate Yan into surrender and then approach Qi. Han Xin followed this, and Yan surrendered.

Han Xin was later made King of Qi. After Xiang Yu's death, Liu Bang demoted him to King of Chu. Han Xin rewarded the old woman who fed him and the young man who humiliated him, making him a lieutenant. When Liu Bang suspected him of rebellion, Han Xin was captured and made Marquis of Huaiyin. He feigned illness, avoiding court. Later, he plotted with Chen Xi to rebel but was betrayed. Empress Lü and Xiao He tricked him into the palace, where he was executed, his clan exterminated. Before death, he regretted not following Kuai Tong's advice.

Peng Yue, from Changyi, was a fisherman and bandit. He led a group of youths, enforcing strict discipline. He joined Liu Bang but later served Xiang Yu. After Xiang Yu's defeat, Liu Bang made him King of Liang. Accused of rebellion, he was captured, exiled, and killed by Empress Lü, his clan destroyed.

Ying Bu, from Liu County, was a convict who escaped to become a bandit. He joined Xiang Liang, then Xiang Yu, who made him King of Jiujiang. He killed the Righteous Emperor for Xiang Yu. Later, Sui He persuaded him to defect to Liu Bang. Ying Bu was made King of Huainan. After Han Xin and Peng Yue's deaths, he feared for his life and rebelled. He was defeated and killed by the emperor's forces.

Lu Wan, from Fengyi, was a childhood friend of Liu Bang. He became King of Yan. Suspected of colluding with Chen Xi and the Xiongnu, he fled to the Xiongnu after Liu Bang's death, dying there.

Wu Rui, a Qin magistrate, led Yue troops to support Liu Bang. Xiang Yu made him King of Hengshan, later Liu Bang made him King of Changsha. He died a year later, his kingdom passed down five generations before ending without an heir.

The historian comments that among the eight non-Liu kings, only Wu Rui remained loyal, his kingdom lasting longest due to his virtue.
