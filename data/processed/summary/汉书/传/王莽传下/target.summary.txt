# Summary of Wang Mang's Reign

In the fourth year of his reign, Wang Mang granted titles and rewards to several officials, including Lu Ta and Xian Zhi, who received the rank of special advance and were treated with the same etiquette as the three princes. He also awarded them houses, money, and ceremonial items. In June, he formally enfeoffed princes at the Mingtang, claiming he had spent nearly nine years planning the land system and feudal hierarchy based on classical texts. However, Wang Mang was known for his empty rhetoric and stinginess, using the undetermined land plan as an excuse to delay actual implementation while granting symbolic titles to appease those who desired rank.

Wang Mang reaffirmed six financial management systems, establishing harsh laws with punishments including execution for violations. He levied taxes on slave owners, requiring 3,600 yuan per slave, which caused widespread resentment and led to the rise of bandits. When officials like Nayan and Feng Chang advised against these policies, Wang Mang removed them from office. He appointed law enforcement officers with powers similar to Han dynasty governors to supervise the six systems. Rebellions erupted across the country, including Guatianyi's occupation of Changzhouyuan and Lu Mu's uprising in Langxie County, where a woman whose son was unjustly killed by a county magistrate gathered followers, attacked Haiqu County, and eventually commanded tens of thousands of troops.

Wang Mang's envoys reported that thieves dispersed but regrouped due to harsh prohibitions, heavy taxes, and the inability of people to make a living. Wang Mang dismissed officials who reported these truths and promoted those who flattered him. In August, he forged a copper weapon shaped like the Big Dipper to intimidate peasant armies, but during its casting, extreme cold killed many officials and horses. Fires broke out at the Northern Army camp, and when newly appointed officials like Fei Xing suggested lenient policies to address the root causes of rebellion, Wang Mang dismissed them. Officials across the country sought illegal profits due to unpaid salaries, amassing fortunes.

Wang Mang's grandson Wang Zong was discovered drawing himself in imperial regalia and engraving seals with rebellious inscriptions. After investigation, Wang Zong committed suicide, and his sister Wang Zhi also died after being implicated in murder and witchcraft. The Red Eyebrow Army, led by Li Zidu and Fan Chong, began their uprising in Langxie County due to famine, gathering ten thousand troops. Wang Mang's attempts to suppress them failed. In the sixth year, Wang Mang calculated a 36,000-year calendar and changed the year name, claiming to follow divine instructions, but people laughed at these practices.

The Kanto region suffered from drought and famine for consecutive years, causing rebel numbers to grow. Wang Mang recruited strong men, condemned criminals, and slaves to form elite troops, collecting one-thirtieth of property from officials and civilians to fund military campaigns. He also recruited specialists with unique skills to attack the Xiongnu, including a man who claimed to be able to fly but only managed a few hundred steps before falling. Wang Mang appointed them all as commanders despite knowing their claims were false.

Wang Mang's policies led to widespread suffering. People abandoned their homes, the old and weak died on roadsides, and the strong joined thieves. Officials like Han Bo tried to ridicule Wang Mang with absurd reports, but Wang Mang executed them. He changed the year name to Emperor of the Earth and issued harsh decrees allowing executions even in spring and summer, terrifying the people. When natural disasters occurred, Wang Mang blamed his officials rather than his own policies.

The rebellion continued to spread, with various leaders emerging across the country. Wang Mang's attempts to suppress them through military force and harsh laws only worsened the situation. His officials grew increasingly fearful, and the people's suffering intensified. The Han dynasty eventually revived under Emperor Gengshi, who captured Chang'an and executed Wang Mang's supporters. The Red Eyebrow Army later overthrew Emperor Gengshi, causing further destruction, until Emperor Shizu restored order and peace to the realm.
