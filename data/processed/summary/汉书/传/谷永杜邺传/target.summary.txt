Gu Yong, styled Ziyun, was from Chang'an. His father, Gu Ji, served as a Sima of the Wei Dynasty and was killed by Shanyu Zhizhi while on a diplomatic mission. Gu Yong wrote a short history of Chang'an in his youth and later extensively studied the classics. During Emperor Jianzhao's reign, Fan Yanshou, the imperial censor, recognized his talents and appointed him as a subordinate, later recommending him as Taichang Cheng. Gu Yong wrote many memorials discussing political gains and losses.

In the winter of the third year of Jianshi's reign, a solar eclipse and earthquake occurred on the same day. The emperor ordered the selection of upright scholars who dared to speak out. Tai Chang Yangcheng Marquis Liu Qingji recommended Gu Yong to await the imperial edict. Gu Yong replied that the emperor, with his pure moral character, was worried about the strange phenomena and sought advice. He stated that when a sage emperor ascends the throne, he must correct his appearance, speech, sight, hearing, and thinking, and establish imperial rules to obey the will of the Emperor of Heaven. If the king indulges in the harem, pleasure, and hunting, disasters will appear. The solar eclipse and earthquake were signs of faults, and the emperor should look for reasons within himself, possibly due to excessive attention to women, lack of political concern, or improper behavior.

Gu Yong cited historical examples: Guo Si caused Zong Zhou's downfall, and Yan's wife's arrogance led to a solar eclipse. He emphasized that the relationship between husband and wife is crucial for national safety. He advised regulating the harem, clarifying superiority and inferiority, and preventing jealousy and arrogance. He urged the emperor to correct himself, reduce private banquets, stop excessive pleasures, and follow etiquette and law. He also stressed the importance of selecting virtuous officials, assessing their merits, and avoiding favoritism. He warned that disasters are warnings from heaven, and if ignored, punishments will increase.

The emperor was surprised by Gu Yong's countermeasures and summoned him. Gu Yong continued, stating that the solar eclipse and earthquake were caused by the exclusive favor of the queen's concubine. He knew that General Wang Feng had just gained power and secretly wanted to rely on him. He argued that the borders were stable, the princes had limited power, and officials were loyal, so the disasters could not be blamed on the uncles. He advised the emperor to understand the warnings, change past mistakes, eliminate biased favor, and ensure all concubines served in turn to secure descendants and end disasters.

Gu Yong was promoted to Doctor Guanglu. He wrote to thank Wang Feng, praising his support. Later, when Wang Feng died, Wang Yin succeeded him. Gu Yong advised Wang Tan to resign from leading the city gate soldiers, which Wang Tan did, causing a rift with Wang Yin. Gu Yong then advised Wang Yin to work diligently, punish evildoers, and promote virtuous people. He warned about celestial signs like Taibai and Yinghuo, urging Wang Yin to change his ways to comply with God's will.

When a black dragon appeared, Gu Yong explained it as a sign of the same surname, possibly indicating a threat from a relative or a cruel successor. He warned about the dangers of women and evil people in power, citing historical examples. He criticized the emperor for indulging in private pleasures, neglecting duties, and causing disasters. He urged the emperor to reform, reduce taxes, stop excessive construction, and care for the people. The emperor was moved but did not fully trust Gu Yong.

Gu Yong served as governor of the North and later died at home. He was proficient in Tianguan and "Jingshi Yi," and his memorials focused on the lord and the harem. Du Ye, styled Zixia, was from Fanyang and studied with Zhang Ji. He was friendly with Wang Yin and advised him to cooperate with Wang Shang. Du Ye later served as governor of Liangzhou but was dismissed due to illness. During Emperor Ai's reign, he criticized the influence of the Fu and Ding families, warning about solar eclipses and earthquakes as signs of imbalance. He urged the emperor to follow ancient laws and satisfy the people's wishes. Du Ye died without an official title.

The commentary notes that during Emperor Xiaocheng's era, power was with relatives, while during Emperor Xiaoai's era, the Fu and Ding families held power. Du Ye dared to accuse the Ding and Fu families, but Gu Yong avoided criticizing the Wang family due to circumstances. Gu Yong's loyalty was shown in his warnings, but he also flattered Wang Feng and alienated others, lacking integrity but excelling in rhetoric.
