Wang Mang, also known as Jujun, was the nephew of Empress Xiaoyuan. During the reigns of Emperors Yuan and Cheng of the Han Dynasty, Empress Yuan's father and brothers all became marquises, held important positions, and assisted in government affairs. Nine family members were granted the title of marquis, and five served as chief ministers. Only Wang Mang's father, Wang Man, died early and was not granted a title. Wang Mang's uncles and brothers, as sons of generals and marquises, lived luxurious lives, but Wang Mang, being lonely and poor, treated others with humility and lived simply. He studied "Etiquette" and "Zhou Rites" under Chen Shen, dressed like an ordinary scholar, served his mother and widowed sister-in-law, raised his fatherless nephew, and behaved with caution. He made friends with outstanding talents and served his uncles with tact and courtesy. During the Yangshuo period, his uncle Wang Feng fell ill, and Wang Mang served him diligently, not combing his hair or washing his face for months. When Wang Feng was dying, he entrusted Wang Mang to the Queen Mother and Emperor Cheng, leading to Wang Mang's appointment as Huang Menlang and later promotion to Shesheng Xiaowei.

Over time, his uncle Chengdu Marquis Wang Shang and other notable figures spoke highly of Wang Mang, leading Emperor Cheng to consider him a wise man. In the first year of Yongshi, Wang Mang was granted the title of Marquis of Xindu with 1,500 households. He was later promoted to Cavalry Captain and Guanglu Doctor, serving as a guard in the palace. As his official position rose, his attitude became more humble. He distributed his wealth to guests, leaving no extra clothing in his house, and supported famous people, making friends with many officials. His reputation spread throughout the government and public, surpassing his uncles. Wang Mang's brother Wang Yong had died, leaving a son named Wang Guang, whom Wang Mang had study under a doctor. During his vacations, Wang Mang visited his teacher with gifts and gave presents to classmates, attracting admiration from scholars and elders. Wang Guang married on the same day as Wang Mang's son Wang Yu, with a hall full of congratulatory guests. Wang Mang once secretly bought a maidservant, claiming he bought her for General Zhu Ziyuan because she could bear many sons, and gave her away that day, hiding his true feelings to pursue fame.

Wang Mang secretly collected crimes of Chun Yuchang, the son of the Queen Mother's sister, and reported him to Emperor Cheng through Grand Sima Wanggen. Chunyu Chang was executed, and Wang Mang gained a reputation for loyalty. Wang Gen then recommended Wang Mang to replace him, and Emperor Cheng promoted Wang Mang to Grand Sima at age thirty-eight. Wang Mang set strict demands on himself, hired virtuous people as subordinates, and used his rewards and fiefdom income to entertain scholars, living frugally. His wife, when greeting guests, wore a sarong that only reached her knees, surprising those who mistook her for a slave. After Emperor Cheng died and Emperor Ai succeeded, the Queen Mother ordered Wang Mang to resign, but Emperor Ai refused his resignation and reappointed him. Wang Mang later clashed with Empress Dowager Fu, leading to his resignation again. He was rewarded with gold and chariots and returned to his palace. Many officials praised him, and the emperor treated him with favor, sending special envoys to serve him at home.

After three years in his feudal state, hundreds of officials petitioned for Wang Mang's recall. In the first year of Yuanshou, a solar eclipse occurred, and virtuous men praised Wang Mang's merits, leading the emperor to recall him. When Wang Mang returned to the capital, Emperor Ai died without a son, and the Queen Mother summoned Wang Mang to take control. Wang Mang reported that Da Sima Gao'an Hou Dong Xian was too young and not suitable, so his seal was confiscated, and Dong Xian committed suicide. The Queen Mother appointed Wang Mang as Grand Sima, and they selected the King of Zhongshan to succeed as Emperor Xiaoping. When Emperor Ping was nine, the Queen Mother acted as regent and entrusted government affairs to Wang Mang. Wang Mang deposed Empress Xiaocheng Tang and Empress Xiaoai Fu, ordering them to commit suicide. He treated Grand Situ Kong Guang with respect and used him to report on officials he disliked, leading to their dismissal or exile. Wang Mang also forced his uncle Wang Li to return to his feudal country, using coercion and blackmail to control the Queen Mother and her ministers.

Wang Mang promoted those who obeyed him and killed those who opposed him. He used false humility to confuse the Queen Mother and the public. When the Yizhou Order offered white-feathered pheasants, Wang Mang reported it to the Queen Mother, and the ministers recommended he be granted the title of Anmo Gong with increased fiefdoms. Wang Mang refused multiple times, but the Queen Mother eventually granted him the title. He accepted the titles of Taifu and An Mogong but resigned from the increased fiefdom and hereditary rewards. He suggested rewarding other officials and implementing benevolent policies. After winning public favor, Wang Mang wanted to be dictatorial and hinted to officials to write memorials saying the Queen Mother should not interfere in trivial matters. The Queen Mother issued an edict allowing Wang Mang to evaluate and decide on official matters, giving him power equal to the emperor.

Wang Mang used false reputations to please the Queen Mother, donating money and land to help the poor. He led ministers in reporting auspicious omens and asked the Queen Mother to conserve her energy. During floods or droughts, Wang Mang only ate vegetables, and the Queen Mother urged him to eat meat for his health. Wang Mang sent envoys to the Xiongnu Chanyu, who changed his name to comply with Chinese customs. Wang Mang also gave his daughter to Emperor Ping as queen to consolidate his power, despite initial refusal. The ministers insisted, and Wang Mang's daughter became queen. Wang Mang declined increased betrothal gifts and distributed them to poor families. Chen Chong, a Grand Situ Sizhi, wrote a memorial praising Wang Mang's virtues and merits, comparing him to the Duke of Zhou and recommending he be granted the title of prime minister. The Queen Mother showed the memorial to the ministers, and they discussed it.

Wang Mang's son Wang Yu, fearing Emperor Ping's resentment, conspired with his teacher Wu Zhang and brother-in-law Lu Kuan to smear Wang Mang's mansion with blood to frighten him. Wang Mang arrested Wang Yu, who drank poison and died. Wang Mang killed Wang Yu's wife after she gave birth and used the incident to kill the Wei family and others, implicating many counties and feudal states. Hundreds of people died, shocking the world. Chen Zuo, the Grand Sima Hujun, praised Wang Mang for punishing his son like the Duke of Zhou. The ministers requested that Wang Mang's admonitions be distributed and treated like the "Book of Filial Piety." In the spring of the fourth year of the Yuan Dynasty, people worshiped heaven and offered sacrifices to Emperor Gaozu and Emperor Xiaowen. Wang Mang's daughter was made queen, and a general amnesty was announced. Eight officials were dispatched to inspect social customs. Taibao Wang Shun and others reported that Wang Mang's merits should be rewarded like Yi Yin and the Duke of Zhou. The ministers requested that Wang Mang be given the title of prime minister, ranking above the three dukes, with various honors and rewards.

Wang Mang refused the rewards, but the Queen Mother insisted. He eventually accepted the titles and rewards, including the prime minister's seal. Wang Mang proposed building a Mingtang, a national imperial college, and other structures, recruiting scholars from across the country. The ministers reported that Wang Mang's merits were evident, and the construction was completed quickly. The Queen Mother agreed to reward Wang Mang with nine kinds of utensils. In the fifth year of the Yuan Dynasty, many princes and officials petitioned for Wang Mang's rewards. Wang Mang wrote a letter declining, but the Queen Mother insisted. The ministers reported that Wang Mang's merits were like those of Yi Yin and the Duke of Zhou, and they requested the reward of nine tins. The Queen Mother approved, and Wang Mang received various honors and items, including a green apron, formal dress, a jade-inlaid saber, chariots, and military equipment. He was also given a red painted door and steps inside the eaves, with guards and officials appointed to assist him.

In the autumn, Wang Mang dug the Ziwu Road due to an auspicious sign from his daughter, Queen Wang. Eight custom envoys returned, reporting that the customs of the world were uniform and forging folk songs praising virtues. Wang Mang reported this and recorded it in documents. He also reported that there were no lawsuits, thieves, or hungry people, and the system of men and women not walking together was implemented. Twelve people, including Liu Xin and Chen Chong, were granted the title of marquis for building the Mingtang. Wang Mang sent envoys to attract the Qiang people, who offered their land to become subjects. Wang Mang proposed establishing Xihai County and revising state names and boundaries. The memorial was approved, and fifty additional laws were added, with violators exiled to Xihai County. The people became resentful. Liu Qing, Marquis of Quanling, wrote a letter suggesting Wang Mang act as regent like the Duke of Zhou. The ministers agreed. When Emperor Ping fell ill, Wang Mang prayed for his safety and offered to sacrifice his own life. Emperor Ping died, and Wang Mang recruited scholars to participate in the decision to mourn for three years. He selected Liu Ying, a two-year-old great-great-grandson of Emperor Xuan, as the successor.

A white stone with vermilion words reading "Announcement of Prince Mang of Anhan to be the emperor" was discovered. Wang Mang asked the princes to report this to the Queen Mother, who initially refused but eventually agreed to let Wang Mang act as regent. The ministers reported that Wang Mang should ascend the throne and exercise imperial power, following the example of the Duke of Zhou. The Queen Mother issued an edict allowing Wang Mang to serve as regent and act as the emperor. Wang Mang then sat on the throne, wore the emperor's clothes, and accepted the worship of the ministers. The next year, the name was changed to Jushe. In the first year of Jushe, Wang Mang worshiped the gods and held ceremonies. Liu Ying was made the crown prince, known as Ruzi. Wang Shun, Zhen Feng, and Zhen Han were appointed as officials. In April, Liu Chong, Marquis of Anzhong, attacked Wancheng but failed. Wang Mang pardoned those who surrendered. Liu Jia wrote a memorial praising Wang Mang and suggesting severe punishment for Liu Chong. Wang Mang was pleased and granted Liu Jia a fiefdom. The ministers reported that Wang Mang's power should be increased to conquer the world. The Queen Mother ordered Wang Mang to call himself the "fake emperor" when seeing her. In December, the ministers petitioned to increase Wang Mang's official offices and household staff. The memorial was approved.

In the spring of the second year, new currencies were minted, and a ban was issued on carrying gold privately. In September, Zhai Yi, the prefect of Dongjun, raised troops and elected Liu Xin as emperor, accusing Wang Mang of poisoning Emperor Ping. Wang Mang was frightened and sent eight generals to attack Zhai Yi. Tang Ming and Huo Hong also raised troops to respond, growing to nearly 100,000 people. Wang Mang sent generals to resist them and appointed Zhen Han as the general. In December, Wang Yi and other generals defeated Zhai Yi. Chen Chong wrote a memorial praising Wang Mang's wisdom. Wang Mang was very happy. In the spring of three years, an earthquake occurred, and Wang Mang announced a general amnesty. Wang Yi and others defeated Ming Dynasty and Huo Hong. Wang Mang held a banquet to reward the generals and wrote a memorial suggesting that the Zhou system be followed for titles and fiefs. The memorial was approved, and several hundred people were granted titles. The ministers reported that Wang Mang's sons should be promoted to the title of Duke. The Queen Mother agreed, and Wang An and Wang Lin were made dukes. Wang Guang was granted the title of Yan Gonghou. Wang Mang then planned to ascend to the true throne.

In September, Wang Mang's mother died. Instead of expressing grief, Wang Mang asked the Queen Mother to discuss his mourning clothes. The doctors and Confucian scholars said that the regent should not wear mourning clothes for his biological mother. Wang Mang implemented their suggestion. Chen Chong reported that Wang Guang had privately told Zhi Jinwu Shi Ji to kill for him. Wang Mang was furious, and Wang Guang and his mother committed suicide. Wang Mang wrote a letter about the prohibition of playing music and asked about the grades of music. In this year, Liu Jing, Hu Yun, and Zang Hong reported auspicious omens. Wang Mang readily accepted them. On Jiazi day in November, Wang Mang wrote a memorial to the Queen Mother, citing various omens and requesting to be called the "false emperor" and to change the calendar. The memorial was approved. The common people learned of his belief in the talisman, and the ministers discussed it widely. Aizhang, a native of Zitong County, made a copper cabinet with inscriptions claiming Wang Mang was the true emperor. Wang Mang went to the Gaodi Temple to receive the copper cabinet, wore the crown, and ascended the throne as the True Dragon Emperor. He declared a new country, changed the calendar, and set the colors of carriages, horses, and clothing to yellow.
