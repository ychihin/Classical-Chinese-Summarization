# Summary of Historical Biographies of Han Dynasty Officials

Gai Kuanrao, from Weijun, rose through the ranks due to his classical knowledge and filial piety. As an imperial censor, he impeached Pengzu, son of General Zhang Anshi, for refusing to dismount at the palace gate. However, since Pengzu had actually dismounted, Kuanrao was demoted for misrepresentation. As Sima of the Guards, he reformed protocols, personally cared for soldiers' welfare, and earned such loyalty that retiring soldiers requested to serve another year. Emperor Xuan promoted him to Sili Xiaowei, where he fearlessly investigated officials, creating peace in the capital. At a celebration for Xu Bo's new residence, Kuanrao sat apart and warned about the impermanence of wealth. He later impeached a performer for disrespectful dancing. Despite his integrity and poverty—using half his salary to pay informants—his harshness and tendency to frame others created enemies. Wang Sheng advised him to be more cautious, but Kuanrao ignored this counsel. He submitted a memorial criticizing the emperor's reliance on eunuchs and suggesting that rulers should yield to the worthy. The emperor deemed this treasonous. Despite Zheng Chang's defense, Kuanrao was imprisoned and committed suicide.

Zhuge Feng, from Langxie, became Sili Xiaowei under Emperor Yuan. He attempted to arrest the favored relative Xu Zhang but was thwarted when Xu fled to the palace. The emperor confiscated Zhuge's talisman, ending the Sili's traditional authority. Zhuge apologized in a memorial but continued criticizing corrupt officials. He was transferred to city gate captain after arresting prisoners in spring and summer. When he accused former colleagues, the emperor removed him from office for untrustworthiness, and he died at home.

Liu Fu, a royal family member, became an admonishing doctor under Emperor Cheng. He opposed making Tang Jiandan queen, arguing it defied divine will. The emperor imprisoned him in a secret prison. Several high officials petitioned for his release, arguing that punishing remonstrators chilled loyal hearts. The emperor reduced his sentence to hard labor.

Zheng Chong served under Emperor Ai, known for his distinctive leather shoes. He opposed ennobling Fu Shang, the emperor's grandmother's cousin, citing precedents where such actions caused celestial disturbances. Despite the Empress Dowager's anger, Zheng persisted. He also criticized Dong Xian's excessive favor. After being alienated, he was accused of colluding with the royal family and died in prison.

Sun Bao served as Yizhou governor, quelling rebellions by offering amnesty to non-leaders. He exposed corruption involving the emperor's uncle Wang Li. As Jing Zhaoyin, he was pressured to protect the knight Du Zhiji but managed to avoid confrontation. He was dismissed when Chunyu Chang fell from power. Recalled under Emperor Ai, he investigated Empress Dowager Feng's death, angering Empress Dowager Fu, who had him imprisoned. Released after appeals, he later defended Zheng Chong but was dismissed. Under Emperor Ping, he opposed praising Wang Mang's virtues, was dismissed again, and lived in retirement.

Wu Jianglong advised Emperor Cheng to recruit the Prince of Dingtao as heir. Under Emperor Ai, he criticized giving weapons from the imperial arsenal to Dong Xian and the emperor's wet nurse. After Empress Dowager Fu's maid purchase dispute, he was demoted. Wang Mang later exiled him for his role in Empress Dowager Feng's case.

He Bing, as Changling magistrate, killed the fugitive Wang Linqing, who had murdered a servant. Though Empress Dowager Qiong Cheng grieved, Emperor Ai promoted He Bing. As Yingchuan prefect, he arrested criminals Zhao Ji and Li Kuan, and drove out Zhong Wei, whose brother was a powerful official. He Bing governed with integrity, his family not living in the government office. On his deathbed, he requested a simple burial.

The commentary praises Gai Kuanrao's integrity but notes he should have heeded Wang Sheng's advice. Zhuge, Liu, and Zheng had unique ambitions. Even these upright officials had flaws, showing how difficult true integrity is. He Bing's character rivaled Yin Wenggui's.
