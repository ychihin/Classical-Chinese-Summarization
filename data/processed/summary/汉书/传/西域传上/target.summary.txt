The Western Regions began communication with the Central Plains during Emperor Wu of Han, originally comprising thirty-six countries that later divided into over fifty kingdoms, all located west of the Xiongnu and south of the Wusun. The region spans more than 6,000 miles east to west and over 1,000 miles north to south, bordered by the Han Dynasty to the east with Yumen Pass and Yang Pass as fortresses, and Congling to the west. Its central river has two sources: one from Congling Mountain and another from Khotan, flowing north to merge and eastward into Puchang Sea (also known as Yanze), a stable lake about 300 miles wide. The lake water flows underground, emerging at Jishi Mountain as the Huanghui in the Central Plains. Two main roads lead from Yumen and Yang Pass to the Western Regions: the south road via Shanshan and Yarkand, crossing Congling to reach Dayuezhi and Anxi; the north road via Cheshi and Shule, crossing Congling to reach Dawan, Kangju, and Yancai.

Most Western Region countries were settled, with cities, fields, and livestock, distinct from Xiongnu and Wusun customs. They were historically enslaved by the Xiongnu, who appointed a boy servant captain to manage the region, collecting taxes from various countries. After the Zhou Dynasty's decline, Rong and Di ethnic groups lived north of the Jingshui and Weishui rivers. Qin Shihuang drove them out and built the Great Wall, but the west did not exceed Lintao County. From the Western Han's establishment to Emperor Wu, Zhang Qian opened the road to the Western Regions. Later, Huo Qubing defeated the Xiongnu, leading to the surrender of King Hunxie and Xiutu. The Han Dynasty built beacons west of Lingju, established Jiuquan County, and later recruited people to settle in Wuwei, Zhangye, and Dunhuang, guarding Yumen and Yang Pass.

After Li Guangli attacked Dayuan, Western Region countries sent envoys to Chang'an. The Han Dynasty built pavilions and barriers from Dunhuang to Yanze, stationed field soldiers in Luntai and Quli, and set up envoys and captains to supervise farming. During Emperor Xuan's reign, Wei Sima oversaw several countries west of Shanshan. After defeating Gu Shi, the Han Dynasty divided it into the former and latter kings of Cheshi and the six kingdoms of Shanbei, initially supervising only the southern route. Later, King Rizhu betrayed Chanyu and surrendered to the Han Dynasty. Zheng Ji, the envoy protecting Shanshan, met King Rizhu, and both were granted marquis titles. The Han Dynasty established the Protectorate, beginning with Zheng Ji, who protected the North Road. The Xiongnu's child servant captain was dismissed, weakening the Xiongnu. The Han Dynasty moved people to farm in Beixu Tar, dividing Shache's land. The Protectorate monitored Wusun, Kangju, and other foreign countries, stationed in Wulei City, 2,738 miles from Yangguan, due to its fertile land and central location.

During Emperor Yuan's reign, Wuji Xiaowei was appointed to man fields. Wang Zilizhi of Baotui Dongpu surrendered with over 1,700 people, and the Protectorate allocated land for them. Since the Xuancha and Wutong period, Li Sculpture Chanyu Xianghui became a vassal, and details of land, mountains, rivers, and distances were recorded. Starting from the nearest west of Yangguan, the Laoqiang Kingdom had 450 households, 1,750 people, and 500 troops, living with livestock and not farming. The Shanshan Kingdom (formerly Loulan) had 1,570 households, 14,100 people, and 2,912 troops, with officials including Fu Guohou and translators. It produced jade, reeds, and willow, and its people lived with livestock. Initially, Emperor Wu was moved by Zhang Qian's words and sent many envoys to Dawan. Loulan and Gushi, on the main road, attacked Han envoys and acted as Xiongnu spies. Emperor Wu sent Zhao Ponu and Wang Hui to attack Gushi and capture the King of Loulan, leading to the construction of pavilions west to Yumen Pass. Loulan surrendered but later sent hostages to both the Han and Xiongnu. After King Loulan died, the Han Dynasty could not send back a castrated hostage, leading to a new king who declined to visit the Han emperor. Later, Wei Tuqi, the king's brother, surrendered to the Han Dynasty. In the fourth year of Yuanfeng, Fu Jiezi assassinated King Loulan, established Wei Tuqi as king, and renamed the country Shanshan. The Han Dynasty sent a Sima and forty officials to garrison Yixun, marking the beginning of official establishment there. Shanshan was a strategic point on the road to the Western Regions, with various countries like Qiemo, Xiaowan, Jingjue, Ronglu, Shanmi, Qule, Khotan, Pishan, Wushuo, Xiye, Puli, Yinai, Wulei, Nandou, and Jibin, each with specific populations, troops, and distances from Chang'an. Jibin, a large country not under the Western Region Protector, had a mild climate, produced various crops and metals, and had a market with gold and silver coins. The Han Dynasty's communication with Jibin began under Emperor Wu, but later conflicts arose, including the killing of Han envoys. Du Qin advised against further engagement due to the dangers and costs, and the Han Dynasty eventually limited contact. Wuyi Mountain, another large country, had similar customs to Jibin, with a hot climate and lush vegetation. Anxi Kingdom, a very large country with hundreds of cities, used silver coins and had a written language. Emperor Wu sent envoys to Parthia, who were greeted by a general with 20,000 cavalry. Dayuezhi, originally a nomadic country, was defeated by the Xiongnu and moved west to Daxia, establishing their capital. Daxia was divided into five tribes, each with its own marquis, all belonging to Dayuezhi. Kangju, with 120,000 households and 600,000 people, had customs similar to Dayuezhi and was restrained by the Xiongnu. During Emperor Xuan's reign, the Han Dynasty killed Zhizhi Chanyu. Under Emperor Cheng, King Kangju was arrogant, and the Protectorate recommended cutting ties, but the court maintained relations. Yancai, northwest of Kangju, had over 100,000 warriors and customs similar to Kangju. Dayuan, with 60,000 households and 300,000 people, produced good horses and used grapes for wine. Emperor Wu sent Li Guangli to attack Dayuan, resulting in the dedication of two Pegasus horses annually. From Dawan to Parthia, residents had different languages but similar customs, with deep-set eyes and long beards. They were skilled in business and obtained gold and silver from the Han Dynasty. After Huhanxie Chanyu submitted to the Han Dynasty, these countries respected the Han. Various other kingdoms like Taohua, Xiuxun, Zhendu, Shache, Shule, and Weitou had specific populations, troops, and distances, with some farming and raising livestock.
