Ji Bu, a native of Chu, was known for his chivalry and served as a general under Xiang Yu, causing difficulties for Liu Bang, the King of Han. After Xiang Yu's defeat, Emperor Gaozu of Han offered a reward for Ji Bu's capture, threatening punishment for anyone who harbored him. Ji Bu hid at Zhou's house in Puyang, and with Zhou's help, disguised himself as a prisoner and was sold to the Zhu family in Lu. Zhu Jia, recognizing Ji Bu, bought him and later persuaded Xia Houying, the Marquis of Ruyin, to intercede with the emperor. Xia Houying conveyed Zhu Jia's argument that pursuing Ji Bu would show a lack of magnanimity and might drive him to enemy states. The emperor pardoned Ji Bu, who then became a doctor.

During Emperor Hui's reign, Ji Bu served as Zhonglang General. When the Xiongnu Chanyu insulted Empress Lu, General Fan Kuai proposed leading 100,000 troops against them. Ji Bu opposed, citing Emperor Gaozu's past defeat with 300,000 soldiers and warning that such action could incite rebellion. The Empress abandoned the plan. Later, as Governor of Hedong, Ji Bu was summoned by Emperor Wen but sent back after a month. He criticized the emperor for being swayed by praise and slander, and Emperor Wen, ashamed, allowed him to return to his post.

Cao Qiusheng, a rhetorician, sought Ji Bu's favor through a letter from Baochangjun. Despite initial anger, Ji Bu welcomed him after Cao explained that he could enhance Ji Bu's reputation. Ji Bu's fame grew due to Cao's promotion. Ji Bu's half-brother Ding Gong, who had spared Liu Bang during a battle, was executed by Emperor Gaozu for disloyalty.

Luan Bu, a former slave and general under Peng Yue, mourned Peng Yue after his execution for treason. Arrested and threatened with death, Luan Bu defended Peng Yue's loyalty and criticized the emperor's harshness. He was released and made a captain. Later, he served as prime minister of Yan and was known for rewarding kindness and avenging wrongs.

Uncle Tian, a native of Zhao, served as a doctor under King Zhang Ao. When Zhang Ao was implicated in a plot against the emperor, Uncle Tian followed him to Chang'an in prison garb. After Zhang Ao's release, Uncle Tian became a county guard. Emperor Wen appointed him prime minister of Lu, where he handled disputes and discouraged King Lu from hunting. He died in office, and his son Tian Ren refused funeral gifts, later serving as a general under Wei Qing. Tian Ren was executed for allowing the crown prince to flee during a rebellion.

The text praises Ji Bu for his resilience and eventual fame, Luan Bu for his loyalty, and Uncle Tian for his integrity, emphasizing that their actions were driven by faith and purpose.
