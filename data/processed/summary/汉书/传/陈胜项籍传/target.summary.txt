Chen Sheng, courtesy name She, was from Yangcheng, and Wu Guang, courtesy name Shu, was from Yangxia. When Chen She was young, he worked as a hired farmhand. Once, while resting, he expressed frustration and told his fellow workers that if they became rich, they should not forget each other. They laughed at him, but Chen Sheng sighed, "How can a sparrow know the ambition of a swan?" In the autumn of the first year of Qin II, the government sent 900 people, including Chen Sheng and Wu Guang as village chiefs, to guard Yuyang. They encountered heavy rain in Qidaze Township, blocking the road and causing them to miss the deadline, which was punishable by beheading. Chen Sheng and Wu Guang discussed that escaping or rising up both led to death, so they decided to die for the state. Chen Sheng noted that the people had long suffered under Qin's harsh rule. He proposed pretending to be Fusu, the eldest son of the First Emperor, and Xiang Yan, a beloved Chu general, to gain support. Wu Guang agreed. They consulted a fortune teller who hinted at using ghosts and gods to gain prestige. They wrote "King Chen Sheng" on silk and hid it in a fish, which soldiers found. Chen Sheng also had Wu Guang imitate a fox's cry at night, shouting, "Great Chu is rising, King Chen Sheng." The soldiers were frightened and talked about it the next day. Chen Sheng and Wu Guang, known for their kindness, killed the two escorting generals after Wu Guang provoked them. Chen Sheng addressed the soldiers, saying that missing the deadline meant death, and that strong men should leave a great name. The soldiers agreed, and they staged an uprising, pretending to be Fusu and Xiang Yan. They attacked Daze Township, Qixian, and other counties, recruiting troops along the way. By the time they reached Chen County, they had six to seven hundred chariots, over a thousand cavalry, and tens of thousands of infantry. They captured Chen County, and the local elders declared Chen Sheng king, known as Zhang Chu. Many counties killed their Qin officials in response. Chen Sheng appointed Wu Guang as acting king to attack Rongyang, and sent others to capture Zhao, Jiujiang, and Wei. Ge Ying, sent to capture territory, established Xiang Qiang as King of Chu but later killed him upon hearing of Chen Sheng's kingship. Chen Sheng then killed Ge Ying. Wu Guang besieged Rongyang but could not capture it. Chen Sheng appointed Zhou Wen as general to attack Qin. Zhou Wen gathered troops and reached Hangu Pass, but Qin general Zhang Han defeated him. Zhou Wen committed suicide after several defeats. Wu Chen proclaimed himself King of Zhao, angering Chen Sheng, but Cai Ci advised against killing his family. Chen Sheng sent an envoy to congratulate Zhao and moved the family to the palace. Zhao decided not to march west but to expand north into Yan. Han Guang, sent to attack Yan, was advised to proclaim himself King of Yan, which he did. Tian Dan killed the magistrate of Di County and proclaimed himself King of Qi, attacking Zhou Shi. Zhou Shi returned to Wei and made Ningling Junjiu King of Wei. Tian Zang and others killed Wu Guang, claiming he was arrogant and incompetent. Chen Sheng made Tian Zang a general, but he was killed by Zhang Han. Deng Yue and Wu Feng fled to Chen County after defeats. Chen Sheng killed Deng Shuo. Qin Jia and others independently raised armies. Chen Sheng sent Wu Ping Junpan to oversee them, but Qin Jia killed him. Zhang Han attacked Chen County, and Chen Sheng personally led the battle but was defeated. In December, Chen Sheng's coachman Zhuangmai killed him and surrendered to Qin. Chen Sheng was buried in Ci County. His old minister Lu Chen raised a "Cangtou Army," killed Zhuangmai, and made Chen County the capital of Chu. Song Liu, sent to pacify Nanyang, surrendered to Qin after hearing of Chen Sheng's death. Qin Jia appointed Jing Ju as King of Chu. Chen Sheng reigned for six months. His old friends came to see him but were killed for speaking freely, causing others to leave. Chen Sheng appointed Zhu Fang and Hu Wu, who were harsh, leading to his failure. Despite his death, the generals he appointed eventually destroyed Qin. Emperor Gaozu arranged for sacrifices to Chen Sheng's tomb, which continued until Wang Mang's time. Xiang Ji, courtesy name Yu, was from Xiaxiang. His uncle was Xiang Liang, son of Xiang Yan, a famous Chu general. The Xiang family had been generals for generations. Xiang Ji learned writing and swordsmanship but gave up, wanting to learn to resist tens of thousands. Xiang Liang taught him the art of war, but Xiang Ji only learned a little. Xiang Liang killed someone and fled to Wuzhong with Xiang Ji. When Qin Shihuang traveled to Kuaiji, Xiang Ji said, "We can replace him," shocking Xiang Liang. Xiang Ji was eight feet two inches tall, could lift a cauldron, and was feared by the youth of Wuzhong. In the first year of Qin II, Yin Tong, the acting governor of Kuaiji, discussed rebellion with Xiang Liang. Xiang Liang had Xiang Ji kill Yin Tong, then took control of the county, raising 8,000 elite soldiers. Xiang Liang became general of Kuaiji, with Xiang Ji as deputy. In the second year, Xiang Liang led 8,000 men across the Yangtze River. Chen Ying, who had raised 20,000 men in Dongyang, joined him on his mother's advice. Xiang Liang crossed the Huai River, and Yingbu and others joined him, totaling 60,000-70,000 men. Xiang Liang attacked Qin Jia, who had betrayed Chen Sheng, and killed him. He then heard of Chen Sheng's death and summoned generals to Xue County. Fan Zeng advised Xiang Liang to support a descendant of King Huai of Chu. Xiang Liang found King Huai's grandson, who was tending sheep, and made him King Huai of Chu. Xiang Liang called himself Lord Wu Xin. He defeated Qin forces at Dong'a and Dingtao but became arrogant. Song Yi warned him, but Xiang Liang ignored him. Qin reinforced Zhang Han, who defeated and killed Xiang Liang at Dingtao. Xiang Yu and Pei Gong retreated. Zhang Han, thinking Chu was weak, crossed the Yellow River to attack Zhao, besieging Julu. King Huai of Chu appointed Song Yi as general, with Xiang Yu as second-in-command, to rescue Zhao. Song Yi stopped at Anyang, refusing to advance. Xiang Yu killed Song Yi, claiming he was a traitor, and became general. He led troops across the Zhang River, sinking ships and burning supplies, and defeated the Qin army at Julu, killing Su Jiao and capturing Wang Li. The Chu army became dominant among the princes. Zhang Han, fearing Zhao Gao, surrendered to Xiang Yu after negotiations. Xiang Yu made Zhang Han King Yong and placed him in the Chu army. He then killed over 200,000 surrendered Qin soldiers at night. Xiang Yu entered Hangu Pass and arrived at Hongmen. Pei Gong had captured Xianyang, and Xiang Yu was angry. Fan Zeng advised attacking Pei Gong, but Xiang Bo saved him. Xiang Yu sacked Xianyang, killed Qin's prince, and burned the palace. He killed Han Sheng for criticizing him. Xiang Yu betrayed the agreement that the first to enter Guanzhong would be king. He moved King Huai to Changsha and divided the world, making Pei Gong King of Han, and others kings. He proclaimed himself Overlord of Western Chu. Tian Rong rebelled, and Xiang Yu attacked Qi, killing many. The King of Han attacked Chu, but Xiang Yu defeated him at Pengcheng, killing over 100,000 Han soldiers. The King of Han escaped and gathered troops. Xiang Yu besieged Rongyang, but the King of Han escaped. Fan Zeng died after being suspected. Xiang Yu defeated Peng Yue but lost Chenggao. He made a pact with the King of Han to divide the world, but the King of Han broke it. Xiang Yu was besieged at Gaixia, hearing Chu songs from all sides. He sang a tragic song, then escaped with 800 cavalry. Only 28 remained. He killed many Han soldiers but was surrounded. He refused to cross the Wu River, saying he had no face to see his people. He killed himself, and his body was divided by five Han soldiers. The King of Han buried him and spared his family. The text concludes with a praise, discussing Qin's rise and fall, and criticizing Xiang Yu's arrogance and failure to learn from the past.
