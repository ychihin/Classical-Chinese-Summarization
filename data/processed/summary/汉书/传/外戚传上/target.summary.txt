Since ancient times, emperors who founded dynasties and monarchs who inherited thrones have relied not only on their own virtues but also on the help of their relatives. The rise of the Xia Dynasty was tied to the Tushan clan, while Jie's exile was due to Moxi; the Yin Dynasty's rise involved thieves and marries, and Zhou's demise came from favoring Daji; the Zhou Dynasty depended on Jiang Xi, Tairen, and Taisi, and King You was captured by Rong Di because of his lewd relationship with Guo Si. Thus, the "Book of Changes" begins with the hexagrams "Qian" and "Kun," "The Book of Songs" starts with "Guan Ju," "Shangshu" praises Tang Yao for marrying his daughters to Yu Shun, and "Spring and Autumn" criticizes Duke Yin of Lu for his marriage. The relationship between husband and wife is a matter of human ethics, and marriage must be handled with caution. Only when music is harmonious can the four seasons be harmonious, and the changes of yin and yang create all things. People can carry forward the great road but cannot control destiny. The affection between spouses is paramount, and even a king or father cannot force his children to surrender. The love between husband and wife is joyful, but some cannot have children or have children who die young—this is destiny. Confucius rarely spoke of the Mandate of Heaven, probably because it was difficult to explain. Without understanding yin and yang, one cannot know life.

The Han Dynasty followed Qin titles: the emperor's mother was the empress dowager, his grandmother the grand empress dowager, his wife the queen, and concubines were called madam. Titles like Beauty, Beloved, Eighth Son, Seventh Son, Long Envoy, and Young Envoy existed. Under Emperor Wu, Jieyu, Xi'e, Suhua, and Chongyi were established, with fourteen levels under Emperor Yuan. Zhaoyi's salary rank equaled the prime minister, and her title equaled princes and kings. Jie Jie equaled a high official, nephew'e equaled two thousand stones, Ronghua equaled two thousand stones, beauty equaled two thousand stones, Bazi equaled Ganshi, Chunyi equaled Qianshi, the seventh son equaled eight hundred stones, a good man equaled eight hundred stones, the chief envoy equaled six hundred stones, a minor envoy equaled four hundred stones, the five senses equaled three hundred stones, and it was usually two hundred stones. Wujuan, Gonghe, Yuling, Baolin, Liangshi, and Yezhe equaled a hundred stones. Sons of upper and middle families equaled fighting for food with rank. Those below the five sense organs were buried outside Simamen after death.

Empress Lu of Emperor Gaozu was the daughter of Lu Gong, who married her to Gaozu when he was poor, seeing him as extraordinary. She gave birth to Emperor Hui and Princess Lu Yuan. Gaozu became king of Han and made Lu Gong the Marquis of Linsi. He later married Qi Ji of Dingtao, who gave birth to King Ruyi of Zhao. Gaozu wanted to replace the kind-hearted prince with Ruyi, but ministers opposed it. Empress Lu, resolute and assisting Gaozu, had brothers Lu Ze and Lu Shizhi as generals. After Gaozu's death, Empress Dowager Lu imprisoned Qi Ji, shaved her hair, and made her pound rice. Qi Ji sang about her son being a king and she a slave, angering the Queen Mother, who summoned King Zhao to Chang'an. Emperor Hui protected him, but the Queen Mother poisoned King Zhao. She then mutilated Qi Ji, calling her a "human pig." Emperor Hui, horrified, fell ill and died seven years later. The Queen Mother, advised by Zhang Piqiang, appointed Lu Tai and Lu Chan as generals, consolidating Lu family power. She killed several of Gaozu's sons and named Lu family members kings. After her death, ministers Zhou Bo and Chen Ping executed the Lu family and established Dai Wang as Emperor Xiaowen.

Empress Zhang of Emperor Xiaohui was the daughter of Zhang Ao and Princess Lu Yuan. Empress Dowager Lu married her to Emperor Hui, hoping for a son. When she had none, the Queen Mother pretended she was pregnant and passed off a beauty's son as hers, killing the mother. After Emperor Hui's death, the prince became emperor but learned the truth and threatened revenge. The Queen Mother imprisoned and deposed him, then made Hengshan King Liu Hong emperor. After her death, ministers killed the Lu family and the non-biological sons of Emperor Hui, leaving only Queen Xiaohui, who died in the first year of Emperor Xiaowen's reign.

Gaozu's Bo Ji was the mother of Emperor Wen. Her father had an affair with Wei Lao, giving birth to Bo Ji. During the Qin Dynasty, Wei Bao proclaimed himself king, and Bo Ji entered his palace. Xu Fu predicted she would give birth to an emperor. Wei Bao betrayed Han, was captured, and Bo Ji was sent to the weaving room. Gaozu saw her and included her in the harem but did not favor her for over a year. She was close to Mrs. Guan and Zhao Zier, who later laughed at her alliance. Gaozu pitied her, summoned her, and she became pregnant, giving birth to Emperor Wen. After Gaozu's death, Bo Ji, rarely favored, followed her son to Dai and became Queen Mother Dai. After Empress Dowager Lu's death, ministers, hating the Lu family, praised Bo Ji's kindness and made King Dai emperor. Bo Ji became Empress Dowager, and her brother Bo Zhao was named Zhihou. She died in the second year of Emperor Jing's reign and was buried in Nanling.

Empress Xiaowen Dou was the mother of Emperor Jing. She was elected to the palace during Empress Dowager Lu's reign. When maids were sent to princes, she wanted to go to Zhao but was mistakenly sent to Dai. In Dai, King Dai favored her, and she gave birth to Emperor Jing. After King Dai became emperor, Dou Ji was made queen. Her brother Dou Guang, sold as a child, was later found and rewarded. Dou Changjun and Dou Shaojun became humble gentlemen. Queen Dou later became blind. After Emperor Wen's death, she became Empress Dowager and granted Dou Guang the title of Marquis Zhangwu. During the rebellion of the seven states, Dou Ying was made a general and later Marquis Weiqi. Empress Dowager Dou liked Huang-Lao teachings and died in the sixth year of Yuanguang, buried with Emperor Wen.

Emperor Xiaojing and Empress Bo were the daughters of Emperor Xiaowen and Empress Dowager Bo. Empress Dowager Bo married her to Emperor Jing as crown prince. After becoming emperor, he made her queen, but she had no sons and was not favored. After Empress Dowager Bo's death, she was deposed and died four years later.

Empress Xiaojing Wang was the mother of Emperor Wu. Her father Wang Zhong died, and her mother Zang'er remarried. Zang'er, divining that her daughters would be noble, sent her eldest daughter to the crown prince's womb. Mrs. Wang dreamed of a sun entering her belly, giving birth to a son. After Emperor Jing became emperor, Mrs. Wang's son became prince. The eldest princess Liu Piao wanted to marry her daughter to the prince, but Li Ji refused. The princess then spoke ill of Li Ji and praised Mrs. Wang's son. Emperor Jing deposed the prince and made Mrs. Wang queen. Her son became Emperor Wu. Empress Dowager Wang reigned for twenty-five years and died in the third year of Yuanshuo, buried with Emperor Jing.

Empress Chen of Emperor Xiaowu was the daughter of the eldest princess. She was favored but had no children for over ten years. When Wei Zifu was favored, Empress Chen used sorcery, leading to her deposition and retreat to Nagato Palace. She died and was buried in Baling.

Empress Xiaowu Wei, also known as Zifu, was a singer of Princess Pingyang. Emperor Wu favored her, and she gave birth to a son, Liu Shi, who was made prince. Wei Qing and Huo Qubing became generals. Later, Empress Wei's appearance deteriorated, and she faced a witchcraft disaster. The prince killed Jiang Chong and raised an army but was defeated. Empress Wei committed suicide. After Emperor Xuan ascended the throne, she was reburied with the posthumous title of Empress Mi.

Mrs. Li of Emperor Xiaowu was a singer. Her brother Li Yannian sang about a beauty, and Emperor Wu summoned her. She gave birth to King Ai of Changyi but died young. Emperor Wu mourned her and later gave her the title of Queen Xiaowu. Her brothers Li Guangli and Li Yannian were rewarded, but the Li family was later exterminated.

Gouyi of Emperor Xiaowu was the mother of Emperor Zhao. Emperor Wu summoned her, and she gave birth to Emperor Zhao after fourteen months. After Crown Prince Wei's defeat, Gou Yizi was made prince. Emperor Wu appointed Huo Guang to assist him. After Emperor Zhao ascended the throne, Gouyi was honored as empress dowager, and her father was named Marquis of Shuncheng.

Emperor Xiaozhao and Queen Shangguan were the granddaughter of Huo Guang. She became queen at six years old. Shangguan Jie and Shangguan An plotted to kill Huo Guang but were exposed and exterminated. The queen, young and uninvolved, was not deposed. After Emperor Zhao's death, she became empress dowager and died at fifty-two, buried with Emperor Zhao.

Shi Liangdi of Crown Prince Wei was the grandmother of Emperor Xuan. She gave birth to Liu Jin, who was killed in a witchcraft disaster. Emperor Xuan, as a baby, was saved and raised by Shi Gong. After becoming emperor, he honored Shi Liangdi as Mourning Queen.

Mrs. Wang Sun, Emperor Xuan's mother, was named Wengxu. She gave birth to Emperor Xuan but was killed in the disaster. Emperor Xuan honored her as Mourning Queen and found his grandmother Wang Yu, who was named Lord Boping. The Wang family had two marquises.

Empress Xu of Emperor Xiaoxuan was the mother of Wuhu. Her father Xu Guanghan was a eunuch. Emperor Xuan, as a great-grandson, married Xu Pingjun, who gave birth to Emperor Yuan. After becoming emperor, Xu was made queen. Huo Guang's wife Huo Xian had her poisoned to make her daughter queen. Queen Xu died and was named Queen Gongai.

Empress Huo of Emperor Xiaoxuan was the daughter of Huo Guang. After Queen Xu's death, she became queen. Huo Xian tried to poison the prince but failed. The murder was exposed, and the Huo family was wiped out. Queen Huo was deposed and committed suicide.

Emperor Xiaoxuan and Queen Wang were from a family with a marquis title. She became queen after Queen Huo's deposition, rarely favored. After Emperor Xuan's death, she became empress dowager and died in her seventies, buried with Emperor Xuan.
