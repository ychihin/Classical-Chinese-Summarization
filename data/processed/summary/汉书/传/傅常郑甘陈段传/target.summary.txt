Fu Jiezi, from the North, became an official through military service. During the Yuanfeng period, he was sent as an envoy to Dayuan and condemned the kings of Loulan and Qiuci for killing Han envoys. After returning, he proposed to General Huo Guang to assassinate the King of Loulan to establish Han prestige. Huo Guang agreed, and Fu Jiezi used gold and silk as bait to lure the king. During a private meeting, two soldiers stabbed the king to death. Fu Jiezi declared the king's guilt and installed a new ruler. He was rewarded with the title of Marquis of Yiyang and 700 households. His title was later abolished due to his son's crime but restored under Wang Mang.

Chang Hui, from Taiyuan, was detained by the Xiongnu for over a decade while accompanying Su Wu. After Emperor Zhao's reign, he was sent to Wusun, where he coordinated a joint Han-Wusun attack on the Xiongnu, resulting in significant victories. He later executed a noble from Qiuci for killing a Han captain. Chang Hui succeeded Su Wu as a vassal state official and served under Emperors Xuan and Yuan, earning the posthumous title Zhuang Genyao.

Zheng Ji, from Kuaiji, served as a military officer and became an expert in foreign affairs. He established agricultural colonies in Quli and defeated the Che division, earning the title of Protector of the Western Regions. He was granted the title of Marquis of Anyuan with 1,000 households. His son died without an heir, ending the title, but it was later restored to his great-grandson.

Gan Yanshou, from Yuzhi, was a skilled horseman and archer. He served as an envoy to the Western Regions and, with Deputy Captain Chen Tang, killed Zhizhi Chanyu. He was named Marquis of Yicheng. His title passed to his great-grandson before ending under Wang Mang.

Chen Tang, from Shanyang, was a learned but poor man who sought office in Chang'an. He was recommended by Zhang Bo but imprisoned for failing to attend his father's funeral. Later, as Deputy Captain of the Western Regions, he and Gan Yanshou led a campaign against Zhizhi Chanyu, who had killed Han envoys. They mobilized over 40,000 troops, besieged Zhizhi's city, and killed the chanyu. Despite their success, they faced criticism for acting without orders. After a lengthy debate, Emperor Yuan granted them titles and rewards, though Chen Tang was later dismissed for misconduct. He was eventually exiled to Dunhuang but recalled before his death in Chang'an.

Duan Huizong, from Tianshui, served as Protector of the Western Regions and was respected for his integrity. He killed a rebellious Wusun prince, Fanqiu, with a small force, earning the title of Marquis of Guannei. He died in Wusun at age 75, mourned by all nations.

The text concludes that from Zhang Qian's time to Wang Mang's era, 18 protectors served, with notable figures like Zheng Ji, Gan Yanshou, and Chen Tang. Chen Tang's unruliness led to his downfall, but his deeds are recorded for posterity.
