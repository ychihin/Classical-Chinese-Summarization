# Summary of Historical Records: Prime Ministers of Emperor Wu and Emperor Zhao of Han

Gongsun He, a native of Beidi Commandery, rose from a knight-errant background through military merit. He became favored due to his marriage to Empress Wei's sister and served as a general under Emperor Wu, earning the title of Marquis of Nanao for attacking the Xiongnu. After losing his marquisate for presenting inferior gold, he eventually became Chancellor and was titled Marquis of Ge Yi. He reluctantly accepted the position, weeping and predicting his own danger. His son Gongsun Jingsheng, arrogant and corrupt, embezzled military funds. To atone for his son, Gongsun He captured the knight Zhu Anshi, who retaliated by reporting Gongsun Jingsheng's affair with a princess and accusations of witchcraft against the emperor. Both father and son died in prison, and their clan was exterminated. This incident sparked the witchcraft disaster that ultimately led to the deaths of the princess, empress, and crown prince.

Liu Quli, son of Prince Jing of Zhongshan, replaced Gongsun He as Left Chancellor. Emperor Wu issued an edict condemning Gongsun He's corruption and appointed Liu Quli as Marquis of Peng. During the witchcraft rebellion, Crown Prince Ju killed the accuser Jiang Chong and raised troops. Liu Quli led imperial forces against the prince, resulting in a five-day battle with tens of thousands dead. The prince fled but was captured twenty days later. The following year, Liu Quli conspired with General Li Guangli to make the Prince of Changyi crown prince. This conspiracy was discovered, and Liu Quli was executed by waist-cutting, while Li Guangli surrendered to the Xiongnu.

Che Qianqiu, originally named Tian, served as guardian of Emperor Gaozu's tomb. After the crown prince's defeat, Che submitted a memorial defending the prince, arguing that a son who mobilizes troops against his father should only be whipped. Emperor Wu, already regretting his actions, was moved and appointed Che as Grand Herald, then Chancellor within months, creating the unprecedented precedent of becoming Chancellor through a single memorial. Though lacking scholarly talents, Che proved competent and kind-hearted. After Emperor Wu's death, Che served under Emperor Zhao alongside General Huo Guang, deferring to Huo's authority while maintaining loyalty. He served as Chancellor for twelve years and was posthumously titled Marquis Ding.

Wang Xin of Jinan Commandery rose from minor official to magistrate. When Imperial Censor Bao Shengzhi investigated and prepared to execute him, Wang Xin pleaded for mercy, arguing that killing him would not increase Shengzhi's prestige. Impressed, Shengzhi spared him and later recommended him. Wang Xin served Emperor Wu competently, becoming You Fufeng. Under Emperor Zhao, he succeeded Che Qianqiu as Chancellor and was titled Marquis of Yichun, dying the following year.

Yang Chang of Huayin Commandery served in General Huo Guang's staff, rising to Minister of Agriculture. Learning of a rebellion conspiracy, Yang Chang, being cautious, feigned illness rather than reporting it. Another official reported the matter and received a marquisate, while Yang Chang was denied this honor. He later became Imperial Censor and succeeded Wang Xin as Chancellor, titled Marquis of Anping. After Emperor Zhao's death, when Huo Guang planned to depose the immoral King of Changyi, Yang Chang initially hesitated but was persuaded by his wife to cooperate. He died shortly after Emperor Xuan's accession.
