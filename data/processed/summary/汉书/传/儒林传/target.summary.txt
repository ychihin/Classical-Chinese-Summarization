The text describes the history and transmission of Confucian classics from ancient times through the Han dynasty. Ancient Confucians studied the "Six Arts," a classic of the Wang Sect used by sages to clarify the Tao, correct human relations, and achieve governance. During the decline of Zhou Dao, Confucius emerged after more than two hundred years. Due to his saintly virtue but encountering difficult times, he sighed about the phoenix not coming and the river not flowing out the map. He traveled among princes, entering Zhou in the west and Chu in the south, facing hardships like being frightened in Kuang and short of food in Chen. He revised music after returning from Wei to Lu, ensuring "Ya" and "Song" found their places. He explored ancient and modern books, praising Yao and Zhou, and reviewed the "Books," "Shao Wu," and "Zhou Nan." He connected Zhou rites with Lu's "Spring and Autumn," listing actions of twelve princes using civil and military methods as standards. In his later years, he liked "Yi," reading it so much that the bamboo slip bindings broke several times, and he wrote a biography for it. After Confucius's death, his seventy disciples scattered among princes, with some becoming masters and others mentors. During the Warring States period, Confucianism was denounced, but scholars in Qilu did not abandon the great way. Mencius and Sun Qing followed Confucius's cause and became famous. When Qin Shihuang burned "Poems" and "Books" and killed magicians, the Six Studies became incomplete. When Chen She became king, Confucian scholars from Lu took Kong's ritual vessels to him, and Kong Jia became his doctor, dying with him. Emperor Gao besieged Lu, but Confucian scholars there still recited rituals and sang. Then Confucian scholars began studying classics and teaching rituals. Shusun Tong created Han rituals, and scholarship flourished. During Xiaohui and Gaohou, ministers were military heroes. Xiaowen liked punishment and naming studies. Xiaojing did not employ Confucian scholars, and Empress Dowager Dou liked Huang Lao's skills. When the Han dynasty arose, various scholars taught different classics: Tiansheng taught "Yi," Fusheng taught "Book," Shen Peigong taught "Poetry" in Lu, Yuan Gusheng in Qi, and Taifu Han in Yan. Lu Gaotang taught "Li," and Hu Wusheng and Dong Zhongshu taught "Spring and Autumn." After Empress Dowager Bao died, Wu'an Hou Tian Ju became prime minister, rejecting Huang Lao and Xing Ming, and employing literary Confucian scholars. Gongsun Hong became prime minister due to his study of "Spring and Autumn," and scholars followed. Gongsun Hong, an academic official, wrote about guiding people with etiquette and educating with music. He proposed establishing education in the countryside, with schools in Xia, Yin, and Zhou. He suggested appointing fifty disciples for doctorate officers, exempting them from corvee. County magistrates should choose those who love literature and classics, respect superiors, and obey rules. After a year, those specializing in one subject and learning more arts could become doctors. Those not proficient would be exempted. The edict was issued, and from then on, many officials were virtuous and literary. During Emperor Zhao's reign, virtuous literature was recommended, and doctoral disciples increased. At the end of Xuanlong's reign, the number doubled. Gencha liked Confucianism, and those mastering classics were exempted from corvee. Later, due to insufficient funds, the number was changed to 1,000, and counties established "Hundred Stones" History of the Five Classics. In Emperor Cheng's last years, some said Confucius raised 3,000 disciples, so the emperor increased disciples to 3,000, but the old system was restored after a year. When Wang Mang was in power during Emperor Ping's reign, children of Yuanshi could receive careers as disciples without quota. Forty in the first class served as doctors, twenty as prince's family, and forty made up for literary stories. The text then details the transmission of "Yi" from Confucius through various scholars, including Tian He, Ding Kuan, and others, leading to schools like Shi, Meng, and Liangqiu. It describes how "Yi" was the only book not banned during Qin, so it was continuously passed on. After the Han rise, Tian He moved to Duling and passed on the teachings. Ding Kuan, a native of Liang, studied with Dan Bao and later taught Wang Se, leading to the Shi, Mencius, and Liangqiu schools. Shi Yu, a native of Pei, studied with Tian Wangsun and became a doctor, teaching Zhang Yu and Lu Bo. Meng Xi, from Lanling, studied with Tian Wangsun and claimed to have received the Book of Yin and Yang Catastrophes, but Liangqiu He argued against his falsehood. Jingfang studied from Jiao Yanshou, but his theory was different from others. Fei Zhi studied "Yi" and used ten classical texts to explain it. Gao Xiang studied with Fei Gong and focused on yin and yang disasters. The text also covers the transmission of "Book" from Fu Sheng, who taught Zhang Sheng and Ouyang Sheng. Ouyang Sheng's learning passed through Ni Kuan to Ouyang Gao and others. Xiahou Sheng and Xiahou Jian developed the great and small Xiahou schools. Zhou Kan taught Mou Qing and Xu Shang, leading to the Kong and Xu schools. Zhang Shan taught Li Xun, Zheng Kuanzhong, and others, forming the Zheng, Zhang, Qin, Jiu, and Li schools. Kong Anguo had an ancient text of "Book," but it was not established as an academic official. The text describes the transmission of "Poetry" from Shen Gong, who taught Wang Zang and Zhao Wan. Yuan Gu argued with Huang Sheng about Tang and Wu's mandate. Han Ying wrote "Nei Zhuan" and "Wai Zhuan" for "Poetry." Mao Gong taught "Poetry" to Guandan Qing and others. For "Li," Lu Gaotangsheng taught seventeen chapters, and Xu Sheng taught his grandsons. Meng Qing taught Houcang, who passed on to Dai De and Dai Sheng, forming the Da Dai, Xiao Dai, and Qing schools. Hu's mother taught "Gongyang Chunqiu," and Dong Zhongshu also taught it. Yan Pengzu and Yan Anle developed the Yan and Yan schools. The text describes the rise of "Guliang Chunqiu" during Emperor Xuan's reign, with Rong Guang and others debating with the Gongyang school. Finally, it mentions the transmission of "Zuo Shi Chunqiu" by Zhang Cang, Jia Yi, and others, with Liu Xin advocating for its establishment. The praise notes that from Emperor Wu's establishment of the Doctorate of the Five Classics, scholarship increased, with branches multiplying. Initially, only Ouyang, Hou, Yang, and Gongyang were established. During Xiaoxuan, Daxiahou Shangshu, Daxiao Daili, Shi, Mencius, Liangqiu Yi, and Guliang Chunqiu were added. In Emperor Yuan's reign, Jingshi Yi was established. During Emperor Ping's reign, Zuo's Spring and Autumn Annals, Mao's Poems, Yi's Rites, and the Ancient Chinese Book of Documents were also established to collect lost texts.
