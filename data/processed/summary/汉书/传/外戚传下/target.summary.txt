# Summary of Historical Records on Imperial Consorts of the Han Dynasty

The Wang family, relatives of Emperor Xiaoyuan's queen, produced ten princes and five chief ministers, making them the most prosperous among all imperial relatives. Empress Xu of Emperor Xiaocheng was the daughter of General Xu Jia. Emperor Yuan selected her for the crown prince after mourning his mother's death. She initially brought joy to the court, but her children died in infancy. After Emperor Cheng ascended, he appointed her empress and appointed his uncle Wang Feng alongside Xu Jia as co-regents. Du Qin advised Wang Feng to respect Xu Jia's superior status according to tradition. Eventually, the emperor appointed only Wang Feng and granted Xu Jia a special retirement with gold. Xu Jia died over a year later.

Empress Xu was intelligent and skilled in calligraphy, enjoying the emperor's favor for many years while other concubines were rarely summoned. When disasters occurred repeatedly, officials Liu Xiang and Gu Yong blamed the harem, leading to reduced expenses for the queen's palace. The empress petitioned the emperor, arguing that her rewards to her family had never exceeded proper limits, that changing times required adapting systems, and that officials were using vague edicts to restrict her unfairly. She requested clarification on what property she could obtain and asked to maintain traditional practices for sacrifices and family rewards. Emperor Cheng responded by citing numerous natural disasters and celestial omens—solar eclipses, white vapors, meteors, floods, earthquakes, and fires—as evidence that excessive yin energy from the harem was causing the royal family's lack of heirs. He urged the empress to practice frugality and virtue.

As Wang Feng dominated the government, three years of famine led critics to blame him, but Gu Yong continued faulting Empress Xu. The emperor's favor diminished, and the empress's sister Xu Ye was discovered cursing pregnant concubines and Wang Feng. The empress dowager had Xu Ye executed, and Empress Xu was deposed after fourteen years. Nine years later, Emperor Cheng showed mercy to the Xu family, allowing them to return to the capital. The deposed empress died after her sister's affair with Chunyu Chang led to a scheme to reinstate her. Chunyu Chang made inappropriate remarks in letters, and when reported, the emperor sent poison for her to commit suicide.

Ban Jiantun entered the harem when Emperor Cheng ascended and soon gained favor. When the emperor wanted her to share his chariot, she refused, citing ancient examples of wise monarchs accompanying ministers rather than favored women. The empress dowager praised her comparison to Fan Ji of ancient times. Ban Jiantun studied classical texts and always followed ancient etiquette. As the emperor's favor shifted to other concubines, she presented her maid Li Ping to the emperor, who became favored and was given the surname Wei. Later, the Zhao sisters rose to even greater prominence. When Zhao Feiyan falsely accused Empress Xu and Ban Jiantun of cursing the harem, Ban Jiantun defended herself by stating that if ghosts and gods were aware, they would not accept improper prayers, and if ignorant, prayers were useless. The emperor pitied her and rewarded her. Fearing the Zhao sisters' jealousy, she requested to serve the empress dowager in Changxin Palace, where she wrote poems lamenting her fate and describing her lonely life in the palace. After Emperor Cheng's death, she guarded his cemetery until her death.

Empress Zhao, originally a palace official in Chang'an, was abandoned at birth but survived. She learned singing and dancing at Princess Yang'a's house and was called Feiyan. Emperor Cheng visited incognito, became enamored with her, and brought her to the palace. Her sister also entered the palace, and both received unprecedented favor. After Empress Xu was deposed, the emperor wanted to make Zhao empress, but the empress dowager objected due to her low birth. Through Chunyu Chang's mediation, the emperor made Zhao's father a marquis, and Zhao became empress. Her sister became Zhaoyi and lived in unprecedented luxury. After more than a decade without children, they were bribed by King Dingtao's grandmother to support him as crown prince. Emperor Cheng died suddenly while in good health, and Zhao Zhaoyi was blamed. An investigation revealed that two other concubines had borne sons who were killed by Zhao Zhaoyi's orders. After Emperor Ai's death, Wang Mang reported to the empress dowager, who demoted and then deposed Empress Zhao, who committed suicide after sixteen years as empress.
