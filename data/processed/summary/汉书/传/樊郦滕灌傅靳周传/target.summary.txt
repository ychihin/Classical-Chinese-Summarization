Fan Kuai, a native of Peixian County, was a dog butcher by trade. He hid with Emperor Gaozu in the Mangshan and Cishan areas to avoid official duties. During Chen Sheng's uprising, Xiao He and Cao Shen sent Fan Kuai to retrieve Gaozu, who became Peigong. Fan Kuai served as a Sheren, attacking Huling, Fangyu, and Fengyi, defeating the Sishui County Supervisor. He pacified Peixian County and defeated the Sishui County Governor at Xuexian. He fought Sima Chao in Ci County, beheading fifteen enemies, earning the title of doctor. Fan Kuai frequently followed Peigong, leading assaults on Puyang, Chengyang, and Hufu, beheading many enemies and receiving promotions. He fought at Chengwu, Boyi, Gangli, and Kaifeng, beheading a commander and sixty-eight enemies, earning the title of minister. He defeated Yang Xiong at Quyu, ascended Wanling first, and was named Xianchengjun. He fought at Changshe, Nianyuan, and the Yellow River ferry, defeating Qin forces at Xixiang and Nianyi. He defeated Nanyang's Shouling at Yangcheng, ascended Wancheng first, and repelled enemies at Li County. At Wuguan and Bashang, he beheaded a captain and ten enemies, capturing 146 and surrendering 2,900 soldiers.

At the Hongmen Banquet, Xiang Yu prepared to attack Peigong. Peigong apologized to Xiang Yu via Xiang Bo. During the feast, Yafu ordered Xiang Zhuang to assassinate Peigong with a sword dance, but Xiang Bo protected him. Fan Kuai, hearing the urgency, entered the camp with sword and shield, confronting Xiang Yu. He drank wine and ate pork leg, condemning Xiang Yu for listening to slander and estranging Peigong. Xiang Yu remained silent. Peigong left, and Fan Kuai escorted him back to Bashang, leaving Zhang Liang to apologize. Xiang Yu abandoned his plan to kill Peigong. Later, Xiang Yu entered Xianyang, burning and killing, and made Peigong King of Han. Fan Kuai was made Marquis of Linwu, then doctor, and followed the king to Hanzhong. He pacified the Three Qin Dynasties, attacking Xixian, Yong County, Yongxian, and Tengcheng, ascending cities first. He attacked Zhang Ping at Haozhi, beheading a magistrate and a deputy, and was promoted to Cavalry General. He defeated Qin chariot and cavalry forces at Shumian and Rangxiang, becoming a general. He attacked Zhao Bi, occupying Jun, Huaili, Liuzhong, and Xianyang, diverting water to irrigate waste hills. The King of Han granted him Fan Township in Du County as a food town.

Fan Kuai followed the king to attack Xiang Yu, slaughtering Zaoyi and defeating Wang Wu and Cheng Chu at Waihuang. He attacked Zouxian, Luxian, Xiaqiu, and Xuexian. After Xiang Yu defeated the king at Pengcheng, Fan Kuai returned to Rongyang, receiving 2,000 households in Pingyin and garrisoning Guangwu Mountain for a year. He surrounded Xiang Yu at Chen County, defeating him and bloodbathing Hu Ling. After Xiang Yu's death, the king became emperor, granting Fan Kuai 800 households. He followed Gaozu to suppress Zang Tu's rebellion, capturing Zang Tu and pacifying Yan. He arrested Han Xin at Chen County, pacifying Chu. Gaozu granted Fan Kuai the title of Marquis of Wuyang, exempting previous titles. He followed Gaozu to attack Chen Xi in Daidi, pacifying Dai land and increasing households by 1,500. He attacked Chen Xi and Manqiuchen, capturing Bairen County, ascending cities first, and pacifying Qinghe and Changshan counties (27 total), destroying Shuyuan County, and becoming Left Prime Minister. He defeated Qi Muyin and Yin Pan at Wuzhong and Guangchang, and Chen Xi's general Wang Huang at Daidi, attacking Han Xin at Shenhe. His men killed Han Xin, defeated Xiongnu cavalry at Henggu, and captured General Zhao Ji, Prime Minister Feng Liang, and others. He pacified 72 counties in Dai. He attacked Lu Wan, pacifying 18 counties and 51 townships in Yan. Gaozu added 1,300 households, totaling 5,400 in Wuyang. Fan Kuai beheaded 176 enemies, captured 287, defeated seven armies, occupied five cities, and pacified six commanderies and 52 counties. He married Empress Lu's sister, Lu Xu, and had a son, Fan Kang. He was closest to the emperor.

During Yingbu's rebellion, Gaozu was ill and refused to see anyone. Fan Kuai opened the palace door, weeping and rebuking Gaozu for isolating himself like Zhao Gao. Gaozu smiled and rose. Later, Gaozu sent Fan Kuai to attack Lu Wan. Slandered for colluding with the Lu family, Gaozu ordered Chen Ping to behead him, but Chen Ping arrested him instead. Gaozu died, and Empress Lu released Fan Kuai, restoring his title. Fan Kuai died in the sixth year of Xiaohui, posthumously named Marquis Wu. His son Fan Kang succeeded. Lu Xu was made Marquise of Linguang, but after Empress Lu's death, ministers killed her and Fan Kang. The title was suspended until Emperor Xiaowen granted it to Fan Kuai's concubine's son, Fan Shi. After Fan Shi's death, his son Fan Tuoguang inherited, but was later deposed for illegitimacy. In the second year of Yuanshi, Emperor Ping granted Fan Zhang, a descendant, the title of Marquis of Wuyang with 1,000 households.

Li Shang of Gaoyang County gathered youths during Chen Sheng's uprising. He surrendered 4,000 men to Peigong, attacking Changshe and ascending first, becoming Xinchengjun. He attacked Fengshi, blocked the Yellow River, defeated Qin at Luoyang, captured Wan and Xing, pacifying 17 counties. He assisted in taking Shanguan Pass and pacifying Hanzhong. Peigong became King of Han, making Li Shang Xinchengjun and Captain of Longxi. He pacified Beidi, defeating Zhang Han's deputies, and received 6,000 households in Wucheng. He attacked Xiang Ji, fighting Zhong Limei, and received the Prime Minister's Seal of Liang. He attacked Xiang Yu for two years, then Hu Ling. After the emperor's ascension, he attacked Zang Tu, defeating him at Longtuo and Yixia, becoming Right Prime Minister and receiving 5,000 households in Zhuojun. He attacked Shanggu and Dai, receiving Zhao Xiang's seal. He pacified Dai and Yanmen with Jianghou, capturing Dai's Prime Minister Cheng Zong and 19 others. He commanded the Imperial Guard for a year. In October, as Right Prime Minister, he attacked Chen Xi, destroying Shuyuan. He attacked Yingbu, breaking barriers and capturing two formations, becoming Marquis of Quzhou with 5,100 households. His military achievements included defeating three rebels, pacifying six counties and 73 counties, capturing one prime minister, one acting prime minister, one general, and 19 officials. He assisted Emperor Xiaohui and Empress Lu. After Empress Lu's death, he was ill. His son Li Ji, friendly with Lu Lu, was used by Zhou Bo to deceive Lu Lu, allowing Zhou Bo to enter the Northern Army and kill the Lu family. Li Shang died that year, posthumously named Jinghou. Li Ji inherited, but was criticized for betraying friends. During Emperor Xiaojing's reign, Li Ji besieged Tong City for seven months without success, but Lebu captured it. Li Ji was stripped of his title for wanting to marry Lord Pingyuan. Emperor Jing named Li Jian, another son, Marquis of Mu. The title passed to Xuansun Su, who was executed for witchcraft. During the Second Beginning, descendants were granted Marquis of Guannei.

Xiahou Ying of Peixian County managed horse breeding and driving. He often spoke with Gaozu at Sishang Pavilion. He served as a county official and became friends with Gaozu. Gaozu injured him while playing, but Xiahou Ying testified for Gaozu, enduring imprisonment and whipping. When Gaozu started his army, Xiahou Ying served as a minor official. Gaozu became Peigong, granting Xiahou Ying the title of seventh-level duke and making him a royal servant. He followed Gaozu to attack Huling, surrendering Ping, the Sishui Supervisor, and receiving the ninth rank. He attacked Qin at Yangdong, captured Jiyang and Hufu, defeated Li You at Yongqiu, and received titles and silk. He attacked Zhang Han at Shu'a and Puyang, using chariots to defeat the Qin army, becoming Zhigui. He attacked Trip Ben at Kaifeng and Yang Xiong at Quyu, capturing 68 and surrendering 850, receiving a cabinet of official seals. He pursued Qin at Luoyang, becoming magistrate of Teng County. He fought at Lantian and Zhiyang to Bashang. Peigong became King of Han, granting him the title of Marquis of Zhaoping and making him a royal servant. He followed the king to Shuhan and pacified the Three Qin Dynasties. At Pengcheng, Xiang Yu defeated the Han army. Xiahou Ying rescued Emperor Xiaohui and Princess Lu Yuan, carrying them despite Gaozu's attempts to abandon them. He sent them to Fengdi. At Rongyang, he received Yiyang as a food city. He attacked Xiang Ji at Xiayi, pursuing to Chen, defeating Chu. At Lucheng, he added Zishi County. After the emperor's ascension, he attacked Zang Tu and captured Han Xin. He was made Marquis of Ruyin with a talisman. He attacked Dai, going to Wuquan and Yunzhong, adding 1,000 households. He attacked Han Xin at Jinyang, defeating the enemy. Surrounded at Pingcheng for seven days, he advised slow retreat, escaping. He received Xiyang as a food town. He attacked Xiongnu cavalry at Juzhu and Pingcheng, receiving 500 households. He attacked Chen Xi and Yingbu, adding 1,000 households, totaling 6,900 in Ruyin. He served Gaozu until his death, then served Emperor Hui and Empress Lu. They thanked him for rescuing Xiaohui and Princess Lu Yuan, giving him a first-class residence. After Empress Lu's death, he supported Emperor Wen, remaining a royal servant. He died eight years later, posthumously named Wenhou. The title passed to Xiahou Po, who married Princess Pingyang but committed suicide, ending the feudal state. Xiahou Ying was originally named Teng Gong, and his descendants changed their surname to Sun.

Guan Ying of Suiyang County was a silk merchant. He served as a follower in Zhongjuan under Peigong. He defeated Qin's Shujun at Chengwu and Gangli, becoming Seventh Doctor. He attacked Qin at Bo, Kaifeng, and Quyu, becoming Xuanling Jun. He attacked west of Yangwu to Luoyang, defeating Qin north of Xi. He crossed the Yellow River, defeated the Nanyang governor, pacified Nanyang, entered Wuguan, fought at Lantian, and became Zhigui and Changwenjun. Peigong became King of Han, making him a doctor and then a lieutenant. He pacified the Three Qin Dynasties, captured Shuoyang, and surrendered to the King of Sai. He besieged Zhang Han at Feiqiu. He followed the king east, defeated King Yin, and pacified his territory. He attacked Xiang Yu's general Long Qie and Xiang Tuo at Dingtao, defeating them. The king granted him the title of Changwen Marquis with a seat in Pingxiang. He followed the king to conquer Ci and attack Pengcheng. After Xiang Yu's victory, he garrisoned Yongqiu, defeating Wang Wu and Wei Gongshen. He captured Wai and went to Rongyang. The king appointed him as a cavalry general with Li Bi and Luo Jia. He attacked Chu cavalry at Rongyang, cutting off their food routes. He attacked Xiang Guan at Lu, killing a You Sima and a cavalry general. He defeated Duke Zhe and Wang Wu at Yan, killing five generals. He defeated Dan Ying at the imperial palace. He led cavalry across the Yellow River, escorted the king, and met the prime minister's army at Yisu. He returned to Aocang, becoming imperial censor. In the third year, he received a food town in Pingxiang. As censor, he led cavalry east, defeating rebels at Lixia, capturing Hua Wushang and 46 officials. He captured Linzi and Qi Prime Minister Tian Guang. He pursued Tian Heng to Ying and Bo, defeating his cavalry. He captured Gu and Bo, killing Tian Su at Qiancheng. He followed Han Xin to attack Longdan at Baoshi, killing Longdan and capturing Zhou Lan. After Qi was pacified, Han Xin became king of Qi, sending Guan Ying to attack Chu's Gong Dai at Lu. He defeated the Xue County governor, capturing a cavalryman. He attacked Fu Yang and marched to Tong, Qili, and Xu. He crossed the silver carp, capturing cities to Guangling. He defeated Xideng and Langsheng at Gongpi, killing Zheng Gong. He defeated Chu cavalry at Wangdang, pacified Pengcheng, capturing Xiang Tuo and Liu, Xue, Pei, Deng, Lu, and Dao. He attacked Xuanse and Zhese, capturing a deputy general. He joined the king at Yixiang, defeating Xiang Lan at Chen. His men killed two generals and captured eight. The king added 2,500 households. He pursued Xiang Ji to Shucheng, defeating him. Five of his men killed Xiang Ji, becoming marquises. He captured 12,000 enemies and all generals. He captured Shucheng and Liyang. He crossed Dansi, defeated the Chengsu county magistrate, and pacified 52 counties north of Jin Hui. After the king's ascension, he added 3,000 households. As a chariot general, he attacked Zang Tu and captured Han Xin. He received a talisman and 2,500 households in Yingyin. He attacked King Xin of Han at Daidi, capturing six counties north of Loufan, beheading the left general. He defeated Xiongnu cavalry at Wuquan. He attacked Han Zhi and Bao Chu at Jinyang, killing a general. He led troops from Yan, Zhao, Qi, Liang, and Chu to defeat Xiongnu at Bishi. Surrounded at Pingcheng, he followed the emperor to attack Chen Xi, killing Hou Chang and five generals. He attacked Quni, Lunu, Shangquyang, Anguo, and Anping. He captured Dongdanse. When Yingbu rebelled, he attacked first, defeating Yingbu's generals at Xiangdi, beheading three deputy generals. He destroyed Yingbu's Shangzhu State and Da Sima. He defeated Fei Zhu. He captured Zuo Sima, and his men killed ten young generals, pursuing to the Huaihe River. The emperor added 2,500 households. After Yingbu's defeat, the emperor granted him 5,000 households in Yingyin. He captured two generals with 2,000 stone salaries, destroyed the enemy 16 times, occupied 46 cities, and pacified one traitor, two counties, and 52 counties. He captured two generals, one Zhuguo, and one Xiang. He served Emperor Hui and Empress Lu. After Empress Lu's death, Lu Lu and others rebelled. Guan Ying was sent to attack the Qi army but stationed at Rongyang, persuading the King of Qi to focus on the Lu family. After the Lu family was punished, he supported Emperor Wen, receiving 3,000 households and 1,000 catties of gold, becoming Taiwei. In the third year of Emperor Wen, he became Prime Minister. He led 85,000 cavalry against the Xiongnu, who retreated. He died as prime minister. The title passed to his grandson Bi Qiang, who lost it for crimes. During Emperor Wu's reign, his grandson Yi Zi was made Marquis of Linru but later deposed.

Fu Kuan, a cavalry officer of Wei, followed Peigong, raising troops in Hengyang. He captured Anyang and Gangli, defeated Zhao Ben at Kaifeng, and attacked Yang Xiong at Quyu and Yangwu, killing 12 enemies, becoming a duke. Peigong became King of Han, making him Gongdejun. He entered Guanzhong as a right-hand cavalry general. After pacifying the Three Qin Dynasties, he received Diaoyin as a food town. He attacked Xiang Ji, following the king at Huai County, becoming Marquis of Tongde. He attacked Xiang Guan, Zhou Lan, and Long Qie, killing a cavalry general. Under Han Xin, he defeated Qi Zaili and attacked Tian Jie. Under Cao Shen, he destroyed Bo County. For pacifying Qi, he received a talisman and 2,600 households in Yangling, becoming Marquis of Yangling. He became Right Prime Minister of Qi, then Prime Minister. In April, he attacked Chen Xi under Zhou Bo. In January, he became Xiangguo of Dai, commanding the garrison. Two years later, he became Prime Minister of Dai. He died in the fifth year of Xiaohui, posthumously named Jinghou. The title passed to his great-grandson Fu Yan, who was executed for treason.

Zhou Jie of Pei followed Gaozu as Zhongjuan, raising troops in Wanqu. He attacked Jiyang, defeated Li You, and attacked Qin at Shumian and Kaifeng, beheading a thousand-general and 57 enemies, capturing 73, becoming Lord Linping. He fought at Lantian, killing two chariot commanders, one cavalry commander, and 28 enemies, capturing 57. At Bashang, Peigong became King of Han, making him Marquis of Jianwu and Captain of Cavalry. He pacified the Three Qin Dynasties, attacking Zhang Ping at Longxi, occupying six counties, killing four chariot commanders and four horsemen. He attacked Chu eastward to Pengcheng. After defeat, he held Yongqiu, defeating Wang Wu. He captured Liang, attacking Xing Shuo at Jun County, capturing two captains, 12 Sima, and surrendering 4,680. He defeated Chu at Rongyang, receiving 4,200 households. He led an army to Hanoi, defeating Chu Bi at Chaoge, capturing two cavalry generals and 250 chariots. He attacked Anyang to Jipu, occupying ten counties. He defeated Tong's army, capturing two generals and four Sima, surrendering 2,400. He captured Handan, leading an army to capture Pingyang, beheading a governor and pacifying Ye County. He attacked Chaoge and Handan, defeating Chang's army, capturing six counties in Handan. He returned to Aocang, defeating Xiang Ji south of Chenggao, cutting off Chu's food routes. He defeated a champion in Ludi, occupying Gao, Tan, Xiapi, Qixian, and Zhuyi. He attacked Xiang Han at Jiyang. He returned to Chen, defeating Xiang Ji's army, pacifying Jiangling.
