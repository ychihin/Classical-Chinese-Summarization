Feng Fengshi, courtesy name Ziming, was a native of Lu County in Shangdang, later moving to Duling. His ancestor Feng Ting, governor of Shangdang under South Korea, surrendered the city to Zhao during Qin's invasion and died at Changping. The Feng family dispersed; some became officials in Zhao, and after Qin's unification, descendants like Feng Wuze served as Qin generals. Under Han, Feng Tang gained fame under Emperor Wen. Feng Fengshi was elected as a Langguan under Emperor Wu, later serving as a military commander under General Han Zeng, accompanying campaigns against the Xiongnu.

During Emperor Xuan's reign, Feng Fengshi was sent as an envoy to the Western Regions. At Yixiu City, he learned that the Shache Kingdom had killed Han-appointed King Wannian and envoy Xi Chongguo, while the Xiongnu attacked Cheshi. Shache threatened the southern kingdoms, cutting off traffic from Shanshan westward. Protector Zheng Ji and Colonel Sima Yi were trapped. Feng Fengshi, with deputy Yan Lu, mobilized 15,000 troops from northern and southern kingdoms, attacked Shache, and captured its city. King Shache committed suicide, and his head was sent to Chang'an. Feng Fengshi's reputation spread across the Western Regions. After returning, Emperor Xuan praised Han Zeng for his recommendation. Feng Fengshi later obtained a famous horse from Dayuan. The court debated rewards: the prime minister and generals favored a title, but Shaofu Xiao Wangzhi argued against it, citing the precedent of unauthorized military action. The emperor agreed, granting Feng Fengshi the titles of Doctor Guanglu and Captain Shuiheng.

Under Emperor Yuan, Feng Fengshi became Jinwu and later Right General of the Dian Kingdom. In the second year of Yongguang, the Qiang rebelled in Longxi. Amid famine, Feng Fengshi volunteered to lead an army, proposing 40,000 troops for a swift campaign. The prime minister and others advocated for only 10,000, but Feng Fengshi warned that insufficient forces would embolden the Qiang and other tribes. He was granted 12,000 troops initially, later reinforced to 60,000 under General Fenwu Ren Qianqiu. After initial defeats, the combined forces defeated the Qiang in November, beheading thousands and driving the rest across the border. Feng Fengshi was appointed General Zuo and Marquis of Guannei with 500 households and gold. He died of illness over a year later, having served as a military general for ten years, with a reputation second only to Tong Chongguo.

After Feng Fengshi's death, Gan Yanshou was made a marquis for killing the Zhizhi Chanyu. Du Qin later petitioned, arguing that Feng Fengshi's similar merits had been overlooked due to Xiao Wangzhi's precedent, creating inconsistency in rewards. The emperor declined to revisit the matter.

Feng Fengshi had nine sons and four daughters. His eldest daughter Feng Yuan became Emperor Yuan's Zhaoyi and mother of Prince Xiao of Zhongshan. His sons—Feng Tan, Feng Yewang, Feng Kui, Feng Li, and Feng Shen—all held high offices. Feng Yewang, known for his character, was recommended for high posts but was passed over due to his sister's imperial favor, leading him to lament that their family was humbled by her status. He later served as prefect of several commanderies but was dismissed after being implicated in a political dispute. Feng Kui was known for his governance and died in his forties. Feng Li served as prefect of five commanderies, praised for his fairness. Feng Shen, cautious and dignified, was made Marquis of Yixiang but committed suicide under Emperor Ai after being framed for treason, along with seventeen family members. The Feng family then returned to their hometown. The historical commentary laments their tragic fate, comparing it to ancient figures like Boqi and Qu Yuan.
