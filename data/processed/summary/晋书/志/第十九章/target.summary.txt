This condition is described as fire overcoming water.

The disaster is dark weather, and the punishment is continuous wind, which harms all things and leads to early death. Associated portents include the demon of fat night, flower evils, cow troubles, strange illnesses among close friends, and yellow disasters. This condition is described as metal, wood, water, and fire overcoming earth.

The punishment is a long period of cloudy weather, and in the extreme, the country becomes weak. The horse, associated with the king in the "Yi," suffers when the monarch's spirit is damaged. The text states that when the ruler's way is damaged, the sun and moon move out of order and stars go retrograde.
