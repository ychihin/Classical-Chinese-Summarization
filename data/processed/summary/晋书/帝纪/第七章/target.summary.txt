# Summary of Emperor Cheng and Emperor Kang's Reigns

Emperor Cheng, named Sima Yan (courtesy name Shigen), was the eldest son of Emperor Ming. He was established as crown prince in the third year of Taining and became emperor following his father's death. He honored Queen Yu as Empress Dowager, who acted as regent. Situ Wang directed ministers' affairs alongside Zhongshu Ling Yu Liang. During his reign, the empire faced numerous challenges including solar eclipses, floods, droughts, famines, and rebellions.

The Xianhe era began with a general amnesty and tax exemptions. Shi Le's forces repeatedly invaded, with generals like Shi Sheng attacking Runan. Internal rebellions erupted, including Liu Xia's generals supporting his son Liu Zhao to resist Guo Mo. Various military officials died or were appointed to new positions. The period saw severe droughts, earthquakes, and the execution of Sima Zong, King of Nanton, for guilt. Liu Yao's generals invaded Zanli territory, while Shi Le's general Shi Cong attacked Shouyang unsuccessfully.

The rebellion of Zu Yue and Su Jun in the second year of Xianhe proved devastating. Su Jun's forces massacred Yuhu, and multiple kings defected to him. The capital was placed under martial law. Yu Liang and others fought Su Jun at Cihu, suffering defeat. The following year, Su Jun crossed the Hengjiang River, attacked the capital, and burned Qingxizha. Many officials were killed, and Yu Liang fled to Xunyang. Su Jun forced the emperor to move to Stone City, causing great suffering. Empress Dowager Yu died during this turmoil.

The rebellion was eventually suppressed. Various armies attacked Shitou, and Su Jun was beheaded after falling from his horse. His brother Su Yi became the new rebel leader but was later captured. The palace was reduced to ashes, and Jianpingyuan was used as the imperial residence. Liu Yao was captured alive by Shi Le. The empire continued facing challenges including Shi Jilong's attacks, Li Xiong's invasions, and natural disasters like floods and comets.

Emperor Cheng died at age twenty-two. He was described as intelligent in youth but restricted by his uncle's influence. He showed concern for frugality, once stopping construction of a shooting hall due to cost. Emperor Kang, named Sima Yue (courtesy name Shitong), was Emperor Cheng's younger brother. He was initially made King of Wu, then King of Langxie, and served as Situ before being named successor. He ascended the throne after Emperor Cheng's death, entrusting Yu Bing and He Chong with government affairs. His reign saw continued conflicts with Shi Jilong and Li Shou, as well as military campaigns by various generals. Emperor Kang died at age twenty-three and was buried in Chongping Mausoleum.
