# Summary of Emperor An and Emperor Gong of Jin

Emperor An, named Dezong, was the eldest son of Emperor Xiaowu. He was established as crown prince in 387 and ascended the throne in 396 after his father's death, granting amnesty to the empire. He appointed Prince Daozi of Kuaiji as regent. During his reign, numerous rebellions and military conflicts occurred across the realm. Various warlords declared themselves rulers, including Wu Gu who founded Southern Liang, and Duan Ye who proclaimed himself Liangzhou Mu. Wang Gong and Yu Kai raised troops against officials Wang Guobao and Wang Xu, leading to their executions to appease the rebels.

The following years saw continued turmoil. Murong Xiang and Murong Lin successively claimed imperial titles. Wang Gong, Yu Kai, Yin Zhongkan, Huan Xuan, and Yang Quanqi rebelled. Huan Xuan defeated imperial forces at Baishi. The court sent Yuan Xian, Wang Shi, and Xie Yan to attack Huan Xuan and others. Liu Laozhi's son defeated Wang Gong, who was captured and beheaded. Huan Xuan became alliance leader. Tuoba Gui of Wei ascended the throne as Tianxing. Sun En, a demon thief, captured Kuaiji, killing several officials. The court sent Xie Yan and Liu Laozhi to repel him.

In 402, Huan Xuan defeated the imperial army at Gushu. Liu Laozhi betrayed and surrendered to Huan Xuan. The imperial army was defeated at Xinting, with Prince Yuanxian of Kuaiji and others killed. Huan Xuan appointed himself to various high positions and moved Prince Daozi to Ancheng. In 403, Huan Xuan usurped the throne, making the emperor a prisoner in Xunyang. In 404, Liu Yu raised troops against Huan Xuan, defeating his forces. Huan Xuan fled with Emperor An but was eventually beheaded. The emperor returned to the capital.

Emperor An was described as unintelligent, unable to speak from childhood, incapable of distinguishing seasons or caring for himself. This explains why Huan Xuan did not kill him during the usurpation. A prophecy stated there would be two emperors after the Ming Dynasty. Liu Yu, wanting to claim the throne, secretly had Emperor An strangled in 418 at age thirty-seven, establishing Emperor Gong to fulfill the prophecy.

Emperor Gong, named Dewen, was Emperor An's half-brother. He had been enfeoffed as King of Langxie and held various positions including General of the Central Army and Grand Sima. After Huan Xuan's defeat, he returned to his former position. In 419, following Emperor An's death, Liu Yu issued a false edict proclaiming Emperor Gong's succession. In 420, following Liu Yu's instructions, Fu Liang persuaded Emperor Gong to abdicate. The emperor willingly complied, stating the Jin family had long lost the empire. He wrote the abdication edict on red paper. Liu Yu made him King of Lingling. In 422, Liu Yu sent soldiers who killed Emperor Gong in his inner chamber at age thirty-six.

As a youth, Emperor Gong had been cruel and impetuous, once shooting horses for sport until someone noted that "Ma" (horse) was the imperial surname, making such killing unlucky. He then regretted his actions and became a devout Buddhist, casting a bronze statue and personally escorting it to Waguan Temple. He was known for his courtesy in serving his brother Emperor An, caring for his daily needs.
