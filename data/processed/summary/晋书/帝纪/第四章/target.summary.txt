Emperor Hui Ji, named Zhong and styled Zhengdu, was the second son of Emperor Wu. He was made crown prince at age nine in the third year of Taishi and ascended the throne on April 20, the first year of Taixi, upon Emperor Wu's death. He granted amnesty, changed the reign name to Yongxi, honored Empress Yang as empress dowager, and established Crown Prince Jia as empress. Emperor Wu was buried in Junyang Mausoleum on May 13. Officials and participants in the funeral received rank promotions, taxes were exempted for a year, and high-ranking officials were granted titles. Taiwei Yang Jun was appointed Taifu to assist in governance.

In the eighth month, Sima Wei, King of Guangling, was made crown prince, with He Shao, Wang Rong, and Yang Ji as his tutors. Generals were sent to garrison various regions. In winter, Sikong Shijian became Taiwei, and Sima Tai, King of Longxi, became Sikong. In the first year of Yongping, Emperor Hui issued an edict expressing his humility and fear, changing the reign name from Yongxi to Yongping. He forbade officials from visiting the mausoleum, and the crown prince came of age and was crowned. In the second month, silks were given to the prince, and Sima Wei and Sima Yun visited. In the third month, Grand Tutor Yang Jun and others were killed, and their clans were exterminated. Empress Jia deposed the empress dowager and killed her mother. Sima Liang, King of Runan, was made Dazai to assist in government, and other nobles received appointments. Over a thousand people were ennobled, but Sima Yao and Sima Mao were later dismissed.

In the fourth month, Sima Xu, King of Liang, was appointed general, and Ruan Tan became governor. In the fifth month, Sima Gui, King of Piling, died, and taxes were exempted for the elderly and widowed. In the sixth month, Empress Jia ordered Sima Wei to kill Sima Liang and Sima Wei, then executed Sima Wei for unauthorized killings. In the seventh month, ten counties were divided into Jiangzhou. In the eighth month, Sima Lun, King of Zhao, and Sima Yong, King of Hejian, received military commands. Sima Kui moved to Changshan, and Ma Fan and Sima Yue were made kings. In the ninth month, Sima Jian, King of Qin, died. In the twelfth month, an earthquake struck the capital. That year, seventeen Dongyi countries and twenty-four Nanyi tribes made contact.

In the second year, Empress Jia killed the empress dowager. In the eighth month, a general amnesty was declared. In the ninth month, Sima Dan, King of Zhongshan, died. In the eleventh month, a great plague occurred. Hail destroyed crops in Pei. In the third year, hail fell in Xingyang and Hongnong. In the fourth year, Shi Jian died, and natural disasters struck Shu and Shouchun. The Xiongnu Hao San rebelled but later surrendered and was killed. Earthquakes and landslides caused casualties and famine. In the fifth year, a comet appeared, and earthquakes, hail, and strong winds caused damage. A fire destroyed the arsenal. Floods struck several provinces, and the emperor sent censors to provide relief. In the sixth year, amnesty was declared, and officials died or were appointed. Earthquakes, frost, and blood on the ground occurred. Floods and rebellions by the Xiongnu and Di peoples led to battles and sieges. Famine and plague struck Guanzhong. In the seventh year, Zhou Chu died in battle, and hail, plague, drought, and famine continued. The emperor allowed the sale of children. In the eighth year, earthquakes and floods occurred, but Yongzhou had a good harvest. In the ninth year, Meng Guan captured Qi Wannian. Sima Xu and Sima Yong received commands. Zhang Chengji’s rebellion was suppressed. The crown prince Sima Wei was deposed and killed.

In the first year of Yongkang, amnesty was declared, and the reign name changed. Solar eclipses, strong winds, and blood rain occurred. Empress Jia killed the deposed prince. Sima Lun and Sima Xu deposed Empress Jia, killed Zhang Hua and others, and Sima Lun became prime minister. Sima Xu became chief minister. Sima Lun killed the deposed empress. The emperor’s grandson Sima Zang was made heir. Sima Yun attacked Sima Lun but failed and was killed. Sima Lun usurped the throne in the first year of Yongning, moving Emperor Hui to Jinyong City. Sima Wei, King of Qi, raised troops against Sima Lun, joined by Sima Ying, Sima Yong, and others. Sima Lun’s forces were defeated, and Emperor Hui was restored. Sima Lun and his party were killed. Sima Wei became chief commander, and Sima Ying and Sima Yong received high offices. In the second year of Tai’an, Sima Xu died, and Li Te rebelled in Shu. Sima Yong and Sima Ying attacked Sima Xi, King of Changsha. Emperor Hui personally led troops but was defeated. Zhang Fang entered the capital, causing destruction and famine. Sima Yue arrested Sima Xi, who was killed. Li Xiong occupied Shu. In the first year of Yongxing, Sima Ying became crown prince, but was later deposed. Emperor Hui was kidnapped to Chang’an by Zhang Fang. Sima Yong controlled the government. In the second year, Sima Yue marched west to rescue the emperor. After battles, Emperor Hui returned to Luoyang. In the first year of Guangxi, Sima Yong killed Zhang Fang. Sima Yue’s forces escorted Emperor Hui back. Emperor Hui died in Xianyang Hall at age 48, possibly poisoned by Sima Yue.

Emperor Hui was deemed unfit for rule, and his reign saw corruption and chaos. He once asked if frogs croaked for public or private reasons and suggested starving people eat meat. His death ended a tumultuous period marked by civil war and decline.
