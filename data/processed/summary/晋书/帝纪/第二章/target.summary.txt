Emperor Jing, named Ziyuan, was the eldest son of Emperor Xuan. He was elegant, calm, and strategic, renowned from youth alongside Xia Houxuan and He Yan. He Yan once said he would achieve great things. Serving as a regular servant of Sanqi in the early Wei Jingchu period, he was promoted to Central Protector, where he formulated talent selection methods without bias. After Queen Xuanmu's death, he showed extreme filial piety. When Emperor Xuan planned to kill Cao Shuang, he secretly strategized with Emperor Jing alone, keeping Emperor Wen unaware until the night before action. A spy found Emperor Jing sleeping calmly, while Emperor Wen could not rest. At dawn, troops gathered at Simamen, and Emperor Jing's calm arrangements impressed Emperor Xuan, who praised him. He had secretly gathered three thousand fearless scholars, who assembled unnoticed. After the incident, he was made Marquis of Changping Township with 1,000 households and later General of Wei. Upon Emperor Xuan's death, he became General of the Fu army to assist in governance.

In the fourth year of Wei Jiaping, he was promoted to general and Shizhong, commanding all armies and recording affairs of the Shangshu. He ordered officials to recommend talents, clarify hierarchies, and aid the poor. He appointed governors like Zhuge Dan and Mu Qiujian, and officials like Wang Ji and Deng Ai, earning admiration. When asked about system changes, he advocated following ancestral laws unless war necessitated change. In the fifth year, Wu's Zhuge Ke besieged Xincheng. Emperor Jing predicted he would concentrate forces in Hefei, not trouble Qingxu, and advised against excessive guards. He sent General Guanqiu Jian and others to resist, ordering high forts for defense. After months, Zhuge Ke's forces were exhausted, and Emperor Jing's counterattack killed over 10,000 enemies.

In the first year of the fifth year, Li Feng, Zhang Ji, and others plotted to replace Emperor Jing. He secretly knew, forced Li Feng to confess, and had him beaten to death. Xia Houxuan and Zhang Ji were arrested, and their three clans were reduced. He advised deposing Queen Zhang, and the emperor added 9,000 households to his estate, totaling 40,000, but he declined. After Xia Houxuan's execution, the emperor felt unsafe. Emperor Jing, fearing disaster, secretly advised the Empress Dowager to depose the emperor. In autumn, the Empress Dowager issued an order citing the emperor's misconduct. Emperor Jing convened ministers, who supported deposition. He deposed the emperor as King of Qi, killing his associates. He proposed King Pengcheng as successor, but the Empress Dowager chose Cao Mao, Duke of Gaogui Township. Emperor Jing reluctantly agreed, but worried when the new emperor showed laziness. He advised the emperor on proper conduct.

The emperor later issued an edict praising Emperor Jing's virtues and granting him the title of Prime Minister, adding 9,000 households, totaling 40,000, with honors like wearing swords in court. Emperor Jing resigned from the prime ministership and advised the emperor to study and be simple. The emperor accepted. In the second year, Mu Qiujian and Wen Qin rebelled, crossing the Huaihe River with 60,000 troops. Emperor Jing led over 100,000 troops to conquer, using strategy to avoid direct conflict. He sent generals to cut off retreat, and the rebels were defeated. Wen Qin fled, and Mu Qiujian was killed. Emperor Jing, suffering from an eye tumor, endured pain during the campaign, hiding it from his troops. He died in Xuchang at age 48. His funeral followed Huo Guang's precedent, and he was posthumously named Duke Wu, later Emperor Jing.

Emperor Wen, named Zishang, was Emperor Jing's half-brother. He served as a lieutenant general in Luoyang, exempting harsh taxes and pleasing the people. During Cao Shuang's campaign against Shu, he advised retreat, which proved correct. After Cao Shuang's execution, he gained 1,000 households. He defended against Shu's Jiang Wei, using strategy to force his retreat. He later led campaigns against Wu, but was defeated and lost his marquis title. He again faced Jiang Wei, predicting his tactics and defeating him. He suppressed a rebellion in Xinping, regaining his title. When Gaogui Township became king, he was made Gaodu Marquis. During Mu Qiujian's rebellion, he stayed in Luoyang. After Emperor Jing's death, he became General of the Guard, then general and Shizhong, commanding all armies. He refused high honors but was eventually made Duke of Gaodu with nine tins.

In the second year, Zhuge Dan rebelled, seeking Wu aid. Emperor Wen led 500,000 troops, using strategy to besiege Shouchun. He defeated Wu reinforcements, and after a long siege, Zhuge Dan was killed. He was made Duke of Jin with nine tins, but resigned nine times. He later planned to attack Shu, building warships and training troops. In the fourth year, he attacked Shu, sending Deng Ai and Zhong Hui. Deng Ai defeated Shu, and Liu Chan surrendered. Zhong Hui rebelled but was killed. Emperor Wen was made King of Jin, with twenty counties. He established five-class titles and sent envoys to Wu. He died at age 55, posthumously named King Wen, later Emperor Wen. His achievements included pacifying rebellions, conquering Shu, and stabilizing the realm, though he was criticized for regicide.
