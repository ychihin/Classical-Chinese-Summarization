# Summary of Historical Records: Emperor Jianwen and Emperor Xiaowu of Jin

Emperor Jianwen, named Sima Yu with the courtesy name Daowan, was the youngest son of Emperor Yuan. He was exceptionally intelligent as a child and beloved by his father. The diviner Guo Pu predicted he would revitalize the Jin Dynasty. Sima Yu grew to be pure-hearted with few desires, particularly skilled in discussing metaphysical matters. He was granted the title of King of Langxie in the first year of Yongchang, and later became King of Kuaiji. Throughout his career, he held various high-ranking positions including General of the Right, General of the Fu Army, and concurrently served as Secretary and Supervisor.

After the deposed emperor was removed, the Empress Dowager issued an edict declaring Sima Yu the rightful heir to the throne. In the eleventh month of the first year of Xian'an, he ascended as emperor. Huan Wen, who had previously deposed the former emperor, maintained significant power. When Huan Wen insisted on executing Sima Xi, the King of Wuling, Emperor Jianwen personally issued an edict refusing, stating that if the Jin Dynasty was destined to continue, Huan Wen should follow his previous edict, but if the dynasty's fortune was exhausted, he would make way for worthier men. Huan Wen was so frightened by this response that he broke into a cold sweat and abandoned the matter.

Emperor Jianwen was known for his graceful demeanor and disregard for material circumstances. He could remain calm even when Huan Wen deliberately created chaotic situations to test him. Despite his knowledge, he lacked grand strategies for saving the world. He was often afraid of being deposed, especially when the disaster star entered Taiwei after his ascension. He died at age fifty-three in the East Hall and was buried in Gaoping Mausoleum with the temple name Taizong.

Emperor Xiaowu, named Sima Yao with the courtesy name Changming, was Emperor Jianwen's third son. He was initially made King of Kuaiji, then Crown Prince, and ascended the throne upon his father's death. His reign saw significant events including the death of Huan Wen, after which the Empress Dowager briefly returned to court. The famous Battle of Fei River occurred during his reign, where Jin forces defeated Fu Jian's massive army, capturing tens of thousands of enemy soldiers.

Emperor Xiaowu was known for his intelligence and enlightenment in youth. After establishing his authority, however, he became indulgent in wine and sensual pleasures, often drinking through entire nights. He showed little concern for celestial omens, once toasting a comet and declaring that no emperor had ever lived ten thousand years. His reign was marked by natural disasters including earthquakes, floods, and droughts. He died suddenly at age thirty-five after drunkenly teasing his favored consort Zhang Guiren about her age, which led to her killing him in anger. He was buried in Longping Mausoleum.

The historical commentary notes that Emperor Jianwen governed during difficult times with Huan family controlling government orders. After Emperor Xiaowu's ascension, treacherous officials perished naturally, and capable ministers like Xie An, Wang Biaozhi, Huan Chong, and Xie Xuan emerged. However, the decline of the Jin Dynasty was ultimately attributed to human failings rather than divine will.
