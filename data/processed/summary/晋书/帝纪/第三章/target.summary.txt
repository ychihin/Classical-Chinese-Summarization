Emperor Wu, named Sima Yan with the courtesy name Anshi, was the eldest son of Emperor Wen of Jin. He was known for his tolerance, kindness, and magnanimity. During the Jiaping period of Wei, he was granted the title of Tinghou of Beiping and held various military positions. He welcomed Changdao Xianggong to the throne in Wuyang, became Zhongfujun, and was granted the title of Xinchang Xianghou in Jin. When the Jin state was established, he was made heir apparent and given the title of General of Fujun. Emperor Wen initially intended to pass the legacy to Sima You, but after opposition from He Zeng and others, Sima Yan was established as crown prince in May of the second year of Xianxi. Upon Emperor Wen's death in August, he inherited the titles of Prime Minister and King of Jin, implementing policies to relax punishments, pardon sinners, and reduce corvée.

In December of the first year of Taishi, Sima Yan ascended the throne as emperor after a formal ceremony in the southern suburbs, attended by numerous ethnic minorities. He issued an edict of general amnesty, changed the reign name, and granted various titles and rewards. He honored his ancestors with posthumous titles and established many kings and dukes. He promoted frugality, distributed palace treasures, and reformed the calendar. He also issued edicts to simplify laws, lift restrictions on certain clans, and allow officials to observe mourning periods. Throughout his reign, he conducted inspections, established institutions, and responded to natural disasters with relief efforts.

Emperor Wu's reign saw numerous military campaigns, including conflicts with Wu and various barbarian tribes. He expanded the empire, eventually conquering Wu in the first year of Taikang, capturing Sun Hao and unifying the realm. He implemented policies to stabilize the newly conquered territories, granted amnesty, and rewarded officials. He also faced internal challenges, such as rebellions and natural disasters, which he addressed through edicts and relief measures.

In his later years, Emperor Wu became neglectful of political affairs, indulging in banquets and favoring the queen's family, leading to the rise of relatives and nobles in power. He failed to depose the incapable crown prince, leading to chaos after his death. Despite his initial successes, his reign ended with turmoil, as the Western Jin dynasty eventually collapsed due to his lack of foresight and poor decisions. Historians note that while he had a good beginning, his later years were marked by decline and disaster.
