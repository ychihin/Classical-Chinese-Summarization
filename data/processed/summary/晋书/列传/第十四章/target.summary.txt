Zheng Miao, a native of Kaifeng, lost his father young and was raised by his uncle Zheng Hun. Known for his insight, he warned Ren Lan to avoid Wei Xie, who later caused disaster. He served as an official under Emperor Wu of Wei, recommended talents like Xu Yun, and governed with education and law, earning the people's love. During Guanqiu Jian's rebellion, he advised Emperor Jing to use defensive tactics. He declined high positions multiple times, eventually becoming Sikong, and died at eighty-five. His son Zheng Mo served as Tingwei, known for integrity and opening granaries during famine, and was posthumously honored.

Li Yin, from Liaodong, was famed for filial piety, mourning his lost grandfather and father. He served as a fair official, enforcing laws strictly, and was appointed Situ. Despite poverty, he refused gifts, and after his death, his family had no savings. His son Li Gu died early, and his grandson Li Zhi inherited the title.

Lu Qin, from Fanyang, was a Confucian scholar who served as a local official, focusing on fair governance and not managing property. He was appointed General Pingnan, securing borders, and died in Xianning. His family was so poor that the emperor granted funds for a house. His son Lu Fu declined high offices due to disability.

Lu Zhi, Lu Qin's nephew, served as a strategist for Sima Ying, advising against rebellion and later escorting the emperor to Luoyang. He was captured and killed by Liu Can. His son Lu Chen, a talented writer, was captured by Shi Jilong and died in Xiangguo, leaving works like "Sacrifice" and commentaries.

Hua Biao, from Gaotang, served as Taichang Qing, retiring due to illness. His son Hua Yu was dismissed for violating mourning rules but later restored. Hua Heng, Hua Yu's son, served as Taichang, establishing ritual systems after wars, and died in poverty with only books.

Shi Jian, from Leling, was a fair official who corrected abuses. He was dismissed for false reports but later restored as Sikong. During Yang Jun's crisis, he refused to attack Sima Liang, earning praise. He died as Taiwei.

Wen Xian, from Taiyuan, was known for intelligence, serving as Minister of Personnel. He defended Zhang Hua's title after his death, arguing against unfair condemnation. He died as Zuo Guanglu Doctor, leaving three sons.
