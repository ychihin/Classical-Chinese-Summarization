Dou Wu, a native of Fufeng, was known for his virtue and Confucian scholarship. He entered officialdom after his daughter became Empress to Emperor Huan, rising to Colonel of the City Gates. He was known for his integrity, recruiting worthy men, and refusing bribes. During a famine, he distributed his own rewards to students and the poor. He strictly disciplined his extravagant nephew, Dou Shao, even requesting his dismissal. When eunuchs seized power and arrested scholars like Li Ying and Du Mi in a party affair, Dou Wu memorialized the emperor, criticizing the eunuchs' corruption and warning of historical disasters like the fall of the Qin dynasty. He defended the arrested scholars and recommended virtuous ministers. After Emperor Huan died without an heir, Dou Wu consulted with an official and installed Liu Hong as Emperor Ling. He was made a general and marquis.

Dou Wu, along with Grand Tutor Chen Fan, planned to eliminate the eunuchs. Chen Fan urged immediate action, citing a solar eclipse as an omen. Dou Wu proposed the plan to the Empress Dowager, but she hesitated, citing tradition. After executing some eunuchs, Dou Wu's plan to kill others like Cao Jie stalled. When an astrological omen appeared, Dou Wu and Chen Fan prepared to act. They appointed allies to key posts and arrested a eunuch, whose confession implicated Cao Jie and Wang Fu. However, a eunuch stole the memorial and alerted his colleagues. They falsely accused Dou Wu of treason to the young emperor, seized the Empress Dowager, and issued an edict for Dou Wu's arrest. Dou Wu refused to submit, rallied troops, but his soldiers were persuaded to defect by the imperial edict. Pursued, Dou Wu and his nephew committed suicide. Their clans were exterminated, and the Queen Mother was moved. Dou Wu's grandson, Dou Fu, was saved by a former student, Hu Teng, who hid him and later helped him establish a family.

He Jin, from Nanyang, rose to power after his half-sister became Empress to Emperor Ling. He was made a general to suppress the Yellow Turban rebellion and later a marquis. He and his brother He Miao defeated a rebellion of thieves. Emperor Ling established a new army with eight colonels, entrusting the eunuch Jian Shuo with overall command. Jian Shuo, fearing He Jin, plotted to send him away. When Emperor Ling died, Jian Shuo planned to kill He Jin and install Prince Xie as emperor, but He Jin was warned and avoided the trap. He Jin then made Prince Bian emperor, with the Empress Dowager as regent. He Jin, hating the eunuchs and Jian Shuo's plot, planned to eliminate them with Yuan Shao's support. However, the Empress Dowager refused, and He Jin's relatives, bribed by eunuchs, also opposed him.

He Jin hesitated, and Yuan Shao suggested summoning provincial generals like Dong Zhuo to threaten the court. Despite advice against this, He Jin agreed. The eunuchs, fearing for their lives, tricked their way back into the palace. When He Jin went to the palace to urge the Empress Dowager to act, the eunuchs ambushed and killed him. His subordinates then attacked the palace. Yuan Shao and others massacred the eunuchs, killing over two thousand. During the chaos, the eunuchs kidnapped the emperor and his brother, but were eventually killed. Dong Zhuo then deposed the emperor, killed the Empress Dowager, and the He family was destroyed, plunging the Han dynasty into chaos. The historian comments that both Dou Wu and He Jin, despite their power and favorable positions, were defeated by eunuchs due to a lack of wisdom and excessive reliance on power.
