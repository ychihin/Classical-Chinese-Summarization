Liu Xuan, also known as Shenggong, was the elder brother of Emperor Guangwu. After his younger brother was killed, he sought revenge but fled to Pinglin when his guests broke the law. He later pretended to be dead to secure his father's release and escaped. During the famine in Wang Mang's last years, Wang Kuang and Wang Feng from Xinshi became leaders of groups foraging for food, and their following grew. Fugitives like Ma Wu joined them, and they attacked Li Xiangju, hiding in the Green Forest Mountains. Their force swelled to seven or eight thousand within months. In the second year of Wang Mang's reign, they defeated Jingzhou Mu's 20,000 troops, seized equipment, captured Jingling, and plundered Yundu and Anlu before returning to the mountains with over 50,000 men, beyond local control.

In the third year, a plague killed nearly half of the Green Forest force, causing dispersal. Wang Chang and Cheng Dan went west as "Xiajiang soldiers"; Wang Kuang, Wang Feng, and Ma Wu went north as "Xinshi soldiers." In July, they failed to take Suixian. Chen Mu and Liao Zhan from Pinglin gathered over a thousand "Pinglin soldiers" to support them. Liu Xuan joined Chen Mu as a supply officer. Emperor Guangwu and his brother Liu Boxheng also raised troops in Chongling and allied with the insurgents. In the first month of the fourth year, they defeated Wang Mang's officers, killing them, and Liu Xuan was promoted to General Gengshi. The generals, lacking unified leadership, made Liu Xuan emperor on the Xinsi day of February at Feishui. Liu Xuan, a coward, sweated profusely and could not speak. He granted amnesty, established the first year of Gengshi, and appointed officials, including his uncle Liu Liang, Wang Kuang, Wang Feng, Zhu Kun, Liu Bo, and Chen Mu.

In May, Liu Boxheng captured Wancheng. In June, Emperor Gengshi made it his capital and granted titles to over a hundred clan members and generals. Jealous of Liu Boxheng's prestige, he killed him. Liu Wang, Marquis of Zhongwu, led an uprising and captured Runan. After Wang Mang's generals Yan You and Chen Mao were defeated at Kunyang, they surrendered to Liu Wang, who declared himself emperor in August. Wang Mang sent Wang Kuang and Aizhang to defend Luoyang. Emperor Gengshi sent forces to attack Luoyang and Wuguan, shaking the Sanfu region. Heroes nationwide rose, killed local officials, and adopted Gengshi's reign name, spreading support within a month. Rebels in Chang'an attacked Weiyang Palace. In September, the Duke of Donghai killed Wang Mang and sent his head to Wancheng. Emperor Gengshi, pleased, hung it in the market. That month, Luoyang was captured, and Wang Kuang and Aizhang were beheaded. In October, Liu Xin killed Liu Wang, Yan You, and Chen Mao. Emperor Gengshi moved his capital to Luoyang, appointing Liu Ci as prime minister. In February of the second year, he moved west to Chang'an, but his horses were spooked before departure. After Wang Mang's defeat, only Weiyang Palace was burned; other buildings remained intact. Emperor Gengshi lived in Changle Palace but was shy and incompetent, entrusting government to Zhao Meng and indulging in drink. Zhao Meng became dictatorial, killing a critic. Li Yi and Zhu Kun acted dictatorially in Shandong, while Wang Kuang and Zhang Yan were tyrannical in Sanfu. Appointed officials were lowly, causing public ridicule. Li Shu advised reform but was imprisoned, leading to widespread resentment and betrayal.

In December, the Red Eyebrow Army entered the pass. In the first month of the third year, Fang Wang made Liu Ying emperor. Gengshi sent forces to defeat them but was later defeated by Red Eyebrow. Wang Kuang and Zhang Xuan proposed plundering and retreating east, but Gengshi refused. When Red Eyebrow made Liu Penzi emperor, Gengshi sent generals to resist. Zhang Xuan and others plotted to hijack Gengshi but were discovered. Gengshi killed some conspirators, but Zhang Xuan and others plundered the city and attacked the palace, defeating Gengshi. Gengshi fled to Zhao Meng, suspected Wang Kuang and others, and killed Chen Mu and Cheng Dan. Wang Kuang joined Zhang Xuan. After a month of fighting, Gengshi moved to Changxin Palace. Red Eyebrow arrived, and Wang Kuang surrendered. Gengshi defended the city but was defeated; Li Song was captured. Li Fan opened the city gate, and Red Eyebrow entered in September. Gengshi fled but was captured by Yan Ben. He surrendered to Red Eyebrow in October, was made Marquis of Weiwei, then King of Changsha. He lived with Xie Lu but was strangled on Xie Lu's orders. Guangwu buried him at Ba Ling. Gengshi had three sons, later enfeoffed by Guangwu.

Liu Penzi, a descendant of Liu Zhang, King of Chengyangjing, was captured by Red Eyebrow. Lu's mother, seeking revenge for her son, gathered followers and killed a county magistrate before returning to the sea. Fan Chong raised troops in Ju, and with others, formed the Red Eyebrow Army, painting their eyebrows red. They defeated Wang Mang's forces and grew to over 100,000. After Lu's mother died, her followers joined Red Eyebrow. They fought across regions, and when Gengshi made Luoyang his capital, Fan Chong and others surrendered but later rebelled. In the winter of the second year, they advanced west to Chang'an. In the first month of the third year, they arrived in Hongnong, winning battles. They divided into thirty battalions. Following a witch's prophecy, they made Liu Penzi emperor in June. Penzi, fifteen, was terrified. Xu Xuan became prime minister, Fan Chong imperial censor, and others held high ranks. They entered Chang'an, and Gengshi surrendered. Penzi lived in Changle Palace, but the generals were disorderly, robbing and killing. Penzi cried daily. Liu Gong taught him to resign, but Fan Chong refused. After more plundering, they left Chang'an westward, suffering heavy losses from snow. They returned, looted tombs, and insulted Empress Lu's body. Deng Yu attacked but was defeated. In September, they returned to Chang'an. After battles with Yancen and others, a great famine struck Sanfu. In December, they returned east, losing many. Guangwu intercepted them, and in the first month of the second year, they surrendered. Guangwu spared them, gave them land in Luoyang, but Fan Chong and Pang An later rebelled and were executed. Liu Penzi became blind and was given land in Xingyang for life.
