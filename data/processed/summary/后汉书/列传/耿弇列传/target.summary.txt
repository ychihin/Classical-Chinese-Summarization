Geng Yan, courtesy name Bozhao, was from Maoling County, Fufeng County. His ancestors migrated from Julu during Emperor Wu's reign. His father, Geng Kuang, was a doctor proficient in Confucian classics who studied Laozi with Wang Mang. Geng Yan was eager to learn in his youth and became interested in military affairs after observing county captains training knights. When Wang Mang was defeated and Gengshi Emperor was established, generals often replaced local officials arbitrarily. Geng Kuang, appointed by Wang Mang, felt uneasy. At twenty-one, Geng Yan left his father to seek reform, bringing tributes to consolidate his position.

Traveling to Songzi, he encountered Wang Lang, who falsely claimed to be Emperor Cheng's son Ziyu and raised troops in Handan. Geng Yan's subordinates Sun Cang and Wei Bao conspired to join Wang Lang, but Geng Yan rebuked them, calling Ziyu a deceitful thief. He went to Chang'an to explain the usefulness of troops from Yuyang and Shanggu, then traveled to Taiyuan and Daijun. Upon returning, he attacked with cavalry, but Sun and Wei refused and surrendered to Wang Lang. Hearing Guangwu was in Lunu, Geng Yan went north to meet him. Guangwu kept him as a subordinate, and Geng Yan persuaded him to send troops to pacify Handan. Guangwu praised his ambition.

Geng Yan followed Guangwu to Ji. When Handan soldiers approached, Guangwu planned to return south. Geng Yan advised against it, proposing to mobilize troops from Yuyang and Shanggu, which would make Handan easy to capture. Despite opposition, Guangwu appointed Geng Yan as his guide north. After a commotion in Ji, Geng Yan found his father Geng Kuang and persuaded him to join forces with Kou Xun and Peng Chong, each providing 2,000 cavalry and 1,000 infantry. Geng Yan, Jing Dan, and Kou Xun marched south, killing over 400 of Wang Lang's officials, capturing 125 seals, and beheading 30,000 people. They subdued 22 counties before meeting Guangwu at Guang'a. Guangwu was delighted and promoted them all.

Geng Yan and others attacked Handan. Gengshi Emperor summoned Zhao Yong, but Geng Kuang advised him to join Guangwu. When Zhang Ye rebelled with Xiongnu and Wuhuan aid, Guangwu sent Geng Yan's brother Geng Shu to defeat him. Gengshi, suspicious of Guangwu's growing power, sent envoys to make Guangwu King of Xiao and ordered him to stop fighting. Geng Yan advised Guangwu to ignore the order, warning of chaos under Gengshi and urging him to seize the world. Guangwu appointed Geng Yan as general to recruit troops from Youzhou. Geng Yan killed Gengshi's governors Wei Shun and Cai Chong, while Wu Han killed Miao Zeng. They led Youzhou's soldiers south, defeating multiple rebel groups. Geng Yan often led the vanguard.

Guangwu ascended the throne and made Geng Yan General Jianwei. In the second year of Jianwu, he was made Marquis of Haoju. In the third year, he defeated Yan Cen and Du Hong, capturing over 3,000 heads and 5,000 prisoners. In the fourth year, he was ordered to attack Yuyang but hesitated due to family ties. The emperor reassured him. Geng Yan later defeated mountain bandits and, with Wu Han, captured Fuping, causing 40,000 thieves to surrender. He then attacked Zhang Bu, using strategy to lure Fei Yi into battle, defeating him and capturing Juli. He took Linzi in half a day, isolating Xi'an. Zhang Bu attacked with 200,000 troops but was defeated twice. Geng Yan pursued him, and Zhang Bu surrendered. Geng Yan pacified the region, capturing 300 cities without defeat.

Geng Yan died at fifty-six, posthumously titled Minhou. His descendants served as generals and married into the imperial family. The historian commented that Geng Yan, like Han Xin, foresaw Guangwu's success. Though he achieved no new feats after conquering Qi, the Geng family's military legacy endured for generations, possibly because they used war to end killing.
