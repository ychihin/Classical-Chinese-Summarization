**Summary of Historical Biographies**

**Deng Biao** (courtesy name Zhibo), a native of Nanyang and of the same clan as Taifu Deng Yuzhi, was the son of Deng Han. In the early Zhongxing Dynasty, he was made Marquis of Monument for his service and served as Prefect of Bohai Sea. Known for filial piety, he ceded his inheritance to his half-brother Jing Feng after his father's death, a decision Emperor Xianzong approved. He held various official posts, including Guiyang magistrate, and was recruited as a servant in Yongping's 17th year. After his stepmother's death, he served as Guanglu Dafu, then Fengche Captain and Dasi Nong, eventually replacing Bao Yu as Taiwei. Known for his integrity, he resigned due to illness after four years. In Yuanhe's first year, he was dismissed with a gift of 300,000 yuan and a lifelong salary of 2,000 dan. Emperor He later appointed him Taifu and Marquis of Guannei. During the Dou family's autocracy, he remained self-cultivating but made no corrections. He died in office in the spring of the fifth year, and the emperor visited his home.

**Zhang Yu** (courtesy name Boda), from Zhao Guoxiang, was the grandson of Zhang Kuang, whose clan sister married Emperor Zukao. Zhang Kuang met Emperor Guangwu and served as Yuan family order, Zhuo County governor, and Changshan Pass chief, dying in battle against the Red Eyebrow Army. Zhang Yu's father, Zhang Xin, sought revenge and later became Prime Minister of Huaiyang. Zhang Yu was honest and thrifty, refusing donations after his father's death and giving his land to his uncle. Promoted to Xiaolian in Yongping's 8th year, he became Yangzhou governor in Jianchu's early years. He famously crossed the river despite fears of the god Zixu, personally interrogating prisoners and resolving cases. As Yanzhou governor, he gained a reputation for fairness. As Xiapi Prime Minister, he irrigated Puyangpo, reclaiming hundreds of hectares and attracting over a thousand poor households. He punished the powerful official Shi Dairun, earning respect. In Yongyuan's 6th year, he became Grand Sima and Taiwei, advising Emperor He against risky travel. Promoted to Taifu in Yanping's 1st year, he lived in the palace under Empress Dowager Deng's orders, advocating for allocating open lands to the poor. He served Emperor An, resigning several times but receiving gifts. He was made Marquis of Anxiang in Yongchu's 1st year. He advised the Empress Dowager to return to the palace during her mother's illness. After years of famine, he requested tax collection for relief. He was dismissed in the 5th year due to yin-yang disharmony and died at home seven years later.

**Xu Fang** (courtesy name Yeqing), from Pei, learned from his father and grandfather, who taught Wang Mang the Book of Changes. Promoted to Xiaolian during Yongping, he became Shangshulang, serving two emperors without fault. Under Emperor He, he served as Sili Xiaowei, Wei County prefect, Shaofu, and DaSinong. In Yongyuan's 14th year, he became Sikong. He proposed reforming the examination system for doctors, advocating for testing based on famous scholars' chapters and verses to prevent private interests and disputes. His suggestions were followed. In the 16th year, he became Situ, then Taiwei in Yanping's 1st year, participating in Shangshu affairs with Taifu Zhang Yu. Emperor An made him Marquis of Longxiang. He was dismissed due to disasters and bandits, the first of the three ministers to be exempted. After his death, his son Xu Heng inherited, later passing the title to his brother Xu Chong.

**Zhang Min** (courtesy name Boda), from Molan, Hejian, was promoted to Xiaolian in Jianchu's 2nd year. He opposed the "Insult Law," arguing it opened the way to killing and promoted sin. He cited Confucius and the Spring and Autumn Annals, advocating for removing complicated laws. Emperor He agreed. In the 9th year, he became Sili Xiaowei, then Runan prefect, known for fairness. After dismissal, he became Yilang and Yingchuan prefect in Yanping's 1st year. In Yongchu's 1st year, he was recruited as Sikong, serving according to law. He requested retirement due to illness but was refused. In the spring of the 6th year, he fell during a grand shooting ceremony and was dismissed, dying at home.

**Hu Guang** (courtesy name Boshi), from Huarong, Nanjun, was a descendant of the virtuous Hu Gang. Orphaned and poor, he worked as a casual official. Recommended by Fazhen, he was tested by Zhang Zhi and deemed the best in the world by Emperor An. He became Shangshulang and Shangshu Pushe. He advised Emperor Shun against drawing lots to choose a queen, advocating for selecting based on virtue, age, and appearance. He opposed Zuo Xiong's proposal to set a minimum age of 40 for officials, arguing for flexibility based on talent. He served as Chenliu prefect, Jiyin prefect, Runan prefect, and Da Sinong. In Han'an's 1st year, he became Situ, then Taiwei, establishing Emperor Huan and becoming Marquis of Yuyang Anle Township. He served multiple terms, including as Sikong and Taiwei, but was dismissed after Liang Ji's death. He later served under Emperor Ling as Taifu. Known for his gentle and discreet nature, he mastered court rules and was praised in the capital: "Ignore everything and ask Bo Shi, there is only Hu Gong among the mediocre." He served six emperors for over 30 years, holding high offices repeatedly. He died at 82 in Xiping's 1st year, receiving grand honors. He also compiled the "Baiguan Zhen" and other works. Emperor Ling later had his portrait painted in the province.
