The Han Dynasty inherited the turbulent Warring States period, fostering arrogant and cunning individuals who used power to dominate regions. Officials acted arbitrarily, killing traitors before reporting, displaying staunch spirit and unyielding power. Countless injustices occurred, with corpses filling pits and blood flowing for miles. Figures like Wang Wenshu and Yan Yannian gained reputations as tiger-crowned officials and butchers. After the Zhongxing period, laws tightened, but eunuchs and relatives harmed the world, leading to excessive acts like Yang Qiu digging up Wang Fu's body and Zhang Jian dissecting Cao Jie's tomb, which vented public anger but were deemed too cruel.

Dong Xuan, from Chenliu, was recommended as a person of excellent performance and became state minister of Beihai. He killed Gongsun Danzi for murdering a passerby to ward off disaster, then arrested and executed over thirty of Gongsun Dan's clan. The governor of Qingzhou reported him, and Dong Xuan was imprisoned but remained calm. Emperor Guangwu pardoned him, demoting him to county magistrate of Huai. Later, as governor of Jiangxia, he intimidated the giant thief Xia happy into surrendering. As magistrate of Luoyang, he killed Princess Huyang's murderous slave, defying the emperor's order to apologize. He died in office at 74, leaving a poor household, and was honored posthumously.

Fan Ye, from Nanyang, was a friend of Guangwu from youth. He served as censor and captain of Hedong, punishing Ma Shikuang and eliminating thieves. As state animal husbandry, he taught farming and management. After Wei Xiao's elimination, he became prefect of Tianshui, applying strict laws like Han Fei. His harsh rule made the road safe at night, and Liangzhou people sang of his severity. He died in office after 14 years, and Emperor Xianzong later granted his family millions.

Li Zhang, from Huai, studied Yan's Spring and Autumn Annals and served under Guangwu. As Yangping Order, he killed the powerful Zhao Gang at a banquet and pacified the area. As prefect of Langxie, he attacked rebels imprisoning the prefect, killing leader Xia Changsi. He was later conscripted for land measurement issues but released due to merit, dying of illness.

Zhou Jin, from Pixu, liked Han Fei's methods. As a county official, he killed dozens of powerful bad guys. As Bo Ping Ling, no one left prison alive. He hid pardon orders to execute criminals first. He was dismissed for killing innocents but later promoted to Zhaoling Prime Minister. He exposed Ting Yu's deception and, as Luoyang Order, intimidated nobles. He clashed with Huangmenlang Dou Du, was imprisoned, pardoned, and later dismissed for harshness. He returned as Yushi Zhongcheng, but was removed again. He later impeached Xia Yanghou, becoming Sili Xiaowei, and died in office.

Huang Chang, from Kuaiji, studied Confucian classics and served as a judge. As magistrate of Wan, he exposed hidden evildoers, catching a thief who stole his hood. As governor of Shu, he handled over 700 lawsuits and arrested bandits. He reunited with his long-lost wife, captured by thieves. He executed Mrs. Peng for watching him from a high building. He served as prefect of Yingchuan and died as Taizhong doctor.

Yang Qiu, from Yuyang, was strict and liked Shen Buhai and Han Fei. He killed a county official who insulted his mother. As prefect of Jiujiang, he eliminated thieves and executed corrupt officials. He was dismissed for cruelty but later promoted to Yilang. As Sili Xiaowei, he arrested and tortured Wang Fu and his son, tearing Wang Fu's body apart. He attempted to impeach Cao Jie but was demoted to Weiwei. He was later executed for plotting against eunuchs.

Wang Ji, adopted son of Wang Fu, was cruel as Prime Minister Pei. He killed over 10,000 people in five years, using harsh methods like killing parents who didn't raise children. He was detained and died in Luoyang prison when Yang Qiu impeached Wang Fu.

The historian commented that ancient people were generous, but in later times, severe torture and killing led to cruel officials. Some were brave and skilled, but their methods varied. Power and punishment caused sloppy behavior, while benevolence and faith could prevent evil. The gains and losses of numerous punishments and lawsuits could be understood from one state to the whole world.
