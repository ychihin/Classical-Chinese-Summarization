Huangfu Song, styled Yizhen, was a nephew of the general Huangfu Gui. His father served as the Prefect of Yanmen. From a young age, Huangfu Song was learned in the *Classic of Poetry* and *Book of Documents*, and skilled in archery, swordsmanship, and horsemanship. He was recommended as a Xiaolian and Maocai, but declined summons from Grand Commandant Chen Fan and General Dou Wu. During Emperor Ling's reign, he was appointed Yilang and later Governor of Beidi.

When Zhang Jiao founded the Way of Great Peace, claiming to cure illness through faith and talismanic water, he amassed hundreds of thousands of followers across eight provinces over a decade. He organized them into thirty-six "squares," each led by a general, and spread the prophecy: "The Azure Heaven is dead; the Yellow Heaven shall rise. In the year of Jiazi, the world will be blessed." In the first year of Zhongping (184 AD), his disciple Tang Zhou betrayed the planned uprising, leading to the execution of Ma Yuanyi and a crackdown in Luoyang. Zhang Jiao then launched a nationwide rebellion, with followers wearing yellow scarves—hence called the "Yellow Turbans"—burning government offices and terrorizing provinces.

The court appointed Huangfu Song as Left General of the Central Army, alongside Zhu Jun, to suppress the rebellion. After an initial defeat, Huangfu Song used a fire attack at Changshe, exploiting a strong wind to burn the enemy camp and rout Bo Cai's forces. Reinforced by Cao Cao, they defeated the rebels, beheading tens of thousands. Huangfu Song was made Marquis of Duxiang. They then pacified Runan and Chen, capturing Bo Cai and Peng Tuo. At Cangting, they captured Bu Ji, killing over 7,000.

Huangfu Song was then ordered to attack Zhang Jiao. After initial setbacks against Zhang Liang at Guangzong, he feigned rest, then launched a surprise dawn assault, killing Zhang Liang and 30,000 rebels, with 50,000 drowning in the Yellow River. Zhang Jiao had died of illness, so Huangfu Song beheaded his corpse. He then killed Zhang Bao at Xiaquyang, capturing over 100,000. He was appointed General of the Left Chariot and Cavalry and Governor of Jizhou, with 8,000 households. He petitioned to reduce Jizhou's land rent, earning popular praise.

Huangfu Song was a benevolent commander who shared hardships with his troops. After pacifying the Yellow Turbans, his fame grew, but court politics worsened. Advisor Yan Zhong urged him to seize power, but Huangfu Song refused, remaining loyal. Later, due to conflicts with eunuchs Zhao Zhong and Zhang Rang, he was demoted to Marquis of Duxiang with 2,000 households.

In 189 AD, when the Liangzhou rebel Kingdom besieged Chencang, Huangfu Song and Dong Zhuo led 20,000 men each. Dong Zhuo wanted to attack immediately, but Huangfu Song argued for a defensive strategy, waiting for the enemy to weaken. After 80 days, the rebels retreated, and Huangfu Song pursued, defeating them and beheading over 10,000. Dong Zhuo, humiliated, grew to hate him.

When Dong Zhuo later seized power, he tried to kill Huangfu Song, but his son Jian Shou pleaded successfully. Huangfu Song was eventually released and served as Yilang and Yushi Zhongcheng. After Dong Zhuo's death, he became Grand Commandant, but was dismissed due to a meteor omen. He later died of illness, posthumously honored as General of Hussars.

Zhu Jun, styled Gongwei, was from Shangyu, Kuaiji. Orphaned young, he was known for filial piety and generosity. He once stole his mother's silk to help a friend repay a debt. He served as a county official and later bribed officials to save Prefect Yin Duan from punishment. Recommended as Xiaolian, he became Magistrate of Lanling, earning commendation.

When the Jiaozhi rebellion erupted, Zhu Jun was appointed Governor of Jiaozhi. He recruited 5,000 troops, pacified the rebels, killed Liang Long, and secured surrender. He was made a Marquis and later served as an admonishment official.

During the Yellow Turban rebellion, Zhu Jun was appointed Right General of the Central Army, alongside Huangfu Song. They suppressed rebels in Yingchuan, Runan, and Chen. Huangfu Song credited Zhu Jun, who was made Marquis of Xixiang.

Zhu Jun then besieged Nanyang Yellow Turban leader Zhao Hong at Wancheng for months. Despite advice to replace him, the court persisted. Zhu Jun killed Zhao Hong, then faced Han Zhong. Feigning weakness, he attacked the city's northeast, forcing Han Zhong to retreat. When Han Zhong sought surrender, Zhu Jun refused, arguing it would encourage rebellion. He lifted the siege, allowing Han Zhong to scatter, then defeated him, beheading over 10,000. After Han Zhong was killed by Prefect Qin Jie, Zhu Jun defeated Sun Xia, ending the rebellion.

Zhu Jun was appointed General of the Right Chariot and Cavalry, then served as Guanglu Doctor. After his mother's death, he returned to office as Master of Works, then Shaofu and Grand Servant.

Later, the Black Mountain bandits, led by Zhang Yan, emerged with over a million followers. Zhu Jun was appointed Governor of Henei to repel them. Under Dong Zhuo's rule, Zhu Jun opposed moving the capital to Chang'an. He later conspired with Shandong generals, fled to Jingzhou, then returned to Luoyang, garrisoning Zhongmou. He called for troops against Dong Zhuo, but was defeated by Li Jue and Guo Si.

After Dong Zhuo's death, Tao Qian and others recommended Zhu Jun as Grand Marshal, but Li Jue and Guo Si summoned him to court. Zhu Jun accepted, becoming Grand Commandant and Minister of Works. He was dismissed due to a solar eclipse, then served as General of Hussars. During Li Jue and Guo Si's conflict, he was taken hostage by Guo Si and died of illness.

The historian comments that both Huangfu Song and Zhu Jun were talented generals who achieved fame but failed to seize the moment for greater change, adhering to minor loyalties. Huangfu Song's humility and refusal to claim credit preserved his family and wealth, earning praise.
