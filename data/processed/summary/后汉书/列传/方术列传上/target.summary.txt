# Summary of Translated Historical Texts

## Preface on Alchemy and Divination

Confucius discussed the "Book of Changes," noting that a gentleman's way includes divination using tortoise shells or grass. Ancient kings used divination to predict fortunes, solve problems, and receive divine guidance. Many ancient records contain theories of Yin and Yang for predicting the future, though certain mystical texts like the jade-cement golden rope are now lost. Various schools of divination exist, including Feng Jiao, Dun Jia, Qi Zheng, and others, along with cloud-reading and energy-calculation methods. These practices are often vague and difficult to explore, which is why Confucius rarely discussed supernatural matters. Since Emperor Wu of the Han dynasty, alchemy became popular. Wang Mang used prophecies to justify his usurpation, and Emperor Guangwu of Eastern Han believed in them, leading to many practitioners gaining positions. Some knowledgeable people opposed these practices. Sima Qian noted that Yin and Yang books make people restrained with many taboos. The text concludes that while everything has biases, understanding the true principles of magic requires rejecting vulgar interpretations.

## Biographies of Diviners and Alchemists

Ren Wengong of Langzhong learned Tianguan Fengjiao from his father. During Emperor Ai's reign, he correctly predicted a storm that killed his companions. Later, he predicted a flood during a drought, saving his family while thousands died. He became famous for divination and fled to Zigong Mountain during the military revolution, surviving by training his family to carry food and run quickly.

Guo Xian of Runan studied under Zhongzi. He advised Zhongzi against visiting Wang Mang, who later became emperor and appointed Guo Xian as a doctor. Guo Xian burned his clothes and fled. Under Emperor Guangwu, he served as Guang Luxun. He predicted a fire in Qi state and advised against military campaigns. He later resigned due to illness and died at home.

Xu Yang of Runan, skilled in arithmetic, served under Wang Mang but fled when Wang usurped the throne. He helped restore irrigation systems in Runan, completing over 400 li of ponds over several years. Despite being falsely accused of bribery, his handcuffs miraculously opened, and he was released. He died of illness, and a temple was built in his honor.

Gao Huo of Runan studied under Ouyang She and had an old relationship with Emperor Guangwu. He refused to change his behavior for an official position. Skilled in astronomy and Dun Jia, he helped bring rain during a drought. He later fled to Jiangnan and died in Shicheng, where a shrine was built for him.

Wang Qiao of Hedong served as magistrate of Ye County during Emperor Xianzong's reign. He traveled to court magically every month, arriving with two water birds. When caught, only one shoe was found. A jade coffin descended from heaven, and Wang Qiao entered it, which closed immediately. The coffin was buried, and a temple called Ye Jun Temple was built. Local officials must pay homage upon arrival.

Xie Yiwu of Kuaiji served as a county official and postal supervisor. He predicted a criminal's death within thirty days, which came true. He became governor of Jingzhou and prefect of Julu, known for good governance. Wu Lun recommended him highly, comparing him to ancient sages. He later knew his death date in advance and died as expected.

Yang You of Chengdu studied the Book of Changes and various divination methods. He correctly predicted a barbarian invasion and other events. He wrote ten books called "Qi Ping" and died at home.

Li Nan of Danyang knew wind angle divination. He predicted good news for Prefect Ma Leng, which arrived as expected. He was later summoned to the palace but could not travel due to illness and died at home. His daughter also knew family skills and predicted her own death from a storm sign.

Li He of Hanzhong was proficient in the Five Classics and Heluo Fengxing. He identified two imperial envoys by observing stars. He advised against associating with General Dou Xian, who later committed suicide. Li He served as Sikong and Situ, showing loyalty. He refused a marquis title and died at home over eighty years old.

Duan Yi of Guanghan studied the Book of Changes and Mingfengjiao. He accurately predicted students' arrivals and provided a plaster that healed a student's injury. He lived in seclusion and died at home.

Liao Fu of Runan studied classics and astronomy. After his father's death, he decided to leave worldly affairs. He predicted famines and distributed grain to relatives. He refused official positions and died at home at age eighty.

Zhe Xiang of Guanghan understood the Book of Changes and Huangdi-Laozi teachings. After his father's death, he dispersed his wealth, citing Laozi's warnings about riches. He knew his death day, bid farewell to guests, and died suddenly at eighty-four.

Fan Ying of Nanyang studied Jingshi Yi and Five Classics, skilled in wind angle and star calculation. He predicted a fire in Chengdu. He refused multiple imperial summons but eventually attended court, where he boldly stated he was ordered by Heaven. He was appointed General of the Five Features but soon returned home. He wrote "Yi Zhang Ju" and taught students. He died at home in his seventies.
