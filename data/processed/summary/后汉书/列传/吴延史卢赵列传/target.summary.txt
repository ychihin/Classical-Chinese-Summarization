# Summary of Historical Biographies

## Biography of Wu You

Wu You, courtesy name Ji Ying, was from Chenliu County. As a child, he advised his father against writing scriptures on bamboo slips while serving as an official in a distant region, warning that such actions might invite suspicion from the court and powerful families, citing historical examples like Ma Yuan and Wang Yang who were falsely accused. His father heeded this advice. After his father's death, Wu You lived in poverty, herding pigs and reciting scriptures, refusing gifts from others. When criticized for this humble work, he continued regardless.

Later recommended as a Xiaolian, Wu You befriended Huang Zhen at a farewell ceremony, despite criticism for his informal behavior. He became known for his integrity. While serving as an official, he helped the impoverished scholar Gongshamu and befriended the young Dai Hong, who later became a renowned Confucian scholar. As an administrator, Wu You led by example, resolving disputes through moral instruction and personal intervention, which reduced litigation. He showed leniency in cases like Sun Xing, who stole money to buy clothes for his father, and Guanqiu Chang, who killed a man for insulting his mother. Wu You allowed Guanqiu Chang to be with his wife in prison so she could conceive a child before his execution. After serving in Jiaodong, Wu You became Prime Minister of Qi. He later argued for Li Gu against Liang Ji's false accusations, angering Liang Ji. He resigned from his final post as Prime Minister of Hejian, returning home to teach until his death at ninety-eight.

## Biography of Yan Du

Yan Du, courtesy name Shujian, was from Nanyang County. He studied under Tang Xidian and Ma Rong, mastering the classics and gaining fame in the capital. After becoming an official, he repaired Gong Sui's tomb and selected his descendants for office. He abandoned his post to attend his teacher's funeral. Emperor Huan appointed him as Yilang, where he worked on writings in Dongguan. He was promoted to Minister and later to Zuo Feng Yi and Jingzhao Yin, governing with tolerance and benevolence, earning praise from the people.

When General Liang Ji sent guests to sell bezoars in Jingzhao, Yan Du arrested and killed them, angering Liang Ji. He was dismissed due to illness and returned home to teach. He wrote a detailed discussion on the relationship between benevolence and filial piety, arguing they share the same origin, with filial piety as the foundation and benevolence as its extension. He declined recommendations for higher office, preferring his scholarly life. Later imprisoned during the party proscriptions, he died at home in the first year of Yongkang. His image was painted in Qu Yuan Temple. He wrote numerous works and made corrections to the scriptures.

## Biography of Shi Bi

Shi Bi, courtesy name Gongqian, was from Chenliu County. He was eager to learn and served as an official. Concerned about the misconduct of Emperor Huan's younger brother, Prince Bohai Kui, Shi Bi secretly wrote to the emperor warning of potential rebellion and urging restraint. The emperor did not investigate due to family ties. Later, when Kui was convicted of treason, Shi Bi was promoted to Shangshu and became Pingyuan Prime Minister.

During the party proscriptions, when other counties reported hundreds of party members, Shi Bi alone reported none. When pressured by an inspector, he refused to falsely accuse innocent people, stating he would rather die than do so. The inspector arrested county officials and reported Shi Bi to the court. When the party ban eased, Shi Bi used his salary to atone for over a thousand people. As an official, he harshly punished the powerful but showed leniency to commoners. As Governor of Hedong, he cut off correspondence from powerful families seeking favors. When Hou Lan sent a letter requesting favors, Shi Bi had the messenger beaten to death. Hou Lan retaliated by forging an edict and having Shi Bi arrested. Wei Xun, a former Xiaolian, disfigured himself to care for Shi Bi in prison. Fellow countrymen bribed Hou Lan to reduce Shi Bi's death sentence to labor reform. After serving his sentence, Shi Bi returned home. He was later recruited as Yilang but was hated by Hou Lan's faction. He became Prime Minister of Pengcheng and died of illness.

## Biography of Lu Zhi

Lu Zhi, courtesy name Zigan, was from Zhuo County. He studied under Ma Rong alongside Zheng Xuan, mastering ancient and modern learning. He was known for his resolute character and integrity. When Dou Wu was about to be enfeoffed, Lu Zhi wrote advising him to decline the reward and preserve his reputation, but Dou Wu ignored him. Lu Zhi refused multiple calls to office until he was recruited as a doctor. As Prefect of Jiujiang, he pacified barbarian rebellions. He wrote "Shangshu Zhangju" and "Exegesis of the Three Body Problems" and requested to establish academic officials for various classics.

When the Yellow Turban rebellion arose, Lu Zhi was appointed General and defeated Zhang Jiao, capturing over ten thousand men. When the city was about to fall, he refused to bribe the imperial envoy Zuo Feng, who falsely reported him. Lu Zhi was recalled and sentenced to death, later reduced. After the emperor's death, he opposed summoning Dong Zhuo, warning of his dangerous nature. When Dong Zhuo deposed the emperor, Lu Zhi alone protested. Cai Yong interceded to save Lu Zhi's life, but he was removed from office. He fled in disguise and lived in seclusion until his death. On his deathbed, he ordered a simple burial. Cao Cao later honored his memory.

## Biography of Zhao Qi

Zhao Qi, courtesy name Bingqing, was from Jingzhao County. He was proficient in classics but despised his wealthy relative Ma Rong. After a severe seven-year illness, he recovered and served as an official. He hated eunuchs and resigned rather than work with one. When his brother offended a powerful eunuch's relative, Zhao Qi fled, and his family was killed. He hid in Beihai, selling cakes, where Sun Song recognized him as extraordinary and sheltered him for years. During this time, Zhao Qi wrote twenty-three chapters of "Etun Song."

After amnesty, he served as Governor of Bingzhou but was dismissed due to party affairs. He was imprisoned for over ten years. Later recruited as Yilang, he was captured by bandits but escaped. He served as an imperial envoy, promoting peace among warring factions. He suggested to Dong Cheng that Liu Biao of Jingzhou could support the court, and Liu Biao sent troops and supplies. Zhao Qi stayed in Jingzhou due to old age and illness. He was appointed Taichang and died at over ninety. He prepared his own tomb with paintings of historical figures and himself. He wrote "Mencius Chapters" and "Sanfu Juelu."
