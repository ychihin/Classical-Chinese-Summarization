**Summary of Translated Historical Biographies**

Tang Tan, courtesy name Zichan, was a native of Nanchang, Yuzhang. He studied the "Jing's Yi", "Han Shi", and "Yan's Spring and Autumn" at Taixue, with a particular interest in astrology of disasters and aliens. Returning home, he taught over a hundred students. In the seventh year of the Yuan Dynasty, when Zhicao grew in the county, Prefect Liu Yu sought his opinion. Tan replied that his relatives were powerful and Yang Dao weak, questioning if it was a good sign, so Liu stopped reporting. In the first year of Yongning, a woman gave birth to four children, and Tan predicted military strength in the capital and disaster within the court. In the fourth year of Yanguang, Sun Cheng raised troops, killed the queen's brother, and established King Jiyin as crown prince, as Tan had divined. In the fifth year of Yongjian, Tan was promoted to Xiaolian and doctor. When a white rainbow pierced the sun, he wrote three things stating its fault, then abandoned his official post. He wrote twenty-eight chapters called "Tang Zi" and died at home.

Gongshamu, courtesy name Wengui, was from Jiaodong, Beihai. Poor and humble, he studied "Han Shi" and "Gongyang Chunqiu", delving into the magic of "He Luo". He lived alone in the built mountains. During a storm, a voice called him repeatedly, but he refused to answer. The caller entered through the window with a strange shape, but Mu recited his sutra as usual, and no other strange thing occurred. Later, he lived in seclusion in Donglai Mountain, teaching students from afar. Wealthy Wang Zhong offered him a million dollars as capital, but Mu declined, saying wealth has its own destiny. He was promoted to Xiaolian and became prime minister of Zeng County. There, Marquis Liu Chang did many illegal things, abolishing his legitimate son for a concubine. Mu remonstrated, and Liu Chang wept, listened, and corrected his ways. Later promoted to Hongnong Order, when borers ate crops, Mu set up an altar to pray, and after heavy rain, the borers died. In the first year of Yongshou, floods surged, and Mu warned people to move to high ground, sparing the Hongnong people. He later became captain of the vassal state of Liaodong, died in office at sixty-six, and all six sons were famous.

Xu Man, from Pingyu, Runan, inherited his grandfather Xu Jun's divination skills. During Emperor Huan's reign, Prefect Feng Zui saw two red snakes escape when opening his ribbon. Xu Man divined that in three years, Zui would become a border general named Dongming, traveling three thousand miles northeast, and in five years, a general marching south. In the first year of Yanxi, Feng Zui became governor of Liaodong, attacking Xianbei, and in the fifth year, general of chariots and cavalry, attacking Wuling barbarians, as divined.

Zhao Yan, from Langyi, had skills. In the third year of Yanxi, thieves killed the captain and captured Langfu County. The court sent Nanyang Zongzi to attack. Zhao Yan explained the method of "Gu Xu", advising to mobilize Wuyang soldiers to attack Guxu. Zongzi reported, and an edict sent Wuyang soldiers. Zhao Yan pushed Dunjia, teaching them to advance at the right time, defeating the traitors in one battle and burning their camps, pacifying Xuzhou and Yanzhou.

Fan Zhi Zhang, from Nanzheng, Hanzhong, was learned and versatile, living in seclusion. He traveled to Longxi and was sent by Qiang general Duan Zhen to conquer the Western Qiang. Surrounded by Qiang, Fan advised that there were no Qiang in the southeast corner, so they could escape and later return to win. Duan obeyed and defeated the traitor. Fan was praised as having the knowledge of Zi Shen, Jiao Yanshou, and Dong Zhongshu, and a special edict was issued, but Fan died of illness.

Shan Yang, courtesy name Wuxuan, from Shanyang Hulu, knew heavenly official and arithmetic. He was promoted to Xiaolian, then Taishi Ling and Shizhong, became prefect of Hanzhong, was dismissed, later became a minister, and died in office. In the last years of Xiping, Huanglong appeared in Qiao. Qiao Xuan asked Shan Yang, who said the country would have a king rise up, and in less than fifty years, the dragon would reappear. In the spring of the 25th year of Jian'an, Huanglong appeared again, and in winter, Wei accepted the abdication.

Han Shuo, courtesy name Shuru, from Shanyin, Kuaiji, was knowledgeable in the Five Classics and graphs and latitudes. He was promoted as Xiaolian and was a good friend with Yilang Cai Yong. He reported disasters and wrote poems. In the tenth month of the first year of Guanghe, he told Emperor Ling of a solar eclipse on the 30th day, asking officials to prepare, and it happened as he said. In February of the second year of Zhongping, he predicted a disaster in the palace, and that day, a big fire occurred in Nangong. He was promoted to governor of Jiangxia, dismissed due to official business, and died at home at seventy.

Dong Fu, courtesy name Mao'an, from Mianzhu, Guanghan, studied prophecy with Yang Hou at Taixue. He taught at home, with students from far away. He was called up ten times by the Zaifu and three times by public transport, but remained ill. During Emperor Ling's reign, General He Jin recommended him as a servant. Dong Fu privately told Taichang Liu Yan of chaos in the capital and an emperor's spirit in Yizhou. Liu Yan became shepherd of Yizhou, and Dong Fu became captain of the vassal state of Shu County. A year after leaving, the emperor died, and the world was in chaos. Dong Fu died at eighty-two. Later, Liu Bei became emperor in Shu, as Dong Fu said.

Guo Yu, from Luo, Guanghan, studied under Cheng Gao, learning the skills of diagnosing the six subtleties and calculating yin and yang. During Emperor He's reign, he became imperial physician. Once, the emperor asked him to diagnose a favored minister and a woman hiding in a tent. Guo Yu said there was yang on the left and yin on the right, suspecting a strange person. The emperor praised him. Guo Yu was kind-hearted, treating poor servants diligently but sometimes ineffective with nobles. The emperor asked a noble to wear ragged clothes, and the disease was cured with one injection. Guo Yu explained that nobles had four difficulties: self-decision, lack of care, weak joints, and inactivity, making treatment difficult. He died while serving as imperial physician.

Hua Tuo, courtesy name Yuanhua, from Pei State, was proficient in scriptures and knew how to cultivate his nature. He was recommended as Xiaolian but did not take the post. He was skilled in prescriptions and acupuncture, using Mafei San for surgery. He cured a man with a throat disease by having him drink sour water chestnuts, causing him to vomit a snake. He treated a county governor by making him angry, causing him to vomit black blood and recover. He performed a caesarean section on a patient, who died ten years later. He cured Chen Deng of worms, but the disease relapsed after three years when Hua Tuo died. Cao Cao summoned Hua Tuo for his head wind, and acupuncture relieved it. Hua Tuo asked for leave, claiming his wife was sick, but Cao Cao discovered the lie and imprisoned him. Despite Xun Yu's plea, Cao Cao killed Hua Tuo. Before death, Hua Tuo gave a medical book to the jailer, who refused, so he burned it. Wu Pu and Fan A studied under Hua Tuo. Wu Pu practiced the Wu Qin Xi, living over ninety with good health. Fan A knew acupuncture, using deep needles effectively. Hua Tuo gave him Lacquer Leaf Qingnian Powder, and Fan A lived over a hundred years.

Xu Deng, from Fujian, was originally a woman who became a husband, good at witchcraft. Zhao Bing, from Dongyang, could serve Yue Fang. During a military turmoil and epidemic, they met on the Wushang River and shared their magic to cure diseases. Xu Deng banned the stream, stopping water flow; Zhao Bing banned a withered tree, making it sprout. They practiced together, with Zhao Bing treating Xu Deng as teacher. They advocated frugality, using east flowing water for worship and mulberry bark for dried meat. Xu Deng died, and Zhao Bing went eastward to Zhang'an. He used a plane tree trunk to support a pot to cook, surprising the owner but not damaging the house. He crossed a river with an umbrella, passing through turbulent currents. The magistrate of Zhang'an arrested and killed him for sorcery. The people built a temple for him in Yongkang, where no mosquitoes enter.

Fei Changfang, from Runan, served as a city official. An old man selling medicine jumped into a pot, and Changfang saw this. The old man took him into the pot, where there was a jade hall with fine wine. The old man, a god punished for a mistake, asked Changfang to go with him. Changfang feared his family would worry, so the old man cut a bamboo stick, hung it behind the house, and the family thought Changfang had hanged himself. Changfang followed the old man into the mountains, facing tigers and snakes without fear. The old man asked him to eat feces, but Changfang felt disgusted. The old man said he was almost enlightened but failed this test. He gave Changfang a bamboo stick to ride home and a talisman to dominate ghosts and gods. Changfang returned, finding more than ten years had passed. He threw the bamboo stick into a pond, and it turned into a dragon. He cured diseases, drove away ghosts, and interrogated criminals. He once caught a ghost that transformed into an old turtle. He locked up Lord Donghai for three years, causing a drought, then released him to make rain. He caught a fox stealing the land father-in-law's horse. Later, his talisman was lost, and he was killed by ghosts.

Ji Zi Xun, from unknown origin, lived in Wanju, Jiyin during the Jian'an period. He had magical spells. He once hugged a neighbor's baby, let it fall to the ground and die, then buried it. More than a month later, he returned with the baby alive. The parents were panicked, but the baby knew them, and they accepted it. They dug up the grave and found only clothes. Zi Xun's name spread, and scholar-bureaucrats admired him. He drove a donkey cart to Xuxia. Passing through Xingyang, his donkey died and maggots crawled out. He beat it with a stick, and the donkey got up and walked. Thousands of people followed to watch. In the capital, hundreds of people waited for him, including ministers. He later escaped, and no one knew where he went. A centenarian said he saw Zi Xun selling medicine in Kuaiji City as a child, with the same face. Later, someone saw him in Dongba City, Chang'an, rubbing bronze figures with an old man, saying they had been made for nearly five hundred years. The person called him, but he walked slowly, and the horse could not catch up.

Liu Gen, from Yingchuan, lived in seclusion in Songshan Mountain. Some good people came to learn Taoism from him. The prefect Shi Qi thought he was a monster, arrested him, and demanded he show a god or be executed. Liu Gen said he could make people see ghosts. He looked back and let out a roar, and Shi Qi's dead father and ancestors walked forward, kowtowing to Liu Gen and scolding Shi Qi. Shi Qi kowtowed and begged for mercy, but Liu Gen refused and left, unknown to where.

Zuo Ci, courtesy name Yuanfang, from Lujiang, received Shinto at a young age. He was a guest at Cao Cao's place. Cao Cao said they lacked seabass from Wu Songjiang. Zuo Ci fished into a copper plate with water and caught a bass. He caught more fish, all over three feet long. Cao Cao said they lacked ginger from Sichuan. Zuo Ci obtained it and also brought a reply from Cao Cao's envoy. Later, Cao Cao traveled to the suburbs with over a hundred officials. Zuo Ci poured wine for everyone with a liter of wine and a pound of dried meat, and all were drunk and full. Cao Cao investigated and found the taverns missing wine and meat. He imprisoned Zuo Ci, but Zuo Ci fled into the wall. Someone found him in the market, but everyone in the city had the same image. Later, someone encountered him on Yangcheng Mountain and chased him into a flock of sheep. Cao Cao sent someone to say he would not kill him, and an old ram stood up and spoke. The pursuers rushed into the flock, but hundreds of sheep turned into old rams, all speaking, and they could not catch him.
