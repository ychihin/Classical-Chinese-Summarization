**Summary of Historical Biographies of the Liu Clan**

**Qi Wu King Liu Yan (Bosheng)** was the eldest brother of Emperor Guangwu. Resolute and generous, he was angered by Wang Mang's usurpation of the Han dynasty and used his family wealth to befriend heroes, planning to restore the Han. During the chaos of Wang Mang's final years, Bosheng gathered heroes and raised troops with relatives and guests, including Deng Chen in Xinye and Guangwu, Li Tong, and Li Yi in Wan. He mobilized 7,000–8,000 disciples from Chongling, calling them the Zhutiandu tribe. After allying with Xinshi and Pinglin soldiers, they captured several locations and attacked Wan. At Xiaochang'an, they were defeated by Wang Mang's officers Zhen Fu and Liangqiu Ci in dense fog, resulting in the deaths of Bosheng's sister Liu Yuan and brother Liu Zhong. Bosheng regrouped at Jiyang. The enemy forces, with 100,000 elite troops, camped between the Huangchun and Pubi Rivers, removing their bridge to show no retreat. Xinshi and Pinglin soldiers wanted to disperse, but Bosheng convinced 5,000 arriving Xiajiang soldiers to join forces. After a three-day rest, they secretly attacked and captured Lanxiang, seizing enemy baggage. The next morning, the Han army attacked from the southwest and Xiajiang from the southeast, defeating Liangqiu Ci's army. Zhenfu's army fled but was blocked by the Huangchun River; over 20,000 were killed or drowned, and Zhenfu and Liangqiu Ci were beheaded. Bosheng then defeated Yan You and Chen Mao at Yuyang, beheading over 3,000, and besieged Wancheng, naming himself General Zhutian. Wang Mang offered a huge reward for Bosheng's death and had his image painted for archery practice. Over 100,000 people surrendered daily. When generals discussed establishing a Liu clan leader, they favored Bosheng but feared his strictness and preferred Sheng Gong (Gengshi), whom they established. Bosheng warned against premature enthronement, citing Chen Sheng and Xiang Ji as examples, and suggested waiting until after defeating Wang Mang and dealing with Chimei. Most agreed, but General Zhang drew his sword and forced the decision. Sheng Gong was enthroned, and Bosheng was made Grand Situ and Marquis of Han Xin, disappointing many heroes. After attacking Xinye and Bawan, Guangwu defeated Wang Xun and Wang Yi, increasing the brothers' fame. Gengshi and his ministers, feeling uneasy, conspired to kill Bosheng. Despite a warning from his uncle Fan Hong about a possible plot, Bosheng dismissed it. Li Yi, whom Guangwu had warned Bosheng against, and others persuaded Gengshi to arrest and kill Bosheng and his general Liu Ji on the same day. Bosheng's sons were later made kings by Guangwu.

**Beihai Jing Wang Liu Xing** was named King of Lu in the second year of Jianwu, succeeding Guangwu's brother Liu Zhong. His mother, Lady Nanton, was gentle and respected, giving birth to three sons (Bosheng, Zhong, Guangwu) and three daughters (Liu Huang, Liu Yuan, Boji). She died of illness when Bosheng's brothers first raised troops. Liu Xing served as magistrate of Fengshi County, known for wisdom and good governance, later becoming prefect of Hongnong. After four years, he resigned and was recalled to court. In the 27th year of Jianwu, he returned to his state. The next year, he was moved to be King of Beihai. Emperor Xianzong valued him and consulted him on major issues. He died after 39 years of reign.

**Zhao Xiaowang Liu Liang** was Guangwu's uncle. During Emperor Ping's reign, he became magistrate of Xiao County. He cared for the young Guangwu after his father's death. When Guangwu raised troops, Liu Liang was initially furious but later joined the army. At Xiaochang'an, his wife and two sons were killed. Gengshi made him the third elder of the state. After Gengshi's failure, Liu Liang fled to Luoyang upon hearing Guangwu's enthronement. He was made King of Guangyang in the second year of Jianwu, moved to Zhao Wang in the fifth year, demoted to Duke Zhao in the 13th year, and died in the capital in the 17th year after 16 years of reign.

**Chengyang Gong Wang Liu Zhi** was the son of Liu Chang, Marquis of Kang Ling. Liu Chang's ancestor was granted Chongling Marquis. Liu Chang moved to Baishui Township due to low terrain and was praised for his modesty. During Wang Mang's usurpation, Liu Zhi was imprisoned for marrying Zhai Xuan's daughter but was pardoned. When Wang Mang usurped the throne, Liu Zhi's titles were abolished. When Guangwu raised troops, Zhen Fu imprisoned Liu Zhi's family. After the Han defeat at Xiaochang'an, Zhen Fu killed Liu Zhi's mother, brother, and wives. Gengshi made Liu Zhi Taichang General and King of Dingtao. After Gengshi's fall, Liu Zhi fled to Luoyang. Guangwu made him King of Chengyang in the second year of Jianwu. He died at 43, posthumously named Prince Gong.

**Sishui Wang Liu She** was Guangwu's grandson. His son Liu Zhong was close to Guangwu since childhood. Liu Zhong killed Huyang Wei during the Han uprising. Gengshi made Liu She King of Yuan and Liu Zhong his attendant. After Gengshi's failure, they fled to Luoyang. In the second year of Jianwu, Liu She was made King of Sishui, and Liu Zhong King of Zichuan. Liu She died in the 10th year. Liu Zhong died of grief after 20 days of crying. Liu She's brother Liu Mao gathered over 100,000 troops, surrendered to Guangwu, and was made King of Zhongshan, later demoted to Marquis Rang.

**Ancheng Xiaohou Liu Ci** was Guangwu's clan brother. After his brother Liu Xian was killed by officials, Liu Ci and his nephew Liu Xin killed the officials in revenge and fled, later pardoned. He followed Bosheng to attack counties. Gengshi made him Guangluxun and Marquis of Guanghan. After Bosheng's death, he acted as Da Situ. He recommended Guangwu to Gengshi for the Hebei mission. Gengshi made him King Wan and Grand Sima. After Chimei broke Gengshi, Liu Ci went to Luoyang. Guangwu made him Marquis Shen in the second year of Jianwu, later Anchenghou. He was kind and generous, often hosting banquets, and died in the 28th year.

**Chengwu Xiaohou Liu Shun** was Guangwu's clan brother and childhood friend. Gengshi made his father Liu Qing King of Yan and Liu Shun General Huya. After Liu Qing was killed by rebels, Liu Shun visited Guangwu and was made Nanyang Prefect, later Chengwuhou. He defeated Liu'an thieves and became prefect of Liu'an. He died in the 11th year of Jianwu.

**Shunyang Huaihou Liu Jia** was Guangwu's clan brother. He studied "Shang Shu" and "Spring and Autumn" with Bosheng in Chang'an. He followed Gengshi and was made partial general, later Xingdehou and general. He defeated Yancen, who later surrendered. Gengshi made him King of Hanzhong. After Yancen rebelled and defeated Liu Jia, Liu Jia collected scattered troops and fought Yancen again, defeating him. He also defeated Red Eyebrow leader Liao Zhan. After Guangwu's message, Liu Jia met Deng Yu in Yunyang. In the third year of Jianwu, he went to Luoyang and became prefect of Qiancheng. He was made Marquis of Shunyang in the 13th year and died in the 15th year.
