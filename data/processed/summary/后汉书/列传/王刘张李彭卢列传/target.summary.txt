Wang Chang, a prince from Handan in Zhao State, was originally a fortune-teller with knowledge of astronomy. He believed Hebei had the aura of a Son of Heaven. Liu Lin, son of King Mu of Zhao, was a chivalrous man with connections to powerful figures, and Wang Chang befriended him. After Wang Mang usurped the throne and killed a man claiming to be Liu Ziyu, son of Emperor Cheng, Wang Lang falsely claimed to be the true son. He fabricated a story about his mother, a singer who became the emperor's consort, and his escape with a doctor. Liu Lin and others, including Li Yu and Zhang Shen, conspired to support Wang Lang. In December of the first year of Gengshi, they entered Handan, made Wang Lang emperor, and appointed Liu Lin as Prime Minister, Li Yu as Grand Sima, and Zhang Shen as General. They sent proclamations to prefectures, declaring Wang Lang as Liu Ziyu and calling for support. Wang Lang's claims gained traction, and much of northern China submitted to him.

The next year, Guangwu attacked Wang Lang, besieging Julu and then Handan. Wang Lang sent Du Wei to negotiate, claiming to be the true son of Emperor Cheng, but Guangwu refused. After a 20-day siege, Li Li, Wang Lang's Shaofu, rebelled and opened the city gates. Wang Lang fled but was killed. Liu Yong, an eighth-generation grandson of King Xiao of Liang, was enfeoffed after Gengshi's ascension. He raised troops, captured 28 cities, and allied with Dong Xian and Zhang Bu. After Gengshi's failure, Liu Yong proclaimed himself emperor. Guangwu sent Gai Yan to attack Liu Yong. Su Mao, initially serving Gengshi, surrendered to Guangwu but later rebelled and joined Liu Yong. Gai Yan besieged Suiyang, capturing it, but Liu Yong fled. After several battles, Liu Yong was killed, and his son Liu Yu was briefly established as king before being defeated. Dong Xian and Pang Meng also rebelled but were eventually killed. Zhang Bu, from Langfu, gathered troops and declared himself General Wuwei. He allied with Liu Yong and later killed Fulong, Guangwu's envoy. Guangwu attacked Zhang Bu, who was defeated and surrendered, but later fled and was killed.

Li Xian, from Xuchang, declared himself King of Huainan and later emperor. Guangwu attacked and captured Shu County, leading to Li Xian's death. Peng Chong, from Wan County, served as a county official and later fled to Yuyang. He supported Guangwu but became resentful when his rewards were insufficient. He rebelled, attacked Zhufu, and allied with the Xiongnu. He declared himself King of Yan but was killed by his servants. Lu Fang, from Sanshui, falsely claimed to be Liu Wenbo, a descendant of Emperor Wu. He allied with the Xiongnu and declared himself emperor. He fought with Guangwu's forces but eventually surrendered, though he later rebelled again and died among the Xiongnu. The historian comments that the Liu family's legacy endured due to the deep kindness of Gaozu and Xiaowen, and that the rebels like Wang, Liu, Zhang, Li, Peng, and Lu merely took advantage of the situation without true foresight or strategy.
