# Summary of Historical Biographies

**Du Gen** (courtesy name Bojian) was from Dingling County, Yingchuan. His father Du An was known for his integrity, entering Imperial College at thirteen and gaining fame as "Qitong." Du Gen was elected Xiaolian in Yongchu's first year and appointed as a doctor. When Empress Dowager Deng controlled the government, Du Gen believed Emperor An was old enough to rule and wrote a direct remonstrance. The Queen Mother had him beaten nearly to death in the palace. He feigned death for three days until maggots appeared in his eyes, then escaped and worked as a busboy in Yicheng for fifteen years. After Deng's execution, he was recruited as an imperial censor. During Emperor Shun's reign, he became prefect of Jiyin, resigned, and died at seventy-eight.

**Cheng Yishi** (courtesy name Jiming) was studious and familiar with Taoism. During Yanguang period, when Fan Feng and Wang Sheng slandered the crown prince, Cheng wrote defenses but was framed and imprisoned. When the prince became Emperor Shun, Sikong Zhang Hao recommended Cheng as Yilang, but Cheng declined, feeling his contribution was insufficient. He was later conscripted again as Yilang. He was unsmiling in court, and all officials respected him.

**Luan Ba** (courtesy name Shuyuan) was from Huang County, Wei County, and practiced Taoism. During Emperor Shun's reign, he served as a eunuch in Yeting against his wishes. He had a simple, upright temperament and studied Confucian classics. After his yang energy returned, he requested to quit the eunuchship, became a doctor, then governor of Guiyang. He established marriage and funeral rites, built schools, and promoted officials based on ability. After seven years, he resigned due to illness. He later became governor of Yuzhang, where he used Taoist skills to destroy demonic sacrifices and eliminate monsters. When Emperor Shun died, Luan Ba remonstrated against destroying small tombs near the imperial mausoleum. Empress Dowager Liang interrogated him, convicted him, and sent him home. Over twenty years later, Emperor Ling ascended, and Luan Ba was recruited as Yilang. When Chen Fan and Dou Wu were killed, Luan Ba was relegated to Yongchang Prefect. He wrote an extreme remonstrance about their unjust case, and the emperor ordered his arrest. Luan Ba committed suicide.

**Liu Tao** (courtesy name Ziqi) was from Yingyin County, Yingchuan, descendant of Liu Bo, King of Jibei. He was simple and made friends only with like-minded people. When General Liang Ji held power and disasters struck, Liu Tao studied at Taixue and submitted a memorial criticizing the emperor for allowing villains to torture the people, causing heaven to send disasters. He recommended Zhu Mu and Li Ying as capable officials. The emperor did not accept his advice. When officials proposed recoining money, Liu Tao argued that food was more urgent than currency, stating that people could live without money but not without food. The emperor stopped minting money. Liu Tao later became chief of Shunyang, where he recruited former criminals to help catch traitors. He was dismissed due to illness, and the people made a song missing him. He annotated the Shangshu and Chunqiu. When Zhang Jiao rebelled, Liu Tao warned the emperor but was ignored. After Zhang Jiao's rebellion caused chaos, the emperor made Liu Tao Marquis of Zhongling Township and promoted him to Shangshu Ling. Because of his remonstrances, powerful ministers feared him and transferred him to Jing Zhaoyin. He refused to buy his position with money and resigned due to illness. The emperor valued his talents and conscripted him as an admonition official. Liu Tao submitted eight urgent matters blaming eunuchs for the country's chaos. The eunuchs framed him, and he was tortured in prison. He wrote hundreds of thousands of words, including "Qi Yao Lun," "Kuang Laozi," "Anti-Han Fei," and "Reply to Mencius." He died in prison.

**Li Yun** (courtesy name Xingzu) was from Ganling County. He was learned in yin-yang and became magistrate of Baima County. In Emperor Huan's second year of Yanxi, after Liang Ji was executed, Shan Chao and others were made marquises, and a popular woman became queen. Disasters struck, and Li Yun submitted a memorial criticizing the emperor's rewards and the disorder of official positions. The emperor became furious and ordered Li Yun's arrest. Du Zhong and Chen Fan pleaded for him, but Li Yun died in prison.

**Liu Yu** (courtesy name Jiji) was from Guangling, descendant of King Jing of Guangling. He was skilled in astronomy and mathematics. In Yanxi's eighth year, Taiwei Yang Bing recommended him as virtuous and upright. He submitted a memorial criticizing eunuchs for creating offspring to inherit titles, the harem's excess, and the people's suffering. He advised the emperor to open the path of remonstrance. When General Dou Wu failed to kill the eunuchs, Liu Yu was killed.

**Xie Bi** (courtesy name Fuxuan) was from Wuyang County, Dongjun. In Jianning's second year, he was recruited as a doctor. When a green snake appeared and strong winds blew, Xie Bi submitted a secret report blaming the emperor's weakness, the Queen Mother's imprisonment, and the execution of Chen Fan. He recommended recruiting Wang Chang and Li Ying. The emperor's attendants sent him to be prime minister of Guangling. He later resigned. Cao Shao, nephew of eunuch Cao Jie, hated Xie Bi and had him tortured to death in prison. In Chuping's second year, Zhao Qian avenged Xie Bi by arresting and killing Cao Shao.
