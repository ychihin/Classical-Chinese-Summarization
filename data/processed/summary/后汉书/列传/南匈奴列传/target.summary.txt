The southern Xiongnu Kuluo corpse pursued Qian Chanyu, named Bi, grandson of Huhanxie Chanyu and son of Wuzhu Liuruo Qian Chanyu. After Huhanxie, his sons served as Chanyu in order. Bi was appointed as the stone king of Tatar, commanding the southern border and Wuhuan. In the early years of Jianwu, Peng Chong rebelled in Yuyang, and Chanyu allied with him, later making Lu Fang emperor in Wuyuan. Guangwu was busy pacifying the Central Plains and had no time for overseas affairs. In the sixth year of Jianwu, envoys were exchanged between Han and Xiongnu, but the Shanyu was arrogant, comparing himself to Maodun. Despite this, the emperor treated the Xiongnu as usual. However, the Xiongnu, together with Lu Fang, invaded the northern border many times. In the ninth year, an attack by Grand Sima Wu Mo failed, and the Xiongnu grew more powerful. By the thirteenth year, they invaded Hedong, and the border population was moved to Changshan Pass and Juyong Pass. The court added soldiers, built sentry boxes, and set up beacons. The Xiongnu, hearing of a reward for Lu Fang, sent him back to surrender, but Lu Fang claimed credit, and the court did not reward the Xiongnu, angering them. Over the next twenty years, the Xiongnu invaded Shangdang, Fufeng, and Tianshui. In the winter of the 21st year, they invaded Shanggu and Zhongshan, killing and plundering, ending peace in the northern border.

Initially, Shanyu's younger brother Shiguli King Yitu knew he should be King Zuoxian, the successor. But Chanyu wanted to pass the throne to his son, so he killed Zhiya Master, Zhaojun's son. Zhaojun, from Nanjun, was given to Huhanxie during Emperor Yuan's reign. After Huhanxie died, Zhaojun married his successor according to Hu customs. Bi, seeing Zhiya Master killed, complained about the succession order, becoming suspicious and rarely attending court gatherings. The Chanyu sent two Guduhou to monitor Bi. In the 22nd year, Shanyu Yu died, and his son Wu Daqianhou became Shanyu, followed by his brother Punu. Bi, resentful at not becoming Chanyu, and with droughts and locusts devastating the Xiongnu, causing mass death, the Chanyu feared Han attack and sought peace. Bi secretly sent Han Guo Heng to demand submission to Han. The Guduhous discovered Bi's plan, and Bi, frightened, gathered 40,000-50,000 troops to kill them. The Guduhous fled and reported to Shanyu, who sent 10,000 cavalry to attack Bi, but Bi retreated.

In the spring of the 24th year of Jianwu, Bi was established as Huhanxie Chanyu, seeking to be a barrier for Han. The emperor agreed. In winter, Bi proclaimed himself Chanyu. In the 25th year, Bi's brother attacked and captured Beidan Shanyu's brother, defeating Beidan's army. Beidan Shanyu retreated a thousand miles. The emperor built chariots with watchtowers to resist the Huns. People recalled a prophecy that in the ninth generation of Han, Beidi would retreat thousands of miles. The territory opened up, and many defected to Nandanyu, who proclaimed himself a vassal, presenting treasures and asking for envoys. In the 26th year, the emperor sent envoys to Nandanyu. The Chanyu, after hesitation, worshipped the edict and proclaimed his vassalage. The emperor gave him various gifts, including crowns, clothes, gold seals, and weapons, and transported food and cattle to help the southern Xiongnu. Zhonglang was ordered to set up an office to handle lawsuits and investigate news. The court established a routine of annual gifts and exchanges.

According to Xiongnu custom, there were three dragon temples a year for worship. Since Nan Danyu surrendered, he also offered sacrifices to the Han Emperor, convening ministries to discuss state affairs. The most honorable ministers were the Four Corners and Six Corners, all sons and brothers of the Chanyu. Ministers with different surnames had various titles. Shanyu's surname was a virtual conjunction, with four different surnames: Huyan, Xub, Qiulin, and Lan. In the winter of the 26th year, the sons of the five Gudu lords defected to the south, but were captured by Bei Shanyu. Nan Shanyu's resistance was unfavorable. The court ordered Shanyu to move to Meiji, Xihe, and sent Zhonglang Duan Chen to protect him. They also exempted eight border counties from taxes. Nan Danyu set up various princes to guard the border. Bei Shanyu, frightened, returned many Han people. In the 27th year, Bei Shanyu requested peace, but the crown prince advised against it, fearing Nan Shanyu's second thoughts. The emperor agreed. In the 28th year, Bei Shanyu again sent envoys, offering horses and furs, begging for marriage and music. The emperor, following Ban Biao's advice, gave rewards but did not send envoys.

Bi reigned for nine years. After his death, his brother succeeded him. The Han Dynasty established a routine of condolences and rewards for each Shanyu's death. Over the years, various Shanyu succeeded each other, with conflicts between the northern and southern Xiongnu. The Northern Xiongnu, weakened by internal strife and attacks from Xianbei and others, eventually collapsed. The Southern Xiongnu, under various Shanyu, sometimes rebelled and sometimes surrendered, causing ongoing border troubles. The Han Dynasty sent generals to attack and defend, with mixed results. Eventually, the Xiongnu split into northern and southern courts, with the southern court serving as a vassal to Han. The Han Dynasty established the Du Liao military camp to guard against both. Despite occasional rebellions, the southern Xiongnu remained largely submissive, while the northern Xiongnu gradually weakened and disappeared. The Han Dynasty's policy of using barbarians to attack barbarians proved effective, but also led to ongoing conflicts and instability.
