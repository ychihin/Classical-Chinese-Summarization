Yang Zhen, styled Boqi, was from Hongnong Huayin. His eighth-generation ancestor Yang Hou was granted the title of Marquis of Chiquan under Emperor Gaozu of Han. His father Yang Bao studied Ouyang Shangshu, lived in seclusion during the reigns of Emperors Ai and Ping, and refused official appointments, disappearing. Emperor Guangwu admired his integrity but he died at home due to old age and illness.

Yang Zhen loved learning, studied Ouyang Shangshu under Taichang Huanyu, and became a renowned scholar, called "Confucius of Kansai." He lived in Hucheng, declining official invitations for decades. At age fifty, he became a state and county official after a crested sparrow brought three fish, interpreted as a sign of promotion. General Deng Zhi recommended him, and he rose to Governor of Jingzhou and Donglai. Passing through Changyi, Wang Mi, a former subordinate, offered him ten pounds of gold at night, saying no one would know. Yang Zhen replied, "Heaven knows, earth knows, I know, you know—how can you say no one knows?" Wang Mi left in shame. As Prefect of Zhuo County, he was honest and refused personal requests, leaving his descendants a legacy of being "descendants of an innocent official."

In the fourth year of Yuanchu, he became a servant and Taichang, reforming doctorate elections by recommending worthy scholars. In Yongning's first year, he became Situ. When Empress Dowager Deng died, Emperor An's nurse Wang Sheng and her daughter Bo Rong became corrupt. Yang Zhen memorialized, citing historical examples to warn against women and villains in politics, urging their removal. Emperor An showed the memorial to Wang Sheng, who held a grudge. Bo Rong's arrogance grew; she married Liu Gui, who took a title. Yang Zhen protested this breach of protocol, but the emperor ignored him.

In Yanguang's second year, Yang Zhen became Taiwei. He refused to recommend Li Gui's brother at Geng Bao's request and rejected Yan Xian's recommendation of relatives, causing resentment. When an edict ordered building a house for the queen mother, Yang Zhen criticized the extravagance during disasters and border unrest, but Fan Feng and others continued unchecked. After an earthquake, Yang Zhen attributed it to excessive yin from corrupt officials, urging the emperor to abandon the arrogant. The emperor was displeased, and Fan Feng resented him but dared not harm him due to his reputation.

When Zhao Teng was arrested for criticizing the government, Yang Zhen pleaded for leniency, but the emperor ignored him, and Zhao Teng was killed. In the third year, Fan Feng framed Yang Zhen for resentment after Zhao Teng's death. The emperor stripped Yang Zhen of his position and ordered him home. At Jiyang Pavilion, Yang Zhen declared he could not face the world with traitors unpunished, took poison, and died at seventy. His coffin was exposed by the roadside. After Emperor Shun ascended, Fan Feng was executed. Yang Zhen's students petitioned, and the emperor posthumously honored him, granting titles and money, and buried him with ceremony. A large bird mourned at his funeral, and a stone bird was erected in his memory. His son Yang Mu became Prime Minister of Fubo.
