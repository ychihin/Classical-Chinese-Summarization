The text summarizes the moral and virtuous deeds of women from historical records, compiled into "Lienv Pian" since the ZTE period. It includes biographies of notable women, emphasizing their conduct and contributions.

Bao Xuan's wife, Shaojun, married him despite his poverty, returned her dowry, and lived frugally, earning praise for her virtue. Wang Ba's wife encouraged him to maintain his integrity over worldly honors, leading him to a life of seclusion. Jiang Shi's wife fetched river water for her mother-in-law daily, and when sent away, secretly provided food through a neighbor. Her filial piety moved bandits to spare her family, and she was later honored.

Zhou Yu's wife, Zhao A, committed suicide after her father-in-law blamed her for her husband's rudeness, unable to correct him. Ban Zhao, a learned widow, completed the "Book of Han" and advised Empress Dowager Deng on political matters, including accepting General Deng Zhi's retirement. She wrote "Female Commandments" and died at seventy, mourned by the empress dowager.

Leyangzi's wife urged her husband to return found gold and complete his studies, cutting cloth to illustrate wasted effort. She supported her mother-in-law and died defending her honor from robbers. Cheng Wenju's wife, Mu Jiang, treated her stepchildren kindly, leading them to reform and become virtuous. Cao E, a filial daughter, drowned seeking her father's body and was honored with a monument.

Wu Xusheng's wife, Lu Rong, supported her mother-in-law, encouraged her husband to study, and avenged his death. She died resisting bandits. Yuan Wei's wife, Ma Lun, defended her family's wealth and her father's reputation, matching her husband's wit. Pang Ao's mother, Zhao E, avenged her father's murder after years, surrendered, and was pardoned.

Liu Changqing's wife mutilated her ear to avoid remarriage after her son's death, earning recognition for chastity. Huangfu Gui's wife cursed Dong Zhuo for his atrocities and was beaten to death, later honored. Yin Yu's wife, Xun Cai, resisted remarriage and hanged herself after being forced to Guo's house. Shengdao's wife, Yuanjiang, took her husband's place in prison, allowing his escape, and was executed.

The filial daughter Uncle Xianxiong drowned herself after her father's death, emerging with his body. Dong Si's wife, Cai Wenji, was captured by Xiongnu, bore children, and was ransomed by Cao Cao. She saved her husband from execution and recited ancient texts from memory. Her poems recount her suffering and loss. The text praises these women's virtues and deeds.
