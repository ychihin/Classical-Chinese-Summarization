# Summary of Historical Records on the Southern and Southwestern Barbarians

During the reign of Gao Xin, the Quan Rong invaded, and the emperor offered rewards for the head of their general, General Wu. The emperor's five-colored dog, Panhu, brought the general's head. Unable to marry his daughter to a dog, the emperor was troubled, but his daughter insisted on honoring the promise and married Panhu. They lived in Nanshan, where she bore twelve children, six boys and six girls. After Panhu's death, the children intermarried, and their descendants became known as barbarians. They wore colorful clothes with tail-like shapes, lived in remote mountains, and were granted famous mountains and swamps by the emperor. Their leaders received seals from the imperial court, and they were exempt from taxes. The current Changsha Wulingman are their descendants.

During the Zhou Dynasty, the barbarian race grew stronger. King Xuan sent Uncle Fang to conquer them. King Ping moved east, and barbarians invaded the Central Plains. Marquis Wen of Jin defeated them. King Wu of Chu faced barbarian attacks, and King Zhuang later subdued them. After the Han Dynasty's rise, Wuling County was established, and barbarian adults paid one piece of cloth annually, children two feet. During the Jianwu period, Jingfu Xiang Shancheng led a massive attack. General Liu Shang was defeated with his entire army. Ma Yuan later defeated Xiang Shancheng, who surrendered. In subsequent years, various barbarian groups rebelled, including Chen Cong, Qin Erjian, and Tan Rong. The court often recruited obedient barbarians to suppress rebellions. In Yonghe's first year, the prefect of Wuling proposed increasing taxes like those of Han people. Shangshu Ling Yu Xie Jin warned against this, but the emperor did not listen. The barbarians rebelled, killing officials. Li Jin was sent to pacify them, serving nine years in Wuling.

During Emperor Huan's reign, Wuling Man Zhanshan rebelled, and Changsha barbarians looted counties with over 10,000 people. Wuling barbarians invaded Jiangling, causing officials to flee. Du Shang and Feng Yu were sent to pacify them. In Emperor Ling's third year, Wuling barbarians rebelled again. The Book of Rites describes southern barbarians as having tattooed foreheads and mixed bathing customs. In Kanren country, firstborn children were eaten, with the king receiving portions. Yueshang Kingdom sent white pheasants via elephant during Zhou Gong's regency. After Zhou's decline, Chu made Baiyue pay tribute. Qin established Nanhai, Guilin, and Xiangjun. Han conquered Nanyue in Yuanding's fifth year, establishing nine counties. Zhuya and Dan'er counties were on an island where chiefs pierced their ears to hang objects. After repeated rebellions, Zhuya was abolished in Yuan's third year.

Wang Mang received rhino tributes. Guangwu中兴时期, Xiguang and Ren Yan taught farming and established marriage rites in Jiaozhi. In Jianwu's twelfth year, Zhang You submitted. In the sixteenth year, Zheng Bian rebelled, capturing sixty-five cities. Ma Yuan defeated her in the eighteenth year. During Emperor Suzong's Yuanhe period, barbarian chiefs presented rhinos and pheasants. In Yongyuan's twelfth year, Rinan barbarians attacked, and the court established military posts. In Yongchu's first year, Yelang barbarians surrendered territory. During Emperor Shun's Yongjian period, King Ye Tiao paid tribute. In Yonghe's second year, Lian Lian attacked Xianglin. Li Gu advised against sending troops from distant provinces, recommending local officials instead. Zhu Liang and Zhang Qiao were appointed, pacifying the region. During Emperor Huan's reign, Zhu Da rebelled, and Xia Fang pacified them. In Emperor Ling's third year, Gu Yong recruited 100,000 Wuyou people.

The Bajunnanjunman originated from Wuluo Zhongli Mountain, with five surnames. Ba's son Wu Xiang became king by hitting a stone cave with a sword and floating a clay boat. He married a salt goddess but later shot her. After death, his soul became a white tiger. King Hui of Qin made Ba leaders marry Qin women. During Han, Lei Qian rebelled, and Liu Shang moved 7,000 Meishan barbarians to Jiangxia. In Yongyuan's thirteenth year, Xu Sheng rebelled over taxes and was defeated. Banmei barbarians originated when a Yi man shot a white tiger for King Zhao of Qin. Emperor Gaozu mobilized them to attack the Three Qins, creating the Bayu Dance. During Emperor Huan's reign, they rebelled multiple times. In Guanghe's second year, they rebelled again. Cheng Bao advised that oppressive officials caused the rebellion, and the emperor sent Cao Qian to pardon them.

The Yelang Kingdom originated from a woman finding a baby in bamboo. Emperor Wu established Yangke County. The King of Dian descended from Zhuang Qi. During Wang Mang's chaos, Dongcan rebelled. Wen Qi resisted Gongsun Shu and later submitted to Guangwu. In Jianwu's eighteenth year, Dongcan rebelled again, and Liu Shang pacified them. During Emperor Ling's Xiping period, Yi tribes rebelled, capturing the prefect. Li Yi and Pangzhi defeated them. The Ailaoyi originated from Sha Yi, who gave birth to ten sons after touching a submerged tree. In Jianwu's twenty-third year, King Xianli attacked Lu Dad but was defeated by storms. In the twenty-seventh year, Xianli surrendered. In Yong's twelfth year, King Liu Maung submitted with 553,711 people. Yongchang County was established. During Emperor Huan's reign, King Leilao rebelled and was killed. In Yongyuan's sixth year, King Mo Yan presented rhinos and elephants. In Yongning's first year, King Yongyou Diao presented musicians who performed illusions.

The Qiongdu Yi were developed by Emperor Wu. During Wang Mang's reign, Changgui killed the prefect and later submitted to Gongsun Shu. Guangwu made him king, but Liu Shang killed him in Jianwu's nineteenth year. During Emperor An's reign, eight Yi tribes submitted. In Yuan's fifth year, Feng Li rebelled, and Zhang Qiao sent Yang Song to defeat him. The Yi capital was developed by Emperor Wu. During Yongping, Zhu Fu pacified over 100 countries including Bailang, with over 6 million people submitting. They presented three poems. During Emperor Ling's reign, the vassal state of Shu was changed to Hanjia. Ran Chiyi was developed by Emperor Wu as Wenshan County. The climate was cold, and Yi people worked in Shu during winter. They built stone houses called "Qiong Cong." The land produced wheat, livestock, and medicinal materials. Zhiwuya was developed in Yuangu's sixth year as Wudu County. The Di people were brave and greedy. During Yuanfeng's third year, they rebelled and were moved to Jiuquan. During Jianwu, the Di people surrendered to Han after Gongsun Shu's defeat.

The argument states that expeditions against barbarians generally succeeded within a year, opening lands of diverse races. Education reached all areas where the sun rises and sets. Although barbarians surrendered and rebelled inconsistently, those with various customs submitted to the Central Plains. Their names were recorded in official offices, and their population numbered in the millions. Precious objects from mountains and seas decorated palace curtains. Tributes of rare birds and beasts filled the imperial treasury. Barbarian songs and dances were performed outside the palace gate. The barbarians lived in mountainous areas connected to Jingzhou and Jiaozhou, distributed outside Bajun and Yong. Their ferocity was slightly weaker than Qiang and Di, so their plundering could not go far. Southwestern barbarians were especially weak. Yongchang garrison was established to guard against distant barbarians, later opening land and becoming a city. The eulogy notes that various barbarians spread across four borders, living in deep mountains, with some in Shu County border areas. Once they gained trust through teaching, they changed customs and brought treasures, leading to Yongchang County's establishment and their inclusion in household registers.
