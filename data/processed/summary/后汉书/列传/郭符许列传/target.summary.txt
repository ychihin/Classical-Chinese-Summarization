Guo Taichuan, also known as Lin Zong, was born into a poor family in Jiexiu, Taiyuan. After his father's early death, he refused his mother's suggestion to work in the county, instead studying under Chenggao Qu Boyan. He became well-versed in many books, eloquent, and renowned for his loud voice. While traveling in Luoyang, he befriended Li Ying of Henan, gaining fame in the capital. Returning home, he crossed the river alone with Li Ying, impressing onlookers. Despite recruitment offers from officials like Situ Huang Qiong, he declined, citing his observations of heaven and society. He was known for his tall, majestic appearance and his habit of folding a corner of his towel, which others imitated as "Lin Zongjin." Fan Pang praised him as a man who could not be treated as a subject or friend. After his mother's death, he was known for extreme filial piety. During the party disaster, he avoided harm by refraining from passionate discussions, teaching behind closed doors with thousands of students. In the first year of Jianning, he mourned the deaths of Chen Fan and Dou Wu, predicting the country's danger. He died at home at forty-two, with over a thousand mourners. Cai Yong wrote his epitaph, feeling no shame in doing so.

Guo Linzong was skilled in identifying and rewarding scholars. He advised Zuo Yuan, a disgraced county student, to reform, citing historical examples. Zuo Yuan later attempted revenge but left out of shame. He recognized Mao Rong's filial piety, persuading him to study and become virtuous. He advised Meng Min, who ignored a broken steamer, to study abroad, leading to his fame. He promoted Yucheng from a servant to a respected scholar. He reformed Song Guo, a vengeful man, into an upright official. He accepted Jia Shu, a sinister man, after his mother's death, leading to Jia's transformation. He predicted Shi Shubin's loss of reputation due to flattery. He warned Huang Yun about losing gains without truth, which came true when Huang dismissed his wife. He noted Xie Zhen and Bian Rang's talents but lack of Taoism, leading to their downfalls. He correctly predicted Wang Rou and Wang Ze's career paths. He also discovered talents among commoners, such as Zhang Xiaozhong and Fan Tezu.

Fulong, from Chenliu, abandoned his post as a capital official and studied under Li Ying. He introduced Guo Linzong to Li Ying, making him famous. Fulong exposed the false reputations of Wenjing and Huang Ziai, causing their decline. He refused official posts and buried his wife simply. Tian Sheng, his close friend, was also skilled in understanding people.

Xu Shao, from Runan, was known for moral responsibility and commenting on people. He influenced Yuan Shao to reduce his luxury. He avoided visiting Chen Chen and Chen Fan, citing their flaws. He famously called Cao Cao a "traitor in peace, hero in trouble." He refused to visit his uncle Xu Xiang due to his flattery of eunuchs. He and his brother Xu Jing held monthly critiques in Runan. He declined official posts, fleeing to Guangling and later to Qu'a to join Liu Yao. He died in Yuzhang at forty-six. His brother Xu Qian was also famous, known as the "two dragons of Pingyu."
