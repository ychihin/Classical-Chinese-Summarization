**Summary of Historical Biographies**

**Zhang Zongchuan**  
Zhang Zong, from Luyang, served as an assistant during Wang Mang’s reign. After Wang Mang’s fall, he led 300–400 people to seize territory and was appointed a partial general by Emperor Gengshi. Seeing political chaos, he moved his family to Anyi. Later, he joined Deng Yu, who recommended him as a partial general. When the Red Eyebrow Army approached, Deng Yu wanted to retreat, but Zhang Zong refused to draw lots for rearguard duty, declaring he would not abandon his post. He defended his camp, and Deng Yu sent reinforcements. Zhang Zong fought bravely, driving back the Red Eyebrows. He later attacked them at night in Chang'an and was wounded. Under Guangwu, he became a captain, fought in Guanzhong, and was promoted. He suppressed bandits in Yingchuan, Qingzhou, and Jizhou, and later in Langyi and Beibu. As Langfu Prime Minister, he governed harshly. He died in office in the second year of Yongping.

**Faxiong**  
Faxiong, from Fufeng, was a descendant of King Qi Xiang. After Qin destroyed Qi, his family used the surname Fa. He served as a meritorious officer and later as chief of Ping clan, known for exposing criminals. In the third year of Yongchu, pirate Zhang Bolu led 3,000 rebels, invading nine counties. Faxiong was promoted to Qingzhou governor to join Wang Zong in attacking them. After initial victories, a pardon arrived. Faxiong advised against immediate attack, suggesting withdrawal to lure the rebels into surrender. Wang Zong agreed, but Donglai soldiers kept their armor, causing rebels to flee to Liaodong. In spring of the fifth year, rebels returned, and Faxiong defeated them. He later became Nanjun prefect, where he reduced tiger attacks by ordering the destruction of traps and halting hunting. He died in office in the early Yuan Dynasty.

**Teng Fu**  
Teng Fu, from Beihai, served as Zhuo County magistrate, known for civil and military talents. In the first year of Jiankang, rebels Fan Rong and Zhou Sheng rose in Jiujiang, and Xu Feng and Ma Mian rebelled in Yinling. The court sent Teng Fu as Jiujiang captain with General Zhao Xu to suppress them. They defeated the rebels, beheading 1,500, including Ma Mian and Fan Rong. Xu Feng was killed by Xie An. Teng Fu became Zhonglang General, overseeing Yangzhou and Xuzhou. He defeated Zhang Ying and later Hua Meng, pacifying the southeast. He was appointed Zuo Fengyi but was deposed by eunuchs due to his upright character. He died at home.

**Feng Kui**  
Feng Kui, from Ma County, studied *Spring and Autumn* and *Sima's Art of War*. His father Feng Huan was falsely accused and imprisoned. Feng Kui suspected the edict was forged and urged his father to appeal, revealing the truth. Feng Kui became famous for his charity. He served as Guanghan captain and censor, later defeating bandits with Teng Fu. As Longxi prefect, he pacified barbarians. He became Jing Zhaoyin and Sili Xiaowei, known for strictness. Later, as Tingwei and Taichang, he led 100,000 troops to suppress barbarian revolts in Changsha and Wuling, beheading 4,000 and surrendering 100,000. He refused rewards and retired, but was recalled. He was dismissed after bandits recurred but later reinstated as Henan Yin and Tingwei. He died in office.

**Du Shang**  
Du Shang, from Shanyin, rose from poverty to become Yu chief, known for strictness. As Wen'an magistrate, he relieved famine. In the fifth year of Yanxi, bandits from Changsha and Lingling invaded several counties. Du Shang was appointed Jingzhou governor, personally leading troops and offering rewards. He defeated the bandits, pursuing them to Nanhai. To motivate his soldiers, he burned his own treasures, then led a surprise attack, destroying the bandits' nest. He was made Marquis of Youxiang and Guiyang prefect. Later, when rebels rose again, he was sent as Zhonglang General, defeating them and beheading 3,500. He became Jingzhou governor again but falsely accused Zhang Pan. After a trial, he was pardoned for prior merits. He later served as Liaodong governor, defeating the Xianbei. He died in office at age fifty.

**Yang Xuan**  
Yang Xuan, from Kuaiji, was a descendant of Yang Mao. His brother Yang Qiao refused to marry a princess and starved to death. Yang Xuan became Lingling prefect during Emperor Ling's reign. When thieves attacked, he used lime-filled carts and burning cloth to blind and scatter them, defeating the rebels. Governor Zhao Kai falsely accused him, but Yang Xuan wrote a blood memorial to the court, clearing his name. He was promoted to Yilang, and Zhao Kai was punished. He later became Bohai prefect but was dismissed. He retired due to illness and died at home.
