# Summary of Historical Biographies

## Biography of Yuan An

Yuan An, courtesy name Shaogong, was from Ruyang County in Runan. He inherited his grandfather Yuan Liang's knowledge of the *Book of Changes*. A dignified and authoritative man respected by his state, he began his career as a county magistrate. When a superior asked him to deliver a private letter, Yuan An refused, stating that official business should use official channels and private matters should not seek merit through him. He was later recommended as Xiaolian and served as magistrate of Yinping and Rencheng counties, where officials and people respected him.

In the twelfth year of Yongping, when King Liu Ying of Chu conspired to rebel, Yuan An was appointed prefect of Chu County to handle the case. Thousands were imprisoned due to confessions, and investigators tortured many to death. Yuan An went directly to the prison, tried those without clear evidence, and reported them for release. Despite officials arguing that associates of Liu Ying were equally guilty, Yuan An took full responsibility. The emperor agreed, and over 400 families were freed.

Yuan An later served as Henan Yin for ten years, maintaining strict but fair governance with no bribery cases. He believed officials should aspire to be prime ministers or prefects, not imprison talented people. He was promoted to imperial servant, then to Sikong and later Situ. When Emperor He ascended as a child, Empress Dowager Dou ruled. Her brother Dou Xian attacked the Xiongnu without provocation. Yuan An and others repeatedly advised against this costly policy, but their memorials were withheld. Yuan An also impeached Dou Jing for illegally mobilizing border troops. When Dou Xian wanted to appoint a surrendered Xiongnu king as Northern Chanyu, Yuan An argued against it, warning it would empty the treasury and break faith with the Southern Chanyu. Dou Xian proceeded anyway, but the appointee soon rebelled as Yuan An predicted. Yuan An died in the spring of four years, deeply mourned by court officials.

## Biography of Zhang Youfu

Zhang Youfu, courtesy name Menghou, was from Xiyang, Runan, a descendant of Zhao King Zhang Ao. He studied *Shangshu* from his grandfather and later served Taichang Huanrong, gathering hundreds of followers. During Yongping, he taught *Shangshu* at the imperial school and lectured before the emperor, who appointed him as a minister and tutor to the crown prince. A simple, upright man who strictly adhered to the classics, he was promoted to governor of Dongjun under Suzong. Though a Confucian scholar, he had a strong character and fought against powerful men. He promoted Wang Qing, whose family had shown three generations of loyalty, to a high position.

When the emperor toured Dongjun, he invited Zhang Youfu and his disciples to lecture on *Shangshu*. After fifteen years of service, Zhang Youfu became prefect of Wei County. When Dou Jing sought revenge against Zheng Ju through Zhang Youfu, Zhang Youfu imprisoned Dou Jing's messenger instead. Later, as Henan Yin, he investigated Dou Jing's men who had injured city soldiers and exiled them. After Dou Xian's fall, Zhang Youfu argued that Dou's crimes should be publicly examined rather than secretly punished. He was promoted to Taiwei but repeatedly requested retirement due to illness. The emperor refused, sending gifts and urging him to remain in service.

Zhang Youfu's father lived in the countryside, and Zhang Youfu visited him whenever his position changed. After his father's death, Zhang Youfu was dismissed for scolding an official in court. He returned home, closed his doors, and refused visitors. Later reinstated as Guang Luxun and then Situ, he died soon after. Before death, he instructed his son to bury him simply without an ancestral hall, using only a straw shelter for offerings.

## Biography of Han Leng

Han Leng, courtesy name Bo Shi, was from Wuyang, Yingchuan, a descendant of Marquis Xiugui. Orphaned at four, he was known for filial piety and gave away his father's wealth to cousins. As a county official, he secretly handled affairs for a paralyzed prefect for two years. Though imprisoned for this, Emperor Xianzong pardoned him. He was promoted to Shangshu Ling and was known for his talent. Emperor Suzong once gave swords inscribed with names to three ministers, including Han Leng, whose sword was inscribed "Longyuan" for his profound strategies.

When Dou Xian assassinated a marquis, Han Leng insisted the thieves were in the capital, not elsewhere. Though the Empress Dowager was angry, the truth proved him correct. When officials wanted to bow to Dou Xian and shout "long live," Han Leng sternly rebuked them. After the Dou family fell, Han Leng investigated their accomplices tirelessly, and the emperor rewarded him with cloth. He became governor of Nanyang, where he exposed villains and maintained strict fairness. Later recalled as a servant, he served as Sikong but died the following year.

## Biography of Zhou Rong

Zhou Rong, courtesy name Pingsun, was from Shu, Lujiang. During Suzong's reign, he served as an official in Situ Yuan An's house. Yuan An valued him highly, and Zhou Rong drafted Yuan An's arguments against Dou Xian regarding the Northern Chanyu. When Dou Xian's guest threatened Zhou Rong with assassins, Zhou Rong declared he was willing to be harmed to awaken the court. After the Dou family fell, Zhou Rong became famous and rose to Shangshu Ling. As governor of Yingchuan, he violated the law but was demoted rather than imprisoned due to his loyalty. He later served as governor of Shanyang with praise, then retired due to illness and died at home. His son Zhou Xing was recommended for his literary talent and served as Shangshu Lang before dying.
