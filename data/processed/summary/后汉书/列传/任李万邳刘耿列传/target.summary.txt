Ren Guang, from Wan County, was loyal in his youth. When Han soldiers arrived, they tried to kill him for his clothes, but Liu Ci rescued him. Ren Guang then led his followers to serve Liu Ci. When Wang Lang rebelled, all prefectures surrendered except Xindu, where Ren Guang, Li Zhong, Wan Xiu, and others held firm. Ren Guang beheaded Wang Lang's messenger and sent elite troops to defend the city. When Shizu (Emperor Guangwu) arrived from Ji, Ren Guang and his officials welcomed him. Shizu wanted to go to Chengtou Zilu and Lizidu for soldiers, but Ren Guang advised against it, suggesting they recruit troops by plundering neighboring counties. Shizu agreed, made Ren Guang General of the Left, and Marquis of Wucheng. Ren Guang wrote propaganda claiming Shizu had millions of soldiers, causing many to surrender. After capturing Handan, Shizu sent Ren Guang back to his county. Ren Guang was later made Marquis of Aling and died in the fifth year of Jianwu.

Li Zhong, from Huang County, was known for his good manners. He and Ren Guang jointly served Shizu, becoming a general and marquis. Shizu praised Li Zhong for not plundering. When Li Zhong's mother and wife were captured by Wang Lang, Shizu offered a reward for their rescue, but Li Zhong refused to care for his family. Ren Guang's attempt to rescue them failed, but Gengshi's generals later conquered Xindu, saving Li Zhong's family. Li Zhong then returned to Xindu and killed hundreds of Wang Lang's supporters. He later became the prefect of Danyang, where he established schools and promoted agriculture, attracting over 50,000 refugees. He was later moved to Yuzhang and died in the 19th year of Jianwu.

Wan Xiu, from Maoling, guarded Xindu with Ren Guang and Li Zhong, welcoming Shizu. He was made a general and marquis. He followed Shizu to pacify Hebei and was later made Marquis of Huaili. He died of illness while attacking Nanyang. His descendants inherited his title, but the fief was eventually abolished and later re-granted to his descendants by Empress Dowager Deng and Emperor Huan.

Pi Tong, from Xindu, initially served Wang Mang. When Shizu reached Xiaquyang, Pi Tong surrendered the city. When Wang Lang rose up, Pi Tong heard Shizu was going to Xindu and sent horses to meet him. When Shizu's subordinates suggested returning to Chang'an, Pi Tong argued against it, praising the Han dynasty's popularity and Wang Lang's weakness. Shizu agreed and made Pi Tong a general. Pi Tong later led troops to defeat bandits and often fought alongside Shizu. When Wang Lang captured Xindu, Pi Tong's family was imprisoned, but he refused to surrender, saying he must not care for his family. His family was spared when Gengshi's generals captured Xindu. Pi Tong was made a marquis and held various official positions, including Taichang and Shaofu. He died, and his title passed to his descendants, though the fief was later abolished and re-granted.

Liu Zhi, from Julu, led clan guests to occupy Changcheng. He opened the city to welcome Shizu and was made a general. He persuaded Liu Yang, the King of Zhending, to surrender. He followed Shizu to capture Handan and pacify Hebei. He was made Marquis of Changcheng and died in battle. His brother Liu happy and Liu Xin succeeded him in command and were also made marquises.

Geng Chun, from Julu, studied in Chang'an. He advised Li Yi against arrogance, and Li Yi made him a cavalry captain. When Shizu arrived in Handan, Geng Chun went to see him. When Wang Lang rebelled, Geng Chun led over 2,000 clan guests to welcome Shizu. He was made a general and marquis. To prevent his clan from having second thoughts, he burned their houses. He defeated Li Yun and followed Shizu to pacify Handan. When attacked at night, he held firm and sent a death squad to attack the thieves from behind. Shizu later made him Marquis of Gaoyang. He was secretly ordered to arrest Liu Yang, the King of Zhending, and killed Liu Yang and his brothers. He later requested to govern a county and became the prefect of Dongjun, pacifying the region. He was later made Marquis of Dongguang. When bandits arose in Dongjun, he was sent to help, and over 9,000 thieves surrendered to him. He died in office in the 13th year of Jianwu.
