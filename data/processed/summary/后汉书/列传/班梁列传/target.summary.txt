Ban Chao, courtesy name Zhong Sheng, was from Pingling County, Fufeng, and the youngest son of Ban Biao. Ambitious and diligent, he supported his mother by copying documents. In the fifth year of Yongping, he followed his brother Ban Gu to Luoyang. Dissatisfied with clerical work, he aspired to emulate Fu Jiezi and Zhang Qian in foreign exploits. A fortune-teller predicted he would become a marquis. Emperor Xianzong appointed him Lantai Lingshi, but he was later dismissed.

In the 16th year of Yongping, Ban Chao served as a fake Sima under Dou Gu, attacking the Xiongnu at Puleihai. Dou Gu then sent him as an envoy to the Western Regions. In Shanshan, Ban Chao deduced that the Huns had arrived when King Guang's hospitality waned. He interrogated a servant, confirmed his suspicion, and gathered his 36 men. At night, he led a fire attack on the Hun camp, killing the envoy and over 30 followers, with 100 burned to death. King Guang, impressed, sent his son as a hostage. Ban Chao reported to Dou Gu, who recommended him to Emperor Ming. Appointed Army Sima, Ban Chao refused extra troops, retaining only his original 36 men.

In Yutian, Ban Chao killed a wizard who demanded his horse, shocking King Guangde into submitting. In Shule, he captured the usurper Douti, installed King Zhong, and released Douti to show prestige. After Emperor Ming's death, Ban Chao faced isolation but held Shule with minimal forces. Recalled by Emperor Suzong, he was persuaded by the people to stay, defeating rebels and stabilizing Shule. In the third year of Jianchu, he led 10,000 troops to capture Gumo, then petitioned for reinforcements to conquer Qiuci, proposing to use local forces and resources. The emperor approved, sending Xu Qian with 1,000 men. Ban Chao defeated Fan Chen, and later, despite slander from Li Yi, he remained loyal, leaving his wife to prove his commitment.

Ban Chao conquered Shadong, killed the treacherous King Zhong, and opened the southern route. In a major battle, he feigned retreat, then attacked Shadong at dawn, killing 5,000 and securing surrender. Yuezhi attacked but were repelled due to supply issues. By the third year of Yongyuan, Qiuci, Gumo, and Wensu submitted. Ban Chao became Protector General, stationed in Tagancheng. In the sixth year, he led 70,000 troops to attack Yanqi, capturing and executing King Guang and others, pacifying over 50 states. He was granted the title Marquis of Dingyuan.

Aged and homesick, Ban Chao petitioned to return, citing his long service. His sister Ban Zhao also pleaded. In the 14th year of Yongyuan, he returned to Luoyang, dying at 71. His son Ban Xiong inherited his title. Ban Chao's advice to successor Ren Shang—to be lenient—was ignored, leading to rebellion.

Ban Yong, Ban Chao's son, served as an army commander. In the first year of Yongchu, he helped withdraw Han forces from the Western Regions. Later, he advised against a costly campaign, recommending a modest garrison in Dunhuang. His strategy was adopted, stabilizing the region. In the second year of Yanguang, he led 500 soldiers to Liuzhong, securing Shanshan and Qiuci's submission. He defeated the Xiongnu and pacified Cheshi. In Yongjian's first year, he attacked Yanqi but was imprisoned for missing a rendezvous, later dying at home.

Liang Jin, a brave officer, served as Deputy Captain of the Western Regions. During a rebellion, he defended Qiuci with King Baiba, defeating tens of thousands of attackers. After the Western Regions were abandoned, he fought Qiang rebels, stabilizing Hexi. He later campaigned against the Xiongnu, accepting Chanyu's surrender. Appointed General Who Conquers the Liao, he died of illness while suppressing Qiang rebels.
