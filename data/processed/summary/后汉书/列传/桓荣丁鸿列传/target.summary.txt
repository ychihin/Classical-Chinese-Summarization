Huan Rong, courtesy name Chunqing, was a native of Longkang, Pei County. He studied the "Ouyang Shangshu" under Zhu Pu in Chang'an, supporting himself through servant labor. He did not return home for fifteen years, only returning after Wang Mang's usurpation. After Zhu Pu's death, Huan Rong built his grave and stayed to teach, amassing hundreds of disciples. During the chaos following Wang Mang's fall, he fled into the valleys with his students, continuing to lecture despite hunger. Later, he taught in the Jianghuai region.

In the 19th year of Jianwu, over sixty, Huan Rong was appointed Grand Situ. When Xianzong became crown prince, Huan Rong's student He Tang was chosen to teach him the "Book of Documents." The emperor summoned Huan Rong, was impressed by his explanation of "Shang Shu," and appointed him Yilang, allowing him to teach the prince. Huan Rong declined a promotion to Dr. Ouyang, recommending his disciples Peng Hong and Gao Hong instead. He was later made a doctor. The emperor praised his gentle, erudite manner and rewarded him. Huan Rong served as the prince's young tutor, and after the prince's studies were complete, he requested retirement but was persuaded to stay. He was later appointed Taichang. When Xianzong ascended the throne, Huan Rong was honored as a master of etiquette. He repeatedly requested retirement due to old age, receiving additional rewards each time. The emperor visited him, showing great respect. Huan Rong died after a long illness, and the emperor personally attended his funeral and granted him a tomb.

Ding Hong, courtesy name Xiaogong, was a native of Dingling, Yingchuan. His father, Ding Lin, surrendered Yingyang to Shizu and was rewarded, later becoming Governor of Henan. Ding Lin chose to be enfeoffed in his hometown, becoming Marquis of Xin'an Township. Ding Hong studied "Ouyang Shangshu" under Huan Rong at thirteen, mastering it in three years. After his father's death, he attempted to cede his title to his younger brother Ding Sheng but fled when his request was ignored. His friend Bao Jun persuaded him to return, citing the importance of upholding his father's legacy. Ding Hong returned, taught disciples, and was later summoned by Emperor Xianzong, becoming a servant and then Captain of Shesheng. He participated in the White Tiger Temple discussions on the Five Classics, where his eloquence was praised. He was promoted to Shaofu and later Taichang. During Empress Dowager Dou's regency, Ding Hong submitted a memorial warning against the Dou family's power, citing solar eclipses as divine warnings. His advice led to the confiscation of Dou Xian's seal and the suicides of Dou Xian and his brothers. Ding Hong also reformed the system for recommending filial and incorrupt candidates. He died in the sixth year of Yongyuan.
