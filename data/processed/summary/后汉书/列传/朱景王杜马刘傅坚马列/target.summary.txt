# Summary of Historical Biographies

**Zhu You**, courtesy name Zhongxian, was from Wan County, Nanyang. Orphaned young, he lived with his maternal grandparents in Fuyang and traveled to Chongling, where Shizu and Bosheng held him dear. Bosheng appointed him guard, and Shizu later made him army guard during the Hebei campaign. Zhu You once suggested that Chang'an's chaos indicated heaven-sent destiny for Shizu, who threatened him. He fought bravely in Hebei, became a partial general, and was named Marquis of Anyang. After Shizu ascended, Zhu You became General Jianyi. He attacked Deng Feng but was captured; after Deng Feng's defeat, Zhu You surrendered, was restored, and pacified Xinye and Suixian. He defeated Yancen, beheading Zhang Cheng, and received ninety-seven seals. He besieged Qin Feng in Liqiu, who surrendered after a year. Wu Han impeached Zhu You for accepting surrender against orders, but the emperor did not punish him. Zhu You, simple and upright, liked Confucianism, stabilized cities, and prohibited plundering, though soldiers complained. He later resisted the Xiongnu, increased his fief to 7,300 households, and suggested changing kings to dukes, which the emperor adopted. He died in Jianwu 24.

**Jing Dan**, courtesy name Sun Qing, from Yueyang, studied in Chang'an. Wang Mang promoted him to Gudehou Xiang. Gengshi made him Shanggu governor. When Wang Lang rose, Jing Dan and Geng Kuang resisted, sending troops south to Shizu. Shizu appointed him partial general and Marquis of Fengyi. He defeated Wang Lang's general Erhong, and Shizu praised his cavalry. After Shizu's ascension, officials recommended Jing Dan for Grand Sima, but Wu Han received the post, and Jing Dan became Hussar General. He was named Marquis of Yueyang, with the emperor citing the saying about returning home in brocade. He joined Wu Han in defeating five schools in Yuyang. Ill, he was forced to serve as Hongnong governor to fight thieves, dying ten days later.

**Wang Liang**, courtesy name Junyan, from Yuyang, was a county official under Prefect Peng Chong. He led troops south to Shizu, became a partial general, and Marquis of Guannei. He pacified Hebei, guarded Tianjing Pass, and Shizu credited him. After ascension, prophecies favored Wang Liang for Grand Sikong, which he received. He disobeyed orders by launching troops without authorization; the emperor sent Zong Guang to execute him, but Zong Guang brought him to the capital instead. The emperor pardoned him, made him Zhonglang General, and later Former General. He captured Feicheng and Wenyang, pacified regions with Du Mao, and rescued Taocheng. As Henan Yin, he dug a canal diverting water to Luoyang, but it failed. He resigned, became Jinan prefect, and died in Jianwu 14.

**Du Mao**, courtesy name Zhugong, from Nanyang, joined Shizu in Hebei as a backbone general. After ascension, he became General and Marquis of Lexiang, later Marquis of Kuxing. He pacified bandits in Weijun, Qinghe, and Dongjun. As Hussar General, he attacked Peijun and captured Mangxian. He fought Lu Fang's general Jia Lan but was defeated. He built fortifications and beacon fires, transported supplies, and pacified Yanmen after Lu Fang fled to the Xiongnu. His fief increased, but he was dismissed for a soldier-killing incident, reduced to Marquis of Shenzang Township. He died in Jianwu 19.

**Ma Cheng**, courtesy name Junqian, from Nanyang, was a county official. Shizu made him Jidu and Jiaxian magistrate. He abandoned his post to join Shizu in Puyang, becoming Qi gate. After ascension, he became General Yangwu, attacking Li Xian in Shu for over a year, massacring the city and beheading Li Xian. He was named Marquis of Pingshu, defeated Weixiao, and became Tianshui prefect. He later guarded the northern border, building forts and beacons from Xihe to Ye. After five or six years, he returned to the capital but was sent back due to border petitions. He became Zhongshan governor, attacked Wuxi Valley barbarians, and died in Jianwu 32.

**Liu Long**, courtesy name Boyuan, from Nanyang, survived a family purge under Wang Mang. He studied in Chang'an, became Cavalry Captain, and joined Shizu in Hanoi. Li Yi killed his family. He became General Who Kills Captives, attacked Li Xian, and garrisoned Wudang. As Nanyang governor, he presented his general's seal. His fief increased to Marquis of Jingling. During land surveys, officials favored powerful families, causing complaints. Liu Long was imprisoned for fraud, demoted to commoner, later restored as Marquis of Fule Township. He attacked Jiaozhi barbarians, beheading over a thousand. After Wu Han's death, he became Hussar General and acting Grand Sima for eight years. He died in Zhongyuan 2, posthumously named Jinghou.

**Fu Jun**, courtesy name Ziwei, from Yingchuan, greeted the Han army as pavilion chief. Xiangcheng executed his family. He became a partial general, defeated Wang Xun, and collected his family's remains. He joined Shizu in Hebei, leading Yingchuan troops. After ascension, he became attendant, Marquis of Kunyang, and General Jinu. He defeated Qin Feng with Cen Peng, pacified Yangzhou, and died in Jianwu 7, posthumously named Weihou.

**Jian Tan**, courtesy name Zidanji, from Yingchuan, was a county official. Shizu summoned him, made him chief bookkeeper and partial general. He pacified Hebei and defeated Yu Lunu. After ascension, he became General Yanghua and Marquis of Xuyin. He attacked Luoyang, opening the east gate with Zhu You, and fought Zhu Tuna until surrender. He pacified Neihuang, captured Nanyang counties, and fought Deng Feng while besieged for a year, eating only vegetables. He was wounded three times saving his troops. He became Marquis of Hefei in Jianwu 6 and died in Jianwu 26.

**Ma Wu**, courtesy name Zizhang, from Nanyang, joined the Green Forest Army and later the Han. Gengshi made him General Zhenwei. Shizu invited him to command Yuyang and Shanggu cavalry, and Ma Wu secretly turned to Shizu. After Xie Gong's execution, Ma Wu surrendered. Shizu made him Shizhong, Qi Duwei, and Shandu Marquis. He attacked Liu Yong, captured Chengwu, defeated Pang Meng, and fought Wei Xiao, charging with a painted halberd to save the army. He became Marquis of Yu, guarded against Xiongnu, and was known for drinking and speaking frankly. The emperor tolerated his faults. He attacked Wuling barbarians and later Western Qiang, beheading 4,600. He died in Yongping 4.

The historian comments that the twenty-eight Zhongxing generals were thought to correspond to constellations. Guangwu's policy of not appointing meritorious officials to high positions was foresighted, avoiding the disasters of Han Xin and Peng Yue. He rewarded heroes with fiefs but kept them from state affairs, maintaining peace. In Yongping, Xianzong painted thirty-two generals on Nangong's Cloud Platform, including Deng Yu, Wu Han, Jia Fu, Geng Yan, Du Mao, Kou Xun, Cen Peng, Feng Yi, Zhu You, Ji Zun, Jing Dan, Gai Yan, Yao Qi, Geng Chun, Zang Gong, Ma Wu, Liu Long, Ma Cheng, Wang Liang, Chen Jun, Du Mao, Fu Jun, Jian Tan, Wang Ba, Ren Guang, Li Zhong, Wan Xiu, Pi Tong, Liu Zhi, Wang Chang, Li Tong, Dou Rong, and Zhuo Mao.
