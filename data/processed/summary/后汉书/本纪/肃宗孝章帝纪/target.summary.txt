Emperor Suzong Xiaozhang, named Huodan, was the fifth son of Xianzong. He became crown prince in the third year of Yongping and ascended the throne on the sixth day of August in the eighteenth year of Yongping at age nineteen. He honored the queen as empress dowager and issued a general amnesty, granting titles and grain to various groups including widows, orphans, and the poor. In his edict, he acknowledged his modest virtue and fear of making mistakes, emphasizing the importance of following proper governance as taught in the Book of Songs. He appointed Zhao, Marquis of Jiexiang, as Taifu and Mourong as Taiwei, urging officials to correct his errors and serve with loyalty.

On November 24, Wu Lun became Sikong. General Geng Bing was ordered to garrison Jiuquan, and Duan Peng was sent to rescue Geng Gong. A solar eclipse occurred on the 30th, prompting Emperor Zhang to avoid the main hall, cease military actions, and not hold court for five days. Officials were instructed to submit criticisms and suggestions. On December 20, officials praised Emperor Xiaoming’s simple virtue and hard work, noting his kindness spread far and wide, defeating ghosts and developing the Western Regions. Emperor Zhang approved the proposal to honor Xianzong in the temple and perform the "Wude" dance.

A cattle plague and severe drought struck the capital and three prefectures, leading to exemptions from land rent and aid for the poor. In the first year of the founding of the People's Republic of China, Emperor Zhang ordered loans for the poor, support for wandering people returning home, and strict oversight to prevent corruption. He encouraged farming and mulberry cultivation, postponed trials except for capital crimes, and urged officials to select virtuous candidates. Duan Peng defeated the Che Division, and the Wuling barbarians rebelled. Earthquakes occurred in Shanyang and Dongping.

Emperor Zhang issued an edict acknowledging disasters and his lack of wisdom, calling for the promotion of talented individuals regardless of background. He ordered each official to recommend one virtuous and upright person. In the fifth month, Xiaolian were recommended, and in the seventh month, land from Shanglin Garden was given to the poor. A comet appeared in August, and the Yongchang Ailao Yi rebelled in September. Wuling County forces defeated the rebels in October, and Fuling King Liu Yan was demoted to marquis in November.

In the second year, Emperor Zhang criticized the extravagance of nobles and close relatives, ordering strict enforcement of laws. He sent troops to Yiwulu and pacified the Ailao rebellion. In April, he allowed 400 families displaced by rebellions to return home. The Shaodang Qiang rebelled in June, and Hao Chong was defeated. Ma Fang was sent to suppress the rebellion in August. A comet appeared in December.

In the third year, Emperor Zhang performed ancestral rites and issued an amnesty. He made the noble Dou family queen and granted titles and grain. Construction of river canals was halted in April. Ma Fang defeated the Shaodang Qiang, and Banchao defeated Gumo. Ma Fang was appointed general of chariots and cavalry. Lingling offered zhicao.

In the fourth year, Taiwei Mou Rong died. Prince Liu Qing was made crown prince, and various kings were transferred. Ma Fang was dismissed. Empress Dowager Ma died, and a cattle plague occurred. Emperor Zhang issued an edict emphasizing Confucian education, leading to the gathering of scholars at Baihu Temple to discuss the Five Classics, resulting in the "Baihu Tong."

In the fifth year, a solar eclipse occurred, and Emperor Zhang called for criticisms. He ordered officials to redress injustices and pray for rain. He criticized corrupt officials and ordered attacks on Wuling barbarians. Banchao defeated Shule. Eight yellow dragons appeared in Quanling.

In the sixth year, several kings died, and a solar eclipse occurred. Deng Biao became Taiwei.

In the seventh year, various kings paid homage. The crown prince was deposed, and Liu Zhao became crown prince. Emperor Zhang performed sacrifices and issued rewards. He inspected various regions, offering condolences and exemptions. He ordered reduced punishments and atonement for crimes.

In the eighth year, King Dongping died. The Northern Huns surrendered. Emperor Zhang emphasized agriculture and ordered support for poor farmers. He promoted the study of Zuo Shi Chun Qiu and other texts. Locusts plagued the capital.

In the first year of Yuanhe, various kings died. Emperor Zhang prohibited cruel torture and changed the era name. He ordered reduced punishments and inspected the south, offering rewards.

In the second year, Emperor Zhang issued edicts on agriculture and leniency. He performed sacrifices and inspected Mount Tai, where phoenixes and yellow dragons appeared. He granted amnesty and rewards.

In the third year, Emperor Zhang inspected the north and emphasized agriculture. He ordered reduced punishments and inspected the south, where a solar eclipse occurred. He granted amnesty and rewards.

In the second year of Zhanghe, various kings paid homage. Emperor Zhang died in the front hall of Zhangde at age thirty-three, following the late emperor's system.
