# Summary of Historical Records on Han Dynasty Empresses

An Si Yan, Queen Yan Ji, was born in Xingyang, Henan. Her grandfather Yan Zhang served as a minister but was never promoted to an important position because Emperor Xianzong considered him a relative of the harem. Yan Ji possessed talent and beauty, and was selected to enter the palace in the first year of Emperor An's Yuan Dynasty. She became empress in the second year. Queen Yan monopolized Emperor An's favor and poisoned the Li family, who had given birth to Prince Liu Bao. After Emperor An's death, Queen Yan and her brothers conspired to conceal his death, then established Liu Yi, the Marquis of Beixiang, as emperor. When the young emperor died, Sun Cheng and others conspired to establish Prince Liu Bao as Emperor Shun. Yan Xian and his accomplices were executed, and Queen Yan was moved to another palace, dying the following year after reigning for twelve years.

Empress Shunlie Liang was the daughter of Liang Shang. From a young age, she was intelligent, well-read in historical records and classics, and kept pictures of virtuous women for self-reflection. At thirteen, she was selected to enter the palace. Emperor Shun made her a noble and later established her as empress. Empress Liang was humble and cautious, declining to monopolize the emperor's favor. After Emperor Shun's death, she became empress dowager and governed during the reigns of Emperors Chong and Zhi. She worked diligently, appointed worthy officials, and suppressed bandit uprisings, bringing peace to the realm. However, her brother Liang Ji poisoned Emperor Zhi and became tyrannical. Empress Dowager Liang also favored eunuchs excessively, causing public disappointment. She died after nineteen years of reign at age forty-five.

Empress Huan Yi Xian Liang, sister of Empress Shunlie, was married to Emperor Huan while Empress Dowager Liang governed. She relied on her sister's influence, indulged in extravagance, and was jealous of concubines who bore sons. After Empress Dowager Liang's death, Emperor Huan's favor diminished, and she died of sorrow after thirteen years. Empress Huan Deng, daughter of Deng Xiang, was raised in Liang Ji's household. She entered the palace and was greatly favored. After Queen Liang's death, she became empress. Emperor Huan later hated the Liang family and changed her surname to Bo, but later restored it to Deng. She was arrogant and jealous, and was deposed after seven years, dying in confinement.

Empress Huansi Dou Miao, granddaughter of Queen Zhangde's brother, was established as empress in the eighth year of Yanxi. Emperor Huan rarely favored her. After Emperor Huan's death, she became empress dowager and established Emperor Ling. She was jealous and cruel, killing favored concubines. Her father Dou Wu conspired to kill eunuchs but was executed. Empress Dowager Dou was moved to Nangong and died after seven years. Empress Xiaoren Dong gave birth to Emperor Ling and was honored as empress dowager. She participated in court affairs, sold offices, and collected wealth. She favored Prince Liu Xie and urged Emperor Ling to make him crown prince, causing conflict with Empress He. After Empress Dowager He came to court, Dong was worried and fell ill, dying after twenty-two years.

Empress Ling Song was established as empress in the third year of Jianning but was not favored. She was falsely accused of witchcraft and died in confinement after eight years. Her father and brothers were killed. Emperor Ling later dreamed of Emperor Huan rebuking him for this injustice. Empress Lingsi He, from a butcher family, entered the palace through bribery. She gave birth to Prince Liu Bian and became empress. She was strong and fierce, poisoning Wang Meiren who gave birth to Prince Liu Xie. After Emperor Ling's death, Prince Liu Bian succeeded, and Queen He became empress dowager. Her brother He Jin was killed by eunuchs. Dong Zhuo entered Luoyang, deposed the young emperor, established Emperor Xian, and poisoned Empress Dowager He after ten years of reign.

Empress Xian Fu Shou entered the palace as a noble and became empress in the second year of Xingping. She witnessed Cao Cao's brutality and wrote to her father asking him to eliminate Cao Cao. The secret was revealed after nineteen years. Cao Cao forced Emperor Xian to depose her, and she died in confinement. Her two sons were poisoned, and over a hundred of her relatives died. Empress Xianmu Cao, daughter of Cao Cao, became empress after Fu Shou's death. When Wei accepted the Han throne, she angrily refused to give up the seal and threw it under the pavilion. She reigned for seven years and was later made Duchess of Shanyang, dying forty-one years later.
