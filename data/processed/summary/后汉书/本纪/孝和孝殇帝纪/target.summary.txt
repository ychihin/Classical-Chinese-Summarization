Emperor Xiaohe, named Zhao, was the fourth son of Emperor Suzong. His mother, Liang Guiren, was framed by Empress Dou and died of melancholy; Empress Dou adopted him. He became crown prince in the seventh year of Jianchu and ascended the throne at age ten in the second year of Zhanghe. Empress Dou was honored as empress dowager and attended court. Early edicts renamed several states and moved kings accordingly. Emperor Xiaozhang was buried in Jingling. The empress dowager issued an edict appointing Dou Xian, her brother, to a role, but he declined; she then appointed Deng Biao as Taifu to oversee secretarial affairs. The late emperor was honored with the temple name Suzong. Kings began entering their fiefdoms. The emperor visited temples. An edict lifted bans on salt and iron, allowing the people to boil salt and cast iron with taxes. A drought occurred in the capital. Dou Xian was appointed chariot and cavalry general to attack the Northern Huns. Parthian envoys offered lions and hornless lins. In the first year of Yongyuan, Dou Xian defeated the Northern Xiongnu at Jiluo Mountain, carved a stone at Yanran Mountain, and returned. The Northern Shanyu sent tribute. Mount Kuaiji collapsed. An edict praised the victory and ordered sacrifices. Dou Xian was made general, and Liu Shang became chariot and cavalry general. Prisoners were released to military camps. Fuling King Liu Yan died. Floods occurred in nine counties. A general amnesty was declared. A solar eclipse occurred. Captaincy officers were re-established. Taishan was divided, and new kings were appointed. Yan Pan attacked the Northern Xiongnu. Liu Wuji and Liu Wei were named kings. The Yuezhi Kingdom attacked Ban Chao, who counterattacked. Zhongshan King Liu Yan died. Dou Xian stationed troops in Liangzhou. The Northern Huns sent envoys to declare vassalage. Ban Gu was sent to Nan Shanyu. The emperor was crowned, with rewards and amnesty. Dou Xian besieged and defeated the Northern Shanyu. Princess Biyang was honored as eldest princess. Fuling King Liu Zhong died. The emperor visited Chang'an, issued edicts, and sacrificed at tombs. The Western Region Protectorate was re-established. The Northern Xiongnu king established himself as chanyu. Situ Yuan An died; Ding Hong succeeded. Dou Xian returned to the capital. A solar eclipse and earthquake occurred. Dou Xian plotted rebellion; the emperor arrested his party, and Dou Xian and his brothers committed suicide. Drought and locust plague occurred. Taiwei Song You committed suicide. Sikong Renkai died; Yin Mu became Taiwei. Liu Fang became Sikong. An edict exempted land rent due to drought and locusts. Barbarians and Qiang rebelled. The emperor worshiped the Five Emperors and granted amnesty. Kings died or were granted titles. Edicts reduced horse stables and gave orchards to the poor. Grand Tutor Deng Biao died. Earthquakes occurred. Edicts addressed talent selection and relief. Hailstorms occurred. King Liu Wansui died. The Xiongnu chanyu was killed. Edicts encouraged food conservation. Taiwei Yin Mu died; Zhang Youfu succeeded. Wuling rebels surrendered. Qiang fled. Nan Dan rebelled. Foreigners presented rhinos and elephants. Situ Dinghong died. Relief was provided to the poor. Xu Yanghou Ma Guang committed suicide. Liu Fang became Situ; Zhang Fen became Sikong. Edicts addressed refugees and talent selection. The Qiang submitted. King Liu Shu died. A solar eclipse occurred. Drought led to reduced sentences. The emperor inspected prisons. Ban Chao defeated Yanqi and Yuli; the Western Regions surrendered. Fenghou fled. Deng Hong attacked Fenghou. Ren Shang defeated Fenghou. Bohai was transferred to Jizhou. Wuling barbarians rebelled. Deng Hong and others were imprisoned. A solar eclipse occurred. The emperor consulted officials. Edicts addressed talent selection. Qiancheng became Le'an. King Pei died. Earthquakes occurred. The noble Yin became queen, with rewards. King Liu Dang died. Relief was provided. Locust plagues occurred. The Xiongnu king rebelled and was killed. The queen of Che Division rebelled. Edicts addressed locust plagues. Guangyang County was restored. Beihai King Liu Wei committed suicide. King Chen died. A fire occurred in Nangong. Barbarians paid tribute. Earthquakes occurred. King Jinan died. Ban Chao killed the queen of Che Division. Liu Xun became king. Locusts and drought occurred. Edicts exempted rent. Locusts flew over the capital. Xianbei bandits plundered. Empress Dowager Dou died. Shaodang Qiang raided. Situ Liu Fang committed suicide. Liang Guizhen was honored as empress dowager. Lu Gai became Situ. Zhang Fen left; Han Xun became Sikong. Ruolu prison officer was restored. Edicts addressed embankments. Floods occurred. Sikong Hanxun died. Taishan was empty. Linxi office was restored. Rain occurred in five states. Shaodang Qiang paid tribute. Liang Wang died. Edicts provided relief. A general amnesty was declared. The right school lieutenant was restored. Edicts addressed lavish burials. Bailang and Zhilou Boyi submitted. Edicts addressed disasters and talent selection. Rewards were given. Rinan barbarians rebelled. Relief was provided. Landslides occurred. A solar eclipse occurred. Taiwei Zhang Youfu was dismissed; Zhang Yu succeeded. Western Region kings were given seals. Shaodang Qiang rebelled. The emperor visited Dongguan. King Rencheng died. Relief was provided. A fire occurred. Zhou Tuna defeated Shaodang Qiang. Edicts exempted rent. Parthian envoys offered lions. Edicts addressed talent selection in border regions. Xianbei raided. Situ Lu Gai was dismissed; Lu Gong succeeded. Wuman rebelled. King Donghai died. Xihai County was repaired. The emperor visited Piyong. Wuman were defeated. Relief was provided. Xianglin office was established. Queen Yin was deposed. Edicts exempted Xianglin. King Changshan died. Rain occurred. Edicts addressed floods. Noble Deng became queen. Sikong Chaokan was dismissed; Xu Fang succeeded. Officials were re-established. Edicts addressed refugees. Relief was provided. A solar eclipse occurred. Edicts allowed fishing. King Jinan died. Gu'an Iron Office was restored. The emperor inspected the south. Rain occurred. The emperor visited Zhangling. King Langyi died. Light punishments were ordered. Edicts addressed poor farmers. Wine-making was banned. Inspectors were sent. King Zhao died. Drought occurred. Edicts addressed officials. Situ Lu Gong was dismissed; Zhang Youfu succeeded. Edicts exempted rent. Situ Zhang Youfu died. Xu Fang became Situ; Chen Chong became Sikong. The emperor climbed Baichui Mountain. Northern Huns paid tribute. The governor of eastern Liaoning was restored. Talents were selected. Gaoguli raided. A general amnesty was declared. Yongdi collapsed. Geng Kui defeated the Qi people. The emperor died at age twenty-seven. Prince Liu Long became crown prince. After Dou Xian's death, the emperor governed personally. He consulted officials during disasters and did not publicize auspicious omens. The South China Sea offered longan and litchi, causing deaths; an edict stopped the tribute. Historians noted population growth and territorial expansion under Emperor He.

Emperor Xiaoshang, named Long, was the youngest son of Emperor He. He became emperor at over a hundred days old. The empress dowager governed. Northern Huns paid tribute. Zhang Yu became Taifu. Xu Fang became Taiwei. Liu Sheng became king. Liang Tuna became Situ. Emperor He was buried. Kings entered fiefdoms. Edicts removed improper priests. Xianbei raided; Zhang Xian died. Deng Zhi became chariot and cavalry general. Sikong Chen Chong died. The empress dowager issued an edict granting amnesty and restoring prisoners. Landslides occurred. Yin Qin became Sikong. Rain occurred. Edicts addressed waste. The empress dowager ordered the release of palace women and slaves. Edicts addressed disasters and corruption. The emperor died at age two. Historians praised Emperor He's calmness and punishment of traitors, while Emperor Shang died young.
