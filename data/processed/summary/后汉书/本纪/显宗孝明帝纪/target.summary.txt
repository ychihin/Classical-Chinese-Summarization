Emperor Xianzong Xiaoming Mingzhuang, the fourth son of Emperor Guangwu, was born with a square face and could read the Spring and Autumn Annals at age ten. He was granted the title of Duke of Donghai in the fifteenth year of Jianwu, promoted to king in the seventeenth year, and made crown prince in the nineteenth year. He studied Shangshu under Dr. Huan Rong. He ascended the throne at age thirty on the fifth day of the second lunar month in the second year of the Zhongyuan Dynasty, honoring his mother as empress dowager and burying Emperor Guangwu in the original mausoleum.

Emperor Ming issued an edict expressing his fear of inheriting the great legacy and his desire to uphold his ancestors' will by putting the people first. He granted titles and rewards to various groups, including men of nobility, the three elders, filial brothers, and the poor. He also ordered the release of prisoners and the return of border civilians taken as wives. He appointed Deng Yu as Taifu and Liu Cang as General of Hussars, granting them titles. The Shaodang Qiang invaded Longxi, and after initial defeat, General Dou Gu was sent to attack them. Another edict emphasized not interfering with agricultural work and allowed criminals to atone for their sins. He criticized corrupt officials and unfair labor levies.

In the first year of Yongping, Emperor Ming led officials to pay homage to the original mausoleum. Taifu Deng Yu and King Liu Qiang of Donghai passed away. The captured general Ma Wu defeated the Shaodang Qiang. Emperor Ming performed ceremonies in Mingtang, Piyong, and Lingtai, with various officials and foreign leaders attending. He held a pension ceremony, honoring the three elders and five brothers, and granted Huan Rong a title. He inspected the west, visiting Chang'an and offering sacrifices. Dou Lin, the captain who protected Qiang, died in prison. Yin Feng killed his wife and committed suicide.

Emperor Ming issued edicts on agriculture, justice, and disaster relief. He appointed officials, made the noble Ma family queen, and granted titles and rewards. A comet appeared, and a solar eclipse occurred, prompting an edict for officials to criticize political affairs. He built the North Palace and various government offices. He plowed fields and prayed for good harvests. He dismissed and appointed officials, and dealt with rebellions and invasions by the Northern Xiongnu. He inspected various regions, worshiped at tombs, and rewarded officials. He established schools and set up teachers for the Five Classics. He repaired the Bian Canal and inspected rivers and canals. He dealt with the rebellion of King Liu Ying of Chu and the death of King Liu Shi of Qi.

Emperor Ming continued to issue edicts on agriculture, justice, and disaster relief. He inspected eastward, plowed fields, and worshiped at Confucius's house. He granted titles and rewards, and sent generals to attack the Northern Xiongnu. He dealt with rebellions and invasions, and established the Protectorate of the Western Regions. He passed away in the front hall of the East Palace at age forty-eight, leaving instructions for a simple burial. Historians commented that Emperor Ming was good at criminal proceedings, had clear laws, and was competent in office, with no fluke or favoritism. However, some officials were dissatisfied with his lack of tolerance.
