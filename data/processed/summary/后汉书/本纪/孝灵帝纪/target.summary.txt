Emperor Xiaoling, Liu Hong, was the great-great-grandson of Emperor Suzong. His great-grandfather Liu Kai was the Xiaowang of Hejian, his grandfather Liu Shu and father Liu Chang were both Marquises of Duting, a title Liu Hong inherited. His mother was Mrs. Dong. After Emperor Huan died without children, Empress Dowager Dou and her father Dou Wu sent officials to escort Liu Hong to Luoyang. He became emperor at age twelve in the first year of Jianning (168 CE), with Chen Fan as Taifu and Dou Wu and Hu Guang assisting in governance.

The early years saw military campaigns against the Xianling Qiang, natural disasters including earthquakes and floods, and political purges. In 169 CE, eunuch Cao Jie falsely claimed an imperial order to kill Chen Fan, Dou Wu, and others, with their families executed. The Empress Dowager was moved to Nangong. Party member persecutions intensified, with over a hundred people imprisoned and their families exiled. Solar eclipses, epidemics, and barbarian rebellions occurred frequently.

In 172 CE, the reign name changed to Xiping. Empress Dowager Dou died, and eunuch Hou Lan committed suicide after being found guilty. The Xianbei raided Bingzhou. In 175 CE, the Five Classics were carved on stone tablets outside the Taixue. Floods, locust plagues, and Xianbei raids continued. In 178 CE, the reign changed to Guanghe, and the Hongdumen school was established. The West Residence began selling official positions, with prices varying by rank. Earthquakes, solar eclipses, and the deposal of Empress Song occurred.

In 179 CE, party members were pardoned. In 180 CE, Empress He was established. The emperor built shops in the inner palace where concubines traded, and he dressed as a merchant, playing with dogs and driving donkey carts. In 184 CE, Zhang Jiao led the Yellow Turban Rebellion, with followers wearing yellow scarves. The emperor appointed He Jin as general, established eight passes, and pardoned party members except Zhang Jiao. Generals Lu Zhi, Huangfu Song, and Zhu Jun suppressed the rebellion, killing Zhang Jiao and his brothers.

In 185 CE, Nangong caught fire, and taxes were levied per mu for palace construction. Black Mountain bandits and Xianbei raids continued. In 186 CE, Zhao Ci rebelled and was killed. In 187 CE, Zhang Chun and Zhang Ju rebelled in Yuyang, claiming emperorship. In 188 CE, the emperor proclaimed himself "Supreme General" and reviewed the army. Eight captains were established in Xiyuan. The Liangzhou bandit kingdom surrounded Chencang.

In 189 CE, Huangfu Song defeated the kingdom. The emperor died in Nangong at age thirty-four. Prince Bian became emperor at seventeen, with Empress Dowager He regent. Eunuch Zhang Rang and others killed General He Jin, leading to Yuan Shu burning the palaces and Yuan Shao massacring eunuchs. The young emperor and Prince Xie were kidnapped but later returned. Dong Zhuo deposed the emperor as Prince Hongnong. Heavy rain fell from June to September.
