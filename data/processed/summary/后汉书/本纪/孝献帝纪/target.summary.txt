**Summary of the Reign of Emperor Xian of Han**

Xiaoxian Emperor Liu Xie, the son of Emperor Ling, ascended the throne at age nine in September 189, moving the Empress Dowager to Yong'an Palace and granting amnesty. Dong Zhuo soon killed the Empress Dowager and appointed himself Prime Minister. In 190, prefectures raised troops against Dong Zhuo, who killed the former emperor Liu Bian and moved the capital to Chang'an, burning Luoyang's palaces. Dong Zhuo destroyed five-baht coins for small coins. In 191, Dong Zhuo became Grand Master, and Sun Jian defeated his general. Dong Zhuo excavated imperial tombs. In 192, Dong Zhuo was executed, and his clan was wiped out. His generals Li Jue and Guo Si rebelled, capturing Chang'an and killing over 10,000 officials and civilians. Li Jue killed Wang Yun and others. In 193, Yuan Shu killed the Yangzhou governor and occupied Huainan. In 194, a severe drought and locust plague caused famine, with grain prices soaring and cannibalism. The emperor personally inspected relief efforts. In 195, Li Jue killed Fan Chou and fought Guo Si, forcing the emperor to flee. The emperor began his journey back to Luoyang, facing attacks from Li Jue and Guo Si, with many officials killed. In 196, the emperor arrived in Luoyang, but the palace was burned, and officials suffered from hunger. Cao Cao took control, moving the capital to Xuchang and appointing himself Sikong. In 197, Yuan Shu claimed emperorship. In 198, Cao Cao killed Lü Bu. In 199, Yuan Shao captured Gongsun Zan. In 200, Dong Cheng's plot to kill Cao Cao failed, and Cao Cao killed him. Cao Cao defeated Yuan Shao at Guandu. In 207, Cao Cao defeated the Wuhuan. In 208, Cao Cao became Prime Minister, defeated Liu Biao's son, but was defeated by Sun Quan at Red Cliffs. In 213, Cao Cao became Duke of Wei. In 214, Cao Cao killed Empress Fu. In 215, Cao Cao captured Hanzhong. In 216, Cao Cao became King of Wei. In 220, Cao Cao died, and his son Cao Pi succeeded. Emperor Xian abdicated, becoming Duke of Shanyang. Liu Bei proclaimed himself emperor of Shu, and Sun Quan became king of Wu, dividing the realm into three. Emperor Xian died in 234 at age 54, buried with Han rites. Historians note that Han's decline began long before his reign, and he was a victim of his era.
