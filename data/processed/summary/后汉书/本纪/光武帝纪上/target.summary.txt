# Summary of the Founding of the Eastern Han Dynasty

Emperor Guangwu Liu Xiu, courtesy name Uncle Wen, was born in Caiyang, Nanyang, as the ninth-generation grandson of Liu Bang. He lost his father at age nine and was raised by his uncle Liu Liang. Described as tall with distinctive features, he was industrious and enjoyed farming, unlike his elder brother Bo Sheng who favored chivalry. During Wang Mang's reign, Liu Xiu studied the "Shang Shu" in Chang'an.

When locust plagues and famine struck, Liu Xiu fled to Xinye County to avoid government persecution. After receiving prophecies about the Liu family's revival, he initially hesitated but eventually planned with the Li family to raise an army. In October, at age 28, he raised troops in Wancheng. When local families saw the cautious Liu Xiu had joined the uprising, they stopped hiding. After initial victories and defeats, the Han army captured several cities.

Wang Mang sent Grand Situ Wang Xun and Grand Sikong Wang Yi with a massive army. Liu Xiu advised unity against the enemy, though generals initially resisted. With only 8,000-9,000 troops in Kunyang, Liu Xiu organized defenses and gathered reinforcements. Wang Mang's forces surrounded Kunyang with overwhelming numbers, using siege towers and tunnels. Despite the enemy's strength, Liu Xiu led a daring counterattack with 3,000 elite troops, breaking the enemy formation and killing Wang Xun. A storm aided the Han forces, causing panic and massive casualties among Wang Mang's army.

After Bo Sheng was killed by Emperor Gengshi, Liu Xiu rushed to apologize without boasting of his achievements. Emperor Gengshi appointed him General Polu and Marquis of Wuxin. When sent to Luoyang, Liu Xiu restored Han dynasty administrative practices, winning popular support. He crossed the Yellow River to pacify northern regions, releasing prisoners and abolishing Wang Mang's harsh policies.

When Wang Lang proclaimed himself emperor in Handan, Liu Xiu faced numerous hardships, including food shortages and dangerous river crossings. After gathering forces, he captured Handan and killed Wang Lang. He burned documents revealing officials' collaboration with Wang Lang, saying this would calm those who were anxious.

As chaos spread across the empire, Liu Xiu pacified various rebel groups including the Bronze Horse Army. He won over surrendered troops by personally inspecting their camps, earning their loyalty. His forces grew to hundreds of thousands.

After repeated requests from his generals, Liu Xiu ascended the throne on June 22, establishing the Jianwu reign period. He granted amnesty, appointed officials, and made Luoyang his capital. He enfeoffed meritorious officials as princes, warning them to remain cautious and respectful.

The Red Eyebrow Army captured Chang'an and looted Guanzhong. Liu Xiu personally led campaigns against various rebels, defeating the Red Eyebrows and recovering the imperial seal. He continued military campaigns against Liu Yong, Dong Xian, and others while implementing administrative reforms, including releasing prisoners during drought and restoring Han dynasty institutions. He established the Imperial Academy and promoted agriculture as wild grains gradually decreased and cultivated fields increased.
