Emperor Gongzong Xiaoan, named Liu Hu, was the grandson of Suzong and son of Liu Qing, the Xiaowang of Qinghe, and Zuo Ji. From childhood, supernatural signs like illuminated rooms and red snakes on his bed were observed. At ten, he studied "Historical Records" and was favored by Emperor He. In the first year of Yanping, after Emperor Shang's death, Empress Dowager Deng and her brother Deng Zhi chose Liu Hu as successor. He was enthroned at thirteen, with the Empress Dowager regent. The early years saw natural disasters, Qiang rebellions, and relief efforts. In Yongchu years, floods, earthquakes, and famines struck, leading to edicts for grain relief, tax exemptions, and crackdowns on corruption. The Qiang conflicts persisted, with battles and occasional victories. In 107, a solar eclipse prompted calls for virtuous officials. By 108, droughts and locust plagues worsened, with cannibalism reported. The emperor crowned in 109, but disasters continued. In 110, pirate Zhang Bolu raided coastal counties. By 111, rebellions and Qiang attacks intensified. In 112, the capital suffered locusts and earthquakes. In 113, the Empress Dowager visited the ancestral temple. In 114, solar eclipses and Qiang raids occurred. In 115, the reign changed to Yuanchu, with amnesties and relief. In 116, floods and Qiang attacks persisted. In 117, solar eclipses and rebellions marked the year. In 118, the Qiang were pacified. In 119, droughts and Xianbei raids continued. In 120, earthquakes and epidemics struck. In 121, the Empress Dowager died, and the emperor assumed full power. In 122, Xianbei and Qiang attacks continued. In 123, the emperor toured east, performing sacrifices. In 124, solar eclipses and rebellions occurred. In 125, the emperor died at thirty-two, succeeded by Beixiang Hou Liu Yi. Historians criticized his reign for weak governance, reliance on Empress Dowager Deng, and decline of Han power, marked by corruption, Qiang troubles, and natural disasters.
