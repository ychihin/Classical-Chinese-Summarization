In the sixth year of spring, Chongling Township was renamed Zhangling County, and its people were exempted from corvée, following the precedent of Fengyi and Pei County. An edict ordered counties with food reserves to relieve the elderly, widowers, widows, orphans, the childless, the seriously ill, and the homeless, in accordance with the Han Code. General Ma Cheng captured Shu County, and Grand Sima Wu Han pacified the area east of the Taihang Mountains. In March, Gongsun Shu invaded Nanjun. Emperor Guangwu visited Chang'an to pay homage at the Gaomiao Temple and the Eleven Tombs, then sent General Gai Yan to attack Gongsun Shu. After returning to Luoyang, Gai Yan was defeated by Kuai Xiao. An edict exempted officials in Tianshui, Longxi, Anding, and Beidi from punishment for crimes less than beheading if implicated in Kuai Xiao's rebellion. Another edict ordered the reduction of officials, resulting in the merger of over 400 counties and a tenfold reduction in staff. Liu Xing, the prefect of Daijun, died in battle against Lu Fang's general. Wang Tiao of Lelang was killed by his own officials, and General Li Tong defeated Gongsun Shu's forces. A locust plague struck, and a solar eclipse occurred. An edict called for recommendations of virtuous and upright individuals. Wang Mang decreed that non-slaves not complying with old laws be made commoners. Grand Sikong Song Hong was dismissed. Emperor Guangwu restored the thirty-to-one tax rate. Kuai Xiao's invasion of Fufeng was repelled by General Feng Yi. Officials began to be dismissed, and the Xiongnu sent envoys.

In the seventh year, an edict released prisoners except those guilty of capital crimes. Emperor Guangwu condemned lavish burials and advocated frugality. The canal guard captain was dismissed, and military forces were reduced. Gongsun Shu made Kuai Xiao King of Shuoning. A solar eclipse led to a five-day suspension of state affairs. An edict blamed the emperor for disasters and called for officials to fulfill their duties. Another edict granted amnesty and ordered recommendations of virtuous individuals. Li Tong became Grand Sikong. Slaves captured during famines or wars were to be released. Continuous rain fell. Wang Chang was appointed general. Liu Shao was made King of Hejian. Wei Xiao was driven back from Anding. Lu Fang's generals surrendered. The Changshui and Shesheng school lieutenants were omitted.

In the eighth year, Zhonglang Lai She attacked Lueyang. Sili school captain Fu Kang died in prison. Wei Xiao failed to capture She. Emperor Guangwu personally campaigned against Wei Xiao, who was defeated and fled to Xicheng. Yingchuan bandits revolted, causing panic in the capital. Floods occurred. Emperor Guangwu returned to Luoyang and subdued the Yingchuan bandits. Zhang Bu rebelled but was captured. Gongsun Shu sent troops to rescue Wei Xiao, but they retreated. Tianshui and Longxi rebelled. The King of Goguryeo paid tribute. Floods continued.

In the ninth year, Wei Xiao died, and his son Wei Chun became king. Yanmen officials were moved to Taiyuan. The Qingjin Left School Lieutenant was appointed. Gongsun Shu occupied Jingmen. Emperor Guangwu climbed Mount Fuyuan. Wu Han attacked Lu Fang's general but was unsuccessful. Lai She led a campaign against Wei Chun. Du Mao was defeated. The Guandu Lieutenant was abolished, and the Qiang School Lieutenant was restored.

In the tenth year, Wu Han drove back Xiongnu cavalry. The Gaomiao Temple in Chang'an was repaired. Feng Yi defeated and killed Gongsun Shu's general. Feng Yi died. Emperor Guangwu visited Chang'an. Wei Xiao's general Gao Jun surrendered. Wei Chun fled to Shu but later surrendered, pacifying Longyou. Lai She defeated the Qiang. Emperor Guangwu returned to Luoyang. Dingxiang County was abolished.

In the eleventh year, an edict stated that killing a slave or maid was still murder. Emperor Guangwu visited Nanyang and Zhangling. Cen Peng defeated Gongsun Shu's generals and captured Ren Man. Feng Jun besieged Tian Rong. Cen Peng pacified Bajun. Lai She defeated Gongsun Shu's generals but was assassinated. Emperor Guangwu personally campaigned against Gongsun Shu. Cen Peng defeated Hou Dan. Zang Gong defeated Yancen. Wang Yuan surrendered. An edict banned the burning of slaves and abolished the law requiring slaves to be executed for shooting someone. Gongsun Shu assassinated Cen Peng. Ma Cheng pacified Wudu. Wu Han led a naval attack on Gongsun Shu. The Shuofang Mu was eliminated.

In the twelfth year, Wu Han killed Shi Xing. An edict freed those robbed as slaves in Long and Shu. Timely rain fell. A yellow dragon appeared. Feng Jun captured Jiangzhou. Wu Han defeated Xie Feng. Zang Gong captured Fucheng. Li Tong was dismissed. Wu Han defeated Gongsun Shu, who died of injuries. Chengdu was massacred. Ma Cheng became Grand Sikong. Zhang You surrendered. Jincheng County was merged into Longxi. Ma Yuan subdued the Shenlang Qiang. An edict allowed border troops to determine pursuit distances. Wang Chang died, and Du Mao led prisoners to build fortifications.

In the thirteenth year, Hou Ba died. An edict stopped counties from contributing exotic delicacies. Ma Wu was sent to guard against the Xiongnu. Lu Fang rebelled. Several kings were demoted to marquises. Nine feudal states were merged. Han Xin became Grand Situ. Ma Cheng was dismissed. Wu Han returned from Shu, and meritorious officials were rewarded. The left and right generals were abolished. Gongsun Shu's treasures were used to complete palace ceremonies. Official documents were reduced to one-tenth. Dou Mu became Grand Sikong. The Xiongnu invaded Hedong. The Baima Qiang surrendered. Japan presented white pheasants and rabbits. An edict freed slaves in Yizhou. Jincheng County was restored.

In the fourteenth year, the Nangong front hall was built. The Xiongnu paid tribute. Kong Zhi was made a marquis. Ren Gui claimed to be governor. Jia Dan surrendered. A plague struck Kuaiji. Shache and Shanshan paid tribute. Slaves in Yi and Liang were freed.

In the fifteenth year, Han Xin committed suicide. Comets appeared. Ouyang She became Grand Situ. Zhu You was dismissed. Residents of Yanmen, Daijun, and Shanggu were relocated. Wu Han requested titles for princes. Officials recommended enfeoffment. Emperor Guangwu agreed. Princes were granted dukedoms. The three lieutenants were restored. An order was issued to verify land and household registrations. Ouyang She died in prison. Dai She became Grand Situ. Lu Fang returned from the Xiongnu. Du Mao and Gai Yan died.

In the sixteenth year, Jiaochangzhi women rebelled. A solar eclipse occurred. Officials were imprisoned for fraud. Bandits caused chaos. An edict allowed bandits to report each other. Lu Fang surrendered. Five baht coins were introduced.

In the seventeenth year, Zhao Gongliang died. A solar eclipse occurred. Emperor Guangwu visited the south. Liu Heng died. Li Guang rebelled but was killed. Queen Guo was deposed, and Noble Yin became empress. Princes were promoted. Emperor Guangwu visited Zhangling. Five phoenixes appeared. Yarkand paid tribute.

In the eighteenth year, Shi Xin rebelled but was defeated. Emperor Guangwu visited Chang'an. An edict abolished harsh laws for border counties. Ma Yuan attacked Jiaozhi. Lu Fang fled to the Xiongnu. Wu Han killed Shi Xin. Emperor Guangwu visited Yicheng. State pastors were dismissed and replaced by governors.

In the nineteenth year, Emperor Xiaoxuan was honored as Zhongzong. Shanchen rebelled but was killed. Ma Yuan captured Jiaozhi. Princes were promoted. Liu Yang became crown prince. Emperor Guangwu visited the south. Nanton County was exempted from rent. The southwestern barbarians invaded. Ren Gui rebelled but was killed. The Hangu Pass Commandery was restored.

In the twentieth year, Emperor Guangwu returned to Luoyang. Dai She died in prison. Dou Rong was dismissed. Wu Han died. The Xiongnu invaded. Cai Mao became Grand Situ. Liu Long became general. Liu Fu was transferred to Pei. Koreans surrendered. Emperor Guangwu inspected the east. The Xiongnu invaded Tianshui. Wuyuan County was abolished.

In the twenty-first year, Liu Shang pacified Yizhou. The Anding Hu rebelled but were quelled. Xianbei invaded Liaodong. Ma Yuan attacked Wuhuan unsuccessfully. The Xiongnu invaded. Sixteen kingdoms requested protection but were refused.

In the twenty-second year, Emperor Guangwu visited Chang'an. A solar eclipse occurred. Su Ye died in prison. An earthquake struck Nanyang. An edict provided relief. Zhufu was dismissed. Du Lin became Grand Sikong. The Xiongnu sought peace. Wuhuan defeated the Xiongnu.

In the twenty-third year, the Nanjun barbarians rebelled but were subdued. Cai Mao and Du Lin died. Yu Kuang became Grand Situ. Zhang Chun became Grand Sikong. Goguryeo joined Lelang. The Wuling barbarians rebelled, and Liu Shang died in battle.

In the twenty-fourth year, a general amnesty was declared. The Xiongnu leader sought peace. The Wuling barbarians invaded. Ma Yuan led an attack. The Xiongnu split into southern and northern parts.

In the twenty-fifth year, the Qi people surrendered. The Southern Shanyu paid tribute. Ma Yuan defeated the Wuling barbarians. Fuyu paid tribute. Wuhuan surrendered.

In the twenty-sixth year, official salaries were adjusted. Shouling construction began. The Southern Shanyu was granted a seal. Border residents returned home.

In the twenty-seventh year, Yu Kuang died. Official titles were changed. The Northern Xiongnu sought peace. Princes entered their fiefdoms.

In the twenty-eighth year, Liu Xing was transferred to Beihai. Queen Mother Pei Guo died. Princes entered their fiefdoms. Prisoners were summoned for punishment. The Northern Xiongnu paid tribute.

In the twenty-ninth year, a solar eclipse occurred. Prisoners were released. Rewards were given.

In the thirtieth year, Xianbei paid tribute. Emperor Guangwu inspected the east. A comet appeared. Liu Yan became king of Zhongshan. Floods occurred. Rewards were given.

In the thirty-first year, floods and locusts occurred. Prisoners were summoned for punishment. The Northern Xiongnu paid tribute.

In the first year of Zhongyuan, princes paid homage. Emperor Guangwu performed sacrifices on Mount Tai. A general amnesty was declared. Sweet spring water and red grass appeared. Emperor Guangwu rejected auspicious omens. Locusts struck. Sikong Zhang Chun died. Feng Li became Sikong. Situ Fengqin died. Emperor Guangwu died at age sixty-two. An edict ordered frugal burial. Historians noted auspicious signs at his birth and throughout his reign.
