Emperor Xiaohuan Liu Zhi, great-grandson of Emperor Suzong Zhang, succeeded his father Liu Yi as Marquis of Liwu. In the first year of this century, Empress Dowager Liang summoned him to Luoyang, intending to marry her sister to him. After Emperor Zhi's death, Empress Dowager Liang and her brother General Liang Ji decided to install Liu Zhi as emperor. He ascended the throne at age fifteen, with Empress Dowager Liang still governing. Emperor Xiaozhi was buried in Jingling.

The emperor issued edicts calling for filial and honest officials to govern well, but noted that officials were negligent and selections were disorderly, harming the people. He urged officials to benefit workers and eliminate corruption. Only those with over ten years of service and special talents could be selected; descendants of corrupt officials were barred. He honored his ancestors posthumously.

In the first year of Jianhe, a solar eclipse occurred. The emperor ordered officials to discuss government affairs, granted amnesty, and provided relief to the needy. Disasters struck various regions, including earthquakes, floods, and famines. The emperor responded by ordering disaster relief, reducing taxes, and recommending virtuous officials. He also addressed corruption, ordering that officials embezzling over 300,000 yuan be punished.

Over subsequent years, the realm faced rebellions, including Liu Wen of Qinghe's attempt to install Liu Lian as emperor, which was suppressed. Former officials Li Gu and Du Qiao were executed. The emperor was crowned in the second year, granted amnesty, and distributed gifts. Earthquakes, fires, and floods continued. The emperor moved to the South Palace after a fire. Rebellions by barbarians and bandits were suppressed.

In the third year, a solar eclipse prompted the emperor to order the return of exiles and the release of prisoners. Disasters persisted, including earthquakes, floods, and landslides. The emperor issued edicts for relief and burial of the dead. In the first year of Heping, he granted amnesty and changed the era name. Empress Dowager Liang died, and the emperor took full control.

Epidemics and famines struck in subsequent years. The emperor ordered medical relief and grain distribution. Rebellions by barbarians and bandits continued. The emperor conducted hunts and visited various locations. Earthquakes, floods, and locust plagues occurred frequently. The emperor issued edicts for relief and tax reductions.

In the first year of Yongshou, amnesty was granted. Famines led to cannibalism; the emperor ordered relief and grain loans. Floods and landslides caused damage. The emperor established military offices and suppressed rebellions. In the second year, officials were allowed mourning leave. Bandits were defeated. Earthquakes and locust plagues continued.

In the third year, amnesty was granted. Rebellions by barbarians were suppressed. Solar eclipses and locust plagues occurred. The emperor established offices and conducted hunts. In the first year of Yanxi, amnesty was granted. Earthquakes and locust plagues continued. The emperor hunted and visited Shanglinyuan. Xianbei invaders were defeated.

In the second year, Xianbei raided Yanmen. The emperor suspended mourning leave for officials. Heavy rains and floods occurred. Queen Liang died. General Liang Ji conspired to rebel; the emperor ordered his execution, along with many relatives. The emperor established Queen Deng and issued an edict condemning Liang Ji. He rewarded eunuchs who helped him. He appointed new officials and visited Chang'an.

In the third year, amnesty was granted. Qiang rebellions were suppressed. Fires and earthquakes occurred. The emperor ordered salary reductions for officials. Rebellions by barbarians and bandits continued. In the fourth year, fires and epidemics struck. The emperor granted amnesty and reduced salaries. Rebellions were suppressed. In the fifth year, fires and earthquakes occurred. The emperor ordered criticisms and reports. Rebellions by bandits and barbarians continued. The emperor reduced salaries and lent funds.

In the sixth year, amnesty was granted. Fires and rebellions occurred. The emperor hunted and visited various locations. In the seventh year, meteorites fell. Hail and earthquakes occurred. Rebellions were suppressed. The emperor visited the south and returned. In the eighth year, a solar eclipse occurred. Queen Deng was deposed. Fires and earthquakes occurred. Rebellions were suppressed. The emperor ordered tax increases.

In the ninth year, a solar eclipse occurred. The emperor ordered tax reductions and relief. Fires and famines occurred. Rebellions by barbarians and bandits continued. The emperor recommended warriors. Officials were executed for corruption. The Southern Xiongnu surrendered. Party members were imprisoned. In the first year of Yongkang, Qiang rebellions were suppressed. Earthquakes and floods occurred. The emperor granted amnesty and lifted party shackles. He died at age thirty-six.

Historians commented that Emperor Huan loved music and decorated gardens, but his reliance on eunuchs led to chaos. Despite loyal officials, his reign was marked by corruption and disaster. He had three queens and many concubines but no heirs.
