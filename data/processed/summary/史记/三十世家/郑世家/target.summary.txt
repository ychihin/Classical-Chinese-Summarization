Duke Huan of Zheng, the youngest son of King Zhou Li, was granted the title of Zhengdi and served as Situ under King You. Loved by the people, he sought advice from Tai Shibo on escaping the Zhou royal family's impending disaster. Tai Shibo advised moving east of Luoshui and south of the Yellow River, where the greedy rulers of Guo and Hao would cede land. Duke Huan relocated his people there, establishing the state of Zheng. However, in the second year, Quan Rong killed King You and Duke Huan at Mount Li. Duke Huan's son, Duke Wu, succeeded him. Duke Wu's wife, Wu Jiang, favored their younger son Shu Duan over the crown prince Wusheng (Duke Zhuang). After Duke Wu's death, Duke Zhuang granted Shu Duan the capital, leading to rebellion. Duke Zhuang defeated Shu Duan, exiled Wu Jiang, but later reconciled with her through a tunnel.

Duke Zhuang's reign saw conflicts with Zhou, Song, and Wei. He defeated King Huan of Zhou in battle but refrained from pursuing him. After Duke Zhuang's death, his son Duke Zhao briefly ruled but was ousted by Duke Li, who was backed by Song. Duke Li killed Ji Zhong but was later driven out, and Duke Zhao returned. Duke Zhao was assassinated by Gao Qumi, leading to a succession of short-lived rulers. Duke Li eventually returned, executed those who betrayed him, and reigned until his death. His son Duke Wen succeeded him.

Duke Wen's reign involved conflicts with Jin and Chu. He was rude to Chong'er (later Duke Wen of Jin), who later besieged Zheng. Duke Wen's son Zilan, favored by Jin, became crown prince and later Duke Mu. Duke Mu's reign saw Qin's failed attack on Zheng, exposed by merchant Xian Gao. Zheng allied with Jin against Qin. After Duke Mu's death, Duke Lin was killed by Zigong over a turtle soup dispute. Duke Xiang succeeded him, facing Chu attacks and shifting alliances between Jin and Chu. Duke Xiang surrendered to Chu but was spared by King Zhuang.

Later rulers included Duke Cheng, who was captured by Jin for allying with Chu, and Duke Li, who was poisoned by Zi Si. Duke Jian's reign featured Zichan, a wise minister who stabilized Zheng through diplomacy and reforms. Zichan's advice to Jin's Duke Ping on gods and governance earned him praise. After Zichan's death, Zheng declined under pressure from Jin's six ministers and Han's invasions. Duke Sheng's reign saw Zichan's death, mourned by all. Confucius wept for Zichan, calling his benevolence a legacy from ancient times.

Zheng's final years were marked by internal strife and Korean aggression. Duke Ai was killed, and subsequent rulers faced Korean attacks. In the twenty-first year of Zheng Junyi's reign, Marquis Ai of Han destroyed Zheng, annexing its territory. Tai Shigong reflected on the fickle nature of power and loyalty, citing Fu Xia's betrayal of Zheng Zi and Li Gong's eventual execution of him, comparing it to Jin's Rick. He noted that even steadfast figures like Xun Xi could not prevent disaster, as unforeseen events often alter outcomes.
