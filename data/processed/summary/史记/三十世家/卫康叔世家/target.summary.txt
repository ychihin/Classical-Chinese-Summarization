**Summary of the Historical Text on the State of Wei**

Uncle Kang of Wei, named Feng, was the half-brother of King Wu of Zhou. After King Wu defeated the Shang dynasty, he granted the remnants of Yin to Wu Geng, King Zhou's son, but worried about rebellion. He sent Guan Shu and Cai Shu to monitor Wu Geng. After King Wu's death, King Cheng was young, and Zhou Gongdan ruled as regent. Guan Shu and Cai Shu, suspecting Zhou Gongdan, rebelled with Wu Geng. Zhou Gongdan suppressed the rebellion, killed Wu Geng and Guan Shu, exiled Cai Shu, and granted the Yin remnants to Kang Shu, making him ruler of Wei. Zhou Gongdan warned Kang Shu to learn from Yin's history, avoid excessive drinking and pleasure, and wrote "Kang Gao," "Jiu Gao," and "Zi Cai" as guidelines. Kang Shu governed well, and the people were content. After King Cheng came of age, he appointed Kang Shu as Sikou and honored him.

After Kang Shu's death, his descendants ruled Wei for generations. Qinghou bribed King Yi of Zhou to have the ruler of Wei titled Marquis. During Lihou's reign, King Li of Zhou fled, and the "Republican Administration" began. Later, Gongbo Yu was forced to commit suicide by his brother He, who became Duke Wu. Duke Wu reformed governance, and the people prospered. He assisted King Ping of Zhou against Quan Rong and was declared duke. After his death, Duke Zhuang succeeded him. Duke Zhuang married a Qi woman who was childless, then married a Chen woman who bore a son who died. Another Chen woman bore Wan, who was made prince. Duke Zhuang also had a son, Zhouxu, who loved military affairs. Minister Shi Que warned Duke Zhuang, but he ignored him. After Duke Zhuang's death, Prince Wan became Duke Huan. Zhouxu, arrogant and extravagant, was deposed and fled. Later, Zhouxu gathered fugitives, killed Duke Huan, and declared himself ruler. He sought to support Zheng Bo's brother Duan, but the people hated him for killing Duke Huan. Shi Que conspired with Chen Hou to kill Zhouxu and established Duke Huan's brother Jin as Duke Xuan.

Duke Xuan's beloved wife Yi Jiang bore a son, Ji, who was made prince. Duke Xuan married a beautiful Qi woman for the prince but took her for himself. She bore Zishou and Zishuo. After Prince Ji's mother died, Zishuo slandered Ji. Duke Xuan, resenting Ji, sent him as envoy to Qi and ordered thieves to kill him. Zishou warned Ji, but Ji refused to disobey his father. Zishou stole Ji's envoy token and was killed first. Ji then arrived and was also killed. Duke Xuan made Zishuo crown prince. After Duke Xuan's death, Zishuo became Duke Hui. The left and right sons resented him, attacked, and made Qian Mou, Ji's brother, ruler. Duke Hui fled to Qi. Later, Duke Xiang of Qi, under Zhou orders, attacked Wei, restored Duke Hui, and killed the left and right sons. Qian Mou fled to Zhou. Duke Hui later attacked Zhou with Yan, forcing King Hui of Zhou to flee. Zheng later restored King Hui. After Duke Hui's death, his son Duke Yi succeeded. Duke Yi loved cranes and indulged in pleasure. When Zhai attacked, soldiers rebelled, and Zhai killed Duke Yi. The people, long dissatisfied with Duke Hui's line, made Zhao Bowan's son Shen, Duke Dai. After his death, his brother Rui became Duke Wen. Duke Wen reduced taxes, judged crimes fairly, and won the people's hearts. He later treated Jin's Chong'er rudely. After Duke Wen's death, his son Duke Cheng succeeded. Duke Cheng refused Jin passage to rescue Song, leading to conflict. Jin attacked Wei, and Duke Cheng fled. He later bribed Zhou officials to avoid poisoning and was restored after Zhou interceded. After Duke Cheng's death, his son Duke Mu succeeded. Duke Mu's reign saw Chu attacks on Chen and Zheng. After his death, his son Duke Ding succeeded, then Duke Xian. Duke Xian whipped musician Cao, who later slandered him. Duke Xian's rude behavior angered Sun Wenzi, who drove him out. Duke Xian fled to Qi. Sun Wenzi and Ning Huizi made Qiu, Duke Xian's brother, Duke Shang. Later, Ning and Sun fought, and Sun fled to Jin, asking Jin to restore Duke Xian. Jin attacked Wei, arrested Duke Shang, and restored Duke Xian. Duke Xian executed Ning happy. After his death, his son Duke Xiang succeeded. Duke Xiang's concubine dreamed of Uncle Kang, who said her son would rule Wei. She bore a son named Yuan, who became Duke Ling. Duke Ling's reign saw Confucius visit Wei. Prince Kuai Chi, resenting Duke Ling's wife Nan Zi, tried to kill her but failed and fled. After Duke Ling's death, his wife made Zi Ying ruler, but he declined, and the people made Zhe, Kuai Chi's son, Duke Chu. Zhao Jianzi tried to restore Kuai Chi, but Wei forces repelled him. Later, Kuai Chi, with help from Kong Kui's mother, returned, hijacked Kong Kui, and became Duke Zhuang. He wanted to kill ministers but relented. After his death, his son succeeded, but Wei faced turmoil. Eventually, Wei was demoted to a small state under Zhao. In the Qin dynasty, Weijun was deposed, ending the Wei lineage.

Tai Shigong remarked on the tragic deaths of Prince Ji and Zishou, noting the sorrow of fathers and sons killing each other and brothers destroying one another.
