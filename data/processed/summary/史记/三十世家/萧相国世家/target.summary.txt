Xiao He, born in Fengyi, Peixian County, was a legal expert and official under the county magistrate. He protected Liu Bang when Liu Bang was a commoner and later as a pavilion chief. When Liu Bang served in Xianyang, Xiao He gave him five hundred coins, more than others. Xiao He became historian of Sishui County, ranking first in assessments, but declined a transfer recommended by a Qin censor.

When Liu Bang became Pei Gong, Xiao He assisted him. After entering Xianyang, while others seized treasure, Xiao He collected Qin legal documents, maps, and household records. This allowed Liu Bang to understand the realm's geography and population. Xiao He recommended Han Xin as general. As prime minister, he governed Bashu and Guanzhong, managing laws, temples, and supplies. He reported to Liu Bang or acted as needed, recruiting soldiers to replenish the army.

In the third year of Han, Liu Bang sent envoys to Xiao He, suspecting him. Bao Sheng advised Xiao He to send his family to the army, which Liu Bang approved. After Xiang Yu's defeat, Liu Bang ranked Xiao He first among heroes, comparing him to a hunter versus hounds, and citing Xiao He's clan contributions. E Qianqiu supported this, noting Xiao He's consistent support during crises. Xiao He was ranked first, allowed special court privileges, and granted fiefs.

During Chen Xi's rebellion, Xiao He helped Empress Lu kill Han Xin. Liu Bang rewarded him with guards, but Zhao Ping warned this indicated suspicion. Xiao He refused the reward and donated to the army, pleasing Liu Bang. During Yingbu's rebellion, a retainer advised Xiao He to ruin his reputation by buying land cheaply to ease Liu Bang's fears. Xiao He complied, and Liu Bang was happy. Later, Liu Bang imprisoned Xiao He for requesting Shanglin Garden for the people, but a guard argued Xiao He's duty, and Liu Bang released him.

Xiao He and Cao Shen were not on good terms, but on his deathbed, Xiao He recommended Cao Shen as his successor. Xiao He bought land in poor areas and built simple homes to teach frugality. He died in the second year of Xiaohui's reign, posthumously named Wenzhong Hou. His descendants lost the title four times but were reinstated, unmatched among heroes. Sima Qian noted Xiao He's rise from a minor official to a prominent figure, adapting laws to people's needs and achieving lasting fame.
