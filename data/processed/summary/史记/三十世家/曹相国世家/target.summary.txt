Cao Shen, Marquis of Pingyang, was born in Peixian County and served as a warden there during the Qin Dynasty. He followed Emperor Gaozu of Han (then Duke of Pei) as a Zhongjuan, leading military campaigns against Qin forces, including attacks on Huling, Fangyu, Xuexian, and Dang County. He defeated generals such as Zhang Han, Li You, and Wang Li, capturing cities and officials. After Xiang Yu named Liu Bang King of Han, Cao Shen was granted the title Marquis of Jiancheng and later promoted to general. He helped pacify the Three Qin Dynasties, capturing territories like Xiabian and Yongxian, and besieged Zhang Han in Feiqiu.

Cao Shen served as acting Prime Minister Zuo, suppressing rebellions by King Bao of Wei and others, capturing Wei and Zhao territories. He followed Han Xin to defeat Qi, pacifying over seventy counties. After Xiang Yu's death, Cao Shen became Prime Minister of Qi under King Liu Fei, governing with Huang Lao principles of purity and inaction for nine years, stabilizing the state. When Xiao He died, Cao Shen succeeded him as Han Prime Minister, adhering strictly to Xiao He's laws without changes. He selected steady officials, dismissed those seeking reputation, and spent his time drinking, avoiding interference in minor affairs. When Emperor Hui questioned his governance, Cao Shen argued that following the established laws of Emperor Gaozu and Xiao He was sufficient.

Cao Shen served as Prime Minister for three years, dying with the posthumous title Yihou. His descendants inherited the title until Cao Zong was executed for involvement in a mutiny, ending the feudal state. The people praised Cao Shen for maintaining Xiao He's clear laws and allowing peace through non-interference.
