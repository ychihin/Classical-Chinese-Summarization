Chen Ping, a native of Humiao Township, Yangwu County, was born into poverty but loved studying. His brother Chen Bo farmed while Chen Ping studied abroad. Tall and handsome, Chen Ping faced criticism for his lack of labor, leading his sister-in-law to complain. Chen Bo divorced her upon hearing this. When Chen Ping sought marriage, wealthy families refused him, and he hesitated to marry a poor woman. Eventually, he married Zhang Fu's granddaughter, who had been widowed five times. Zhang Fu, impressed by Chen Ping's appearance and potential, lent him money for the wedding, advising his granddaughter to serve the Chen family respectfully. After marriage, Chen Ping's wealth and connections grew.

Chen Ping presided over cutting sacrificial meat for the earth god, distributing it evenly, and remarked, "If I ruled the world, I would divide it like this." During the Chen Sheng uprising, he served Wei Wang Jiu but left after his advice was ignored. He then joined Xiang Yu, who appointed him Lord Xinwu. Fearing execution after a failed campaign, Chen Ping fled to the Han king, Liu Bang, who made him a captain. Despite slander from generals like Zhou Bo and Guan Ying about his past and alleged bribery, Chen Ping defended himself, and Liu Bang rewarded him generously, appointing him to supervise the army.

Chen Ping advised Liu Bang to use gold to sow discord among Xiang Yu's ministers, leading to Xiang Yu's suspicion of Fan Zeng and others. Fan Zeng resigned and died. Chen Ping then helped Liu Bang escape Xingyang by sending women out the east gate while they fled west. Later, he advised against attacking Han Xin directly, instead capturing him during a meeting in Chen County. Liu Bang granted Chen Ping the title of Huyouhou, which Chen Ping attributed to Wei Wuzhi's recommendation.

Chen Ping devised six secret strategies, including rescuing Liu Bang from a Xiongnu siege at Pingcheng. After Liu Bang's death, Chen Ping avoided Empress Lu's suspicion by pretending to indulge in drinking and women. He conspired with Zhou Bo to eliminate the Lu clan and install Emperor Xiaowen. Chen Ping later ceded the position of Right Prime Minister to Zhou Bo, citing Zhou Bo's greater role in suppressing the Lu family. When Emperor Xiaowen questioned the prime ministers about state affairs, Zhou Bo faltered, while Chen Ping explained the prime minister's role in regulating yin and yang and ensuring officials fulfilled their duties. Zhou Bo resigned, and Chen Ping served as sole prime minister until his death in 166 BC.

Chen Ping's descendants inherited his title but lost it due to crimes. He once remarked that his secretive strategies were taboo in Taoism, predicting his descendants' decline. Sima Qian praised Chen Ping for his wisdom and ability to navigate crises, maintaining a glorious reputation as a virtuous prime minister.
