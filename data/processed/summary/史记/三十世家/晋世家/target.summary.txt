# Summary of the Historical Text on the State of Jin

Tang Shuyu of Jin was the son of King Wu of Zhou and younger brother of King Cheng. His mother dreamed that God would give her a son named Yu who would receive the land of Tang. When born, the character "Yu" was found on his palm, so he was named Yu. After King Wu died, King Cheng succeeded and destroyed the rebellious Tang state. While playing, King Cheng cut a tung tree leaf into a jade shape and gave it to Shuyu, saying he would enfeoff him. When asked to formalize this, King Cheng claimed he was joking, but Shi Yi insisted the emperor has no jokes. Thus, Tang was granted to Shuyu, who became known as Tang Shuyu.

The succession continued through generations: Xie became Marquis of Jin, followed by Marquis Wu, Marquis Cheng, Marquis Li, and Marquis Jing. From Tang Shuyu to Jinghou across five generations, reign lengths were unrecorded. In Jinghou's 17th year, King Li of Zhou fled due to popular unrest, and ministers governed during the "Republic" period. After Jinghou died, his son Lihou succeeded. The line continued through Xianhou, Muhou, and others. During Muhou's reign, his wife gave birth to a prince named Qiu (meaning hatred) and a younger son named Chengshi (meaning success). A Jin teacher warned these names would bring chaos.

After Muhou died, his brother Uncle Shang seized power, forcing Prince Qiu to flee. In the fourth year of Uncle Shang's reign, Prince Qiu attacked and established himself as Wenhou. In Wenhou's 10th year, King You of Zhou was killed, and the Zhou royal family moved eastward. Wenhou's son Zhaohou granted Quwo to his younger brother, who became Uncle Huan of Quwo. Quwo grew larger than the capital, and a gentleman predicted turmoil would originate there. In Zhaohou's seventh year, Pan's father killed Zhaohou and tried to install Uncle Huan, but Jin forces defeated him and established Zhaohou's son as Xiaohou.

The conflict between Quwo and Jin continued for generations. Quwo Zhuangbo killed Xiaohou, and later Quwo Wugong killed Aihou and Xiaozi. Finally, in the 28th year of Hou Min, Duke Wu of Quwo destroyed Jin, presented its treasures to King Li of Zhou, and was appointed ruler of Jin. Duke Wu had reigned 37 years in Quwo before becoming Duke Wu of Jin. He died two years after replacing the Jin ruler, and his son Xian Gong succeeded.

Duke Xian attacked Li Rong, obtained Li Ji and her sister, and favored them greatly. Li Ji gave birth to Xi Qi. Duke Xian planned to depose Crown Prince Shen Sheng, sending him to garrison Quwo while sending Chong'er and Yiwu elsewhere. Li Ji falsely accused Shen Sheng of poisoning food meant for Duke Xian. Unable to prove his innocence, Shen Sheng committed suicide. Chong'er and Yiwu fled in fear. Duke Xian sent troops against them, and Chong'er escaped to Di while Yiwu fled to Liang.

After Duke Xian died, Li Ke killed Xi Qi and his brother, then invited Chong'er to return, but he refused. Yiwu was brought back with Qin's help and became Duke Hui. He broke promises to grant land to Qin and reward Li Ke, instead forcing Li Ke to commit suicide. When Qin suffered famine, Jin refused aid and attacked Qin instead. Duke Hui was captured at the Battle of Han Yuan but later released. After Duke Hui died, his son Duke Huai succeeded but was killed by Qin forces escorting Chong'er back.

Chong'er, Duke Wen, had fled for 19 years, traveling through Di, Qi, Cao, Song, Zheng, Chu, and finally Qin. He returned at age 62 and was welcomed by most Jin people. After securing his position, he rewarded followers but initially forgot Jie Zitui, who hid in Mianshang Mountain and died there. Duke Wen later granted the mountain to Jie Zitui's memory. Duke Wen became hegemon after assisting King Xiang of Zhou and defeating Chu at Chengpu. After his death, his son Duke Xiang succeeded.

The text continues through many generations of Jin rulers, including Duke Ling (who was killed for his tyranny), Duke Cheng, Duke Jing, Duke Li, and Duke Mourning. The power of the six ministerial families grew while the ducal house weakened. Eventually, Zhao, Han, and Wei divided Jin among themselves. In the second year of Duke Jing, these three families destroyed Jin, reduced the duke to commoner status, and ended the state's sacrifices.
