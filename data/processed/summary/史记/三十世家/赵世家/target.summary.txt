The Zhao family shares a common ancestor with the Qin people. Their lineage traces back to Zhongyan, who served as a charioteer for Emperor Taiwu of the Shang Dynasty. His descendant Feilian had two sons: Eilai, who served King Zhou and was killed by the Zhou people, becoming the ancestor of the Ying Qin family; and Ji Sheng, whose descendants became the Zhao family. Ji Sheng's line included Meng Zeng, favored by King Cheng of Zhou, and eventually Zaofu, who served King Mu of Zhou. Zaofu presented eight fine horses to King Mu and drove his chariot westward, where the king met the Queen Mother of the West. When King Xu Yan rebelled, Zaofu drove King Mu thousands of miles in a day to crush the rebellion, and was rewarded with the city of Zhao, establishing the Zhao family name.

Six generations later, Yanfu served King Xuan of Zhou as a charioteer and saved him at the Battle of Thousand Acres. His son Shudai left the debauched court of King You of Zhou to serve Marquis Wen of Jin, beginning the Zhao family's rise in Jin. Five generations later, Zhao Su conquered the states of Huo, Wei, and Geng for Duke Xian of Jin. When a drought struck Jin, divination revealed the god of Mount Huo Tai was angry, so Zhao Su restored the king of Huo to appease the god, and the drought ended. Zhao Shui, a descendant, used divination to decide to serve Prince Chong'er, following him into exile for nineteen years. After Chong'er became Duke Wen of Jin, Zhao Shui became a high official and helped him become hegemon.

After Zhao Shui's death, his son Zhao Dun succeeded him. When Duke Xiang of Jin died, Zhao Dun initially planned to install a younger brother as ruler due to the young age of the crown prince, but the prince's mother's pleas led him to install the crown prince as Duke Ling of Jin. Duke Ling grew tyrannical, and when he tried to kill Zhao Dun, Zhao Dun fled. Before he could escape the country, Zhao Chuan killed Duke Ling. Zhao Dun returned to preside over state affairs, but historians recorded that he killed the king because he did not punish the rebels. Zhao Dun's son Zhao Shuo married Duke Cheng of Jin's sister. During Duke Jing's reign, the official Tu Anjia, seeking to destroy the Zhao family, falsely accused Zhao Dun of regicide and attacked the Zhao residence, killing Zhao Shuo and his brothers, though Zhao Shuo's pregnant wife escaped to the palace.

Zhao Shuo's wife gave birth to a son, Zhao Wu. Tu Anjia searched for the baby, but it was hidden. Zhao Shuo's retainers, Cheng Ying and Gongsun Chujiu, devised a plan: Chujiu hid with a substitute baby, and Cheng Ying "betrayed" him to the generals. The generals killed Chujiu and the substitute, believing the Zhao line extinct. Cheng Ying then hid the real Zhao Wu in the mountains for fifteen years. When Duke Jing fell ill, divination revealed the Zhao family's sacrifices were causing trouble. Han Jue, knowing the truth, told the duke, who then restored Zhao Wu and destroyed Tu Anjia's family. After Zhao Wu came of age, Cheng Ying committed suicide to report to his fallen comrades.

Zhao Wu's descendants grew powerful. His son Zhao Yang, Zhao Jianzi, became a dominant minister. He defeated the Fan and Zhonghang clans, expanded Zhao territory, and was named Shangqing, effectively monopolizing Jin's government. His son Zhao Wuxie, Zhao Xiangzi, succeeded him. Xiangzi killed the king of Dai and annexed his land. When Zhibo demanded territory, Xiangzi refused, leading to a siege of Jinyang. With divine guidance from the gods of Mount Huotai, Xiangzi allied with Han and Wei to destroy Zhibo and divide his lands. Zhao became stronger than Han and Wei.

Xiangzi refused to make his own son heir, instead passing rule to his grandson Zhao Huan, who became Marquis Xian. After a brief usurpation by Xiangzi's brother, Xianhou's line continued. Subsequent rulers included Liehou, Jinghou, Chenghou, and Suhou. During Suhou's reign, Shang Yang of Qin attacked Wei. Suhou's son, King Wuling, ascended the throne. King Wuling, seeking to strengthen Zhao against enemies like Zhongshan and the Hu tribes, famously adopted Hu clothing and cavalry archery, overcoming opposition from nobles. He conquered Zhongshan and expanded Zhao's territory significantly. He later abdicated in favor of his son, King Huiwen, but a succession struggle led to his death by starvation after his son Zhao Zhang rebelled.

Under King Huiwen and his successor King Xiaocheng, Zhao faced the rising power of Qin. King Xiaocheng accepted the surrender of Shangdang from Korea, provoking Qin. This led to the disastrous Battle of Changping, where Zhao Kuo replaced Lian Po and was defeated by Bai Qi, with over 400,000 Zhao soldiers killed. Qin besieged Handan, but Zhao was saved by reinforcements from Chu and Wei. Later, King Xiaocheng's son, King Daoxiang, and his grandson, King Youmiao, ruled as Qin's power grew. Li Mu, a capable general, won victories against Qin, but was killed due to slander. Finally, in 228 BCE, Qin captured Handan, ending the Zhao state. Zhao Jia, a brother of the last king, briefly ruled in Dai before being defeated, and Zhao was made into a Qin commandery.
