Zhang Liang, also known as Liu Hou, was a descendant of Korean nobility. His grandfather and father served as prime ministers to five generations of Korean kings. After Qin destroyed Korea, Zhang Liang, though young and never an official, used his family's wealth to hire an assassin to attack Qin Shi Huang at Bolangsha, but mistakenly struck an auxiliary carriage. Forced to flee, he changed his name and hid in Xiapi.

While wandering on a bridge in Xiapi, Zhang Liang encountered an old man who tested him by throwing his shoes and demanding Zhang Liang retrieve and put them on. After passing this test, the oldman gave him the "Tai Gong's Art of War," declaring Zhang Liang would become the emperor's teacher. Zhang Liang studied the book diligently.

During the rebellions against Qin, Zhang Liang gathered followers and joined Pei Gong (Liu Bang), becoming his trusted advisor. He used strategies from the Art of War to advise Pei Gong, who appreciated his counsel. Zhang Liang persuaded Xiang Liang to restore Han Cheng as King of Han, and Zhang Liang served as his Situ. He later rejoined Pei Gong, advising him against attacking the Qin army at Yao Pass directly, instead suggesting bribery and deception, leading to Qin's surrender.

After entering Xianyang, Zhang Liang convinced Pei Gong to leave the Qin palace and avoid luxury, warning it would harm his cause. At the Hongmen Banquet, Zhang Liang helped Pei Gong reconcile with Xiang Yu. When Pei Gong became King of Han, Zhang Liang advised burning the plank road to show no intention of returning east, stabilizing Xiang Yu. He later recommended using Yingbu, Peng Yue, and Han Xin to defeat Chu.

Zhang Liang opposed enfeoffing descendants of the six kingdoms, arguing it would undermine Liu Bang's support. He convinced Liu Bang to grant Han Xin the title of King of Qi. After the Han dynasty was established, Liu Bang praised Zhang Liang's strategic contributions and offered him 30,000 households, but Zhang Liang accepted only a county, becoming Marquis of Liu.

Zhang Liang advised moving the capital to Guanzhong for its strategic advantages. When Liu Bang wanted to depose the crown prince, Zhang Liang suggested inviting four reclusive sages to support the prince. Their presence convinced Liu Bang the prince's position was secure. Zhang Liang later practiced Taoist techniques, abstained from grain, and sought to travel with the immortal Chisongzi. He died eight years after Liu Bang, posthumously named Wencheng Hou. His son inherited the title but lost it due to misconduct.
