Taibo Wu and his younger brother Zhong Yong were sons of Gu Gong Danfu, King Tai of Zhou. Because their father wished to pass the throne to their younger brother Ji Li, and eventually to Ji Li's virtuous son Chang, Taibo and Zhong Yong fled to the land of Jingman. There, they adopted local customs by tattooing their bodies and cutting their hair to show they could not inherit the throne. The Jingman people, finding Taibo righteous, followed him and respected him as Taibo Wu. Taibo died without a son, and Zhong Yong succeeded him. The lineage continued until Zhou Zhang, who was officially granted the land of Wu by King Wu of Zhou after he defeated the Yin dynasty. The kingdom of Wu was passed down through generations, eventually becoming prosperous under King Shoumeng, who was the nineteenth generation from Taibo.

Shoumeng had four sons: Zhufan, Yuji, Yumei, and the virtuous Jizha. Shoumeng wanted Jizha to succeed him, but Jizha refused. The throne was then passed from brother to brother with the intention that it would eventually reach Jizha. After Yumei died, Jizha again fled, so the people established Yumei's son Liao as king. Prince Guang, the son of Zhufan, believed he should be king and secretly recruited a warrior named Zhuan Zhu. During a banquet, Zhuan Zhu assassinated King Liao, and Prince Guang became King Helu. Jizha, upon returning, accepted the new king, stating that as long as the ancestral sacrifices continued, the new king was his sovereign.

Under King Helu, Wu Zixu and Bo Pei were appointed to official positions. Helu, with the help of Wu Zixu, Sun Wu, and the states of Tang and Cai, launched a major attack on Chu, defeating the Chu army five times and capturing the capital, Ying. Wu Zixu and Bo Pei dug up the body of King Ping of Chu to avenge their fathers. Later, Helu's brother Fugai attempted to usurp the throne but was defeated and fled to Chu. In the nineteenth year, King Helu attacked Yue but was wounded and died. Before his death, he told his son Fuchai to remember that Gou Jian of Yue killed his father.

King Fuchai avenged his father by defeating Yue at Fujiao. King Gou Jian of Yue was forced to hide in Kuaiji Mountain and sought peace through the Grand Prime Minister Bo Pei. Wu Zixu advised against this, comparing Gou Jian to Shaokang, who restored the Xia dynasty from a small territory. He warned that Gou Jian was a threat, but the King of Wu listened to Bo Pei and made peace with Yue. Fuchai then focused on northern campaigns against Qi, ignoring Zixu's warnings about the greater threat from Yue. Zixu was eventually forced to commit suicide, asking for his eyes to be placed on the east gate of Wu's capital to see Yue's invasion. While Fuchai was at the Huangchi meeting vying for supremacy with Jin, Yue attacked Wu, capturing the crown prince. After the meeting, Fuchai sought peace with Yue. Eventually, Yue grew stronger, defeated Wu in battle, and besieged its capital. King Fuchai, regretting not listening to Zixu, committed suicide. The King of Yue destroyed Wu and killed Dazai Bo Pei for his disloyalty.
