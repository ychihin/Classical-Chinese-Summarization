Zhou Bo, a native of Peixian County, began his career as a weaver and funeral musician before becoming a warrior. He followed Liu Bang (later Emperor Gaozu) from the early days of his uprising, participating in numerous battles against the Qin dynasty and other forces. Zhou Bo distinguished himself in combat, often being the first to scale city walls, and was granted titles and rewards, including the Marquis of Weiwu and later the Marquis of Jiang, with a fief of 8,180 households. He continued to serve as a general under Gaozu, suppressing rebellions by Zang Tu, Han Xin, Chen Xi, and Lu Wan, pacifying many counties and capturing numerous enemy officials. Zhou Bo was known for his simplicity, strength, and loyalty, though he had little regard for scholarly learning.

After Gaozu's death, Zhou Bo served Emperor Hui as a prince and later as Taiwei. Following Empress Lu's death, the Lu family attempted to seize power. Zhou Bo, alongside Chen Ping, plotted to annihilate the Lu clan and established Emperor Wen. For this, he was appointed Right Prime Minister and given generous rewards. However, fearing his own power, he resigned and returned to his fief. Later, he was falsely accused of rebellion, imprisoned, and humiliated by jailers. He was eventually released after his daughter (the emperor's daughter-in-law) and Empress Dowager Bo interceded. He died in the eleventh year of Emperor Wen's reign, posthumously titled Marquis Wu.

Zhou Bo's son Zhou Shengzhi inherited the title but lost it due to murder. Emperor Wen then selected Zhou Yafu, another son, as Marquis of Tiao. Xu Negation predicted Zhou Yafu would be a marquis, general, and prime minister but would starve to death. Zhou Yafu became a general, famously commanding the Xiliu camp with strict discipline, impressing Emperor Wen. During the Rebellion of the Seven States, he served as Taiwei, successfully defeating Wu and Chu by cutting off their food supplies, though this earned him the enmity of King Xiao of Liang. He later became prime minister but fell out of favor with Emperor Jing over disputes regarding the appointment of marquises, including opposing the enfeoffment of the queen's brother Wang Xin and Xiongnu defectors.

Zhou Yafu was eventually dismissed as prime minister. After a banquet where he showed dissatisfaction with the lack of chopsticks, Emperor Jing viewed him as unfit for service. His son's purchase of burial armor led to accusations of rebellion. Zhou Yafu was imprisoned, refused to eat for five days, and died of vomiting blood. His fief was abolished. Sima Qian commented that while Zhou Bo saved the state from crisis, Zhou Yafu, despite his military dignity, lacked humility and met a tragic end.
