# Summary of the Zhou Dynasty Historical Records

Hou Ji, ancestor of the Zhou Dynasty, was named Qi. His mother Jiang Yuan, a concubine of the emperor, became pregnant after stepping on a giant footprint. She bore a son but tried to abandon him multiple times. Miraculously, animals protected him, so she raised him and named him "Abandoned." As a child, Qi showed great ambition, excelling at planting crops. When he grew up, he became skilled at farming, observing land characteristics to choose suitable grains. Emperor Yao appointed him as a farmer to teach agriculture, and Emperor Shun enfeoffed him in Tai, calling him Hou Ji, with Ji as his surname.

After Hou Ji died, his son Buchu succeeded. During Buchu's later years, the Xia dynasty declined and abandoned agriculture. Buchu fled to the Rongdi region. His descendant Gong Liu revived Hou Ji's agricultural practices, crossing rivers to obtain timber, improving people's lives. Many people moved to join him, marking the beginning of Zhou's prosperity. Gong Liu's descendant Gu Gongdanfu further accumulated virtues and benevolence. When Rongdi tribes attacked, seeking land and people, Gu Gong refused to fight, saying he could not bear sacrificing people for his own sake. He moved with his relatives to the foot of Qishan Mountain, where many people followed him. He built cities, established towns, and created five official positions.

Gu Gong's eldest son Taibo and second son Yu Zhong fled to Jingman, giving way to their younger brother Ji Li, whose son Chang (King Wen) would achieve greatness. King Wen inherited the legacy of Hou Ji and Gong Liu, practicing benevolence, respecting elders, and treating wise men with courtesy. Many scholars and officials submitted to him. Chonghou Hu slandered him to King Zhou of Yin, who imprisoned him in Youli. Officials presented rare treasures to King Zhou, who freed King Wen and gave him the right to conquer neighboring princes. King Wen secretly did good deeds, and when people from Yu and Rui came to him with disputes, they saw the humility of Zhou's people and felt ashamed, resolving their conflicts peacefully.

King Wen conquered several states and moved the capital to Feng. After his death, his son King Wu ascended. In the ninth year of his reign, King Wu marched eastward to Mengjin, where eight hundred princes formed an alliance. Two years later, hearing of Yin Zhou's increasing tyranny, King Wu led 300 chariots, 3,000 warriors, and 45,000 armored soldiers eastward. At Muye, he swore an oath condemning King Zhou for listening to women, abandoning ancestors, and oppressing the people. Although King Zhou's army numbered 700,000, they had no will to fight and turned their weapons against him. King Zhou fled to the Deer Platform and burned himself to death. King Wu shot arrows at his corpse and beheaded him.

After conquering Yin, King Wu released prisoners, distributed wealth to the poor, repaired Bigan's tomb, and divided feudal territories. He honored descendants of ancient sage kings and granted lands to his brothers and officials. King Wu could not sleep at night, worrying about stabilizing the kingdom. He planned to build Zhou city in Luoyi. Two years after defeating Yin, King Wu fell ill and died. His young son King Cheng ascended, with Duke Zhou acting as regent. Guan Shu and Cai Shu rebelled with Wu Geng, but Duke Zhou suppressed the rebellion, killed Wu Geng and Guan Shu, and exiled Cai Shu.

King Cheng and King Kang's reigns saw peace for over forty years without using punishments. The Zhou royal family declined during King Zhao's reign. King Mu, who ascended at age fifty, restored order but foolishly attacked the Quanrong, causing distant states to stop paying tribute. King Li was greedy and tyrannical, suppressing criticism by killing those who spoke out. Duke Zhao warned him that blocking people's mouths was more dangerous than blocking water, but King Li refused to listen. After three years, the people rebelled and King Li fled. The country was governed by two dukes during the Republic period.

King Xuan restored Zhou's prestige, but King You foolishly lit beacon fires to amuse his concubine Bao Si, causing the princes to lose trust. He deposed his queen and prince, provoking Marquis Shen to join with Quanrong to attack and kill King You. King Ping moved the capital east to Luoyi. The Zhou royal family declined as powerful states like Qi, Chu, Qin, and Jin grew strong. Over subsequent generations, Zhou kings lost authority, with political orders coming from hegemonic monarchs. Eventually, Qin conquered the Western Zhou, obtaining the sacred nine tripods. Seven years later, Qin destroyed the Eastern Zhou, ending the Zhou Dynasty's sacrificial traditions.
