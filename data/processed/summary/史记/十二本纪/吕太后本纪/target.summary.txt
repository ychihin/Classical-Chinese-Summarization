Empress Dowager Lu was the humble wife of Emperor Gaozu and mother of Emperor Xiaohui and Princess Lu Yuan. Gaozu favored Concubine Qi, who bore King Ruyi of Zhao, and often considered deposing the kind and weak Crown Prince Xiaohui in favor of Ruyi. Empress Lu, growing estranged from Gaozu, was resolute and assisted in pacifying the realm and killing ministers. After Gaozu's death in 195 BCE, Xiaohui succeeded him.

Empress Lu resented Concubine Qi and King Ruyi. She imprisoned Concubine Qi and summoned Ruyi to the capital. Emperor Xiaohui protected Ruyi, but during a hunt, the Queen Mother poisoned Ruyi. She then mutilated Concubine Qi, calling her a "human pig." Xiaohui, horrified, fell ill and abandoned court affairs, dying in 188 BCE. During his funeral, the Queen Mother cried without tears until ministers, following advice, appointed Lu family members as generals, securing her power.

The Queen Mother then ruled as regent, intending to make Lu family members kings. She consulted ministers; Wang Ling opposed, citing Gaozu's oath, while Chen Ping and Zhou Bo supported her. She deposed Wang Ling and promoted Chen Ping. She made Lu Tai King of Lu, and after his death, Lu Jia succeeded. She also made several of Xiaohui's sons kings, though they were not his biological children. The Queen Mother deposed and killed a young emperor who threatened rebellion, replacing him with Liu Yi, renamed Hong.

The Queen Mother continued to consolidate Lu power, killing or deposing several Zhao kings, including Liu You, who starved to death after opposing the Lu family. She made Lu Lu King of Zhao and Lu Chan King of Liang. In 180 BCE, she fell ill and warned Lu Chan and Lu Lu to control the army, fearing rebellion. She died, leaving edicts for rewards and amnesty.

After her death, the Lu family plotted rebellion. King Qi of Liu led forces against them, and ministers like Zhou Bo and Chen Ping conspired to remove the Lu. Zhou Bo, using deception, gained command of the Northern Army, and the soldiers supported the Liu family. Lu Lu surrendered his command, and Lu Chan was killed. The Lu family was exterminated, including Lu Lu and Lu Yu.

The ministers, fearing the young emperor was not truly Xiaohui's son, chose Liu Heng, King of Dai, as emperor due to his maturity and virtue. He became Emperor Xiaowen, reigning for 23 years. The historian notes that during Empress Lu's rule, the people recovered from war, punishments were rare, and agriculture flourished, despite her cruelty and nepotism.
