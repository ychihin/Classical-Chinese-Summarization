Emperor Xiaojing, eldest son of Emperor Xiaowen and Queen Mother Dou, succeeded to the throne after the former queen and her three sons died. In his first year, he granted amnesty, reduced land rent, built Taizong Temple, and signed a peace treaty with the Xiongnu. In the second year, he ennobled Xiao He's grandson and set the age of registration for men at twenty. Queen Mother Dou died, and officials like Shen Tujia passed away. Comets, hailstorms, and celestial anomalies occurred. In the third year, a rebellion by seven kings, including Wu and Chu, was suppressed after executing Chao Cuo. The emperor appointed Dou Ying and Zhou Yafu to lead troops, defeating the rebels. He then established new kings and marquises.

In the fourth year, the crown prince was established, and Liu Che was made King of Jiaodong. Reforms included setting up checkpoints at ferries. In the fifth year, Yangling was built, and people were recruited to move there. In the sixth year, several officials were ennobled. In the seventh year, Prince Li was deposed, and Zhou Yafu became prime minister. The crown prince was renamed Toru. In the first year of Zhongyuan, amnesty was granted, and restrictions on merchants and criminals were lifted. Earthquakes and hail occurred. In the second year, the Xiongnu invaded, and King Linjiang died. In the third year, Xiongnu kings surrendered, and Zhou Yafu was dismissed. In the fourth year, Deyang Palace was built, and locusts plagued the land. In the fifth year, severe flooding led to renaming officials. In the sixth year, the emperor worshipped the Five Emperors, and the Liang Kingdom was divided. Official titles were reformed.

In the first year of Houyuan, officials were renamed, and amnesty was granted. Earthquakes and solar eclipses occurred. In the second year, General Zhi attacked the Xiongnu, and restrictions on grain and clothing were imposed. Drought and plague spread. In the third year, celestial anomalies appeared, and Crown Prince Liu Che was crowned. Emperor Xiaojing died, granting titles and coins to the people, releasing palace maids. Prince ascended as Emperor Xiaowu. Tai Shigong noted that Emperor Xiaojing faced the seven kingdoms' rebellion due to strong princely power, which was later stabilized by Zhufu Yan's strategies.
