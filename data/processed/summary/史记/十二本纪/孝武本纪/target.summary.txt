Emperor Xiaowu, the middle son of Emperor Xiaojing, ascended the throne after his father's sixteen-year reign. Upon becoming emperor, he showed particular devotion to worshipping ghosts and gods. During the early years of his reign, the Han Dynasty had enjoyed over sixty years of peace, and officials hoped the emperor would perform the Feng and Shan sacrifices to heaven and earth. The emperor, favoring Confucian scholarship, recruited talented individuals like Zhao Wan and Wang Zang, who proposed building a Mingtang for political and religious clarification. However, Empress Dowager Dou, who favored Huang-Lao teachings, opposed Confucianism, leading to Zhao Wan and Wang Zang's suicides and the abandonment of their plans. After the empress dowager's death six years later, the emperor recruited Gongsun Hong and others, and began performing suburban sacrifices every three years.

The emperor invited a divine spirit, originally a woman from Changling who died grieving for her son, to be worshipped in the Shanglin Garden. He also honored Li Shaojun, an alchemist who claimed to be seventy years old and capable of driving ghosts and making people immortal. Li Shaojun demonstrated his powers by identifying an ancient bronze vessel and advised the emperor to worship the stove, turn cinnabar into gold, and seek immortals on Penglai Mountain to achieve immortality. The emperor followed his advice, worshipping the stove and sending alchemists to the sea. After Li Shaojun's death, the emperor believed he had ascended to immortality and continued seeking alchemists.

Various alchemists presented methods for worshipping gods, including Bo Youji's teachings on Taiyi, the most noble god. The emperor built altars and performed sacrifices accordingly. Later, a young man named General Wencheng claimed to summon spirits and was rewarded, but his methods eventually failed, and he was executed after being caught deceiving the emperor. The emperor fell ill but recovered after consulting a wizard in Ganquan Palace, where he built the Longevity Palace and continued worshipping gods.

The emperor adopted year names based on auspicious signs, such as Yuanguang and Yuanshou. He performed the Hou Tu sacrifice at Fenyin and inspected various counties. Luan Da, recommended by Marquis Lecheng, claimed to have met immortals and was granted high honors, including marriage to a princess. However, his methods also failed, leading to his execution. The emperor continued seeking immortals, building temples and altars, and eventually performed the Feng and Shan sacrifices at Mount Tai in the first year of Yuanfeng.

The emperor established a system of suburban sacrifices every three years and Feng and Shan ceremonies every five years. Despite numerous efforts by alchemists to find immortals, none succeeded. The emperor grew tired of their claims but continued hoping to meet real immortals. Taishi Gong, who accompanied the emperor on his tours, recorded these events to reveal the true nature of the worship practices, noting that specific details of rituals were handled by the responsible officials.
