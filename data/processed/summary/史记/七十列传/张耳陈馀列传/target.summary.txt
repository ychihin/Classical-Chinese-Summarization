Zhang Er, a native of Daliang in Wei State, was once a guest of Wei Prince Wuji. After being expelled from the local register, he fled to Waihuang, where a wealthy woman left her foolish husband to marry him on the advice of her father's friend. With her family's support, Zhang Er traveled widely and gained fame, eventually becoming a county magistrate. Chen Yu, also from Daliang, was a Confucian scholar who married into a wealthy family. He served Zhang Er like a father, and they formed a close bond.

After Qin destroyed Wei, both men became wanted fugitives. They changed their names and worked as guards in Chendi. When a minor official whipped Chen Yu, Zhang Er restrained him from resisting, advising patience. When Chen She rebelled, Zhang Er and Chen Yu advised him not to proclaim himself king too early but to restore the Six Kingdoms to weaken Qin. Chen She ignored them and made himself king. They then helped Wu Chen lead an army to conquer Zhao, where they persuaded him to declare himself King of Zhao. Chen Yu became general, and Zhang Er became prime minister.

When Qin forces besieged Julu, Zhang Er and King Zhao Xie were trapped inside. Chen Yu, with insufficient troops, hesitated to rescue them. Zhang Er sent messengers to rebuke him, but Chen Yu's cautious approach led to a rift. After Xiang Yu defeated Qin at Julu, Zhang Er blamed Chen Yu for the deaths of his messengers. Chen Yu angrily offered his seal, which Zhang Er accepted, deepening their enmity.

Xiang Yu enfeoffed Zhang Er as King of Changshan but gave Chen Yu only three counties. Chen Yu, resentful, allied with Qi to attack Zhang Er, who fled to Han. Han Xin helped Zhang Er defeat and kill Chen Yu, and Zhang Er was made King of Zhao. He died in the fifth year of Han, succeeded by his son Zhang Ao.

Zhang Ao married Princess Lu Yuan, daughter of Emperor Gaozu. When Gaozu treated him arrogantly, Zhang Ao's retainers Guan Gao and Zhao Wu plotted to assassinate the emperor. The plot was discovered, and Zhang Ao was arrested. Guan Gao took full responsibility, enduring torture to prove Zhang Ao's innocence. Gaozu eventually pardoned Zhang Ao, and Guan Gao committed suicide. Zhang Ao was later made Marquis of Xuanping. His descendants held high offices until the Lu clan's downfall led to their demotion, though some were later restored.

The historian comments that Zhang Er and Chen Yu, once loyal friends, became bitter enemies over power and territory, revealing that their bond was rooted in self-interest rather than true virtue.
