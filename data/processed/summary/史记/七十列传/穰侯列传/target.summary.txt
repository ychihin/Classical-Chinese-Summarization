Ranghou Wei Ran, younger brother of Queen Mother Xuan, was a key figure in the Qin state during King Zhao's reign. After King Wu died without heirs, Wei Ran supported King Zhao's ascension, quelled rebellions, and eliminated rivals. King Zhao appointed him general to guard Xianyang, and later prime minister, a position he held multiple times. Wei Ran's military campaigns expanded Qin's territory significantly, including victories over Han, Wei, and Chu, capturing numerous cities and killing hundreds of thousands of enemies. His wealth and power rivaled the royal family's.

In the 32nd year of King Zhao, while besieging Daliang, Wei Ran was persuaded by Wei official Xu Jia to withdraw, citing the risks of overextension and the wisdom of accepting smaller territorial concessions. Xu Jia argued that Qin's successes were due to luck, not superior strategy, and that attacking Daliang with 300,000 defenders was unwise. Wei Ran heeded this advice and lifted the siege.

Later, Wei Ran led further campaigns against Wei, Zhao, and Han, but was eventually dismissed after Fan Sui warned King Zhao about the excessive power of Queen Mother Xuan and her relatives. Wei Ran died in Taoyi, which was later reclaimed by Qin as a county. Sima Qian noted that Wei Ran's efforts were crucial to Qin's expansion and dominance, yet his downfall came swiftly after losing royal favor.
