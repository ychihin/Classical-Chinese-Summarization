Wu Zixu, a native of Chu, was a famous official whose father Wu She and brother Wu Shang were killed by King Ping of Chu due to the slander of Fei Wuji. Fei Wuji, originally the young tutor of Prince Jian, manipulated King Ping into marrying the prince's intended bride from Qin, then slandered Prince Jian, leading to his exile. Wu She was imprisoned, and his sons were summoned under threat of death. Wu Shang chose to go and die with his father, while Wu Zixu escaped, vowing revenge. He fled to Song, then Zheng, and eventually to Wu after Prince Jian was killed in Zheng. Wu Zixu arrived in Wu, where he befriended Prince Guang and recommended Zhuanzhu to assassinate King Liao, allowing Prince Guang to become King Helu of Wu.

Under Helu, Wu Zixu became a key advisor, leading campaigns against Chu. In 506 BCE, Wu, with allies Tang and Cai, defeated Chu at the Battle of Boju, capturing the capital Ying. King Zhao of Chu fled. Wu Zixu, unable to find King Zhao, exhumed King Ping's corpse and whipped it 300 times in revenge. Shen Baoxu, a friend of Wu Zixu, sought aid from Qin, crying for seven days until Qin agreed to help. Qin forces defeated Wu, and internal strife forced Helu to return to Wu. Wu Zixu and Sun Wu's strategies made Wu dominant over Chu, Qi, Jin, and Yue.

After Helu's death, his son King Fucha of Wu succeeded. Wu Zixu warned against sparing King Goujian of Yue after a victory, but Fucha, influenced by the corrupt official Bo Pi, accepted Yue's surrender. Wu Zixu repeatedly advised against attacking Qi, urging the elimination of Yue first, but was ignored. Fucha sent Wu Zixu as an envoy to Qi, where he entrusted his son to the Bao family, foreseeing Wu's doom. Bo Pi slandered Wu Zixu, leading Fucha to order his suicide. Wu Zixu, before dying, asked for his eyes to be hung on the eastern gate to watch Yue's invasion. Fucha, enraged, had his body placed in a leather bag and thrown into the river. The people of Wu built a shrine for him.

Years later, Yue destroyed Wu, fulfilling Wu Zixu's prophecy. Prince Jian's son, Bai Gong Sheng, returned to Chu but later rebelled, killing officials Zixi and Ziqi, and kidnapping King Hui. Ye Gong suppressed the rebellion, and Bai Gong Sheng committed suicide. Sima Qian commented on Wu Zixu's endurance and revenge, contrasting his fate with Bai Gong Sheng's failed rebellion.
