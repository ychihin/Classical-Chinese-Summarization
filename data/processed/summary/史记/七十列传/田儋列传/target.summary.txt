Tian Dan, a member of the Qi royal family, proclaimed himself King of Qi during the Warring States period after killing the Di County magistrate. He raised troops but was later killed by Qin general Zhang Han at Linji. His cousin Tian Rong gathered the remnants and fled east. After Tian Dan's death, the Qi people installed Tian Jia as king, but Tian Rong, angered by this, attacked and drove Tian Jia and his ministers into exile. Tian Rong then made Tian Dan's son Tian Shi king and appointed himself prime minister, with Tian Heng as general.

When Xiang Liang requested Qi's aid against Zhang Han, Tian Rong demanded the execution of the exiled Qi leaders as a condition, which Chu and Zhao refused. Qi withheld support, and Zhang Han subsequently defeated and killed Xiang Liang. Later, Xiang Yu, after defeating Qin, divided the territories, demoting Tian Shi to King of Jiaodong and installing Tian Du as King of Qi. Tian Rong, resentful at being excluded, attacked and killed Tian Du and Tian An, proclaiming himself King of Qi. Xiang Yu retaliated, defeating and killing Tian Rong. Tian Heng then gathered forces, regained Qi territory, and installed Tian Rong's son Tian Guang as king, with himself as de facto ruler.

Years later, Han king Liu Bang sent Li Shiqi to negotiate Qi's submission. Tian Heng agreed and lifted defenses, but Han general Han Xin launched a surprise attack, capturing Linzi. Believing Li Shiqi had betrayed them, Tian Guang and Tian Heng executed him. Han forces defeated Qi and Chu allies, killing General Long Qie and capturing Tian Guang. Tian Heng then proclaimed himself king but was defeated by Han forces and fled to Liang. After Liu Bang became emperor, Tian Heng, fearing execution, fled to an island with 500 followers. Summoned by the emperor, Tian Heng committed suicide near Luoyang, ashamed to serve under Liu Bang after having killed Li Shiqi. His two retainers and later the 500 followers also died, demonstrating his ability to inspire loyalty. The historian Sima Qian praised Tian Heng's virtue, noting that despite his death, no painter has depicted his story.
