Han Anguo, a native of Liang State and a student of Han Feizi and Zajia, served as a middle official under King Xiao of Liang. During the Rebellion of the Seven Kingdoms, he and Zhang Yu defended Liang’s eastern front, preventing the Wu army from crossing. After the rebellion, their reputations grew. King Xiao of Liang, favored by Empress Dowager Dou, acted with imperial-like grandeur, angering Emperor Jing. As Liang’s envoy, Han Anguo pleaded with the eldest princess, explaining King Liang’s filial piety and loyalty during the rebellion, and his use of imperial gifts to display the emperor’s favor. The empress dowager relented, and Emperor Jing reconciled, rewarding Han Anguo generously.

Later, Han Anguo was imprisoned for a crime but became Liang’s domestic history after a jailer’s insult. He forgave the jailer. When Liang’s internal history post opened, the empress dowager ordered King Liang to appoint Han Anguo. Gongsun Gui and Yang Sheng plotted to make King Liang heir, assassinating Yuan Ang. Emperor Jing sent envoys to capture them, but they hid in King Liang’s palace. Han Anguo warned King Liang of the dangers, citing historical precedents, and the king surrendered the conspirators, who committed suicide. This resolved the crisis, and Han Anguo gained favor.

After King Xiao’s death, Han Anguo lost his post but later became a chief minister. During the Xiongnu peace debate, he argued against war, favoring peace. The emperor agreed. In the Mayi incident, Nie Wengyi lured the Xiongnu, but the plan failed when the Chanyu retreated. Wang Hui, who proposed the plan, was executed despite pleas. Han Anguo, known for strategy and loyalty but greedy, recommended many scholars. He served as imperial censor for four years, then as lieutenant and guard. After Wei Qing’s campaigns, Han Anguo was stationed in Yuyang but was deceived by prisoners, leading to Xiongnu raids. Demoted and alienated, he died of illness in 123 BCE. Sima Qian praised his loyalty and the integrity of Liang’s elders.
