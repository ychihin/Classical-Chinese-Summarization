Wei Qing, a native of Pingyang County, was the illegitimate son of Zheng Ji and a concubine of the Pingyang Marquis household. His half-sister Wei Zifu gained favor with Emperor Wu of Han, leading Wei Qing to adopt the surname Wei. As a youth, he served as a servant and shepherd, enduring mistreatment from his half-brothers. A prisoner once prophesied his future nobility, but Wei Qing dismissed it. He later became a cavalryman for the Pingyang Marquis.

When Wei Zifu entered the palace and became pregnant, Empress Chen, jealous, attempted to kill Wei Qing. His friend Gongsun Ao rescued him, and Emperor Wu subsequently appointed Wei Qing as a palace supervisor, elevating his status. Wei Zifu later became empress, and Wei Qing was promoted to Dazhong doctor.

In 129 BCE, Wei Qing led a cavalry attack on the Xiongnu, achieving modest success. Subsequent campaigns saw him capture the Henan region, driving out Xiongnu kings and establishing Shuofang Commandery. For these victories, he was enfeoffed as Marquis of Changping. Further campaigns against the Xiongnu resulted in significant victories, including the capture of King Youxian's forces and vast numbers of livestock. Emperor Wu granted Wei Qing additional households and enfeoffed his sons as marquises, though Wei Qing humbly declined, crediting the emperor and his officers.

Wei Qing's nephew, Huo Qubing, emerged as a brilliant commander, earning the title of Champion Marquis for his daring raids. Huo Qubing's campaigns were marked by deep penetrations into Xiongnu territory, capturing kings and vast numbers of enemies. He became increasingly favored by the emperor, eventually matching Wei Qing in rank and power. Huo Qubing famously declined a mansion, stating, "The Xiongnu have not yet been eliminated, so I have no intention of considering private matters." However, he was known for his lack of concern for his soldiers' welfare, often discarding food while they starved.

After Huo Qubing's death, Wei Qing's influence waned. Wei Qing died four years later, and his descendants lost their titles due to legal infractions.
