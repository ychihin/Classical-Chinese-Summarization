The text describes various Yi tribes and their customs. Some wear hair in buns, cultivate fields, and live in towns, while others, like the Xi and Kunming, move with livestock and have no fixed residence or commander. From Wei northeast, many rulers exist, with Yi and Zuo being most powerful, followed by Ran Zhen. These are barbarians outside Bajun and southwest of Shujun.

King Wei of Chu sent General Zhuang Qi, a descendant of King Zhuang of Chu, to capture areas west of Bajun, Shujun, and Qianzhong County. He reached Dianchi Lake, a fertile area, and pacified it for Chu. Unable to return due to Qin's capture of Bajun and Qianzhong, he became king of Dian, adopting local customs.

During Qin, Chang Yan briefly opened the Five Feet Road and set officials. After Qin's fall, Han abandoned these areas, using Shu County's boundaries as fortresses. Bajun and Shujun grew wealthy through secret trade with Zuo, Bo, and others.

In Emperor Wu's sixth year, Wang Hui attacked Dongyue, and Tang Meng learned from Nanyue about wolfberry sauce from Shu, traded via Yelang and the Zoke River. Tang Meng proposed using Yelang's elite troops to attack Nanyue, and Emperor Wu agreed. Tang Meng entered Yelang, made agreements, and established Qianwei County. Roads were built, but difficulties and rebellions led to temporary cessation of development.

Later, Zhang Qian reported seeing Shu cloth and Qiong bamboo sticks in Daxia, suggesting a route to Shendu. Emperor Wu sent envoys to find Shendu via Dian, but they were blocked by Kunming. Dian and Yelang kings, unaware of Han's size, questioned its magnitude.

When Nanyue rebelled, Qielan rebelled and killed Han envoys. Han forces conquered Qielan and Nanyue, establishing Zoke County. Yelanghou submitted and was named king. Other tribes, shocked, requested Han officials. Han set up Yuesi, Shenli, Lunshan, and Wudu counties.

The Dian king, with tens of thousands of troops, initially refused to submit. After Han destroyed Laojin and Mimo, Dian surrendered, becoming Yizhou County. The Dian king received a royal seal, favored by Han.

Tai Shigong noted that Chu's descendants, including the Dian king, survived Qin's destruction. Han pacified southwestern barbarians, with Dian favored. The pacification began with sightings of Gojiang in Panyu and Qiong bamboo sticks in Daxia, leading to the division of Xiyi into seven counties.
