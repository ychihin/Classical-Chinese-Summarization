Lu Buwei, a wealthy merchant from Yangzhai, amassed a fortune through low-cost buying and high-price selling. During the reign of King Zhao of Qin, the crown prince died, and An Guojun was appointed as the new prince. An Guojun had many sons, including Zichu, a middle-ranking son sent as a hostage to Zhao. Zichu, neglected and impoverished, caught Lu Buwei's attention. Lu Buwei saw him as a rare commodity to be hoarded and offered to help him gain power.

Lu Buwei traveled to Qin to lobby An Guojun and his favored concubine, Mrs. Huayang, who had no son. He persuaded Mrs. Huayang to adopt Zichu as her heir, ensuring her future influence. An Guojun agreed, and Zichu became the designated heir. Lu Buwei gave Zichu funds and married him to a beautiful dancer, Zhao Ji, who bore a son, Ying Zheng.

During a siege of Handan, Zichu escaped to Qin with Lu Buwei's help. After King Zhao's death, An Guojun became king, and Zichu became prince. An Guojun died shortly after, and Zichu ascended as King Zhuang Xiang, appointing Lu Buwei as prime minister. King Zhuang Xiang died three years later, and Ying Zheng became king, with Lu Buwei as regent.

Lu Buwei, competing with other lords, hosted 3,000 scholars and compiled the *Lüshi Chunqiu*, a comprehensive text. He later introduced Lao Ai, a man with a large penis, to the Queen Mother, who had an affair with him. Lao Ai gained power and fathered two sons with her. When discovered, Lao Ai rebelled but was executed. Lu Buwei was implicated, dismissed, and exiled. Fearing further persecution, he committed suicide by drinking poison.
