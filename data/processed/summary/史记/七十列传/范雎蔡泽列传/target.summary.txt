Fan Ju, a man of eloquence, initially sought to serve the King of Wei but was too poor to fund his journey. He first served under Xu Jia, a senior official in Wei. When Xu Jia was sent as an envoy to Qi, Fan Ju accompanied him. King Qi Xiang, impressed by Fan Ju's eloquence, sent him gifts of gold, beef, and wine, which Fan Ju refused. Xu Jia suspected Fan Ju had betrayed Wei secrets to Qi and reported this to the Wei prime minister, Wei Qi. Wei Qi had Fan Ju beaten severely, breaking his ribs and teeth, then wrapped him in a mat and threw him in a toilet, where drunken guests urinated on him. Fan Ju convinced a guard to let him escape, and later hid with the help of Zheng Anping, changing his name to Zhang Lu.

When King Zhao of Qin sent envoy Wang Ji to Wei, Zheng Anping arranged a secret meeting between Fan Ju and Wang Ji. Recognizing Fan Ju's talent, Wang Ji took him to Qin. On the way, they encountered Marquis Rang, the powerful Qin prime minister, but Fan Ju hid to avoid detection. Once in Qin, Wang Ji recommended Fan Ju to King Zhao, but the king was skeptical and housed Fan Ju poorly for over a year. During this time, Qin had expanded its territory significantly, but the king distrusted lobbyists. Marquis Rang and other relatives of the queen mother held immense power, with private wealth exceeding the state's.

Fan Ju wrote to King Zhao, arguing that a wise ruler rewards merit and talent, and that he was willing to die if his advice proved useless. Impressed, King Zhao summoned Fan Ju. At the palace, Fan Ju deliberately provoked the king by claiming Qin only had a queen mother and Marquis Rang, not a king. King Zhao apologized for the delay and sought Fan Ju's counsel. After three rounds of questioning, Fan Ju finally spoke, citing historical examples to emphasize the importance of trust and the dangers of powerful relatives. He outlined a strategy of befriending distant states and attacking nearby ones, starting with Han and Wei, to consolidate Qin's power.

King Zhao appointed Fan Ju as a guest minister and followed his advice, capturing cities from Wei and Han. Over time, Fan Ju gained the king's trust and exposed the overreach of the queen mother and Marquis Rang, leading to their expulsion. Fan Ju became prime minister, and the king granted him the title of Marquis Ying. When Xu Jia later visited Qin as an envoy, Fan Ju humiliated him for past wrongs but spared his life due to a gift of a robe. Fan Ju also recommended Zheng Anping as a general and repaid all who had helped him.

However, Fan Ju's later years were troubled. Zheng Anping surrendered to Zhao, and Wang Ji was executed for colluding with other states. King Zhao expressed concern about Qin's lack of capable generals, unsettling Fan Ju. Cai Ze, a strategist from Yan, then arrived and warned Fan Ju of the dangers of holding power too long, citing historical figures like Shang Yang and Wu Qi who died after their achievements. Fan Ju heeded the advice and recommended Cai Ze to King Zhao, eventually resigning as prime minister. Cai Ze served briefly but also resigned due to slander, living on in Qin for many years.
