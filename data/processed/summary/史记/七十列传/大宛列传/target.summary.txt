Zhang Qian, a Langguan from Hanzhong during the Jianyuan reign of Emperor Wu of the Han Dynasty, discovered Dawan. The emperor, learning from surrendered Xiongnu that the Yuezhi had been defeated by the Xiongnu and sought allies, recruited envoys to contact the Yuezhi. Zhang Qian responded, leaving Longxi with Ganfu, a former Xiongnu slave. Captured by the Xiongnu, he was detained for over ten years, married, and had children, but kept his envoy's talisman. Eventually, he escaped westward to Dawan.

Dawan, eager to connect with the wealthy Han Dynasty, welcomed Zhang Qian and provided guides to Kangju, then to Dayuezhi. The Yuezhi king, having conquered Bactria, was content and unwilling to attack the Xiongnu. After over a year, Zhang Qian returned via the Nanshan Mountains but was recaptured by the Xiongnu. He escaped during internal Xiongnu chaos, returning to the Han Dynasty with his Hu wife and Ganfu. He was granted the title Taizhong Dafu.

Zhang Qian reported on Dawan and neighboring states: Dawan, southwest of the Xiongnu, had settled agriculture, wine, and fine horses. It had over 70 towns and hundreds of thousands of people. Kangju, a nomadic state with 80,000-90,000 warriors, served the Yuezhi and Xiongnu. Dayuezhi, west of Dawan, was nomadic with 100,000-200,000 warriors, having defeated Daxia. Anxi, a large settled state with towns and trade, used silver coins. Tiaozhi, near the Western Sea, was hot and humid. Daxia, south of the Guishui River, had weak military but strong commerce, ruled by the Yuezhi.

Zhang Qian noted that in Daxia, he saw Qiong bamboo sticks and Shu cloth, bought from Shendu (India), suggesting a route from Shu. The emperor, intrigued by these wealthy states, sent envoys from Shu, but they were blocked by tribes like Kunming. Zhang Qian later joined General Wei Qing against the Xiongnu, earning the title Marquis of Bowang. After a failed campaign with Li Guang, he was reduced to commoner status.

The emperor, seeking to ally with Wusun, sent Zhang Qian as Zhonglang General with 300 men, gifts, and deputy envoys. Wusun's king, Kunmo, was hesitant due to Xiongnu influence and internal divisions. Zhang Qian dispatched deputies to various states. Wusun sent envoys to the Han Dynasty, impressed by its wealth. Zhang Qian died as Daxing, a high official.

Subsequently, the Han Dynasty established ties with Western Regions states, though envoys faced challenges from Xiongnu and local resistance. The emperor launched campaigns against Dawan for its horses, leading to two expeditions under Li Guangli. The first failed due to lack of supplies, but the second succeeded with a large army, capturing Dawan and securing horses. The Han Dynasty expanded its influence, setting up commanderies and protecting trade routes.
