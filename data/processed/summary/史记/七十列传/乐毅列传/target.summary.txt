Le Yi, a descendant of Le Yang who served Wei Wenhou and captured Zhongshan, was a virtuous man fond of military affairs. After King Wuling of Zhao died, Le Yi left Zhao for Wei. Hearing that King Yan Zhao sought revenge against Qi, Le Yi went to Yan as an envoy and became a minister. King Min of Qi grew arrogant and powerful, prompting King Yan Zhao to consult Le Yi about attacking Qi. Le Yi advised forming an alliance with Zhao, Chu, and Wei. King Yan Zhao sent Le Yi to forge alliances, and the allied forces defeated Qi at Jishui. The other states withdrew, but Le Yi’s Yan army pursued Qi to Linzi, capturing treasures and ritual objects. King Yan Zhao granted Le Yi the title of King of Chang Kingdom. Over five years, Le Yi captured over 70 Qi cities, leaving only Ju and Jimo unconquered.

After King Yan Zhao died, King Yan Hui, who resented Le Yi, fell for Qi’s counterintelligence and replaced Le Yi with Qi Jie. Fearing execution, Le Yi surrendered to Zhao, which granted him Guanjin. Qi’s Tian Dan defeated Qi Jie, recovering all Qi cities. King Yan Hui regretted his actions and blamed Le Yi for defecting. Le Yi replied with a letter explaining his loyalty to the late king, his strategic advice, and his fear of unjust punishment. He compared himself to Wu Zixu, emphasizing the importance of preserving the late king’s achievements. King Yan Hui later appointed Le Yi’s son Le Jian as King of Chang State. Le Yi traveled between Zhao and Yan, dying in Zhao.

Le Jian advised against attacking Zhao but was ignored. Zhao’s Lianpo defeated Yan, capturing Lifu and Lecheng. Le Jian fled to Zhao. King Yan regretted his decision. Later, Lecheng and Lian Po besieged Yan, forcing Yan to sue for peace. After King Zhao Xiaocheng died, Lecheng replaced Lian Po, but both fled. Qin eventually destroyed Zhao. Han Gaozu later granted Le Yi’s grandson, Uncle Le, the title of Huachengjun. The Le family also included Duke Le Xia and Duke Le Chen, who fled to Qi Gaomi. Duke Le Chen studied Huangdi and Laozi, becoming a renowned teacher. Sima Qian noted that reading Le Yi’s letter moved him to tears.
