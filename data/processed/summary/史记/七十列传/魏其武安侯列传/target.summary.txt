Dou Ying, the Marquis of Weiqi, was a relative of Empress Dowager Dou during the Han Dynasty. He served as the Prime Minister of Wu but was dismissed due to illness. During Emperor Jing's reign, he opposed the emperor's suggestion to pass the throne to his brother, King Xiao of Liang, angering Empress Dowager Dou. When the seven kingdoms rebelled, Emperor Jing appointed Dou Ying as a general. He recommended talented men, distributed his gold reward to subordinates, and successfully suppressed the rebellion, earning the title of Marquis of Weiqi. He later served as the tutor of Prince Li but was deposed after arguing for the prince. He retired but was persuaded to return to court. Despite Empress Dowager Dou's recommendation, Emperor Jing refused to make him prime minister, citing his arrogance.

Tian Fen, the Marquis of Wu'an, was the half-brother of Emperor Jing's queen. Initially humble, he rose to prominence after Emperor Jing's death, becoming a favored official. He became prime minister and Taiwei, promoting Confucianism and clashing with Empress Dowager Dou's preference for Taoism. After her death, Tian Fen became prime minister, growing arrogant and powerful. He built lavish residences and accumulated wealth, while Dou Ying lost influence and was alienated.

Guan Fu, a brave general, gained fame during the seven kingdoms' rebellion by charging into enemy lines. He later became a governor but was dismissed for legal violations. He was known for his straightforwardness, generosity to the poor, and disdain for the powerful. He accumulated wealth and became tyrannical in Yingchuan. After losing power, he befriended Dou Ying, and they supported each other.

Tian Fen once promised to visit Dou Ying but failed to show, causing Guan Fu to confront him. Tian Fen later sought to seize Dou Ying's land, leading to a feud. Tian Fen accused Guan Fu of tyranny, and Guan Fu retaliated by exposing Tian Fen's corruption. At a banquet, Guan Fu insulted Tian Fen, who had him arrested and charged with disrespect. Dou Ying tried to save Guan Fu but failed. Tian Fen slandered Dou Ying, leading to his imprisonment. Dou Ying claimed to have an imperial edict from Emperor Jing, but it was not found in records, resulting in his execution for forgery. Guan Fu and his family were also executed. Tian Fen later fell ill, haunted by the ghosts of Dou Ying and Guan Fu, and died. His son's title was revoked after a crime.

The historian comments that Dou Ying and Tian Fen rose through family connections, while Guan Fu gained fame through bravery. Dou Ying failed to adapt to changing circumstances, and Guan Fu lacked humility, leading to their downfall. Tian Fen abused his power and framed innocent men, resulting in his own demise.
