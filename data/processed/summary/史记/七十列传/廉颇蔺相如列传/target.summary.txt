Lian Po, a renowned general of Zhao, conquered Qi and captured Yangjin, earning the title of Shangqing. Lin Xiangru, a retainer of eunuch Miao Xian, was sent as an envoy to Qin after King Zhao of Qin offered fifteen cities for the He's Bi jade. Lin Xiangru successfully returned the jade intact to Zhao after King Qin failed to honor the exchange. Later, at the Mianchi meeting, Lin Xiangru forced King Qin to play a fou, maintaining Zhao's dignity. For his efforts, he was named Shangqing, ranking above Lian Po.

Lian Po, resentful of Lin Xiangru's higher rank, threatened to humiliate him. Lin Xiangru avoided conflict, prioritizing national unity over personal grievances. When Lian Po learned of this, he apologized, and the two became close friends. Lian Po later led successful campaigns against Qi and Wei.

Zhao She, a tax official, gained King Zhao's favor after punishing Lord Pingyuan's family for tax evasion. He was appointed to oversee national taxation, which prospered the people and treasury. During the Qin siege of Yanyu, Zhao She led a daring rescue, defeating the Qin army and earning the title of Ma Fujun.

After King Zhao Huiwen's death, King Xiaocheng ascended. In the Changping campaign, Lian Po initially held defensive positions against Qin. King Xiaocheng replaced him with Zhao Kuo, despite warnings from Lin Xiangru and Zhao Kuo's mother. Zhao Kuo's arrogance led to a disastrous defeat, with 450,000 Zhao soldiers killed. The Qin army later besieged Handan, which was relieved by Chu and Wei forces.

Lian Po later defeated Yan, earning the title of Lord Xinping. After King Xiaocheng's death, Lian Po fled to Wei following a conflict with Lecheng. Zhao attempted to recall him, but a bribed envoy falsely reported his frailty, and he was not reinstated. Lian Po died in Chu.

Li Mu, a capable general, defended Zhao's northern border against the Xiongnu. Initially perceived as timid, he later ambushed and defeated the Xiongnu, securing peace for over a decade. He also achieved victories against Qin, earning the title of Lord Wu'an. However, King Zhao, influenced by rumors, executed Li Mu and removed Sima Shang. Three months later, Qin destroyed Zhao.

Tai Shigong praised Lin Xiangru's courage and wisdom, noting his ability to intimidate enemies and later yield to Lian Po, prioritizing the state's interests.
