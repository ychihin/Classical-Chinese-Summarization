Chun Shenjun, a native of Chu named Huang Xie, served King Qingxiang of Chu. He was sent to Qin after King Qingxiang recognized his eloquence. At that time, Qin, under King Zhao, had been attacking Chu's allies, and Huang Xie learned of Qin's plan to invade Chu. Worried for Chu's survival, he wrote to King Zhao, arguing that Qin and Chu, as the two strongest states, should not fight each other, as this would benefit weaker states like Han and Wei. He warned that Qin's past victories had made it powerful, but attacking Chu would strengthen its enemies and lead to future trouble. Citing historical examples, he advised Qin to ally with Chu instead.

King Zhao agreed, halted the attack, and formed an alliance with Chu. Huang Xie returned with Prince Wan of Chu as a hostage in Qin. When King Qingxiang fell ill, Huang Xie persuaded the Qin prime minister to let Prince Wan return, arguing it would secure Chu's loyalty. When Qin hesitated, Huang Xie helped Prince Wan escape disguised as a charioteer, staying behind to face punishment. King Zhao initially wanted to execute him but, on advice, spared him and sent him back. Three months later, King Qingxiang died, and Prince Wan became King Kaolie of Chu, appointing Huang Xie as prime minister and granting him the title Lord Chunshen with twelve counties.

As prime minister, Chun Shenjun governed effectively, moving his fief to Jiangdong and building a capital in the old Wu city. He competed with other lords in attracting talented guests. In his fifth year, he led Chu forces to relieve the siege of Handan, Zhao's capital. In his eighth year, he conquered Lu for Chu, restoring Chu's strength. However, in his twenty-second year, a six-state alliance against Qin, led by Chu, was defeated at Hangu Pass, leading to his alienation by King Kaolie.

Chun Shenjun later became involved with Li Yuan, who presented his pregnant sister to him. She convinced Chun Shenjun to offer her to the childless King Kaolie, ensuring his son would become king. The king favored her, and she bore a son, who became prince. Li Yuan, fearing Chun Shenjun would reveal the secret, secretly raised assassins. When King Kaolie fell ill, a guest named Zhu Ying warned Chun Shenjun of Li Yuan's plot, but he dismissed it. Seventeen days after the king's death, Li Yuan ambushed and killed Chun Shenjun at the palace gate, then massacred his family. Li Yuan's sister's son became King You of Chu.

The historian comments that Chun Shenjun's early wisdom in persuading Qin and risking his life for the prince was admirable, but he later fell victim to Li Yuan's schemes, failing to act on Zhu Ying's advice, which led to his downfall.
