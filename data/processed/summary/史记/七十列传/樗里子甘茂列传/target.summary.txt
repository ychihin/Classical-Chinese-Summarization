Chuilizi, named Ji, was the younger brother of King Hui of Qin, born to a Korean mother. Known for his eloquence and resourcefulness, he was called "the wise man" by the people of Qin. In the eighth year of King Hui's reign, he was granted the title of Yougeng and led an army to attack Quwo in Wei, driving out the inhabitants and annexing the land. In the twenty-fifth year, he captured General Zhuang Bao of Zhao and seized Linyi. The following year, he assisted Wei Zhang in defeating Chu, capturing the Hanzhong area. King Hui granted him the title Chuilizi, with the title Yan Jun.

After King Hui's death, King Wu expelled Zhang Yi and Wei Zhang, appointing Chuilizi and Gan Mao as left and right prime ministers. King Wu sent Gan Mao to attack South Korea, capturing Yiyang, while Chuilizi led a hundred chariots to the Zhou capital. The King of Zhou greeted him respectfully, angering the King of Chu. You Teng persuaded the King of Chu that the Zhou king's actions were precautionary, not disrespectful, and the King of Chu became appeased.

Under King Zhao, Chuilizi was even more respected. In the first year of King Zhao's reign, he attacked Pucheng in Weiguo. Hu Yan, on behalf of Pucheng's chief, argued that attacking Pucheng would strengthen Wei and endanger Qin, leading Chuilizi to abandon the siege. Chuilizi later attacked Pishi but withdrew when it did not surrender. He died in the seventh year of King Zhao and was buried east of Zhangtai, south of the Wei River. Before his death, he prophesied that an emperor's palace would flank his tomb, which came true with the construction of Changle and Weiyang Palaces during the Han Dynasty.

Gan Mao, from Xiacai, studied under Mr. Shi Ju and met King Hui through Zhang Yi and Chuilizi. He helped capture Hanzhong and later pacified Shu under King Wu, who appointed him left prime minister. King Wu wished to pass through Sanchuan to see the Zhou capital, so Gan Mao proposed attacking Yiyang. Despite doubts from Chuilizi and Gongsun Shi, Gan Mao captured Yiyang after five months, killing 60,000 enemies. King Wu died after reaching the Zhou capital.

Under King Zhao, Queen Mother Xuan, a Chu native, initially refused to aid South Korea against Chu. Gan Mao advised sending troops, and the Chu army withdrew. Xiang Shou, a relative of the Queen Mother, was sent to pacify Yiyang. Su Dai advised Xiang Shou to ally with South Korea against Chu, but Xiang Shou hesitated. Gan Mao eventually fled to Qi after conflicts with Xiang Shou and Gongsun Shi. Su Dai persuaded King Zhao to welcome Gan Mao back, but Gan Mao refused and served Qi instead. He later died in Wei.

Gan Mao's grandson, Gan Luo, served Lu Buwei at age twelve. He persuaded Zhang Tang to go to Yan as prime minister and then convinced King Zhao of Zhao to cede five cities to Qin, leading to Zhao's attack on Yan and Qin's gain of eleven cities. Gan Luo was rewarded with a ministerial position and Gan Mao's former lands.

Tai Shigong noted that Chuilizi's favor came from his royal lineage, while Gan Mao rose from commoner status to fame. Gan Luo's youthful ingenuity earned him lasting renown, though neither was a loyal gentleman, both were true counselors of the Warring States period, reflecting the era's trend toward cunning strategies.
