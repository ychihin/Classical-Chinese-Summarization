Emperor Gaozu Wu, named Yan, with the courtesy name Shuda and nickname Lian'er, was a descendant of Xiao He, the Han Dynasty Prime Minister, from Zhongduli in Nanlanling. His lineage traced back through generations of officials, including his father Shunzhi, who was granted the title of Marquis of Linxiang County for assisting the emperor. Gaozu was born in the eighth year of the Ming Dynasty in Moling County, displaying unusual traits like connected bones in his legs, a protruding head, and a tattoo of "Martial" on his right hand. He grew up knowledgeable, skilled in strategy, and talented in civil and military affairs, often surrounded by clouds and mist in his residence.

He began his official career as a king of Baling and was later promoted to general. Wang Jian predicted he would become a poet within thirty years. Gaozu socialized with notable figures like Xie Tiao and Wang Rong, known as the Eight Friends. Wang Rong respected him greatly, believing he would rule the world. After his father's death, Gaozu served as General Ningshuo to guard Shouchun, then as crown prince's concubine and assistant minister. He was granted the title of male from Jianyang County for his role in establishing the emperor.

In the second year of Jianwu, the Northern Wei Dynasty invaded Sizhou. Gaozu, as champion general, led reinforcements. Despite fear among the troops, he marched ahead, surprising the Northern Wei army and forcing them to retreat. He was later appointed as Sima and Grand Administrator of Huailing. In the fourth year, he defended against another invasion, leading his army to resist and kill nearly a hundred enemies, returning intact while others suffered losses. He was then appointed to conduct affairs in Yongzhou.

After Emperor Ming's death, six officials took turns governing, causing chaos. Gaozu warned his brother Zhang Hongce about the dangers of multiple rulers, predicting internal conflict. He advised gathering his brothers and preparing for potential turmoil. He secretly manufactured military equipment and stored bamboo in Tanxi River for shipbuilding. In the second year of Yongyuan, after Xiao Yi's murder, Gaozu summoned his staff and planned an uprising. He recruited over 10,000 soldiers, 1,000 horses, and 3,000 ships, building warships from bamboo.

Gaozu sent letters to various states, anticipating that Liu Shanyang, sent by Donghun to attack Xiangyang, would be killed. His strategy succeeded, and Shanyang was beheaded. Gaozu then marched from Xiangyang in February of the third year, leaving his brothers to guard key cities. He issued a proclamation condemning the cruel ruler for his tyranny, including killing loyal ministers, indulging in debauchery, and causing widespread suffering. He called for a righteous uprising to restore order.

The army advanced, defeating enemy forces at various locations. Gaozu's forces captured key cities, and many officials surrendered. In December, Donghun was beheaded, and Gaozu entered the martial arts hall, issuing orders for a general amnesty and reforms. He abolished harsh taxes and punishments, promoted frugality, and established new regulations. He was appointed as Grand Sima and Duke of Jian'an, later promoted to Duke of Liang with nine gifts.

Gaozu refused the title of King of Liang multiple times but eventually accepted after repeated requests from officials and auspicious signs. He implemented reforms in the election system, emphasizing talent over family background. In April, Queen Xuande issued an order for the abdication of the Southern Qi throne to King Liang, citing his virtues and achievements. Gaozu initially declined but eventually accepted, becoming Emperor Gaozu of the Liang Dynasty.
