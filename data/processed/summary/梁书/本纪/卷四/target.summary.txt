Emperor Taizong, named Gang, was the third son of Emperor Gaozu and half-brother of Prince Zhaoming. Born in Xianyang Hall in 464 AD, he was granted the title King of Jin'an in 467 AD with 8,000 households. Over the years, he held various military and civil posts, including General Yunhui, governor of multiple states, and commander-in-chief of military affairs. He was appointed crown prince in 531 AD after Prince Zhaoming's death and became emperor in 549 AD upon Emperor Gaozu's death.

Upon ascending the throne, he issued edicts to reduce sentences, release northern slaves, and appoint officials. He faced challenges such as rebellions, famines, and invasions. In 550 AD, Hou Jing forced him to visit Xizhou and later proclaimed himself prime minister. Hou Jing deposed Taizong as King of Jin'an in 551 AD, imprisoned him, and killed many of his family members. Taizong died in Yongfu Province at age 49, suffocated by a bag of soil. He was posthumously named Emperor Ming, later changed to Emperor Jianwen.

Taizong was exceptionally intelligent, able to compose at age six, and known for his broad-mindedness and literary talents. He wrote numerous works, including "The Biography of Prince Zhaoming" and "Changchun Yiji." Despite his virtues, he was controlled by traitors and met a tragic end.
