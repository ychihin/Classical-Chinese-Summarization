# Summary of Historical Records: Biographies of Zhang Huishao, Feng Daogen, Chen Mian, and Chang Yizhi

Zhang Huishao, courtesy name Deji, was from Yiyang. During the reign of Emperor Qi Ming of the Southern Dynasty, he served as Zhihe and garrison commander of Jingling Hengsang. After his mother's death in the early Yongyuan period, he returned home for burial. Upon hearing of Liang Gaozu's righteous army, he joined as a soldier and was appointed General Ningshuo. He defeated Shen Nandang's navy, seized weapons, and made many military exploits at Xinlin and Zhuque. After Jiankang was captured, he became General of the Auxiliary State and was later granted the title of Marquis of Shiyang County with 500 households. When remnants of Donghun Hou's forces attacked the palace gates, Huishao led his men to defeat them, killing many. He was promoted to Crown Prince's Right Guard and increased his residence by 200 households. In the fourth year of Tianjian, he attacked Suyu and captured the city lord Ma Chenglong. However, after Wei reinforcements arrived and defeated his general Lan Huaigong, Huishao fled to Huaiyin. In the sixth year, he fought at Zhongli under Cao Jingzong, contributing to the defeat of the Wei army. He later served as Governor of Northern Yanzhou, where he managed affairs well and was beloved by officials and people. He died at age sixty-three in the eighteenth year of Tianjian, receiving posthumous honors.

Feng Daogen, courtesy name Danji, was from Luluo. Orphaned young and poor, he worked to support his mother, gaining fame for filial piety at thirteen. At sixteen, he rescued fellow villager Tomb Daowang from barbarians, killing many enemies. During the Yongyuan period, after his mother's death, he joined Gaozu's righteous army, leading village youths. He served under Cai Daofu and later commanded his troops after Daofu's death. After Gaozu ascended, Daogen was made General Xiaoqi and Male of Zengcheng County with 200 households. He suppressed Chen Bozhi's rebellion. As Grand Administrator of Muliang County, he prepared defenses despite ridicule, then defeated General Yu Shifu's 20,000 troops by opening the city gates and sending out 200 elite soldiers. He later captured Wei army's Gujiao Army honor guard, cutting off their food supply. At the battle of Zhongli, he built fortresses and attacked the Wei army's bridge, contributing to their defeat. Daogen was cautious and generous, never boasting of his achievements. Gaozu compared him to General Big Tree. He lived frugally in a simple house with no walls or guards. Though uneducated in youth, he later became proficient in writing. He died at fifty-eight in the first year of the ordinary period, with Gaozu personally mourning him.

Chen Mian, from Huashan County, had ancestors from Chenjuli who migrated during the Jin Dynasty. Outstanding in youth, he was called to serve as secretary of Xicao. After his mother's death, he became Grand Administrator of Huashan County, where he treated people with sincerity. When Gaozu raised troops, Chen Mian led 3,000 brave men and 250 horses to join him. He was granted the title of Male of Nan'an County with 300 households. He rescued Liang Su from Wei siege and later served as Governor of Northern Yanzhou. He supervised construction of the Jingshan Weir on the Huai River, mobilizing 200,000 workers. The weir was nine miles long, but the Huai River's currents repeatedly damaged it. Iron tools were sunk to counter dragons, and trees were cut for construction. An epidemic killed seventy-eight percent of workers. In the fifteenth year, the weir was completed, but later broke. Chen Mian served as Governor of Sizhou for three years, overhauling city moats. He died at fifty-seven in the first year of the ordinary period. He was generous and cheerful, giving cotton-padded clothes to poor officials in winter.

Chang Yizhi, with a strong body in youth, followed Xuanxu on expeditions. When Gaozu's righteous army rose, he was made General of the Auxiliary State. He fought bravely at Qianhuang and Yuquexiao. After Jiankang was pacified, he became General Zhihe and Lord of Ma Youjiahu. As Governor of Zhongli, he defeated Wei invaders. In the sixth year, when Wei armies besieged Zhongli with only 3,000 defenders, Yizhi led resistance, using mud to repair walls damaged by siege engines. He was skilled in archery, killing many enemies. Cao Jingzong's relief army arrived and defeated the Wei forces. Yizhi pursued them to Luokou. He later supervised construction of Jingshan Weir under Chen Mian. He had a generous nature, soothing people's hearts as a general. He was granted the title of Marquis of Yingdao County. He died in October of the fourth year of Xuanbi's reign, with Gaozu deeply regretting his death and issuing a posthumous edict.
