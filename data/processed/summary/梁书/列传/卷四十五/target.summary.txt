Wang Sengbian, son of General Wang Shennian, began his military career during the Tianjian period, serving under various princes in positions such as Zuo Changshi and military advisor. He quelled a rebellion in Wuning County and held several governorships, including Wuning, Guangping, and Anlu. When Hou Jing rebelled, Wang Sengbian led 10,000 naval troops to aid the capital, but the palace was captured. He briefly surrendered to Hou Jing before rejoining Shizu (Emperor Yuan of Liang). Ordered to attack the governor of Xiangzhou, Wang Sengbian delayed departure due to insufficient troops, angering Shizu, who struck him with a sword and imprisoned him. He was later pardoned to defend against King Yueyang's attack, successfully defeating him and replacing Bao Quan to pacify Xiangzhou.

Wang Sengbian then defended Baling against Hou Jing's invasion, employing strategies like sinking ships and stockpiling supplies. Despite fierce assaults, including fire attacks and siege equipment, the rebels were repelled. Rewarded as General of the East Conquest and Duke of Changning, he led forces to capture Lushan and Yingcheng, defeating Song Zixian and capturing him alive. He advanced to Jiushui, and after learning of the emperor's death, urged Shizu to take the throne. Marching toward Jianye, he allied with Chen Baxian at Baimaozhou, swearing an oath to destroy Hou Jing. Their combined forces defeated the rebels at Stone City, and Hou Jing fled. Wang Sengbian captured rebel leaders and restored order, earning the title of General of the Town Guard and Situ.

Later, Wang Sengbian suppressed the rebellion of Lu Na in Xiangzhou, using cautious tactics to surround and defeat him. He then guarded against Qi invasions, defeating their forces at Dongguan. After his mother's death, he received honors and a eulogy from the emperor. When Western Wei attacked Jiangling, Wang Sengbian was summoned but failed to arrive before the capital fell. He supported Emperor Jing and negotiated with Qi to accept Xiao Yuanming as emperor, sending hostages and meeting him at Jiangningpu. This led to conflict with Chen Baxian, who attacked Jiankang. Wang Sengbian, caught off guard, was captured and killed by Chen Baxian. His sons met various fates: Wang Chan died early, Wang Jie fled to Qi, Wang Hao died of grief, and Wang Ban was captured by Western Wei. The historian notes that despite his merits, Wang Sengbian's decision to install a puppet emperor under Qi coercion led to his downfall.
