Wang Liang, a sixth-generation descendant of Jin Dynasty Prime Minister Wang Dao, was selected as a prince consort in the late Song Dynasty due to his prestigious family background. He held various official positions, including Secretary Lang and prefect of Hengyang, but declined the latter due to the region's humid climate. Known for his honesty and fair governance as governor of Jinling, he was praised by Emperor Qi Ming. During the Jianwu era, he served as Minister of the Ministry of Personnel, often dissenting in his role. Initially close to Jiang Zhe, their relationship cooled, yet Jiang remained friendly. After Jiang's death, Wang could not prevent corrupt appointments. When the benevolent army arrived, Wang did not welcome them, but later met Emperor Gaozu, who spared him. He was granted titles like Duke of Yuning County but was stripped of his rank and made a commoner in the fourth year of Tianjian after being impeached for disrespect. He later lived in seclusion, served as Secretary Supervisor, and died in the ninth year of Tianjian, posthumously named Yangzi.

Zhang Ji, known for his filial piety, began his career after mourning his parents for six years. He defended Longgang County during Tang Yu's rebellion and held military posts, including defending Yuzhou against the Wei Dynasty with a million soldiers. He later served as governor of various regions and was involved in the rebellion against Marquis Shuhun, killing him in the Hande Hall. After Emperor Gaozu's ascension, Zhang was made Marquis of Jiang'an County. He held numerous high-ranking positions, such as Minister Zuopushe, and was known for his generous political measures. He was killed at age 63 during a rebellion in Yuzhou, and his title was posthumously revoked. His daughter Chu Yuan died protecting him. Zhang Ji was straightforward, had no savings, and gave his salary to relatives.

Wang Ying, also from Linyi, married a princess and held various posts, including prefect of Yixing and Wuxing. He was known for his benevolent governance. During the Yongyuan era, he resisted Cui Huijing's attack but fled after his army collapsed. After Jiankang was pacified, he served Emperor Gaozu as Zuo Changshi and later became Duke of a feudal city. He held positions like Shangshu Ling and General Yunhui, known for his noble and prudent character. In the fifteenth year of Tianjian, he was promoted to Zuo Guanglu but died six days after taking office, with his seal mysteriously damaged six times during casting.

Historian Yao Cha commented that Wang Liang's actions in troubled times resembled the three benevolent men of Yin, but his later career led to shame and waste. Zhang Ji's adaptability was a product of his era, while Wang Ying's seal destruction suggested divine jealousy.
