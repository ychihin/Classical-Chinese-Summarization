The text provides biographical accounts of several officials from the Liang Dynasty, focusing on their careers, conduct, and contributions.

Fan Xiu, a native of Kaocheng, was known for his filial piety and extensive knowledge of ritual traditions. He held various official positions, including county magistrate, minister, and general, serving under the Song, Qi, and Liang dynasties. Despite his literary talents being overshadowed by Shen Yue, his reputation for integrity and adherence to etiquette was widely praised. He was frugal in his personal life, using a decades-old scarf box and only making two dental pens during his tenure in Jinling. He died at seventy-five, leaving behind works like "Li Lun" and "Zi Xun."

Fu Zhao, from Lingzhou, was orphaned at six and showed remarkable maturity. He was known for his quiet demeanor, simplicity, and adherence to righteousness, avoiding political entanglements during turbulent times. He served in various roles, including minister and prefect, and was noted for his honest governance, such as sharing resources with the people and refusing gifts. He died at seventy-five, and his brother Fu Ying, also an official, was praised for his filial piety and literary skills, though he did not seek fame for his writings.

Xiao Chen, from Lanling, was eloquent and cheerful, rising through official ranks under the Qi and Liang dynasties. He was known for his wit, as seen in his diplomatic exchanges with Wei envoys, and his adherence to ritual protocols. He governed large counties without accumulating wealth, and his later years were marked by a love for books and wine. He died at fifty-two, leaving instructions for a simple burial.

Lu Gao, from Wu County, was skilled in calligraphy and painting, and known for his strong character and fearlessness in prosecuting corrupt officials, such as Yu Jian. He served as censor and prefect, earning a reputation for fairness and integrity. A devout Buddhist, he wrote "The Life of Samana" and died at seventy-four. His brother Lu Xu also held official positions and authored historical works.
