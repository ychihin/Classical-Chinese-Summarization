Fan Yun, courtesy name Yanlong, was born in Wuyin, Nanxiang, and was a sixth-generation descendant of Jiewang, a Jin Dynasty general. At age eight, he impressed Yin Yan, a Song Dynasty governor, by composing a poem on the spot. He studied diligently with his relative Yuan Zhao, who predicted he would become a talented person. Fan Yun was intelligent, insightful, and skilled in writing various literary styles. He lived with his father, Fan Kang, in Fubao and befriended Shen Yue, Yu Daizhi, and Fan Kangtong.

Fan Yun began his official career as Shuzuo of Xicao in Yingzhou. During Shen Youzhi's rebellion, Fan Yun was captured but remained calm under interrogation, earning his release. He later delivered a message for Youzhi, risking death to protect his family. Longshi Ryu Shiryong saved him. In the early Qi Dynasty, Fan Yun served Prince Jingling, impressing him by reading ancient inscriptions on Qinwang Mountain. He became a trusted chief administrator and was favored by Emperor Gao of Qi for interpreting a white crow as an auspicious sign.

Fan Yun held various positions, including prison officer, chief secretary, and Zhonglang in the Shangshu Palace. As internal history of Lingling, he governed with integrity, reducing burdens on the people. He later served as internal history of Shixing, where he pacified powerful families and barbarian tribes through kindness, removing surveillance posts and ensuring safety for merchants. He was promoted to General Jianwu and Governor of Guangzhou.

Fan Yun was recalled to the capital due to a dispute involving his friend's brother and was imprisoned but pardoned. In the second year of Qi Yongyuan, he became Dr. Guozi. He met Liang Gaozu at Prince Jingling's residence and later served as a staff officer during the rebellion against Saihunhou. After the Liang Dynasty's establishment, Fan Yun became Shizhong and remonstrated with Gaozu about marrying Qi Shuhunhou's concubine Yu, advising him to prioritize state affairs. Gaozu accepted his advice and rewarded him.

In the first year of Tianjian, Fan Yun participated in the southern郊 sacrifice. Gaozu praised his caution, and Fan Yun was appointed Changshi of Sanqi, Minister of the Ministry of Personnel, and Marquis of Xiaocheng County. He was promoted based on his friendship with Gaozu and served loyally. Gaozu instructed his sons to treat Fan Yun as a brother. Fan Yun later became crown prince's concubine and right servant of the minister but was dismissed for employing people against the emperor's will.

Fan Yun was honest, easy-going, and devoted to his widowed sister-in-law. He helped the poor, such as transporting the deceased commander Shi Wangke's body for burial. He advised Prince Jingling on political matters without flattery. Emperor Wu of Qi initially considered Fan Yun mediocre but later praised his sincerity after reading his admonitions. Fan Yun advised Prince Qi Wenhui to understand farming hardships. As an official, he handled documents efficiently but was criticized for his straightforwardness. He distributed his income to relatives and friends, leaving no savings.

Fan Yun died at age fifty-three in the second year of Tianjian. Gaozu mourned deeply and issued an edict praising his loyalty and wisdom. He was posthumously given titles and honors, including Shizhong and General of the Guard. His collected works comprised thirty volumes. His son Fan Xiaocai inherited his title.

Shen Yue, courtesy name Xiuwen, was born in Wukang, Wuxing. His grandfather was General Shen Linzi, and his father was Prefect Shen Yilong. After his father's execution, Shen Yue hid to avoid persecution. He studied diligently, often reading at night despite his mother's concerns. He served as a court official and impressed Cai Xingzong, who appointed him as a recorder. Shen Yue later held various positions, including Shangshu Duzhilang and captain of the infantry school. He was close to the prince and served in the East Palace.

Shen Yue joined Prince Jingling's circle of talented individuals. He held positions such as Minister Zuocheng and Yushi Zhongcheng. Under Emperor Ming of Qi, he became General of the Auxiliary State and Minister of the Five Arms. After Emperor Ming's death, he wrote decrees for the prime minister. He later served as Left Guard General and Zhi Sanqi. In the second year of Yongyuan, he resigned due to his mother's age but was awarded titles.

Shen Yue advised Gaozu to ascend the throne, citing divine signs and public support. Gaozu agreed, and Shen Yue drafted the abdication decree. After the Liang Dynasty's establishment, Shen Yue became a regular servant of Sanqi, Minister of the Ministry of Personnel, and Marquis of Changxian County. His mother was honored as Mrs. Jianchang. He later held positions such as Zuopushe and Shangshu Ling. Despite his high status, Shen Yue lived frugally and built a house in Dongtian, writing "Residence in the Suburbs" to express his feelings.

Shen Yue died at age seventy-three in the twelfth year of Tianjian. He was given posthumous honors. He was extremely intelligent, with two pupils in his left eye and a purple mole on his waist. He owned 20,000 volumes of books. Despite past grievances, he appointed a clansman who had insulted him as a county minister. He was knowledgeable about laws and regulations and excelled in poetry and writing. However, he was arrogant and obsessed with fame, and after becoming prime minister, he failed to recommend talents. He died of fear after Gaozu condemned him for protecting his family. He wrote numerous works, including "Book of Jin," "Song Book," and "Four Tone Spectrum," though Gaozu disliked the latter.

Yao Cha, the Minister of Chen Libu, commented that during the Qi Dynasty's decline, Gaozu relied on Fan Yun and Shen Yue to strategize and complete his imperial career. Fan Yun was alert and capable, while Shen Yue was talented and knowledgeable, second only to Sima Qian and Dong Hu.
