Pei Sui, styled Yuanming, was a native of Wenxi in Hedong and a descendant of Pei Chuo, a Wei Dynasty governor. His grandfather Pei Shousun served as a former army commander under Emperor Wu of the Song Dynasty, and his father Pei Zhongmu was a general of cavalry. Pei Sui could write essays by age ten and was skilled in the *Zuo Shi Chun Qiu*. During the early Jianwu era of the Southern Qi Dynasty, Governor Xiao Yaochang recommended him as a state government governor. Xiao erected a monument at Bagongshan Temple in Shouyang County and commissioned Pei Sui to write the inscription, which was highly praised. After passing the imperial examination with top honors, Pei Sui was appointed as a minister. When Donghunhou ascended the throne, Xiao Yaoguang, the King of Shi'an, recommended Pei Sui as an army adjutant. After Xiao Yaoguang's defeat, Pei Sui returned to Shouyang, where Governor Pei Shuye surrendered to the Wei State. Pei Sui was forced to move north, but Emperor Xuanwu of Wei valued him and appointed him as Situ, Zhongshu Lang, and Prefect of Wei County. Pei Sui secretly planned to return south and, during the early Tianjian era of the Liang Dynasty, escaped from Wei. He was appointed as a consultant to the rear army and later requested to serve on the border, becoming General of the Auxiliary Country and Governor of Lujiang County. When Wei General Lu Po attacked Lujiang with 50,000 troops, Pei Sui defeated him and was promoted to General of the Right Army.

In the fifth year, during the expedition to Shaoyangzhou, Pei Sui built fortresses and won every battle against the Wei army's long bridge across the Huaihe River. Taking advantage of heavy rain and rising water levels, he led a shipborne assault on the bridge, defeating the Wei army. He then conquered Yangshi City, killing City Lord Yuan Kang, and captured Huoqiu City, beheading City Lord Ning Yongren. He also pacified Xiaoxian and captured Hefei. For his merits, Pei Sui was granted the title of Son of Yiling County with 300 households. He was later moved to Champion Changshi and Grand Administrator of Guangling County. After discussing the emperor's achievements in Wei Wu Temple, his wife's nephew Wang Zhuanzhi falsely reported him for disloyalty, leading to his demotion as Governor of Shi'an County. Determined to serve on the border, Pei Sui wrote to Lu Sengzhen, lamenting his situation as "Third Shi." Before reaching Shi'an, the Wei army attacked Suyu, and Pei Sui was ordered to resist, causing the Wei army to retreat. He later served as Governor of Tongling County, where he cultivated farmland for public and private benefit. He was eventually appointed as General Jiajie, Mingwei General, Xirong Xiaowei, and Governor of Beiliang and Qinzhou Prefectures, creating thousands of hectares of farmland and stabilizing the border. After returning to court, he held various high-ranking positions.

In the second year of the ordinary era, Yizhou Governor Wen Sengming rebelled and defected to Wei. Pei Sui led an army to attack, penetrating deep into Wei territory and surprising the enemy. He defeated the Wei-appointed Yizhou Governor Qian Zhou, pacifying Yizhou. He was awarded the title of Controller and Governor of Northern Xuzhou, later moving to Governor of Yuzhou, Northern Yuzhou, and Huozhou, based in Hefei. Four years later, he was promoted to General Xuanyi. During the Liang Dynasty's Northern Expedition, Pei Sui led 3,000 cavalry to attack Shouyang, fighting nine rounds in one day but withdrawing due to lack of support. He later reorganized his army, captured several cities, and massacred garrisons. In the following years, he captured Xincai County and expanded Liang territory. When Wei generals Chang Sun Zhi and Wang Yuanchen led 50,000 troops to challenge, Pei Sui used a feigned retreat to ambush and defeat them, beheading over 10,000. Chang Sun Zhi fled and held the city. In May of the same year, Pei Sui died in the military camp. He was posthumously given the title of Shizhong and Zuowei General, promoted to Marquis, and granted 700 households. His posthumous name was Lie.

Pei Sui was taciturn, deep-thinking, and strategic. He was tolerant in politics and won the soldiers' hearts. After his death, people in the Huaihe and Danshui regions mourned, believing that if he had not died, Luoyang would have been captured. His son Pei Zhili succeeded him, serving in various military and governmental roles. Pei Zhili's son Pei Zheng served as Minister of Huangmen. Pei Zhigao, Pei Sui's nephew, was a learned man who followed Pei Sui on expeditions and was highly regarded. He pacified Shouyang and was awarded titles. During the Hou Jing Rebellion, Pei Zhigao led troops to rescue the capital. He later served under Emperor Yuan of Liang and died at age seventy-three. His son Pei Ji fought and died when the Western Wei captured Jiangling. Pei Zhiping, Pei Zhigao's younger brother, also followed Pei Sui on expeditions and was named Dutinghou. He resisted Hou Jing and held various posts. Pei Zhiheng, another younger brother, was initially indulgent but later reclaimed wasteland and became wealthy. He served under Prince Xiao Gang and resisted Hou Jing, eventually dying in battle at age forty-one. He was posthumously given the title of Shizhong and Sikong Gong.

Xiahou Dan, styled Shilong, was the eldest son of Chariot General Xiahou Xiang. He began his official career in the Southern Qi Dynasty and served as an imperial minister. During Cui Huijing's rebellion, he was awarded General of Valiant Cavalry for protecting the emperor. When Emperor Gaozu raised an army, Xiahou Xiang and Xiao Yingzhou raised the righteous flag, and Xiahou Dan carried a warrant from Queen Xuande to order King Nankang to compile the throne. After Jiankang City was pacified, he served as Shangshu Libulang and later Shizhong, dedicating the emperor's jade seal to Gaozu. In the first year of Tianjian, he served as Prefect of Xuancheng County and later as a regular servant of Sanqi and General of Youxiaoqi. After his father's death, he observed mourning rites and gave away his inheritance to his younger brothers. In the eighth year, he was appointed as Chijie, supervising Sizhou's military affairs, and Governor of Sizhou. After the mourning period, he was granted the title of Duke of Fengcheng County. Xiahou Dan was dignified and helpful, favored by border people. He returned to court in the twelfth year and held various high-ranking positions, including Duguan Shangshu and General Shizhong. He served as Governor of Wuxing County, where he implemented benevolent policies, earning statues and stone tablets from the people. In the third year of the ordinary era, he was summoned as a regular servant of Sanqi and General of Youxiaoqi. He was dismissed due to official affairs but later reinstated.

In the sixth year of the Liang Dynasty's Northern Expedition, Pei Sui died while attacking Shouyang. Xiahou Dan replaced him and fought Wei generals, winning every battle. In the seventh year, the Huaihe River dike caused flooding, and Xiahou Dan, along with Zhan Sengzhi, defeated the Wei army. They attacked Lijing, and Wei Fang joined from the North Road. Together, they captured 52 cities, taking 75,000 prisoners and 200,000 shi of rice. Xiahou Dan was appointed as Governor of Yuzhou and the five states along the Huaihe River. He implemented policies to reduce penalties and taxes, allowing the people to prosper. In the second year of Datong, he was titled General Pingbei. He died in office three years later, and Emperor Wu of Liang mourned him, posthumously awarding him General of Chariots and Cavalry. His posthumous name was Xiang. The people petitioned to build a temple for him. Xiahou Dan was personable, generous, and well-read. He was frugal but loved music in his later years, keeping geishas. He had two sons, Xiahou Yi and Xiahou Sun, both of whom died in the Hou Jing Rebellion.

Xiahou Kui, styled Ji Long, was Xiahou Dan's younger brother. He began his official career in the Southern Qi Dynasty and served in various posts. After his father's death, he mourned and later took charge of building Tai Chi Hall. He served as an adjutant to Pei Sui during the expedition against Zhanye. He replaced his brother as Governor of Yuxing and later served as Governor of Sizhou. In the eighth year, he led an attack on the three passes of Pingping, Muling, and Yinshan, capturing them. He joined Zhan Sengzhi to cut off the Wei army's retreat, and Yuan Qinghe surrendered. Xiahou Kui gave credit to Zhan Sengzhi, who insisted Xiahou Kui accept the surrender. Xiahou Kui climbed the city wall, raised Liang banners, and accepted the surrender of over 40,000 people. He pursued Yuan Xianbo, capturing over 20,000. He later massacred Chu City, cutting off the North Road of Yiyang from Wei. In the second year of Datong, Wei Yuan Yuanda surrendered, and Xiahou Kui was appointed Governor of Beisi Prefecture. He was later granted the title of Marquis of Baocheng County. After his mother's death, he resigned but was soon appointed as Governor of Southern Yuzhou. In the sixth year, he was transferred to Governor of Yuzhou, where he built a dike in Canngling, irrigating over a thousand hectares and harvesting over one million shi of millet. Both Xiahou Kui and his brother Xiahou Dan were beloved by the people, who sang songs praising them. Xiahou Kui served for seven years, maintaining 10,000 troops and 2,000 horses. He was bold and extravagant, with hundreds of geishas and concubines, and enjoyed entertaining guests. He died in Yuzhou at age fifty-six, posthumously given the title of Shizhong and General Anbei. His son Xiahou Zan succeeded him, and his younger son Xiahou Fan was rough and later joined Hou Jing's rebellion, plundering the capital.

Yu Hong, from Xiangyang, was tall and handsome. He served as a vanguard in many expeditions and as Prefect of several counties. He was known for his hedonistic lifestyle, with over a hundred concubines and luxurious possessions. He died in office. Wei Fang, styled Yuan Zhi, was the son of General Wei Rui. He served as a chief clerk and later as Prefect of Xuyi County. He was granted the title of Marquis of Yongchang County and served as Grand Administrator of Jingling County. During the Northern Expedition, he led troops from the North Road to rendezvous in Shouchun. He attacked Guoyang, defeating the Wei army despite being outnumbered. He captured City Lord Wang Wei and took 4,200 prisoners. He later served as Governor of Liangzhou and Nanqinzhou, and died at age fifty-nine. Wei Fang was generous and loyal, especially to his brothers. He kept his promise to marry his children to the children of his deceased friend Zhang Su. His son Wei Can succeeded him. The historian comments that Pei Sui and Xiahou Dan were talented and accomplished officials, fitting for their civil and military roles, and should be remembered as famous officials of the Liang Dynasty.
