Liu Ji, also known as Huixu, was from Pengcheng. His father, Liu Sisi, rose to prominence in the Song Dynasty as a younger brother of Emperor Gaozu, reaching the rank of Jin Ziguanglu. Liu Ji had a strong reputation and served as an upright official early on. After Emperor Qi Gao ascended the throne, he killed many Song relatives, but Liu Ji was spared due to the intervention of Grand Prime Minister Chu Yuan. During the Jianyuan period, Liu Ji became Minister Zuocheng. In the Yongming era, he held various posts, including internal history of Jiangxia and Guangling counties. Later, he served as Huangmen minister and prince's concubine. During the Jianwu period, he was long history of Xiao Yaoxin and prefect of Lu Su.

Liu Ji's nephew, Ji Xing, was handsome and eloquent, valued by Luwei, but his arrogance led to conflict with Xiao Yaoxin. Liu Ji secretly reported Xiao Yaoxin to Emperor Ming, who then appointed Xiao Yaoxin as governor of Yongzhou. Grateful, Emperor Ming made Liu Ji auxiliary general and governor of Yizhou. Liu Ji's father had been a greedy governor in Yizhou, but the people treated Liu Ji well. Upon taking office, Liu Ji comforted old officials and wept with them. He appointed Gong Sui as chief administrator.

In the first year of Yongyuan, Liu Ji was recruited as right guard general but did not take office due to road blockages. Hearing of Marquis Shuhun's moral decay, Liu Ji became proud and cruel, causing local resentment. In September, he gathered 5,000 men, claiming to practice martial arts, but sent them to attack Zhongshui. The battle was unfavorable, and many counties rebelled. In October, Le Shichun and Li Nandang killed their governor, with Le Shichun naming himself governor of Nan Qinzhou and Li Nandang governor of Yizhou. In December, Liu Ji sent Cui Maozu with 2,000 soldiers, but they were defeated due to cold and blocked roads, with seven to eight tenths dead.

In the second year, further rebellions occurred. Yong Daoxi led 10,000 thieves to invade Brazil County, but was captured and beheaded by Lu Xiulie and Kui Lu. Li Fengbo pursued remaining thieves against advice and was defeated. In June, Cheng Yanyan rebelled and killed the prefect. In October, Zhao Xubo rebelled with 20,000 people, claiming divine mandate. Liu Ji defeated him with Zhao Yuetang and Li Fengbo. Later, Liu Ji's son, Liu Zhongse, responded to the Zhidan uprising.

In the spring of the next year, the emperor sent Chen Jiansun to escort Liu Ji's brother and sons, announcing the emperor's intent. Liu Ji prepared to return home. Emperor Gaozu appointed Deng Yuanqi as governor of Yiye. Deng Yuanqi, previously mistreated by Liu Ji, was advised by Zhu Daochen to check supplies first. Deng agreed. When Sai Dao arrived, he was disrespectful, causing panic. Liu Ji, fearing Deng would kill him, gathered troops again, killed Zhu Daochen, and summoned Li Ying and Zhu Shilue, who refused. Liu Ji killed Zhu Shilue's three sons.

In June of the first year of Tianjian, Deng Yuanqi arrived in Brazil County. Liu Ji sent Li Fengbo to resist, but after a prolonged battle, Li Fengbo retreated to Chengdu. Deng Yuanqi surrounded the city. In winter, a plot to surrender failed, and Liu Ji killed the conspirators. After two years of chaos, food ran out, and people starved or resorted to cannibalism. Liu Ji drank gruel for months. In the first month of the second year, Emperor Gaozu sent Zhao Jingyue to order Liu Ji's surrender. Liu Ji surrendered, and Deng Yuanqi treated him with courtesy. Liu Ji was pardoned but demoted to civilian. In the first month of the fourth year, he was killed by Lin Daogong, whose father he had killed in Shu.
