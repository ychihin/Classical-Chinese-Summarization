Xie Ju, courtesy name Yan Yang, was the younger brother of Xie Lan. He was an eager learner from a young age, skilled in discussing mystical principles, and shared fame with Xie Li. He began his career as Secretary Lang and later held various positions, including Prince Sheren and Cao Shi of Qingchao Gong. He was deeply appreciated by Prince Zhaoming. Emperor Gaozu once asked Xie Ju for advice, and Xie Ju humbly replied that his knowledge was far superior but he was not as good in drinking, which pleased the emperor. He served as General Ningyuan and Internal History of Yuzhang, where his tolerant administration won the people's hearts. He later held several high-ranking positions, including Minister of the Ministry of Personnel and Shizhong. Xie Ju was well-read, especially in Laozi, Zhuangzi, and Buddhist philosophy. He once corrected Lu Guang's errors during a lecture, earning Lu's respect. He died in the besieged capital during Hou Jing's invasion. His works were lost in the war.

He Jinrong, courtesy name Guoli, was born in Lujiang. He married Princess Changcheng and was awarded the title of Prince Consort. He served as Secretary Lang and later as Internal History of Jian'an, where he was praised for his honest and effective governance. He was known for his solemn demeanor and meticulous selection of officials. He served as Minister of the Ministry of Personnel and later as Shangshu Ling. He was diligent in government affairs, working day and night, but was despised by the world for his focus on administrative tasks rather than literary pursuits. He was convicted of deceiving the emperor for personal gain and was dismissed from office. He later died in the besieged capital during Hou Jing's rebellion. He Jinrong had a premonition of Hou Jing's rebellion, which came true. He was posthumously awarded General Renwei.
