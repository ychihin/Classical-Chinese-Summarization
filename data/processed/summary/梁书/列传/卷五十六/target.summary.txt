Hou Jing, courtesy name Wanjing, was from Shuofang, possibly Yanmen. Wild in youth, he became a strong, skilled horseman and archer, serving as a garrison in Beizhen. During Wei Xiaochang's first year, Xianyu Xiuli rebelled in Dingzhou, joined by Tujin Luozhou, amassing over 100,000 troops. After Xianyu Xiuli was killed, Ge Rong gathered his forces, killed Tujin Luozhou, and became known as "Ge Thief." In the fourth year, Emperor Wei Ming died, and Empress Dowager Hu ruled. Er Zhurong raised troops, killed her, and appointed Hou Jing as a general. Hou Jing served as vanguard against Ge Rong, capturing him alive, earning promotion to governor of Dingzhou and Duke of Puyang.

Hou Jing later surrendered to Gao Huan, becoming Emperor Shenwu of Qi. He was cruel but shared plunder with soldiers, ensuring loyalty. He held military power equal to Emperor Shenwu, controlling Henan with 100,000 troops. When Emperor Shenwu fell ill, he warned his son Gao Cheng of Hou Jing's cunning. Hou Jing, fearing for his life, surrendered to Liang's Emperor Gaozu in Taiqing's first year, citing Gao Cheng's threats. Emperor Gaozu accepted, making Hou Jing King of Henan. Qi's Murong Shaozong besieged Hou Jing, who sought Western Wei aid, forcing a retreat. Hou Jing then occupied Xuanhu and Xiangcheng.

Qi's Emperor Wenxiang wrote to Hou Jing, urging his return with promises of rank and family safety. Hou Jing replied, justifying his actions and rejecting the offer. In December, Hou Jing besieged Qiaocheng, failed, but captured Chengfu. He proposed selecting a Yuan descendant as Wei's lord for a Northern Expedition; Emperor Gaozu agreed. Qi's Murong Shaozong pursued Hou Jing to Woyang, where Hou Jing's army disintegrated due to starvation. He fled to Shouchun with 800 horses, where Emperor Gaozu treated him favorably, appointing him shepherd of Yuzhou.

In Shouchun, Hou Jing rebelled, conscripting residents and stopping taxes. He requested brocade for uniforms but received green cloth, which he used. After a defeat, he sought funds from the court, which were granted. In the second year, Emperor Gaozu discussed peace with Wei, alarming Hou Jing, who sent arrogant memorials opposing it. Poyang King Xiao Fan and others reported Hou Jing's rebellion intentions, but official Zhu Yi dismissed them. Hou Jing secretly allied with Linhe King Xiao Zhengde. In August, Hou Jing rebelled, attacking Ma Tou and Muzha. Emperor Gaozu ordered multiple governors to counter him.

In October, Hou Jing feigned an attack on Hefei but took Qiaozhou, capturing Fengcheng Hou Xiaotai. He crossed the Yangtze from Caishi with hundreds of horses and thousands of soldiers, unnoticed. He divided troops to attack Gushu, then reached Cisu. Defenders fled, and Hou Jing advanced to the palace city, burning gates. After failed assaults, he built a barrier, cutting off supplies. He established Xiao Zhengde as emperor in November, naming himself Prime Minister. Hou Jing attacked Dongfu City, capturing it and killing over 2,000 officials. He built earth mountains to monitor the city, forcing labor, with weak killed to fill them.

Reinforcements arrived, but Hou Jing defeated them. Food ran out, leading to cannibalism. Hou Jing sought peace, demanding four Yangtze-right states and hostages. An alliance was made, but Hou Jing broke it, attacking again. The palace city fell; Hou Jing plundered, captured officials, and burned corpses. He falsely issued edicts, demoted Xiao Zhengde, and appointed his own governors. Emperor Gaozu died in May, reportedly from anger after Hou Jing's actions. Hou Jing hid the death for over twenty days before proclaiming the crown prince emperor.

Hou Jing continued campaigns, killing Xiao Zhengde and appointing puppet kings. He faced rebellions, including Zu Hao's in Guangling, which he crushed, killing all residents. In Dabao's first year, he granted himself titles and privileges. He defeated various forces, capturing cities. In October, he killed Wulin Hou Xiao Zhen. He falsely issued an edict, granting himself the title "General of the Universe." Qi's Xin Shu besieged Yangping but retreated. Xu Wensheng defeated Hou Jing's navy, but Hou Jing later captured Yingzhou. He failed to take Baling due to disease and fled after Ren Yue's defeat.

Hou Jing deposed Taizong, imprisoning him, and established Xiao Dong as emperor. He killed Taizong with a silk cloth weighted with earth. In October, he forced Xiao Dong to abdicate, proclaiming himself emperor with the era name Taishi. He changed Liang's regulations to Han's, but his ignorance of ancestry caused ridicule. In the second year, his forces were defeated by Wang Sengbian. Hou Jing fled east with his sons in leather bags. At Hudouzhou, former prince's servant Yang Kun killed him. His body was displayed, and people ate his flesh, burned his bones, and mixed ashes with wine.

Hou Jing was under seven feet tall with handsome features but was cruel, often mutilating victims before death. He wore a white gauze hat and green robe, sometimes with a tooth comb in his bun. He rode a white horse; it neighed before victories and lowered its head before defeats. Emperor Gaozu's dream of中原 prefectures surrendering led him to accept Hou Jing, a decision later regretted. Omens, including a poem by Tao Hongjing and a prophecy about a dog and sheep, foretold Hou Jing's rise. A monk named Seng Tong, whom Hou Jing respected, made cryptic remarks about salt, foreshadowing Hou Jing's body being salted after death. Wang Wei, Hou Jing's adviser, was later cooked in the market. The historian noted that Hou Jing's cruelty surpassed previous usurpers, attributing his rise to divine timing and human folly.
