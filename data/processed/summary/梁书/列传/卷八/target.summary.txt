Prince Zhaoming Xiao Tong, the eldest son of Emperor Wu of Liang, was born in Xiangyang in September of the first year of Zhongxing in Qi. His mother was Ding Guibi. After Emperor Wu ascended the throne, Xiao Tong was made crown prince in November of the first year of Tianjian. He was intelligent from a young age, learning the classics by age five. In the fifth year, he moved to the East Palace but often missed his parents, visiting them every five days. In the eighth year, he explained the "Book of Filial Piety" to his father. He was crowned in the 14th year, wearing a Jinbo Mountain crown. Xiao Tong was handsome, well-behaved, and had a remarkable memory, composing poems effortlessly. He believed in Buddhism, built the Huiyi Hall for lectures, and authored the Two Truths Dharmakaya Sutra. In the seventh year, his mother fell ill; he cared for her tirelessly and mourned deeply after her death, eating only one liter of rice porridge daily despite imperial pleas. He lost significant weight. After his coronation, he handled state affairs with diligence, correcting errors without punishment, and showed leniency in judgments. He was generous, nurturing talent, and fostered a literary culture with over 30,000 volumes in the East Palace. He enjoyed nature, rejecting additions like female musicians. He lived frugally, aided the poor, and provided coffins for the dead. He opposed a canal project that would disrupt farming. Xiao Tong died on April 6 at age 31, mourned by all. He authored many works, including the "Selected Works." Prince Ai, named Renzong, was the eldest son of Taizong, born in May of the fourth year. He was granted titles and positions, and during Hou Jing's rebellion, he was made governor. After Taizong's ascension, he became crown prince. In the second year of Dashi, Hou Jing deposed Taizong and killed the prince, who faced death calmly. He was posthumously named Prince Ai. Prince Minghuai, Fang Ju, was the fourth son of Shizu, initially Marquis of Nan'an County. He held military and civil posts, was made prince, and later crown prince. He was killed by Wei soldiers. Yao Cha praised Zhaoming's virtues, comparing him to Shun.
