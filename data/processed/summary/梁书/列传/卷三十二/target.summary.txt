Chen Qingzhi, courtesy name Ziyun, from Yixingguoshan, served Emperor Gaozu from youth. Gaozu loved chess, playing tirelessly; while others tired and slept, Qingzhi remained alert and responsive, earning high regard. He followed Gaozu to pacify Jianye, served as chief secretary, and recruited soldiers with dispersed funds. He was granted an imperial invitation. In the ordinary years, Wei's Xuzhou governor Yuan Faseng sought attachment to Pengcheng. Qingzhi, as a powerful general, led Hu Longya and Cheng Jingjun to escort him. After returning, he became General Xuanmeng and Commander Wende, leading 2,000 troops to guard Xuzhou with Xiao Zong. Wei sent Prince Yanming of Anfeng and Prince Yuan of Linhuai with 20,000 troops to resist at Zhikou. Yanming's general Qiu Dagan built a fort at Xunliang; Qingzhi defeated him in a single battle. Later, King Yuzhang fled to Wei, causing a rout. Qingzhi cut bolts, breached the city, and retreated at night, saving his sergeants.

In the seventh ordinary year, General Yuan Shu of Anxi went to Shouchun. Wei's Yuzhou governor Li Xian sent his son to build forts; Qingzhi attacked, Li Xian surrendered, and Qingzhi occupied the city. He was transferred to Donggong Zhihe and titled Marquis of Guanzhong. In Datong's first year, a subordinate leader fortified and conquered Yan. General Changshan Wang Yuanzhao led 150,000 cavalry and infantry to rescue, reaching Tuojian, 40 miles from Guoyang. Qingzhi wanted to fight, but Tong Zhen advised caution. Qingzhi argued the Wei were tired and unsuspecting; he led 200 cavalry to defeat their front army. The Wei were horrified. Qingzhi then returned, and the generals attacked together, occupying Woyang City. From spring to winter, hundreds of battles ensued. The army grew exhausted, and Wei reinforcements threatened. Zhongzong and others planned retreat, but Qingzhi insisted on fighting, capturing four of thirteen Wei fortifications. Wang Wei of Woyang surrendered. Qingzhi displayed captured ears, beat drums, and attacked, causing the enemy to collapse. Over 30,000 men and women were captured. West Xuzhou was established on Guoyang's land. Emperor Gaozu praised Qingzhi in a personal letter.

In early Datong, Wei's Wang Yuanhao surrendered due to internal chaos, seeking to become lord of Wei. Emperor Gaozu appointed Qingzhi as Jiejie and Biaoyong General, sending Yuan Hao north. Yuan Hao declared himself Emperor of Wei at Huanshui. Qingzhi, as Chijie and commander, advanced from Liao County to Suiyang. Wei general Qiu Dagan had 70,000 troops in nine forts; Qingzhi captured three by afternoon, and Daqian surrendered. Wei's Jiyin Wang Yuanhuiye led 20,000 troops to rescue; Qingzhi built a fortress on water, captured the city, and seized 7,800 tax vehicles. Heading to Dafun, enemies surrendered at the sight of his flag. Qingzhi became General of the Guard and Governor of Lansu. He led forces west. Wei's Yang Yu, Xi'a Wang Yuanqing, and Yuan Xianggong led 70,000 troops to hold Rongyang. Qingzhi failed to capture it. Wei general Yuan Tianmu's army approached; Qingzhi, with only 7,000 troops against 300,000 captives, rallied his men, conquered Rongyang, and defeated Tianmu's army. He entered Hulao, and Zhu Shichen fled. Yuan Ziyou fled, and Yuan Hao entered Luoyang Palace, ascending the throne. Qingzhi was appointed servant and general, with increased households. Wei generals led 40,000 troops to capture Daliang; Qingzhi's white-robed subordinates were invincible. A Luoyang nursery rhyme warned: "Thousands of troops should avoid white robes." From Zhixian to Luoyang, in 140 days, Qingzhi leveled 32 fortresses and fought 47 battles, unstoppable.

Yuan Hao indulged in wine and sex, plotting against Qingzhi. Qingzhi planned secretly but was alienated. Yuan Hao refused to send more troops. Qingzhi's deputy warned of danger, suggesting killing Yuan Hao, but Qingzhi refused. Yuan Hao appointed Qingzhi governor of Xuzhou but feared him and didn't send him. Wei's Ji Zhufu and others, claiming millions, attacked Yuan Hao. Qingzhi crossed the Yellow River to defend Beizhonglang City, fighting eleven times in three days. Ji Zhurong nearly retreated but was advised to stay. He crossed the river, defeated Yuan Hao, and Luoyang fell. Qingzhi, with thousands, formed a formation and returned east. Ji Zhurong pursued; Songgao Mountain flooded, scattering his soldiers. Qingzhi shaved his head, became a monk, and traveled to Yuzhou. He was secretly sent to the capital, awarded General Youwei and Yongxing County Fortress. Later, as Chijie and governor of Northern Yanzhou, he defeated the demon thief monk Tan Qiang, killing him in twelve days. In Zhongdatong's second year, he became military governor of four states. He opened 6,000 hectares of land, filling warehouses. In Datong's second year, Wei's Hou Jing invaded Chuzhou; Qingzhi defeated him in snowy conditions. He was promoted to General Renwei. During a famine in Yuzhou, he opened warehouses for relief. He died at fifty-six, posthumously honored. Qingzhi was cautious, wore no silk, disliked music, and comforted his soldiers well.

His son Chen Zhao was heir. Fifth son Chen Ting, courtesy name Junzhang, could ride and shoot at seven. At twelve, he followed his father to Luoyang but fell ill. He impressed Zhu Yi by drawing maps in soil. In Datong's fourth year, he was appointed Changshi and leader of the right guard, sent to Yiyang. He defeated Yao Xiong, a fierce Wei general. In the sixth year, he was titled General Weiyuan and Lord of Xiaoxian. In the tenth year, he pacified the demon thief Wang Qinzong. In Taiqing's second year, Hou Jing surrounded Liyang. Chen Ting advised guarding the quarry but was captured by Hou Jing. Hou Jing tried to use him, but Chen Ting refused. He was killed by Hou Jing at thirty-three. Lanqin, courtesy name Xiu Ming, was from Zhongchang Wei. His father Lan Yun served as governor of Jizhou. Lanqin was decisive and agile, following his father in the northern expedition. In Datong's first year, he attacked Wei Xiao City and defeated various generals. He captured Juegu and defeated Liu Haiyou. He pacified barbarians in Guiyang and Yangshan, was titled male in Anhuai County. He defeated Duiqi Manshuai and rescued Yuan Qinghe. He was granted command of four states, captured Tongsheng, and pacified Luan and Yi. He was promoted to General Zhiwu. Later, as governor of Hengzhou, he defeated Wei's Dong Shao and Zhang Xian. He governed Guangzhou, defeating Chen Wenche. In Hengzhou, he was promoted to General Zinan and Duke of Qujiang County. Officials requested a monument for his virtues. He was recruited as a regular servant but was poisoned by a cook at forty-two. His son Zi Xia Li attacked Hou Jing at Liyang and died. The historian comments that Chen Qingzhi and Lanqin rivaled ancient generals in military strategy. Qingzhi was intelligent and served Zhidan long, earning honors through caution and respect.
